# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Najm ClinicNote V2 is for doctors and internal clinical testers who need a fast, structured way to draft documentation from a chosen workflow during limited internal testing. The typical user is already clinically trained, working quickly, and wants less interface friction while still keeping documentation reviewable and safe.

## Product Purpose
Najm ClinicNote V2 turns a large structured workflow library into a calmer doctor-facing drafting tool: choose a workflow, enter clinician-confirmed findings, and generate a clinician-review draft. It exists to reduce documentation friction during internal testing without acting as clinical decision support, and success looks like faster draft creation, clearer workflow selection, and fewer unsafe or confusing interface moments.

## Brand Personality
Calm, trustworthy, focused. The product should feel like a serious clinical productivity workspace rather than a generic AI assistant. Its tone should support concentration, reduce noise, and reinforce that outputs remain clinician-review drafts.

## Anti-references
Avoid bright, playful AI-SaaS styling, hype-driven “magic” language, crowded dashboards, neon-heavy gradients, and consumer-health visual tropes that imply diagnosis or autonomous recommendations. Do not make the product feel experimental, whimsical, or gamified.

## Design Principles
1. Put the doctor workflow first: workflow search, structured input, and draft review should feel immediate and obvious.
2. Keep safety visible without panic: warnings should be clear, persistent, and professional.
3. Reward scanability over decoration: hierarchy, spacing, and chunking should reduce cognitive load.
4. Make documentation intent explicit: selected findings, suggested defaults, and outputs should always feel reviewable rather than automatic.
5. Stay conservative where the product is conservative: excluded workflows, testing-only language, and local-only draft behavior should remain easy to understand.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target a clear, readable interface with strong contrast in dark mode, visible keyboard focus states, restrained motion, generous hit areas, and layouts that remain usable on mobile and desktop. The interface should support quick comprehension during clinical work and avoid reliance on color alone to communicate safety or state.
