<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg109-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>This guideline refers to different types of syncope. See the <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#terms-used-in-this-guideline" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#terms-used-in-this-guideline">section on terms used in this guideline for definitions</a>.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Initial assessment" id="cg109-initial-assessment">
    <h3 class="title" id="initial-assessment">1.1 Initial assessment</h3>
    <div class="section" title="1.1.1 Gathering information about the event and initial decision making" id="cg109-gathering-information-about-the-event-and-initial-decision-making">
      <h4 class="title" id="gathering-information-about-the-event-and-initial-decision-making">1.1.1 Gathering information about the event and initial decision making</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person with suspected <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#transient-loss-of-consciousness-tloc" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#transient-loss-of-consciousness-tloc">transient loss of consciousness (TLoC)</a> has sustained an injury or they have not made a full recovery of consciousness, use clinical judgement to determine appropriate management and the urgency of treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask the person who has had the suspected TLoC, and any witnesses, to describe what happened before, during and after the event. Try to contact by telephone witnesses who are not present. Record details about:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>circumstances of the event</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>person's posture immediately before loss of consciousness</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prodromal symptoms (such as sweating or feeling warm/hot) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>appearance (for example, whether eyes were open or shut) and colour of the person during the event</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>presence or absence of movement during the event (for example, limb-jerking and its duration) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any tongue-biting (record whether the side or the tip of the tongue was bitten) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>injury occurring during the event (record site and severity)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>duration of the event (onset to regaining consciousness)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>presence or absence of confusion during the recovery period</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>weakness down 1 side during the recovery period.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When recording a description of the suspected TLoC from the patient or a witness, take care to ensure that their communication and other needs are taken into account. This is particularly important when communicating with a child or young person, or person with special communication needs.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="section" title="Determining whether the person had TLoC" id="cg109-determining-whether-the-person-had-tloc">
        <h5 class="title" id="determining-whether-the-person-had-tloc">Determining whether the person had TLoC</h5>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Use information gathered from all accounts of the suspected TLoC (see recommendation 1.1.1.2) to confirm whether or not TLoC has occurred. If this is uncertain it should be assumed that they had TLoC until proven otherwise. But, if the person did not have TLoC, instigate suitable management (for example, if the person is determined to have had a fall, rather than TLoC, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg161" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg161">NICE's guideline on falls in older people: assessing risk and prevention</a>). </p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.1.2 Obtaining patient history, physical examination and tests" id="cg109-obtaining-patient-history-physical-examination-and-tests">
      <h4 class="title" id="obtaining-patient-history-physical-examination-and-tests">1.1.2 Obtaining patient history, physical examination and tests</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess and record:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>details of any previous TLoC, including number and frequency</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the person's medical history and any family history of cardiac disease (for example, personal history of heart disease and family history of sudden cardiac death)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>current medication that may have contributed to TLoC (for example, diuretics)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>vital signs (for example, pulse rate, respiratory rate and temperature) – repeat if clinically indicated</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>lying and standing blood pressure if clinically appropriate</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other cardiovascular and neurological signs.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Record a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#lead-electrocardiogram-ecg" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#lead-electrocardiogram-ecg">12-lead electrocardiogram (ECG)</a> using automated interpretation. Treat as a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#red-flags" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#red-flags">red flag</a> (see recommendation 1.1.4.2) if any of the following abnormalities are reported on the ECG printout:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>conduction abnormality (for example, complete right or left bundle branch block or any degree of heart block)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>evidence of a long or short QT interval <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any ST segment or T wave abnormalities. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a 12-lead ECG with automated interpretation is not available, take a manual 12-lead ECG reading and have this reviewed by a healthcare professional trained and competent in identifying the following abnormalities:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>inappropriate persistent <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#bradycardia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#bradycardia">bradycardia</a></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any ventricular <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#arrhythmia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#arrhythmia">arrhythmia</a> (including ventricular ectopic beats)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>long QT (corrected QT more than 450 ms) and short QT (corrected QT less than 350 ms) intervals</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#brugada-syndrome" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#brugada-syndrome">Brugada syndrome</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ventricular pre-excitation (part of Wolff-Parkinson-White syndrome)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>left or right ventricular hypertrophy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>abnormal T wave inversion</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pathological Q waves</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>atrial arrhythmia (sustained)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>paced rhythm.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If during the initial assessment, there is suspicion of an underlying problem causing TLoC, or additional to TLoC, carry out <strong>relevant</strong> examinations and investigations (for example, check blood glucose levels if diabetic hypoglycaemia is suspected, or haemoglobin levels if anaemia or bleeding is suspected; see also <a class="link" href="recommendations#suspected-epilepsy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#suspected-epilepsy">recommendation 1.2.2.1 for information about the use of electroencephalogram [EEG]</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.1.3 Recording the event information and transfer of records" id="cg109-recording-the-event-information-and-transfer-of-records">
      <h4 class="title" id="recording-the-event-information-and-transfer-of-records">1.1.3 Recording the event information and transfer of records</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Record carefully the information obtained from all accounts of the TLoC. Include paramedic records with this information. Give copies of the ECG record and the patient report form to the receiving clinician when care is transferred, and to the person who had the TLoC. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.1.4 Making a judgement based on initial assessment" id="cg109-making-a-judgement-based-on-initial-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="making-a-judgement-based-on-initial-assessment">1.1.4 Making a judgement based on initial assessment</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Red flags: people requiring urgent assessment and treatment" id="cg109-red-flags-people-requiring-urgent-assessment-and-treatment">
        <h5 class="title" id="red-flags-people-requiring-urgent-assessment-and-treatment">Red flags: people requiring urgent assessment and treatment</h5>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.1</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If TLoC is secondary to a condition that requires immediate action, use clinical judgement to determine appropriate management and the urgency of treatment.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.2</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Refer urgently for cardiovascular assessment, with the referral reviewed and prioritised by an appropriate <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#specialist" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#specialist">specialist</a> within 24 hours, anyone with TLoC who also has any of the following:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>an ECG abnormality (see recommendations 1.1.2.2 and 1.1.2.3)</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>heart failure (history or physical signs)</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>TLoC during exertion</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>family history of sudden cardiac death in people aged younger than 40 years and/or an inherited cardiac condition</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>new or unexplained breathlessness</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a heart murmur.<br /><br />Consider referring within 24 hours for cardiovascular assessment, as above, anyone aged older than 65 years who has experienced TLoC without prodromal symptoms.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="No further immediate management required" id="cg109-no-further-immediate-management-required">
        <h5 class="title" id="no-further-immediate-management-required">No further immediate management required</h5>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.3</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Diagnose uncomplicated <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#faint" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#faint">faint</a> (uncomplicated <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#vasovagal-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#vasovagal-syncope">vasovagal syncope</a>) on the basis of the initial assessment when:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>there are no features that suggest an alternative diagnosis (note that brief seizure activity can occur during uncomplicated faints and is not necessarily diagnostic of epilepsy) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>there are features suggestive of uncomplicated faint (the 3 'P's) such as:</p>
                <p>
                  <div class="itemizedlist">
                    <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>
                          <strong>P</strong>osture – prolonged standing, or similar episodes that have been prevented by lying down</p>
                      </li>
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>
                          <strong>P</strong>rovoking factors (such as pain or a medical procedure)</p>
                      </li>
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>
                          <strong>P</strong>rodromal symptoms (such as sweating or feeling warm/hot before TLoC).</p>
                      </li>
                    </ul>
                  </div>
                </p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Diagnose <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#situational-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#situational-syncope">situational syncope</a> on the basis of the initial assessment when: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>there are no features from the initial assessment that suggest an alternative diagnosis <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>syncope is clearly and consistently provoked by straining during micturition (usually while standing) or by coughing or swallowing.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If a diagnosis of uncomplicated faint or situational syncope is made, and there is nothing in the initial assessment to raise clinical or social concern, no further immediate management is required. If the presentation is not to the GP, the healthcare professional should:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>advise the person to take a copy of the patient report form and the ECG record to their GP</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>inform the GP about the diagnosis, directly if possible; if an ECG has not been recorded, the GP should arrange an ECG (and its interpretation as described in <a class="link" href="recommendations#obtaining-patient-history-physical-examination-and-tests" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#obtaining-patient-history-physical-examination-and-tests">recommendation 1.1.2.3</a>) within 3 days. </p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Further immediate management required" id="cg109-further-immediate-management-required">
        <h5 class="title" id="further-immediate-management-required">Further immediate management required</h5>
        <article id="cg109-1_1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4.6</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If the person presents to the ambulance service, take them to the emergency department unless a diagnosis of an uncomplicated faint or situational syncope is clear.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Further assessment and referral" id="cg109-further-assessment-and-referral">
    <h3 class="title" id="further-assessment-and-referral">1.2 Further assessment and referral</h3>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.1 Suspected postural hypotension" id="cg109-suspected-postural-hypotension">
      <h4 class="title" id="suspected-postural-hypotension">1.2.1 Suspected postural hypotension</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_2_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suspect <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#postural-hypotension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#postural-hypotension">postural hypotension</a> on the basis of the initial assessment when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there are no features suggesting an alternative diagnosis <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the history is typical.<br /><br />See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/chapter/Recommendations#postural-hypotension" target="_top">recommendations 1.1.5 to 1.1.7 of NICE's guideline on hypertension</a> for details of how to measure and manage postural hypotension. <strong>[2010, amended 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_2_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If blood pressure measurements do not confirm postural hypotension despite suggestive symptoms refer the person for further specialist cardiovascular assessment. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.2 Suspected epilepsy" id="cg109-suspected-epilepsy">
      <h4 class="title" id="suspected-epilepsy">1.2.2 Suspected epilepsy</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_2_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people who present with 1 or more of the following features (that is, features that are strongly suggestive of epileptic seizures) for an assessment by a specialist in epilepsy; the person should be seen by the specialist within 2 weeks (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217">NICE's guideline on epilepsies in children, young people and adults</a>):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a bitten tongue</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>head-turning to 1 side during TLoC</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>no memory of abnormal behaviour that was witnessed before, during or after TLoC by someone else</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>unusual posturing</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prolonged limb-jerking (note that brief seizure-like activity can often occur during uncomplicated faints)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>confusion following the event</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prodromal <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#déjà-vu" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#déjà-vu">déjà vu</a>, or <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#jamais-vu" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#jamais-vu">jamais vu</a>. <br /><br />Consider that the episode may not be related to epilepsy if any of the following features are present:</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prodromal symptoms that on other occasions have been abolished by sitting or lying down</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>sweating before the episode</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prolonged standing that appeared to precipitate the TLoC</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pallor during the episode. <br /><br />Do not routinely use EEG in the investigation of TLoC (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217">NICE's guideline on epilepsies in children, young people and adults</a>).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.3 Referral for specialist cardiovascular assessment" id="cg109-referral-for-specialist-cardiovascular-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-for-specialist-cardiovascular-assessment">1.2.3 Referral for specialist cardiovascular assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_2_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer all people with TLoC (apart from the exceptions below) for a specialist cardiovascular assessment by the most appropriate local service. Exceptions are: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with a firm diagnosis, after the initial assessment, of:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>uncomplicated faint</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>situational syncope</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>postural hypotension</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people whose presentation is strongly suggestive of epileptic seizures. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Specialist cardiovascular assessment and diagnosis" id="cg109-specialist-cardiovascular-assessment-and-diagnosis">
    <h3 class="title" id="specialist-cardiovascular-assessment-and-diagnosis">1.3 Specialist cardiovascular assessment and diagnosis</h3>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.1 Assessment and assignment to type of syncope" id="cg109-assessment-and-assignment-to-type-of-syncope">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment-and-assignment-to-type-of-syncope">1.3.1 Assessment and assignment to type of syncope</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Carry out a specialist cardiovascular assessment as follows:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Reassess the person's:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>detailed history of TLoC including any previous events</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>medical history and any family history of cardiac disease or an inherited cardiac condition</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>drug therapy at the time of TLoC and any subsequent changes.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Conduct a clinical examination, including full cardiovascular examination and, if clinically appropriate, measurement of lying and standing blood pressure.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Repeat 12-lead ECG and obtain and examine previous ECG recordings. <br /><br />On the basis of this assessment, assign the person to 1 of the following suspected causes of syncope.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>suspected <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#structural-heart-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#structural-heart-disease">structural heart disease</a></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>suspected cardiac arrhythmic</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>suspected neurally mediated</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>unexplained. <br /><br />Offer further testing as directed by <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">recommendations 1.3.2.1 to 1.3.2.10</a> or other tests as clinically appropriate.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with suspected structural heart disease, investigate appropriately (for example, cardiac imaging). Because other mechanisms for syncope are possible in this group, also <br />consider investigating for a cardiac arrhythmic cause (as <br />described in <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">recommendation 1.3.2.4</a>), and for postural hypotension (often caused/exacerbated by drug therapy – see <a class="link" href="recommendations#suspected-postural-hypotension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#suspected-postural-hypotension">recommendation 1.2.1.1</a>) or for <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#neurally-mediated-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#neurally-mediated-syncope">neurally mediated syncope</a> (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">recommendations 1.3.2.5 and 1.3.2.6</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.2 Diagnostic tests for different types of syncope" id="cg109-diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">
      <h4 class="title" id="diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">1.3.2 Diagnostic tests for different types of syncope</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use the person's history to distinguish people whose <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#exercise-induced-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#exercise-induced-syncope">exercise-induced syncope</a> occurred during exercise (when a cardiac arrhythmic cause is probable) from those whose syncope occurred shortly after stopping exercise (when a vasovagal cause is more likely). </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who have experienced syncope during exercise, offer urgent (within 7 days) exercise testing, unless there is a possible contraindication (such as suspected aortic stenosis or hypertrophic cardiomyopathy requiring initial assessment by imaging). Advise the person to refrain from exercise until informed otherwise following further assessment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the mechanism for exercise-induced syncope is identified by exercise testing, carry out further investigation or treatment as appropriate in each individual clinical context. Otherwise, carry out further investigations assuming a suspected cardiac arrhythmic cause.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with a suspected cardiac arrhythmic cause of syncope, offer an ambulatory ECG and do not offer a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#tilt-test" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#tilt-test">tilt test</a> as a first-line investigation. The type of ambulatory ECG offered should be chosen on the basis of the person's history (and, in particular, frequency) of TLoC. For people who have:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TLoC at least several times a week, offer <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#holter-monitorrecorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#holter-monitorrecorder">Holter monitoring</a> (up to 48 hours if necessary). If no further TLoC occurs during the monitoring period, offer an <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#external-event-recorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#external-event-recorder">external event recorder</a> that provides continuous recording with the facility for the patient to indicate when a symptomatic event has occurred.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TLoC every 1 to 2 weeks, offer an external event recorder. If the person experiences further TLoC outside the period of external event recording, offer an <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#implantable-event-recorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#implantable-event-recorder">implantable event recorder</a>. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TLoC infrequently (less than once every 2 weeks), offer an implantable event recorder. A Holter monitor should not usually be offered unless there is evidence of a conduction abnormality on the 12-lead ECG.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer a tilt test to people who have a diagnosis of vasovagal syncope on initial assessment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with suspected vasovagal syncope with recurrent episodes of TLoC adversely affecting their quality of life, or representing a high risk of injury, consider a tilt test only to assess whether the syncope is accompanied by a severe cardioinhibitory response (usually <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#asystole" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#asystole">asystole</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with suspected <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#carotid-sinus-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#carotid-sinus-syncope">carotid sinus syncope</a> and for people with unexplained syncope who are aged 60 years or older, offer <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#carotid-sinus-massage" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#carotid-sinus-massage">carotid sinus massage</a> as a first-line investigation. This should be conducted in a controlled environment, with ECG recording, and with resuscitation equipment available.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Diagnose carotid sinus syncope if carotid sinus massage reproduces syncope due to marked bradycardia/asystole and/or marked hypotension. Do not diagnose carotid sinus syncope if carotid sinus massage causes asymptomatic transient bradycardia or hypotension (see recommendation 1.3.2.9).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For all people with unexplained syncope (including after negative carotid sinus massage test in those for whom this is appropriate), offer ambulatory ECG (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnostic-tests-for-different-types-of-syncope">recommendation 1.3.2.4</a>). Do not offer a tilt test before the ambulatory ECG. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_3_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering a person an implantable event recorder, provide one that has both patient-activated and automatic detection modes. Instruct the person and their family and/or carer how to operate the device. Advise the person that they should have prompt follow-up (data interrogation of the device) after they have any further TLoC. The timing of the follow-up is dependent on the storage on the device and the condition of the person.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 If the cause of TLoC remains uncertain" id="cg109-if-the-cause-of-tloc-remains-uncertain">
    <h3 class="title" id="if-the-cause-of-tloc-remains-uncertain">1.4 If the cause of TLoC remains uncertain</h3>
    <article id="cg109-1_4_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If a person has persistent TLoC, consider <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#psychogenic-non-epileptic-seizures-pnes" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#psychogenic-non-epileptic-seizures-pnes">psychogenic non-epileptic seizures (PNES)</a> or psychogenic <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#pseudosyncope" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#pseudosyncope">pseudosyncope</a> if, for example:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the nature of the events changes over time</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>there are multiple unexplained physical symptoms</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>there are unusually prolonged events.<br /><br />The distinction between epilepsy and non-epileptic seizures is complex; therefore, refer for neurological assessment if either PNES or psychogenic pseudosyncope is suspected.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg109-1_4_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Advise people who have experienced TLoC to try to record any future events (for example, a video recording or a detailed witness account of the event), particularly if the diagnosis is unclear or taking a history is difficult.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg109-1_4_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If after further assessment the cause of TLoC remains uncertain or the person has not responded to treatment, consider other causes including the possibility that more than 1 mechanism may coexist (for example, <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#ictal-arrhythmia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#ictal-arrhythmia">ictal arrhythmias</a>).</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Information for people with TLoC" id="cg109-information-for-people-with-tloc">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-for-people-with-tloc">1.5 Information for people with TLoC</h3>
    <div class="section" title="1.5.1 General information" id="cg109-general-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="general-information">1.5.1 General information</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When communicating with the person who had TLoC, discuss the:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>possible causes of their TLoC</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>benefits and risks of any tests they are offered</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>results of tests they have had</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>reasons for any further investigations they are offered</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>nature and extent of uncertainty in the diagnosis.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.5.2 Driving" id="cg109-driving">
      <h4 class="title" id="driving">1.5.2 Driving</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give advice about eligibility to drive when a person first presents with TLoC. Please refer to the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/neurological-disorders-assessing-fitness-to-drive" target="_top">Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) guidance on neurological disorders: assessing fitness to drive</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise all people who have experienced TLoC that they must not drive while waiting for a specialist assessment. Following specialist assessment, the healthcare professional should advise the person of their obligations regarding reporting the TLoC event to the DVLA. Please refer to the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/neurological-disorders-assessing-fitness-to-drive" target="_top">DVLA guidance on neurological disorders: assessing fitness to drive</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.5.3 Health and safety at work" id="cg109-health-and-safety-at-work">
      <h4 class="title" id="health-and-safety-at-work">1.5.3 Health and safety at work</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people who have experienced TLoC of the implications of their episode for health and safety at work and any action they must take to ensure the safety of themselves and that of other people. Please refer to <a class="link" href="http://www.hse.gov.uk/legislation/hswa.htm" target="_top">Health and Safety Executive's Health and Safety at Work etc Act 1974</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.5.4 Safety advice for people who have had TLoC" id="cg109-safety-advice-for-people-who-have-had-tloc">
      <h4 class="title" id="safety-advice-for-people-who-have-had-tloc">1.5.4 Safety advice for people who have had TLoC</h4>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with an uncomplicated faint (uncomplicated vasovagal syncope) or situational syncope:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>explain the mechanisms causing their syncope</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>advise on possible trigger events, and strategies for avoiding them; if the trigger events are unclear, advise people to keep a record of their symptoms, when they occur and what they were doing at the time, in order to understand what causes them to faint</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>reassure them that their prognosis is good</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>advise them to consult their GP if they experience further TLoC, particularly if this differs from their recent episode.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with postural hypotension:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>explain the mechanisms causing their syncope</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss and review possible causes, especially drug therapy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss the prognostic implications and treatment options available</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>advise people what to do if they experience another TLoC.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people waiting for a specialist cardiovascular assessment:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what they should do if they have another event</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if appropriate, how they should modify their activity (for example, by avoiding physical exertion if relevant) and not to drive. Please refer to the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/guidance/neurological-disorders-assessing-fitness-to-drive" target="_top">DVLA guidance on neurological disorders: assessing fitness to drive</a>. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg109-1_5_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer advice to people waiting for specialist neurological assessment for their TLoC as recommended in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng217">NICE's guideline on epilepsies in children, young people and adults</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
