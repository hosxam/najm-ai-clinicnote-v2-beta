<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng75-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off‑label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Weight loss in the early days of life" id="ng75-weight-loss-in-the-early-days-of-life">
    <h3 class="title" id="weight-loss-in-the-early-days-of-life">1.1 Weight loss in the early days of life</h3>
    <p>Some weight loss in the first days after birth (referred to in this guideline as the early days of life) is normal and usually relates to body fluid adjustments. Sometimes there may be reason for concern about weight loss in the early days of life, which may need assessment and intervention. For this reason weight loss in the early days of life is dealt with separately in this guideline from concerns about inadequate weight gain in older <a class="link" href="recommendations#infant" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#infant">infants</a> and <a class="link" href="recommendations#child" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#child">children</a>, which is often related to nutritional intake.</p>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>it is common for infants to lose some weight during the early days of life</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>this weight loss usually stops after about 3 or 4 days of life</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>most infants have returned to their birth weight by 3 weeks of age.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If infants in the early days of life lose more than 10% of their birth weight:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>perform a clinical assessment, looking for evidence of dehydration, or of an illness or disorder that might account for the weight loss</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>take a detailed history to assess feeding (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194">NICE's guideline on postnatal care</a>)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>consider direct observation of feeding</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>ensure observation of feeding is done by a person with appropriate training and expertise (for example, in relation to breastfeeding and bottle feeding)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>perform further investigations only if they are indicated based on the clinical assessment.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide feeding support (see recommendations in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194">NICE's guideline on postnatal care</a>) if there is concern about weight loss in infants in the early days of life, for example if they have lost more than 10% of their birth weight.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If infants lose more than 10% of their birth weight in the early days of life, or they have not returned to their birth weight by 3 weeks of age, consider:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>referral to paediatric services if there is evidence of illness, marked weight loss, or failure to respond to feeding support (see recommendations in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194">NICE's guideline on postnatal care</a>) </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>when to reassess if not referred to paediatric services.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If an infant loses more than 10% of their birth weight in the early days of life, measure their weight again at appropriate intervals depending on the level of concern, but no more frequently than daily.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that supplementary feeding with infant formula in a breastfed infant may help with weight gain, but often results in cessation of breastfeeding.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If supplementation with an infant formula is given to a breastfed infant:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>support the mother to continue breastfeeding</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>advise expressing breast milk to promote milk supply <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>feed the infant with any available breast milk before giving any infant formula.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Faltering growth after the early days of life" id="ng75-faltering-growth-after-the-early-days-of-life">
    <h3 class="title" id="faltering-growth-after-the-early-days-of-life">1.2 Faltering growth after the early days of life</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Thresholds" id="ng75-thresholds">
      <h4 class="title" id="thresholds">Thresholds</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using the following as thresholds for concern about faltering growth in infants and children (a centile space being the space between adjacent centile lines on the <a class="link" href="http://www.rcpch.ac.uk/growthcharts/" target="_top">UK WHO growth charts</a>):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a fall across 1 or more weight centile spaces, if birthweight was below the 9th centile</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a fall across 2 or more weight centile spaces, if birthweight was between the 9th and 91st centiles</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a fall across 3 or more weight centile spaces, if birthweight was above the 91st centile</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>when current weight is below the 2nd centile for age, whatever the birthweight.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Measurement of weight and height or length" id="ng75-measurement-of-weight-and-height-or-length">
      <h4 class="title" id="measurement-of-weight-and-height-or-length">Measurement of weight and height or length</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there is concern about faltering growth (for example, based on the criteria in recommendation 1.2.1):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>weigh the infant or child</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>measure their length (from birth to 2 years old) or height (if aged over 2 years)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>plot the above measurements and available previous measurements on the <a class="link" href="http://www.rcpch.ac.uk/growthcharts/" target="_top">UK WHO growth charts</a> to assess weight change and linear growth over time.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there are concerns about an infant's length or a child's length or height, if possible obtain the biological parents' heights and work out the mid-parental height centile. If the child's length or height centile is below the range predicted from parental heights (more than 2 centile spaces below the mid-parental centile) be aware this could suggest <a class="link" href="recommendations#undernutrition" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#undernutrition">undernutrition</a> or a primary growth disorder.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there is concern about faltering growth or <a class="link" href="recommendations#linear-growth" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#linear-growth">linear growth</a> in a child over 2 years of age, determine the BMI centile:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>using the UK WHO centiles and the accompanying BMI centile 'look‑up chart' <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>by calculating the BMI (weight in kg/height in metres squared) and plotting this on the <a class="link" href="http://www.rcpch.ac.uk/child-health/research-projects/uk-who-growth-charts/uk-growth-chart-resources-2-18-years/school-age" target="_top">BMI centile chart</a>.<br /><br />Then:</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the BMI is below the 2nd centile, be aware this may reflect either undernutrition or a small build</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the BMI is below the 0.4th centile, this suggests probable undernutrition that needs assessment and intervention.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Record all growth measurements in the parent- or carer-held Personal Child Health Record.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Assessment" id="ng75-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment">Assessment</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there is concern about faltering growth:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>perform a clinical, developmental and social assessment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>take a detailed feeding or eating history</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider direct observation of feeding or meal times</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider investigating for:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>urinary tract infection (follow the principles of assessment in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng224" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng224">NICE's guideline on urinary tract infection in under 16s</a>)</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>coeliac disease, if the diet has included gluten-containing foods (follow the principles of assessment in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20">NICE's guideline on coeliac disease</a>)</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>perform further investigations only if they are indicated based on the clinical assessment.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If observation of eating or feeding is needed because of concern about faltering growth, ensure this is done by a person with appropriate training and expertise.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that the following factors may be associated with faltering growth:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>preterm birth</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>neurodevelopmental concerns</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>maternal postnatal depression or anxiety.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Recognise that in faltering growth:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a range of factors may contribute to the problem, and it may not be possible to identify a clear cause</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there may be difficulties in the interaction between an infant or child and the parents or carers that may contribute to the problem, but this may not be the primary cause.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Based on the feeding history and any direct observation of feeding, consider whether any of the following are contributing to faltering growth in milk-fed infants:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ineffective suckling in breastfed infants</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ineffective bottle feeding</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>feeding patterns or routines being used</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the feeding environment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#food-or-feeding-aversion" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#food-or-feeding-aversion">feeding aversion</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>parent/carer–infant interactions</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how parents or carers respond to the infant's feeding cues</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>physical disorders that affect feeding.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Based on the feeding history and any direct observation of mealtimes, consider whether any of the following are contributing to faltering growth:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>mealtime arrangements and practices</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>types of foods offered</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#food-or-feeding-aversion" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#food-or-feeding-aversion">food aversion</a> and avoidance</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>parent/carer–child interactions, for example responding to the child's mealtime cues</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>appetite, for example a lack of interest in eating</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>physical disorders that affect feeding.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider asking the parents or carers of infants and children with faltering growth to keep a diary recording food intake (types and amounts) and mealtime issues (for example, settings, behaviour) to help inform management strategies and assess progress.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that investigations (other than those recommended in recommendation 1.2.6) are unlikely to reveal an underlying disorder in a child with faltering growth who appears well with no other clinical concerns.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a child with faltering growth develops new clinical symptoms or signs after the initial assessment, reconsider whether investigations are needed.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Interventions for faltering growth" id="ng75-interventions-for-faltering-growth">
      <h4 class="title" id="interventions-for-faltering-growth">Interventions for faltering growth</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Together with parents and carers, establish a management plan with specific goals for every infant or child where there are concerns about faltering growth. This plan could include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>assessments or investigations</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>interventions</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>clinical and growth monitoring</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>when reassessment to review progress and achievement of growth goals should happen.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide feeding support (see recommendations in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng194">NICE's guideline on postnatal care</a>) if there is concern about faltering growth in the first weeks of life. Consider whether such feeding support might be helpful in older milk-fed infants, including those having complementary solid foods.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that while supplementary feeding with infant formula may increase weight gain in a breastfed infant if there is concern about faltering growth, it often results in cessation of breastfeeding.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If supplementation with an infant formula is given to a breastfed infant because of concern about faltering growth after the early days of life:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>support the mother to continue breastfeeding</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>advise expressing breast milk to promote milk supply <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>feed the infant with any available breast milk before giving any infant formula.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When there are concerns about faltering growth, discuss the following, as individually appropriate, with the infant's or child's parents or carers:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>encouraging relaxed and enjoyable feeding and mealtimes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>eating together as a family or with other children</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>encouraging young children to feed themselves</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>allowing young children to be 'messy' with their food</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>making sure feeds and mealtimes are not too brief or too long</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>setting reasonable boundaries for mealtime behaviour while avoiding punitive approaches</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>avoiding coercive feeding</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>establishing regular eating schedules (for example 3 meals and 2 snacks in a day).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If necessary, based on the assessment, advise on food choices for infants and children that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are appropriate to the child's developmental stage in terms of quantity, type and food texture</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>optimise energy and nutrient density.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In infants or children who need a further increase in the nutrient density of their diet beyond that achieved through advice on food choices, consider:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>short-term dietary fortification using energy-dense foods</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>referral to a paediatric dietitian.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise the parents or carers of infants or children with faltering growth that drinking too many energy-dense drinks, including milk, can reduce a child's appetite for other foods.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.23</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider a trial of an <a class="link" href="recommendations#oral-liquid-nutritional-supplement" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#oral-liquid-nutritional-supplement">oral liquid nutritional supplement</a> for infants or children with continuing faltering growth despite other interventions (see recommendations 1.2.16 to 1.2.22).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.24</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Regularly reassess infants and children receiving an oral nutritional supplement for faltering growth to decide if it should be continued. Take into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>weight change</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>linear growth</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>intake of other foods</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>tolerance</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adherence</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the views of parents or carers.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_25" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.25</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Only consider enteral tube feeding for infants and children with faltering growth when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there are serious concerns about weight gain <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an appropriate specialist multidisciplinary assessment for possible causes and contributory factors has been completed <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other interventions have been tried without improvement.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_26" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.26</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If enteral tube feeding is to be used in an infant or child with faltering growth, make a plan with appropriate multidisciplinary involvement for:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the goals of the treatment (for example, reaching a specific weight target)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the strategy for its withdrawal once the goal is reached (for example, progressive reduction together with strategies to promote oral intake).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Monitoring" id="ng75-monitoring">
      <h4 class="title" id="monitoring">Monitoring</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_27" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.27</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there are concerns about faltering growth (see recommendation 1.2.1), measure the weight at appropriate intervals taking account of factors such as age and the level of concern, but usually no more often than:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>daily if less than 1 month old</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>weekly between 1–6 months old</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>fortnightly between 6–12 months</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>monthly from 1 year of age.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_28" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.28</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Monitor weight if there are concerns about faltering growth (see recommendation 1.2.1), but be aware that weighing children more frequently than is needed (see recommendation 1.2.27) may add to parental anxiety (for example, minor short-term changes may cause unnecessary concern).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_29" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.29</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that weight loss is unusual except in the early days of life, and may be a reason for increased concern and more frequent weighing than is recommended (see recommendation 1.2.27).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_30" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.30</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there are concerns about faltering growth monitor length or height at intervals, but no more often than every 3 months.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Referral" id="ng75-referral">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral">Referral</h4>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_31" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.31</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If an infant or child with faltering growth has any of the following discuss with, or refer to, an appropriate paediatric specialist care service:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms or signs that may indicate an underlying disorder</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a failure to respond to interventions delivered in a primary care setting</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>slow linear growth or unexplained short stature (see recommendation 1.2.3)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>rapid weight loss or severe undernutrition</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>features that cause safeguarding concerns (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg89" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg89">NICE guideline on child maltreatment</a>).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng75-1_2_32" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.32</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not admit infants or children with faltering growth to hospital unless they are acutely unwell or there is a specific indication requiring inpatient care, such as a plan to begin tube feeding (see recommendation 1.2.25).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Organisation of care" id="ng75-organisation-of-care">
    <h3 class="title" id="organisation-of-care">1.3 Organisation of care</h3>
    <article id="ng75-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure there is a pathway of care for infants and children where there are concerns about faltering growth or weight loss in the early days of life that:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>clearly sets out the roles of healthcare professionals in primary and secondary care settings</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>establishes and makes clear the process for referral to and coordination of specialist care in the pathway.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide community-based care for infants and children where there are faltering growth concerns or weight loss in the early days of life with a team (the 'primary care team') that includes, for example:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>a midwife</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>a health visitor</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>a GP.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that the primary care team has access to the following healthcare professionals with expertise relevant to faltering growth:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>infant feeding specialist</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>consultant paediatrician</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>paediatric dietitian</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>speech and language therapist with expertise in feeding and eating difficulties</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>clinical psychologist</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>occupational therapist.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider identifying a lead healthcare professional to coordinate care and to act as the first point of contact for parents of children with faltering growth, for example if several professionals are involved.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Information and support" id="ng75-information-and-support">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support">1.4 Information and support</h3>
    <article id="ng75-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Recognise the emotional impact that concerns about faltering growth or weight loss in the early days can have on parents and carers and offer them information about available:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>professional support</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>peer support.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Follow the principles in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">NICE guidelines on patient experience in NHS services</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng204" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng204">babies, children and young people's experience of healthcare</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">shared decision making</a> in relation to communication (including different formats and languages), information and shared decision making.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide information on faltering growth or weight loss in the early days of life, to parents or carers that is:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>specific to them and their child</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>clearly explained and understandable to them</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>spoken and in writing.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng75-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If there is concern about faltering growth in an infant or child or weight loss in the early days of life, discuss with the parents or carers:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the reasons for the concern, and how the growth measurements are interpreted</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>any worries or issues they may have</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>any possible or likely causes or factors that may be contributing to the problem</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the management plan (see recommendation 1.2.15).</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>

  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng75-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Child" id="ng75-child">
      <h4 class="title" id="child">Child</h4>
      <p>Pre-school children from 1 year of age.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Food or feeding aversion" id="ng75-food-or-feeding-aversion">
      <h4 class="title" id="food-or-feeding-aversion">Food or feeding aversion</h4>
      <p>Behaviours sometimes observed in infants or children indicating a persistent unwillingness to eat. Such behaviours, depending upon age, might include signs of distress when presented with food, spitting of food or avoidance behaviour.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Infant" id="ng75-infant">
      <h4 class="title" id="infant">Infant</h4>
      <p>A baby up to 1 year of age.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Linear growth" id="ng75-linear-growth">
      <h4 class="title" id="linear-growth">Linear growth</h4>
      <p>This is the increase in length (under 2 years of age) or height (2 years or older) over time in infants and children.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Oral liquid nutritional supplement" id="ng75-oral-liquid-nutritional-supplement">
      <h4 class="title" id="oral-liquid-nutritional-supplement">Oral liquid nutritional supplement</h4>
      <p>A high-energy liquid feed designed for enteral use, usually selected and prescribed after specialist advice from a paediatric dietitian.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Undernutrition" id="ng75-undernutrition">
      <h4 class="title" id="undernutrition">Undernutrition</h4>
      <p>This is what happens when nutrition is not sufficient. An infant or child with undernutrition may be abnormally thin, may weigh less than expected for their length or height, and if prolonged, undernutrition can lead to stunting (length or height less than expected for age).</p>
    </div>
