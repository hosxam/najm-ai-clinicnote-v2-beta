<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg184-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>These recommendations apply to adults (aged 18 and over) with symptoms of dyspepsia, symptoms suggestive of gastro-oesophageal reflux disease (GORD), or both.</p>
  <p>In this guideline, GORD refers to endoscopically determined oesophagitis or endoscopy-negative reflux disease.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 The community pharmacist" id="cg184-the-community-pharmacist">
    <h3 class="title" id="the-community-pharmacist">1.1 The community pharmacist</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Community pharmacists should offer initial and ongoing help for people with symptoms of dyspepsia. This includes advice about lifestyle changes, using over-the-counter medication, help with prescribed drugs and advice about when to consult a GP. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Community pharmacists should record adverse reactions to treatment and may participate in primary care medication review clinics. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Common elements of care" id="cg184-common-elements-of-care">
    <h3 class="title" id="common-elements-of-care">1.2 Common elements of care</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer simple lifestyle advice, including advice on healthy eating, weight reduction and smoking cessation. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Advise people to avoid known precipitants they associate with their dyspepsia where possible. These include smoking, alcohol, coffee, chocolate, fatty foods and being overweight. Raising the head of the bed and having a main meal well before going to bed may help some people. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide people with access to educational materials to support the care they receive. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Recognise that psychological therapies, such as cognitive behavioural therapy and psychotherapy, may reduce dyspeptic symptoms in the short term in individual people. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Encourage people who need long-term management of dyspepsia symptoms to reduce their use of prescribed medication stepwise: by using the effective lowest dose, by trying 'as-needed' use when appropriate, and by returning to self-treatment with antacid and/or alginate therapy (unless there is an underlying condition or comedication that needs continuing treatment). <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Referral guidance for endoscopy" id="cg184-referral-guidance-for-endoscopy-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="referral-guidance-for-endoscopy-2">1.3 Referral guidance for endoscopy</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people presenting with dyspepsia together with significant acute gastrointestinal bleeding, refer them immediately (on the same day) to a specialist. Also see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg141" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG141">NICE guideline on acute upper gastrointestinal bleeding in over 16s: management</a>. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review medications for possible causes of dyspepsia (for example, calcium antagonists, nitrates, theophyllines, bisphosphonates, corticosteroids and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs [NSAIDs]). In people needing referral, suspend NSAID use. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Think about the possibility of cardiac or biliary disease as part of the differential diagnosis. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If people have had a previous endoscopy and do not have any new alarm signs, consider continuing management according to previous endoscopic findings. <strong>[2004]<br /><br /></strong>For more information about alarm signs and when to refer people to specialists when they present with symptoms that could be caused by cancer, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12">NICE guideline on suspected cancer: recognition and referral</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Interventions for uninvestigated dyspepsia" id="cg184-interventions-for-uninvestigated-dyspepsia-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="interventions-for-uninvestigated-dyspepsia-2">1.4 Interventions for uninvestigated dyspepsia</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that dyspepsia in unselected people in primary care is defined broadly to include people with recurrent epigastric pain, heartburn or acid regurgitation, with or without bloating, nausea or vomiting. Also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#common-elements-of-care" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#common-elements-of-care">section on common elements of care</a>. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Leave a 2‑week washout period after proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use before testing for Helicobacter pylori (hereafter referred to as H pylori) with a breath test or a stool antigen test. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer empirical full-dose PPI therapy (see table 1 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) for 4 weeks to people with dyspepsia<strong>. [2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer H pylori 'test and treat' to people with dyspepsia.<strong> [2004]</strong></p>

  <div class="section" title="1.7 Interventions for peptic ulcer disease" id="cg184-interventions-for-peptic-ulcer-disease-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="interventions-for-peptic-ulcer-disease-2">1.7 Interventions for peptic ulcer disease</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer H pylori eradication therapy to people who have tested positive for H pylori and who have peptic ulcer disease. Also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#helicobacter-pylori-testing-and-eradication" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#helicobacter-pylori-testing-and-eradication">section on H pylori testing and eradication</a>. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people using NSAIDs with diagnosed peptic ulcer, stop the use of NSAIDs where possible. Offer full-dose PPI (see table 1 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) or H<sub>2</sub>RA therapy for 8 weeks and, if H pylori is present, subsequently offer eradication therapy. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer people with gastric ulcer and H pylori repeat endoscopy 6 to 8 weeks after beginning treatment, depending on the size of the lesion. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer people with peptic ulcer (gastric or duodenal) and H pylori retesting for H pylori 6 to 8 weeks after beginning treatment, depending on the size of the lesion. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer full-dose PPI (see table 1 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) or H<sub>2</sub>RA therapy for 4 to 8 weeks to people who have tested negative for H pylori who are not taking NSAIDs. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people continuing to take NSAIDs after a peptic ulcer has healed, discuss the potential harm from NSAID treatment. Review the need for NSAID use regularly (at least every 6 months) and offer a trial of use on a limited, 'as-needed' basis. Consider reducing the dose, substituting an NSAID with paracetamol, or using an alternative analgesic or low-dose ibuprofen (1.2 g daily). <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>In people at high risk (previous ulceration) and for whom NSAID continuation is necessary, consider a COX‑2 selective NSAID instead of a standard NSAID. In either case, prescribe with a PPI. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>In people with an unhealed ulcer, exclude non-adherence, malignancy, failure to detect H pylori, inadvertent NSAID use, other ulcer-inducing medication and rare causes such as Zollinger–Ellison syndrome or Crohn's disease. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If symptoms recur after initial treatment, offer a PPI to be taken at the lowest dose possible to control symptoms. Discuss using the treatment on an 'as-needed' basis with people to manage their own symptoms. <strong>[2004, amended 2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg184-1_7_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer H<sub>2</sub>RA therapy if there is an inadequate response to a PPI. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.9.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people who have had previous exposure to clarithromycin and metronidazole a 7‑day course of treatment with:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a PPI (see table 3 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>amoxicillin <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>tetracycline (or, if a tetracycline cannot be used, levofloxacin). <br /><strong>[new 2014, amended 2019]</strong><br /><br />In September 2024, this was an off-label use of levofloxacin. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg184-1_9_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.9.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people who are allergic to penicillin (and who have not had previous exposure to a fluoroquinolone antibiotic) a 7‑day, twice-daily course of treatment with:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a PPI (see table 3 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>metronidazole <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>levofloxacin.<br /><br />For the assessment of allergy to beta-lactam antibiotics and referral to specialist care, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg183" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG183">NICE guideline on drug allergy</a>. <strong>[new 2014, amended 2019]</strong><br /><br />In September 2024, this was an off-label use of levofloxacin. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.<br /><br />See the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/fluoroquinolone-antibiotics-must-now-only-be-prescribed-when-other-commonly-recommended-antibiotics-are-inappropriate" target="_top">MHRA January 2024 advice on restrictions and precautions for using fluoroquinolone antibiotics</a> because of the risk of disabling and potentially long-lasting or irreversible side effects. </p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg184-1_9_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.9.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people who are allergic to penicillin and who have had previous exposure to a fluoroquinolone antibiotic a 7‑day course of:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a PPI (see table 3 in <a class="link" href="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors" data-original-url="#appendix-a-dosage-information-on-proton-pump-inhibitors">appendix A</a>) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>bismuth <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>metronidazole <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>tetracycline.<br /><br />For the assessment of allergy to beta-lactam antibiotics and referral to specialist care, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg183" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG183">NICE guideline on drug allergy</a>. <strong>[new 2014, amended 2019]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="cg184-1_9_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.9.12</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Seek advice from a gastroenterologist if eradication of H pylori is not successful with second-line treatment. <strong>[new 2014]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.10 Laparoscopic fundoplication" id="cg184-laparoscopic-fundoplication">
    <h3 class="title" id="laparoscopic-fundoplication">1.10 Laparoscopic fundoplication</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_10_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.10.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider laparoscopic fundoplication for people who have:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>a confirmed diagnosis of acid reflux and adequate symptom control with acid suppression therapy, but who do not wish to continue with this therapy long term</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>a confirmed diagnosis of acid reflux and symptoms that are responding to a PPI, but who cannot tolerate acid suppression therapy. <strong>[new 2014]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.11 Referral to a specialist service" id="cg184-referral-to-a-specialist-service-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="referral-to-a-specialist-service-2">1.11 Referral to a specialist service</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_11_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.11.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider referral to a specialist service for people:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>of any age with gastro-oesophageal symptoms that are non-responsive to treatment or unexplained (in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12">NICE guideline on suspected cancer: recognition and referral</a>, 'unexplained' is defined as 'symptoms or signs that have not led to a diagnosis being made by the healthcare professional in primary care after initial assessment [including history, examination and any primary care investigations]')</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>with suspected GORD who are thinking about surgery</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>with H pylori that has not responded to second-line eradication therapy. <strong>[new 2014]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.12 Surveillance for people with Barrett's oesophagus" id="cg184-surveillance-for-people-with-barretts-oesophagus-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="surveillance-for-people-with-barretts-oesophagus-2">1.12 Surveillance for people with Barrett's oesophagus</h3>
    <article id="cg184-1_12_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.12.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider surveillance to check progression to cancer for people who have a diagnosis of Barrett's oesophagus (confirmed by endoscopy and histopathology), taking into account:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the presence of dysplasia (also see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng231" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng231">NICE guideline on Barrett's oesophagus and stage 1 oesophageal adenocarcinoma</a>)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the person's individual preference</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the person's risk factors (for example, male gender, older age and the length of the Barrett's oesophagus segment).<br /><br />Emphasise that the harms of endoscopic surveillance may outweigh the benefits in people who are at low risk of progression to cancer (for example, people with stable non-dysplastic Barrett's oesophagus). <strong>[new 2014] </strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
