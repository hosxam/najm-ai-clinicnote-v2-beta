<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng238-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Identifying and assessing cardiovascular disease risk for people without established cardiovascular disease" id="ng238-identifying-and-assessing-cardiovascular-disease-risk-for-people-without-established-cardiovascular">
    <h3 class="title" id="identifying-and-assessing-cardiovascular-disease-risk-for-people-without-established-cardiovascular">1.1 Identifying and assessing cardiovascular disease risk for people without established cardiovascular disease</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Identifying people for full formal risk assessment" id="ng238-identifying-people-for-full-formal-risk-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="identifying-people-for-full-formal-risk-assessment">Identifying people for full formal risk assessment</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For the primary prevention of cardiovascular disease (CVD) in primary care, use a systematic strategy to identify people who are likely to be at high risk of CVD. <strong>[2008, amended 2014]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Prioritise people based on an estimate of their CVD risk before doing a full formal risk assessment. Estimate their CVD risk using CVD risk factors already recorded in primary care electronic medical records. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Review estimates of CVD risk on an ongoing basis for people over 40. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Prioritise people for a full formal risk assessment if their estimated 10‑year risk of CVD is 10% or more. <strong>[2008, amended 2014]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss the process of risk assessment with the person identified as being at risk, including the option of declining any formal risk assessment. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use opportunistic assessment as the main strategy in primary care to identify CVD risk in unselected people. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Full formal risk assessment" id="ng238-full-formal-risk-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="full-formal-risk-assessment">Full formal risk assessment</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use the QRISK3 tool to calculate the estimated CVD risk within the next 10 years for people aged between 25 and 84 without CVD. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use the QRISK3 tool for people with type 2 diabetes aged between 25 and 84. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--primary">
        <p>
          <strong>Practical information about using the QRISK3 tool</strong>
        </p>
        <p>Until electronic clinical systems in which QRISK2 is embedded are updated with QRISK3, it may be necessary to use QRISK2.</p>
        <p>When assessing risk for people taking corticosteroids or atypical antipsychotics or people with systemic lupus erythematosus, migraine, <a class="link" href="recommendations#severe-mental-illness" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#severe-mental-illness">severe mental illness</a> or erectile dysfunction, use QRISK3 because QRISK2 does not take these risk factors into account and may underestimate the 10‑year CVD risk in these populations.</p>
      </div>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use a risk assessment tool for people who are at high risk of CVD, including people with: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>type 1 diabetes (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#statin-treatment-for-people-with-type-1-diabetes" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#statin-treatment-for-people-with-type-1-diabetes">section on primary prevention of CVD for people with type 1 diabetes</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an estimated glomerular filtration rate less than 60 ml per minute per 1.73 m<sup>2</sup> and/or albuminuria (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#statins-for-primary-and-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease-in-people-with-chronic-kidney" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#statins-for-primary-and-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease-in-people-with-chronic-kidney">section on primary and secondary prevention of CVD for people with chronic kidney disease [CKD]</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>familial hypercholesterolaemia (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg71" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg71">NICE's guideline on familial hypercholesterolaemia</a>) or other inherited disorders of lipid metabolism. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Recognise that CVD risk tools may underestimate risk in certain groups of people, including but not limited to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people treated for HIV</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people already taking medicines to treat CVD risk factors</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people who have recently stopped smoking</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people taking medicines that can cause dyslipidaemia, such as immunosuppressant drugs</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#severe-mental-illness" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#severe-mental-illness">severe mental illness</a></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with autoimmune disorders, and other systemic inflammatory disorders. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider people aged 85 or older to be at increased risk of CVD because of age alone, particularly people who smoke or have raised blood pressure. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The committee agreed that the evidence suggested QRISK3 performed best among tools evaluated in a UK population to assess the risk of a person without established CVD having a CVD event within the next 10 years. They agreed that no tool is very good at accurately discriminating between who will and who will not have a CVD event, but that tools are useful for guiding decisions about interventions to prevent CVD based on estimated risk. </p>
            <p>A small amount of evidence suggested that the additional fields included in QRISK3 (such as severe mental illness, regular corticosteroid use and atypical antipsychotic use) enabled the tool to perform better than QRISK2 at predicting CVD events for people with these risk factors. Use of QRISK3 should, therefore, result in more people within these groups being appropriately considered for risk reduction approaches including statin treatment. </p>
            <p>It was noted that the group of people with severe mental illness used to develop and validate QRISK3 included a high proportion of people with severe and moderate depression. This is reflected in the definition of severe mental illness in QRISK3 but does not reflect the definition used in electronic clinical systems in primary care. The committee agreed, informed by their clinical experience and expert opinion, that people with moderate to severe depression are not considered to have as great an increased risk of CVD as people with schizophrenia, bipolar disorder and other psychoses. By using data that grouped these conditions together, QRISK3 may underestimate CVD risk for people with schizophrenia, bipolar disorder and other psychoses. Despite this the committee agreed to recommend use of the tool for people with severe mental illness (however defined), but clinical judgement should inform interpretation.</p>
            <p>The committee was aware that the <a class="" href="https://www.healthcheck.nhs.uk/commissioners-and-providers/national-guidance/" target="_top">NHS Health Check best practice guidance</a> states that gender should be recorded as reported by the individual. If the individual discloses gender reassignment, they should be provided with CVD risk calculations based on both genders and advised to discuss with their GP which calculation is most appropriate for them as an individual. They agreed that healthcare professionals are expected to follow this guidance when undertaking formal risk assessments. </p>
            <p>An age range is given for QRISK3 because it is only intended for people aged between 25 and 84 years (inclusive). </p>
            <p>The committee agreed that the use of a risk tool remains appropriate in people with type 2 diabetes to support shared decision making. They agreed, based on the evidence, that QRISK3 performed better than QRISK2 for the population as whole and so should be used for people with type 2 diabetes. </p>
            <p>The committee agreed to remove a 2014 recommendation to complete as many fields of the risk assessment tool as possible because QRISK3 explains that the tool can overestimate risk if fields are left blank. They also noted that BMI, ethnicity and family history of CVD should be recorded in people's medical records, so the committee agreed that the 2014 recommendation on recording this information was no longer needed.</p>
            <p>Evidence on the performance of QRISK3 was not considered sufficient to suggest changing the 2014 recommendations against using a CVD risk tool in people with type 1 diabetes or CKD as it had not been validated in a separate population from that in which the tool was developed. The committee agreed these groups should be considered high risk, as should people with familial hypercholesterolaemia. </p>
            <p>Based on their clinical experience, the committee identified a list of factors for which all CVD risk tools underestimate risk. They highlighted the importance of using clinical judgement to interpret risk scores. As risk scores are used to guide decisions about interventions to prevent CVD, the committee agreed it was particularly important to ensure people are not incorrectly considered to be at low risk. </p>
            <p>The 2014 recommendation to consider people aged 85 and older to be at increased risk of CVD was retained as the committee agreed with this statement and there are still no tools for this age group. The committee highlighted it is important that this group be considered for interventions to prevent CVD even though a formal risk assessment would not be carried out.</p>
            <p>The evidence for lifetime risk tools was not considered sufficient to recommend their use instead of 10‑year risk tools. However, the committee agreed they can have value in communication of risk. See the <a class="invalid-link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/proxy/?sourceUrl=_Communication_about_risk" target="_top" data-original-url="_Communication_about_risk">section on communication about risk assessment, lifestyle changes and treatment</a>.</p>
            <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/a-cvd-risk-assessment-tools-primary-prevention-pdf-13253901661" target="_top">evidence review A: CVD risk assessment tools: primary prevention</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>QRISK2 is currently integrated into electronic clinical systems, so 10‑year CVD risk assessments can be generated using data already available in a person's electronic records. At the time of development, the committee was aware of ongoing discussions about continuation of the inclusion of QRISK in electronic clinical systems.</p>
            <p>Using QRISK3 instead of QRISK2 will require clinical systems to be updated by software developers for the impact on practice to be minimised. <a class="" href="https://www.healthcheck.nhs.uk/commissioners-and-providers/national-guidance/" target="_top">Public Health England issued guidance in August 2021 on using QRISK3 in NHS health checks and how to deal with the transition period</a> (responsibility for the NHS Health Check programme has transferred to the Office for Health Improvement and Disparities, but the guidance produced by Public Health England remains current). </p>
            <p>QRISK3 requires some additional clinical information that was not required for QRISK2. However, if integrated into electronic clinical systems, QRISK3 is not likely to require additional resources over QRISK2. There may be some implementation costs as healthcare professionals become familiar with the additional information included in QRISK3 and in managing the transition period. </p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
    </div>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>To encourage the person to participate in reducing their CVD risk:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>find out what, if anything, the person has already been told about their CVD risk and how they feel about it</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>explore the person's beliefs about what determines future health (this may affect their attitude to changing risk)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">

            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>inform them of potential future management options based on current evidence and best practice</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>involve them in developing a shared management plan</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>check that they have understood what has been discussed. <strong>[2008, amended 2014]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person's CVD risk is at a level where treatment is recommended but they decline the offer of treatment, advise them that their CVD risk should be reassessed in the future. Record their choice in their medical records. <strong>[2008, amended 2014]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The committee agreed that the evidence did not support using lifetime CVD risk assessment tools to guide decisions on the need for statin treatment because their accuracy could not be reliably assessed. </p>
            <p>However, the committee noted that the usefulness of lifetime risk tools is primarily in communicating risk. They agreed by consensus that lifetime risk tools should be considered to help inform discussions about risk and motivate lifestyle changes. The committee highlighted that these tools may underestimate the ongoing benefit of lipid-lowering treatments as they do not predict risk reduction from taking medicines, and noted this should be considered when interpreting the results. They agreed lifetime risk calculation would not be necessary for everyone, but it may be particularly useful for people with a QRISK3 score less than 10% or under 40s who have CVD risk factors.</p>
            <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/a-cvd-risk-assessment-tools-primary-prevention-pdf-13253901661" target="_top">evidence review A: CVD risk assessment tools: primary prevention</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>Lifetime risk tools are not routinely used in current clinical practice. The committee noted that there may be resource implications for calculating lifetime risk score estimates because lifetime risk tools are not currently embedded into electronic clinical systems, so scores are not automatically generated. There may also be implementation costs related to educating healthcare professionals about lifetime risk calculators. It is not clear if use of lifetime risk tools will result in longer consultations.</p>
            <p>The committee agreed that the online calculators for lifetime risk tools such as QRISK-lifetime were easy to complete and provided some interpretation of the risk scores to aid discussions, but acknowledged that lifetime risk assessment would not be done for everyone. </p>
            <p>The committee believe that using lifetime risk tools may have a long-term benefit in encouraging people to participate in lifestyle changes or engage in treatment, if appropriate. Given this, any additional time costs were considered likely to improve management of CVD risk and reduce future CVD events.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
    </div>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer for urgent specialist review if a person has a triglyceride level of more than 20 mmol per litre that is not a result of excess alcohol intake or poor glycaemic control. <strong>[2014]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng238-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>In people with a triglyceride level between 10 mmol and 20 mmol per litre: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>repeat the triglyceride measurement with a fasting test (after an interval of 5 days, but within 2 weeks) <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>review for potential secondary causes of hyperlipidaemia <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>seek specialist advice if the triglyceride level remains at more than 10 mmol per litre. <strong>[2014]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng238-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>In people with a triglyceride level between 4.5 mmol and 9.9 mmol per litre: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>be aware that the CVD risk may be underestimated by risk assessment tools <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>optimise the management of other CVD risk factors present <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>seek specialist advice if non-HDL cholesterol level is more than 7.5 mmol per litre. <strong>[2014]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>

    <h3 class="title" id="discussions-and-assessment-before-starting-statins">1.5 Discussions and assessment before starting statins</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Discuss risks and benefits of statins" id="ng238-discuss-risks-and-benefits-of-statins">
      <h4 class="title" id="discuss-risks-and-benefits-of-statins">Discuss risks and benefits of statins</h4>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Make decisions about starting statin treatment after an informed discussion between the clinician and the person about the risks and benefits of statins. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023]</strong></p>

      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take into account potential benefits from lifestyle changes, the person's preferences, the presence of any comorbidities, whether they are on multiple medications, whether they are frail and their life expectancy. (See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56">NICE's guideline on multimorbidity</a>.) <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023]</strong></p>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people who are being offered a statin that the risk of muscle pain, tenderness or weakness associated with statin use is small and the rate of severe muscle adverse effects (rhabdomyolysis) because of statins is extremely low. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Discuss possible interactions between statins and other substances" id="ng238-discuss-possible-interactions-between-statins-and-other-substances">

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>

          <p>Advise people who are being treated with a statin:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">

            <li class="listitem">

    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Perform baseline blood tests and clinical assessment" id="ng238-perform-baseline-blood-tests-and-clinical-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="perform-baseline-blood-tests-and-clinical-assessment">Perform baseline blood tests and clinical assessment</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Before starting statins perform baseline blood tests and clinical assessment. Include all of the following in the assessment:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>smoking status</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>alcohol consumption</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>blood pressure (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136">NICE's guideline on hypertension in adults</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>BMI or other measure of obesity (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246">NICE's guideline on overweight and obesity management</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#full-lipid-profile" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#full-lipid-profile">full lipid profile</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>diabetes status</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>renal function</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>transaminase level (alanine aminotransferase or aspartate aminotransferase)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>thyroid-stimulating hormone level in people with symptoms of underactive or overactive thyroid. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely exclude from statin treatment people who have liver transaminase levels that are raised but are less than 3 times the upper limit of normal. <strong>[May 2023] </strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Before offering a statin, ask the person if they have had persistent generalised unexplained muscle symptoms (pain, tenderness or weakness), whether associated or not with previous lipid-lowering treatment. If they have, measure creatine kinase levels. If creatine kinase levels are:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>more than 5 times the upper limit of normal, re-measure creatine kinase after 7 days; if creatine kinase levels are still 5 times the upper limit of normal, do not start statin treatment (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#treatment-if-statins-are-contraindicated-or-not-tolerated" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#treatment-if-statins-are-contraindicated-or-not-tolerated">section on treatment if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>raised but less than 5 times the upper limit of normal, start statin treatment at a lower dose. <strong>[May 2023] </strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>New evidence on adverse effects while on statins supported the 2014 recommendation. Evidence on the risk of muscle pain and rhabdomyolysis with statin use demonstrated a real effect, but the large body of evidence showed this was a very small increased risk when compared with similar populations not on statins; that is, when using <a class="" href="recommendations#high-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#high-intensity-statin">high-intensity statins</a> approximately 16% of people reported experiencing muscle pain, but of these cases only around 1 in 12 were likely to be due to the statin. </p>
            <p>The committee agreed to strengthen the recommendation to reassure people that the risk of these adverse effects occurring is low. </p>
            <p>The evidence supported the other 2014 recommendations, so they were retained. The committee agreed a <a class="" href="recommendations#full-lipid-profile" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#full-lipid-profile">full lipid profile</a> should be provided before starting statins and amended the recommendation on lipid measures accordingly. See the <a class="invalid-link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/proxy/?sourceUrl=_Assessing_response_to" target="_top" data-original-url="_Assessing_response_to">section on assessing response to treatment</a> for further details. </p>
            <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/c-statins-efficacy-and-adverse-effects-pdf-13253901663" target="_top">evidence review C: statins: efficacy and adverse effects</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The recommendation on adverse effects has been strengthened to emphasise the low risk of experiencing severe muscle adverse effects because of statin treatment. It is not expected to have an impact on resource use as discussions on adverse effects are already an important part of current practice in prescribing and monitoring statins.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
    </div>

        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>Evidence on both the effectiveness and adverse effects of statins showed <a class="" href="recommendations#high-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#high-intensity-statin">high-intensity statins</a> are clinically effective and cost effective compared to no statins, <a class="" href="recommendations#low-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#low-intensity-statin">low-intensity statins</a>, or <a class="" href="recommendations#medium-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#medium-intensity-statin">medium-intensity statins</a> for preventing CVD in people without CVD. </p>
            <p>The committee agreed to retain 20 mg as the recommended starting dose for all people starting atorvastatin for primary prevention of CVD. Although there was committee consensus that higher doses have a greater effect, they agreed that starting at the lowest effective dose was likely to be preferable to people, but that up-titration of the dose should be considered as appropriate, following <a class="" href="recommendations#optimising-statin-treatment-4" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#optimising-statin-treatment-4">recommendation 1.9.1</a>. </p>
            <p>The evidence supported the 2014 recommendations on optimising lifestyle changes and treating comorbidities and secondary causes of dyslipidaemia before starting statins, so they were retained. </p>
            <p>The committee agreed to retain the following recommendations for research because there is still a lack of direct evidence in these areas:</p>
            <div class="itemizedlist">
              <ul class="itemizedlist">
                <li class="listitem">
                  <p>
                    <a class="" href="recommendations-for-research#statin-treatment-for-older-people" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations-for-research" data-original-url="#statin-treatment-for-older-people">statin treatment for older people</a>
                  </p>
                </li>
                <li class="listitem">
                  <p>
                    <a class="" href="recommendations-for-research#lipid-lowering-treatment-for-people-with-type-1-diabetes" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations-for-research" data-original-url="#lipid-lowering-treatment-for-people-with-type-1-diabetes">lipid-lowering treatment for people with type 1 diabetes</a>.</p>
                </li>
              </ul>
            </div>
            <div class="section" title="Statins and QRISK score" id="ng238-statins-and-qrisk-score">
              <h6 class="title" id="statins-and-qrisk-score">Statins and QRISK score</h6>
              <p>Evidence showed that statins are cost effective for people with 10‑year CVD risk scores less than 10%. </p>
              <p>The committee agreed that if more people took statins there would be a greater reduction in CVD events. However, they also recognised that practical considerations needed to be taken into account. </p>
              <p>They agreed that risk scores are an important aid to shared decision making on statins. National audit data (<a class="" href="https://www.cvdprevent.nhs.uk/home" target="_top">CVDPREVENT</a>) suggests that 60% of people without CVD and a QRISK score of 20% or more are prescribed lipid-lowering treatment, compared with 50% for people with scores of 10% or more. Therefore, the committee consensus was that an even smaller proportion of people with scores less than 10% may choose to take statins. </p>
              <p>The committee agreed that focusing on increasing uptake among people with the most potential to benefit would have more impact than lowering the statin treatment threshold. The 10% 10‑year QRISK score was therefore retained as the threshold for offering statins. Although QRISK3 is specified in the recommendations, it is acknowledged that QRISK2 may be used in some circumstances until QRISK3 is embedded in electronic clinical systems (see the <a class="" href="recommendations#full-formal-risk-assessment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#full-formal-risk-assessment">panel after recommendation 1.1.8</a> for details). The 10% threshold applies whether QRISK2 or 3 is used. </p>
              <p>Despite this, the committee agreed that a more person-centred approach should be adopted and recommended atorvastatin 20 mg as an option for people who want to take statins, irrespective of their QRISK3 score, or where clinical judgement suggests the person may be at high risk of CVD (for example, if the person has CVD risk factors not covered by QRISK3).</p>
              <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/c-statins-efficacy-and-adverse-effects-pdf-13253901663" target="_top">evidence review C: statins: efficacy and adverse effects</a>.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>Most recommendations about statin treatment have been retained from the 2014 update of the guideline and so should not require a change in practice. </p>

    <h3 class="title" id="lipid-lowering-treatment-for-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">1.7 Lipid-lowering treatment for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease</h3>
    <p>These recommendations apply to people with and without type 1 and 2 diabetes. Also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#optimising-statin-treatment-4" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#optimising-statin-treatment-4">sections on optimising statin treatment</a> and <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessing-response-to-treatment-5" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessing-response-to-treatment-5">assessing response to treatment</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Lipid target for people taking lipid-lowering treatments" id="ng238-lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments">
      <h4 class="title" id="lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments">Lipid target for people taking lipid-lowering treatments</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For secondary prevention of CVD, aim for LDL cholesterol levels of 2.0 mmol per litre or less, or non-HDL cholesterol levels of 2.6 mmol per litre or less. <strong>[December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <div class="section" title="Lipid target" id="ng238-lipid-target">
              <h6 class="title" id="lipid-target">Lipid target</h6>
              <p>The committee agreed LDL cholesterol and non-HDL cholesterol levels should be reduced as much as possible in people with CVD. However, people respond differently to statins and other lipid-lowering treatments, and it is not cost effective to offer the full range of treatments to everyone with CVD. </p>
              <p>The clinical evidence consisted of 34 randomised control trials (RCTs). Clinically significant reductions in LDL cholesterol and non-HDL cholesterol levels compared to placebo were seen for all 4 lipid-lowering treatments covered by the clinical trials: alirocumab, evolocumab, ezetimibe and inclisiran. The majority of people in the trials were also taking statins. </p>
              <p>Modest reductions in major CVD events such as myocardial infarction, stroke and related deaths were also seen for all 4 medicines. The committee recognised that some of the trials involved short follow-up periods of 1 year or less, so these medicines are likely to have a bigger impact on CVD events over the long term. There was no clinically important increased risk of adverse events. Injection site reactions were more frequent with alirocumab, evolocumab and inclisiran than with placebo but these were mild and not persistent. </p>
              <p>An economic model was developed using estimates of the impact of lipid-lowering treatments on LDL cholesterol (from a network meta-analysis of the 34 RCTs), combined with estimates of the impact of LDL cholesterol reduction on major cardiovascular events (from a published meta-analysis of statin RCTs). The economic model calculated, for all possible baseline LDL cholesterol levels, the reduced admissions to hospital for stroke, myocardial infarction and cardiovascular procedures and the associated life expectancy increases, quality of life improvements and treatment cost savings as a result of taking lipid-lowering treatments. This was offset against the cost of lipid-lowering treatments and associated monitoring costs. The data for the 2 PCSK9 inhibitors (alirocumab and evolocumab) were combined and were not analysed separately because treatment effects were found to be the same in the network meta-analysis. </p>
              <p>The model estimated the absolute LDL cholesterol target at which it was cost effective to escalate treatment for people on a high-intensity statin. The analysis explored different combinations of lipid-lowering treatments. </p>
              <p>Escalation of treatment was cost effective for people on statins with LDL cholesterol levels of more than 2.2 mmol per litre. There was a little more uncertainty about the cost effectiveness of escalating treatment for people with LDL cholesterol levels between 2.0 mmol and 2.2 mmol per litre. An LDL cholesterol target of 1.8 mmol per litre was not cost effective. The committee decided that 2.0 mmol per litre was likely to be cost effective and would allow more people to be treated than 2.2 mmol per litre. </p>
              <p>Even though the main economic analysis was based on the impact of lipid-lowering treatment on LDL cholesterol levels, the committee recognised the need to identify a non-HDL cholesterol target for use when LDL cholesterol levels have not been requested or calculated. </p>
              <p>Using the distribution of LDL cholesterol levels for the population with CVD and on a statin in the clinical practice research datalink (CPRD) dataset, 42% of people had LDL cholesterol levels of 2.0 mmol per litre or more. Using the same data, the threshold for non-HDL cholesterol that would produce an identical number of people being escalated for treatment was 2.6 mmol per litre. An alternative approach would be to use the Friedewald equation and insert the mean triglyceride level of 1.4 mmol per litre along with the LDL cholesterol of 2.0 mmol per litre. This approach also indicates a non-HDL cholesterol target of 2.6 mmol per litre. </p>
              <p>Both target measures are slightly higher than other national and international targets because, unlike other targets, the LDL cholesterol target is based on the cost effectiveness of treatment escalation. However, the committee thought it was sufficiently similar and, because it was more affordable, was more likely to be implemented. </p>
            </div>
            <div class="section" title="Statin plus ezetimibe" id="ng238-statin-plus-ezetimibe">
              <h6 class="title" id="statin-plus-ezetimibe">Statin plus ezetimibe</h6>
              <p>In a separate analysis, escalation with statin plus ezetimibe (but no injectable treatment) was evaluated at different LDL cholesterol levels. Ezetimibe was cost effective regardless of LDL cholesterol, so the committee agreed that it could be considered for people with lipid levels below the agreed target. They noted that the trade-off between further reducing risk and increasing medication should be taken into account. These should be considered and fully discussed with the person as part of informed shared decision making. Furthermore, the committee agreed that adherence may be lower for people on 2 pills rather than 1, especially if they are below the target.</p>
              <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/d-escalation-of-lipidlowering-treatment-for-secondary-prevention-of-cvd-pdf-13253908141" target="_top">evidence review D: escalation of lipid-lowering treatment for secondary prevention of CVD</a>.</p>
            </div>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>It is expected that recommending a specific lipid target for secondary prevention of CVD will lead to an increased use of lipid-lowering treatments. The committee was aware that NHS England had recently introduced a target as part of the Quality and Outcomes Framework (QOF). The target recommended in this guideline is similar to the 2023/24 QOF, although data showed that, in many people, the QOF target is not being met. In June 2023, the <a class="" href="https://www.nhsbenchmarking.nhs.uk/cvdpreventlanding" target="_top">CVDPREVENT audit</a> reported that 28.7% were meeting the target.</p>
            <p>Increased uptake of statins, ezetimibe and other lipid-lowering treatments will result in higher medication and monitoring costs to the NHS. It will also contribute to an increased workload in primary care, including for GP practices and pharmacies, and in laboratories that process lipid profile and liver function tests. The committee agreed that increased uptake of lipid-lowering treatments is necessary for an overall improvement in population health, but that the extra cost of lipid-lowering treatment would be partly offset by savings due to a reduction in CVD events (including hospital admissions for stroke, heart disease and cardiovascular procedures).</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary">
        <p>As part of the December 2023 update, a new NICE indicator was developed to support quality improvement in managing cholesterol levels for people with CVD. This NICE indicator is suitable for inclusion in local and national general practice measurement frameworks, including those underpinned with financial incentives:</p>
        <p>IND278: <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/indicators/ind278-cardiovascular-disease-prevention-cholesterol-treatment-target-secondary-prevention" target="_top">The percentage of patients with cardiovascular disease (CVD) in whom the last recorded LDL or non-HDL cholesterol level (measured in the preceding 12 months) is 2.0 mmol per litre or less for LDL cholesterol or 2.6 mmol per litre or less for non-HDL cholesterol</a>. </p>
      </div>

    <article id="ng238-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If the lipid target for primary or secondary prevention of CVD (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#lipid-target-for-people-taking-statins" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#lipid-target-for-people-taking-statins">recommendation 1.6.1</a> and <a class="link" href="recommendations#lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments">recommendation 1.7.1</a>) is not met:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>discuss adherence and timing of statin dose with the person</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>encourage them to continue improvements to their diet and lifestyle, and to make further changes if appropriate</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>consider increasing the statin intensity/dose if the person is not currently taking a <a class="link" href="recommendations#high-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#high-intensity-statin">high-intensity statin</a> at the maximum tolerated dose. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023] </strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng238-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If the person reports adverse effects when taking a high-intensity statin, discuss the following strategies with them:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>stopping the statin and trying again when the symptoms have resolved to check if the symptoms are related to the statin </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>changing to a different statin in the same intensity group (rosuvastatin if already receiving atorvastatin)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>reducing the dose </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>changing to a lower-intensity statin. <strong>[2014, amended May 2023 and December 2023] </strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If a person is not able to tolerate a high-intensity statin, aim to treat with the maximum tolerated intensity and dose of statin. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>

  <div class="section" title="1.10 Treatment if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated" id="ng238-treatment-if-statins-are-contraindicated-or-not-tolerated">
    <h3 class="title" id="treatment-if-statins-are-contraindicated-or-not-tolerated">1.10 Treatment if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated</h3>
    <p>Also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessing-response-to-treatment-5" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessing-response-to-treatment-5">section on assessing response to treatment</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Primary prevention of cardiovascular disease" id="ng238-primary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="primary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">Primary prevention of cardiovascular disease</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_10_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ezetimibe is recommended as an option in NICE technology appraisal guidance for treating primary hypercholesterolaemia in adults if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated. For full details, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta385/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">guidance on ezetimibe (TA385, 2016)</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_10_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Bempedoic acid with ezetimibe is recommended as an option in NICE technology appraisal guidance for treating primary hypercholesterolaemia or mixed dyslipidaemia if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated, and ezetimibe alone does not control LDL cholesterol well enough. For full details, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta694/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">guidance on bempedoic acid with ezetimibe (TA694, 2021)</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease" id="ng238-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">Secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_10_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer ezetimibe instead of a statin to people for whom statins are contraindicated or, if after documented discussion of the strategies outlined in <a class="link" href="recommendations#optimising-statin-treatment-4" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#optimising-statin-treatment-4">recommendations 1.9.2 and 1.9.3</a>, it is recognised the person cannot tolerate statins of any intensity or dose. This applies whatever the person's cholesterol level.<br /><br />Ezetimibe is recommended as an option in NICE technology appraisal guidance for treating primary hypercholesterolaemia in adults if statins are contraindicated or not tolerated. For full details, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta385/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">guidance on ezetimibe (TA385, 2016)</a>. <strong>[December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_10_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person is taking ezetimibe but the lipid target for secondary prevention is not met (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#lipid-target-for-people-taking-lipid-lowering-treatments">recommendation 1.7.1</a>), consider alternative or additional lipid-lowering treatments recommended as options in NICE's technology appraisal guidance on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta393/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">alirocumab (TA393, June 2016)</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta694/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">bempedoic acid with ezetimibe (TA694, April 2021)</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta394/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">evolocumab (TA394, June 2016)</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta733/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">inclisiran (TA733, October 2021)</a>. <br /><br />See the relevant NICE technology appraisal guidance for full details.  <strong>[December 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The committee did not review the evidence for the clinical effectiveness of lipid-lowering treatments in people who are statin intolerant or for whom statins are contraindicated but based their recommendations on <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta393" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta393">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on alirocumab</a>, <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta694" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta694">bempedoic acid</a>, <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta394" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta394">evolocumab</a>, <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta385" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta385">ezetimibe</a> and <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta733" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta733">inclisiran</a>.  </p>
            <p>The committee emphasised that statin treatment is known to be the most effective method of reducing the risk of CVD events and that this should be the main treatment for most people. They highlighted the importance of reviewing statin medication in response to adverse effects before deciding someone is statin intolerant. </p>
            <p>An economic analysis estimated the absolute LDL cholesterol target at which it was cost effective to escalate treatment for people on ezetimibe who are statin intolerant. The analysis explored different combinations of lipid-lowering treatments. </p>
            <p>The committee discussed whether the lipid target should be different because of the different treatment options and associated costs. However, it was noted that this may introduce inequality regarding access to lipid-lowering treatment. Also, the target at which escalation of lipid-lowering treatment is cost effective did not change when the statin intolerant population was included in the economic model alongside the statin tolerant population, largely because the prevalence of statin intolerance is relatively low. Therefore, the committee agreed that the target for people who cannot take statins should be the same as for those who can take them. </p>
            <p>They recommended offering ezetimibe to people who cannot take statins (in line with NICE's technology appraisal guidance on ezetimibe) and, if this does not achieve the lipid target in this guideline, to offer alternative or additional lipid-lowering treatments (in line with other technology appraisal guidance). </p>
            <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/d-escalation-of-lipidlowering-treatment-for-secondary-prevention-of-cvd-pdf-13253908141" target="_top">evidence review D: escalation of lipid-lowering treatment for secondary prevention of CVD</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>Increased uptake of statins, ezetimibe and other lipid-lowering treatments will result in higher medication and monitoring costs to the NHS. It will also contribute to an increased workload in primary care, including for GP practices and pharmacies, and in laboratories that process lipid profile and liver function tests. The committee agreed that increased uptake of lipid-lowering treatments is necessary for an overall improvement in population health, but that the extra cost of lipid-lowering treatment would be partly offset by savings due to a reduction in CVD events (including hospital admissions for stroke, heart disease and cardiovascular procedures).</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
    </div>

    <div class="section" title="When to repeat blood tests" id="ng238-when-to-repeat-blood-tests">
      <h4 class="title" id="when-to-repeat-blood-tests">When to repeat blood tests</h4>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Measure liver transaminase and <a class="link" href="recommendations#full-lipid-profile" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#full-lipid-profile">full lipid profile</a> at 2 to 3 months after starting or changing lipid-lowering treatment. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023] </strong></p>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.2</h5>

    <div class="section" title="When to measure creatine kinase" id="ng238-when-to-measure-creatine-kinase">
      <h4 class="title" id="when-to-measure-creatine-kinase">When to measure creatine kinase</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_11_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people who are being treated with a statin to seek medical advice if they develop unexplained muscle symptoms (pain, tenderness or weakness). If this occurs, measure creatine kinase. <strong>[May 2023] </strong></p>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.5</h5>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider an annual full lipid profile to inform discussions about primary prevention of CVD. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023]</strong></p>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>During the annual medication review:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss and encourage medicines adherence, if the shared decision is to continue with lipid-lowering treatment </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss and encourage dietary and lifestyle changes if appropriate </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>address CVD risk factors. <strong>[May 2023, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_11_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with people who are stable on a <a class="link" href="recommendations#low-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#low-intensity-statin">low-intensity statin</a> or <a class="link" href="recommendations#medium-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#medium-intensity-statin">medium‑intensity statin</a> the likely benefits and potential risks of changing to a <a class="link" href="recommendations#high-intensity-statin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#high-intensity-statin">high-intensity statin</a> when they have a medication review and agree with the person whether a change is needed. <strong>[May 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>Why the committee made these recommendations</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The committee agreed that more flexibility in the timing of blood tests to measure lipid levels after starting high-intensity statins was reflective of actual clinical practice and recommended a timeframe of 2 to 3 months, rather than at 3 months of treatment as recommended in the 2014 guideline. They also recommended blood tests should be done 2 to 3 months after changing treatment.</p>
            <p>They agreed that blood tests should provide a <a class="" href="recommendations#full-lipid-profile" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#full-lipid-profile">full lipid profile</a> and that LDL cholesterol levels can be calculated rather than measured directly. They recommended that a full lipid profile should be done to inform annual medication reviews about secondary prevention of CVD, and considered for reviews about primary prevention of CVD. </p>
            <p>These changes were also applied to the recommendations on baseline blood tests before starting statins and statin treatment after an acute coronary syndrome. </p>
            <p>The evidence supported the 2008 and 2014 recommendations on when to measure creatine kinase, not stopping statins because of an increase in blood glucose or HbA1c and restarting statins if stopped because of drug interactions or illness, so they were retained.</p>
            <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/evidence/c-statins-efficacy-and-adverse-effects-pdf-13253901663" target="_top">evidence review C: statins: efficacy and adverse effects</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
      <div class="panel panel--primary panel--details panel-ok" xmlns="">
        <details>
          <summary>
            <span>How the recommendations might affect practice</span>
          </summary>
          <div class="panel--reveal">
            <p>The requirement to check lipid levels after changing lipid-lowering treatment, as well as after starting treatment, will result in higher monitoring costs. However, calculating LDL cholesterol as well as non-HDL cholesterol should not add to the cost per test. </p>
          </div>
        </details>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.12 Lipid-lowering treatments that should not be used or not used routinely" id="ng238-lipid-lowering-treatments-that-should-not-be-used-or-not-used-routinely">
    <h3 class="title" id="lipid-lowering-treatments-that-should-not-be-used-or-not-used-routinely">1.12 Lipid-lowering treatments that should not be used or not used routinely</h3>
    <p>These recommendations apply to primary and secondary prevention of CVD, including people with diabetes and CKD. </p>
    <p>They do not apply to people with familial hypercholesterolaemia. For guidance on using fibrates, bile acid sequestrants and combination treatment for this population group, follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg71/chapter/Recommendations#management" target="_top">recommendations on drug treatment in NICE's guideline on familial hypercholesterolaemia</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Adherence to statin treatment" id="ng238-adherence-to-statin-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="adherence-to-statin-treatment">Adherence to statin treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer coenzyme Q10 or vitamin D to increase adherence to statin treatment. <strong>[2014]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Fibrates" id="ng238-fibrates">
      <h4 class="title" id="fibrates">Fibrates</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer fibrates to prevent CVD. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nicotinic acid" id="ng238-nicotinic-acid">
      <h4 class="title" id="nicotinic-acid">Nicotinic acid</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer nicotinic acid (niacin) to prevent CVD. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Bile acid sequestrants (anion exchange resins)" id="ng238-bile-acid-sequestrants-anion-exchange-resins">
      <h4 class="title" id="bile-acid-sequestrants-anion-exchange-resins">Bile acid sequestrants (anion exchange resins)</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer a bile acid sequestrant (anion exchange resin) to prevent CVD. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Omega 3 fatty acid compounds" id="ng238-omega-3-fatty-acid-compounds">
      <h4 class="title" id="omega-3-fatty-acid-compounds">Omega 3 fatty acid compounds</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer omega 3 fatty acid compounds to prevent CVD.<br /><br />Icosapent ethyl is an exception to this if used as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on icosapent ethyl with statin therapy for reducing the risk of cardiovascular events in people with raised triglycerides (TA805, 2022)</a>. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Tell people that there is no evidence that omega 3 fatty acid compounds help to prevent CVD, except use of icosapent ethyl as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on icosapent ethyl with statin therapy for reducing the risk of cardiovascular events in people with raised triglycerides (TA805, 2022)</a>. <strong>[2014]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Combination treatment" id="ng238-combination-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="combination-treatment">Combination treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng238-1_12_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>To prevent CVD, do not offer the combination of a statin with: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a bile acid sequestrant (anion exchange resin), a fibrate or nicotinic acid <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an omega 3 fatty acid compound, except icosapent ethyl as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta805">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on icosapent ethyl with statin therapy for reducing the risk of cardiovascular events in people with raised triglycerides (TA805, 2022)</a>. <strong>[2014, amended December 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng238-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline. </p>
    <div class="section" title="Full lipid profile" id="ng238-full-lipid-profile">
      <h4 class="title" id="full-lipid-profile">Full lipid profile</h4>
      <p>This involves taking a blood sample to measure total cholesterol, HDL cholesterol and triglyceride levels and then calculating non-HDL cholesterol and LDL cholesterol (a fasting sample is not mandated). LDL cholesterol results may not be reported in participants with triglyceride levels more than 4.5 mmol per litre or 9 mmol per litre depending on the formula used by local laboratories.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="High-intensity statin" id="ng238-high-intensity-statin">
      <h4 class="title" id="high-intensity-statin">High-intensity statin</h4>
      <p>The following doses for statins are high intensity, based on the percentage reduction in LDL cholesterol they can produce:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>atorvastatin: 20 mg to 80 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>rosuvastatin: 10 mg to 40 mg.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Medium-intensity statin" id="ng238-medium-intensity-statin">
      <h4 class="title" id="medium-intensity-statin">Medium-intensity statin</h4>
      <p>The following doses for statins are medium intensity, based on the percentage reduction in LDL cholesterol they can produce:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>atorvastatin: 10 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>fluvastatin: 80 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>rosuvastatin: 5 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>simvastatin: 20 mg to 40 mg.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Low-intensity statin" id="ng238-low-intensity-statin">
      <h4 class="title" id="low-intensity-statin">Low-intensity statin</h4>
      <p>The following doses for statins are low intensity, based on the percentage reduction in LDL cholesterol they can produce:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>fluvastatin: 20 mg to 40 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>pravastatin: 5 mg to 40 mg</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>simvastatin: 10 mg.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Severe mental illness" id="ng238-severe-mental-illness">
      <h4 class="title" id="severe-mental-illness">Severe mental illness</h4>
      <p>A diagnosis of schizophrenia, bipolar disorder or other psychoses. (In line with the criteria for severe mental health conditions used in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nhs.uk/mental-health/social-care-and-your-rights/annual-health-check-smi/" target="_top">NHS annual health check for people with severe mental health conditions</a>.)</p>
    </div>
  </div>
