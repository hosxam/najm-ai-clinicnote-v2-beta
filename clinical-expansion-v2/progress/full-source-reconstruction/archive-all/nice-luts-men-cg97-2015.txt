<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg97-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off‑label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>The following guidance is based on the best available evidence. The <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG97/evidence" target="_top">full guideline</a> gives details of the methods and the evidence used to develop the 2010 recommendations. The <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG97/evidence" target="_top">guideline addendum</a> gives details of the methods and the evidence used to develop the 2015 recommendations.</p>
  <p>In this guideline, 'mild' refers to an International Prostate Symptom Score (IPSS) of 0 to 7, 'moderate' refers to an IPSS of 8 to 19 and 'severe' refers to an IPSS of 20 to 35.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Initial assessment" id="cg97-initial-assessment">
    <h3 class="title" id="initial-assessment">1.1 Initial assessment</h3>
    <p>Initial assessment refers to assessment carried out in any setting by a healthcare professional without specific training in managing lower urinary tract symptoms (LUTS) in men.</p>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, offer men with LUTS an assessment of their general medical history to identify possible causes of LUTS, and associated comorbidities. Review current medication, including herbal and over-the-counter medicines, to identify drugs that may be contributing to the problem. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, offer men with LUTS a physical examination guided by urological symptoms and other medical conditions, an examination of the abdomen and external genitalia, and a digital rectal examination. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, ask men with bothersome LUTS to complete a urinary frequency volume chart. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, offer men with LUTS a urine dipstick test to detect blood, glucose, protein, leucocytes and nitrites. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, offer men with LUTS information, advice and time to decide if they wish to have prostate-specific antigen (PSA) testing if: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their LUTS are suggestive of bladder outlet obstruction secondary to benign prostate enlargement (BPE) <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their prostate feels abnormal on digital rectal examination <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>they are concerned about prostate cancer. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Manage suspected prostate cancer in men with LUTS in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng131" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng131">NICE guidelines on prostate cancer</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12">suspected cancer</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, offer men with LUTS a serum creatinine test (plus estimated glomerular filtration rate [eGFR] calculation) only if you suspect renal impairment (for example, the man has a palpable bladder, nocturnal enuresis, recurrent urinary tract infections or a history of renal stones). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer cystoscopy to men with uncomplicated LUTS (that is, without evidence of bladder abnormality) at initial assessment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer imaging of the upper urinary tract to men with uncomplicated LUTS at initial assessment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer flow-rate measurement to men with LUTS at initial assessment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer a post-void residual volume measurement to men with LUTS at initial assessment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.12</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At initial assessment, give reassurance, offer advice on lifestyle interventions (for example, fluid intake) and information on their condition to men whose LUTS are not bothersome or complicated. Offer review if symptoms change. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.13</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men referral for specialist assessment if they have bothersome LUTS that have not responded to conservative management or drug treatment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.14</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer men for specialist assessment if they have LUTS complicated by recurrent or persistent urinary tract infection, retention, renal impairment that is suspected to be caused by lower urinary tract dysfunction, or suspected urological cancer. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_1_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.15</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men considering any treatment for LUTS an assessment of their baseline symptoms with a validated symptom score (for example, the IPSS) to allow assessment of subsequent symptom change. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Specialist assessment" id="cg97-specialist-assessment">
    <h3 class="title" id="specialist-assessment">1.2 Specialist assessment</h3>
    <p>Specialist assessment refers to assessment carried out in any setting by a healthcare professional with specific training in managing LUTS in men.</p>
    <article id="cg97-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men with LUTS having specialist assessment an assessment of their general medical history to identify possible causes of LUTS, and associated comorbidities. Review current medication, including herbal and over-the-counter medicines to identify drugs that may be contributing to the problem. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men with LUTS having specialist assessment a physical examination guided by urological symptoms and other medical conditions, an examination of the abdomen and external genitalia, and a digital rectal examination. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At specialist assessment, ask men with LUTS to complete a urinary frequency volume chart. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At specialist assessment, offer men with LUTS information, advice and time to decide if they wish to have PSA testing if:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their LUTS are suggestive of bladder outlet obstruction secondary to BPE <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their prostate feels abnormal on digital rectal examination <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>they are concerned about prostate cancer. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men with LUTS who are having specialist assessment a measurement of flow rate and post-void residual volume. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain to men with post-micturition dribble how to perform urethral milking. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

      <div class="recommendation__body">

    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer a choice of containment products to manage storage LUTS (particularly urinary incontinence) based on individual circumstances and in consultation with the man. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer men with storage LUTS suggestive of overactive bladder supervised bladder training, advice on fluid intake, lifestyle advice and, if needed, containment products. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h4>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">

      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h4>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer penile clamps to men with storage LUTS (particularly urinary incontinence). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer external collecting devices (for example, sheath appliances, pubic pressure urinals) for managing storage LUTS (particularly urinary incontinence) in men before considering indwelling catheterisation (see recommendation 1.3.11). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer intermittent bladder catheterisation before indwelling urethral or suprapubic catheterisation to men with voiding LUTS that cannot be corrected by less invasive measures. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>

    <article id="cg97-1_3_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.11</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering long-term indwelling urethral catheterisation to men with LUTS: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>for whom medical management has failed and surgery is not appropriate <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>who are unable to manage intermittent self-catheterisation <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>with skin wounds, pressure ulcers or irritation that are being contaminated by urine <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>who are distressed by bed and clothing changes. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.12</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If offering long-term indwelling catheterisation, discuss the practicalities, benefits and risks with the man and, if appropriate, his carer. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

    <article id="cg97-1_3_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.13</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain to men that indwelling catheters for urgency incontinence may not result in continence or the relief of recurrent infections. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

    <article id="cg97-1_3_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.14</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider permanent use of containment products for men with storage LUTS (particularly urinary incontinence) only after assessment and exclusion of other methods of management. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>

  <div class="section" title="1.4 Drug treatment" id="cg97-drug-treatment">
    <h3 class="title" id="drug-treatment">1.4 Drug treatment</h3>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h4>

        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">

      <div class="recommendation__body">

See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>

See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.5 Surgery for voiding symptoms" id="cg97-surgery-for-voiding-symptoms">
    <h3 class="title" id="surgery-for-voiding-symptoms">1.5 Surgery for voiding symptoms</h3>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For men with voiding symptoms, offer surgery only if voiding symptoms are severe or if drug treatment and conservative management options have been unsuccessful or are not appropriate. Discuss the alternatives to and outcomes from surgery. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If offering surgery for managing voiding LUTS presumed secondary to BPE, offer monopolar or bipolar transurethral resection of the prostate (TURP), monopolar transurethral vaporisation of the prostate (TUVP) or holmium laser enucleation of the prostate (HoLEP). Perform HoLEP at a centre specialising in the technique, or with mentorship arrangements in place. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer transurethral incision of the prostate (TUIP) as an alternative to other types of surgery (see recommendation 1.5.2) to men with a prostate estimated to be smaller than 30 g. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Only offer open prostatectomy as an alternative to TURP, TUVP or HoLEP (see recommendation 1.5.2) to men with prostates estimated to be larger than 80 g. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If offering surgery for managing voiding LUTS presumed secondary to BPE, do not offer minimally invasive treatments (including transurethral needle ablation [TUNA], transurethral microwave thermotherapy [TUMT], high-intensity focused ultrasound [HIFU], transurethral ethanol ablation of the prostate [TEAP] and laser coagulation) as an alternative to TURP, TUVP or HoLEP (see recommendation 1.5.2). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If offering surgery for managing voiding LUTS presumed secondary to BPE, only consider offering botulinum toxin injection into the prostate as part of a randomised controlled trial. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If offering surgery for managing voiding LUTS presumed secondary to BPE, only consider offering laser vaporisation techniques, bipolar TUVP or monopolar or bipolar transurethral vaporisation resection of the prostate (TUVRP) as part of a randomised controlled trial that compares these techniques with TURP. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>

          <li class="listitem">

    <article id="cg97-1_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering bladder wall injection with botulinum toxin to men with detrusor overactivity only if their symptoms have not responded to conservative management and drug treatments and the man is willing and able to self-catheterise. <strong>[2010]<br /><br /></strong>In June 2015, botulinum toxin A and botulinum toxin B did not have UK marketing authorisations for this indication. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>

    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering implanted sacral nerve stimulation to manage detrusor overactivity only to men whose symptoms have not responded to conservative management and drug treatments. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_6_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer myectomy to men to manage detrusor overactivity. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_6_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering intramural injectables, implanted adjustable compression devices and male slings to manage stress urinary incontinence only as part of a randomised controlled trial. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_6_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering urinary diversion to manage intractable urinary tract symptoms only to men whose symptoms have not responded to conservative management and drug treatments, and if cystoplasty or sacral nerve stimulation are not clinically appropriate or are unacceptable to the patient. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_6_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering implantation of an artificial sphincter to manage stress urinary incontinence only to men whose symptoms have not responded to conservative management and drug treatments. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>

  <div class="section" title="1.7 Treating urinary retention" id="cg97-treating-urinary-retention">
    <h3 class="title" id="treating-urinary-retention">1.7 Treating urinary retention</h3>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Immediately catheterise men with acute retention. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer an alpha blocker to men for managing acute urinary retention before removal of the catheter. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering self- or carer-administered intermittent urethral catheterisation before offering indwelling catheterisation for men with chronic urinary retention. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Carry out a serum creatinine test and imaging of the upper urinary tract in men with chronic urinary retention (residual volume greater than 1 litre or presence of a palpable or percussable bladder). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Catheterise men who have impaired renal function or hydronephrosis secondary to chronic urinary retention. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering intermittent or indwelling catheterisation before offering surgery in men with chronic urinary retention. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering surgery on the bladder outlet without prior catheterisation to men who have chronic urinary retention and other bothersome LUTS but no impairment of renal function or upper renal tract abnormality. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider offering intermittent self- or carer-administered catheterisation instead of surgery in men with chronic retention who you suspect have markedly impaired bladder function. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Continue or start long-term catheterisation in men with chronic retention for whom surgery is unsuitable. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_7_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide active surveillance (post-void residual volume measurement, upper tract imaging and serum creatinine testing) to men with non-bothersome LUTS secondary to chronic retention who have not had their bladder drained. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Alternative and complementary therapies" id="cg97-alternative-and-complementary-therapies">
    <h3 class="title" id="alternative-and-complementary-therapies">1.8 Alternative and complementary therapies</h3>
    <article id="cg97-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer homeopathy, phytotherapy or acupuncture for treating LUTS in men.<strong> [2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Providing information" id="cg97-providing-information">
    <h3 class="title" id="providing-information">1.9 Providing information</h3>
    <article id="cg97-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that, if appropriate, men's carers are informed and involved in managing their LUTS and can give feedback on treatments. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Make sure men with LUTS have access to care that can help with:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their emotional and physical conditions <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>relevant physical, emotional, psychological, sexual and social issues. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg97-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide men with storage LUTS (particularly incontinence) containment products at point of need, and advice about relevant support groups. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
