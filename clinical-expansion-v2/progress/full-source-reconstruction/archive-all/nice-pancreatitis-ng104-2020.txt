<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng104-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/what-nice-does/our-guidance/about-nice-guidelines/about-shared-decision-making" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/what-nice-does/our-guidance/about-nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Information and support" id="ng104-information-and-support">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support">1.1 Information and support</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Patient information" id="ng104-patient-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="patient-information">Patient information</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give people with pancreatitis, and their family members or carers (as appropriate), written and verbal information on the following, where relevant, as soon as possible after diagnosis:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pancreatitis and any proposed investigations and procedures, using diagrams</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hereditary pancreatitis, and pancreatitis in children, including specific information on genetic counselling, genetic testing, risk to other family members, and advice on the impact of their pancreatitis on life insurance and travel</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the long-term effects of pancreatitis, including effects on the person's quality of life </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the harm caused to the pancreas by smoking or alcohol. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with pancreatitis where they might find reliable high-quality information and support after consultations, from sources such as national and local support groups, regional pancreatitis networks and information services.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give people with pancreatitis, and their family members or carers (as appropriate), written and verbal information on the following about the management of pancreatitis, when applicable:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>why a person may be going through a phase where no treatment is given</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that pancreatitis is managed by a multidisciplinary team </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the multidisciplinary treatment of pain, including how to access the local pain team and types of pain relief </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>nutrition advice, including advice on how to take pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy if needed</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>follow-up and who to contact for relevant advice, including advice needed during episodes of acute illness</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>psychological care if needed, where available (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222">NICE guideline on depression in adults</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pancreatitis services, including the role of specialist centres, and primary care services for people with acute, chronic or hereditary pancreatitis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>welfare benefits, education and employment support, and disability services.<br /><br />In September 2024, supplies of pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy were disrupted, so availability varies. Use the <a class="link" href="https://www.sps.nhs.uk/articles/prescribing-and-ordering-available-pancreatic-enzyme-replacement-therapies/" target="_top">Specialist Pharmacy Service's prescribing and ordering available pancreatic enzyme replacement therapies</a> resource and tool to help identify equivalent licensed products.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For more guidance on giving information, including providing an individualised approach and helping people to actively participate in their care, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">NICE guideline on patient experience in adult NHS services</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people with severe acute pancreatitis, and their family members or carers (as appropriate), that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a hospital stay lasting several months is relatively common, including time in critical care</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>for people who achieve full recovery, time to recover may take at least 3 times as long as their hospital stay </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>local complications of acute pancreatitis may resolve spontaneously or may take weeks to progress before it is clear that intervention is needed</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>it may be safer to delay intervention (for example, to allow a fluid collection to mature)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people who have started to make a recovery may have a relapse</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>although children rarely die from acute pancreatitis, approximately 15% to 20% of adults with severe acute pancreatitis die in hospital.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Tell adults with pancreatitis that there is a <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG138">NICE guideline on patient experience in adult NHS services</a> that will show them what they can expect about their care.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Passing information to GPs" id="ng104-passing-information-to-gps">
      <h4 class="title" id="passing-information-to-gps">Passing information to GPs</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that information passed to GPs includes all of the following, where applicable: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>detail on how the person should take their pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (including dose escalation as necessary) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that the person should be offered HbA1c testing at least every 6 months and bone mineral density assessments every 2 years. <br /><br />In September 2024, supplies of pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy were disrupted, so availability varies. Use the <a class="link" href="https://www.sps.nhs.uk/articles/prescribing-and-ordering-available-pancreatic-enzyme-replacement-therapies/" target="_top">Specialist Pharmacy Service's prescribing and ordering available pancreatic enzyme replacement therapies</a> resource and tool to help identify equivalent licensed products.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Lifestyle interventions: alcohol" id="ng104-lifestyle-interventions-alcohol">
      <h4 class="title" id="lifestyle-interventions-alcohol">Lifestyle interventions: alcohol</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with pancreatitis caused by alcohol to stop drinking alcohol.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with recurrent acute or chronic pancreatitis that is not alcohol-related, that alcohol might exacerbate their pancreatitis. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on alcohol-use disorders, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg100" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg100">NICE guidelines on the diagnosis and management of physical complications of alcohol-use disorders</a> and the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115">diagnosis, assessment and management of harmful drinking and alcohol dependence</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Lifestyle interventions: smoking cessation" id="ng104-lifestyle-interventions-smoking-cessation">
      <h4 class="title" id="lifestyle-interventions-smoking-cessation">Lifestyle interventions: smoking cessation</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware of the link between smoking and chronic pancreatitis and advise people with chronic pancreatitis to stop smoking in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng209" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng209">NICE guideline on tobacco</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Acute pancreatitis" id="ng104-acute-pancreatitis">
    <h3 class="title" id="acute-pancreatitis">1.2 Acute pancreatitis</h3>
    <p>People with acute pancreatitis usually present with sudden-onset abdominal pain. Nausea and vomiting are often present and there may be a history of gallstones or excessive alcohol intake. Typical physical signs include epigastric tenderness, fever and tachycardia. Diagnosis of acute pancreatitis is confirmed by testing blood lipase or amylase levels, which are usually raised, although raised blood lipase or amylase levels may occur in other conditions. If raised levels are not found, abdominal CT may confirm pancreatic inflammation. </p>
    <div class="section" title="Identifying the cause" id="ng104-identifying-the-cause">
      <h4 class="title" id="identifying-the-cause">Identifying the cause</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not assume that a person's acute pancreatitis is alcohol-related just because they drink alcohol. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If gallstones and alcohol have been excluded as potential causes of a person's acute pancreatitis, investigate other possible causes such as: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>metabolic causes (such as hypercalcaemia or hyperlipidaemia) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prescription drugs </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>microlithiasis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hereditary causes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>autoimmune pancreatitis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ampullary or pancreatic tumours </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>anatomical anomalies (pancreas divisum).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Preventing infection" id="ng104-preventing-infection">
      <h4 class="title" id="preventing-infection">Preventing infection</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer prophylactic antimicrobials to people with acute pancreatitis. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Fluid resuscitation" id="ng104-fluid-resuscitation">
      <h4 class="title" id="fluid-resuscitation">Fluid resuscitation</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on fluid resuscitation, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg174" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG174">NICE guidelines on intravenous fluid therapy in adults in hospital</a> and in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng29" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng29">children and young people in hospital</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutrition support" id="ng104-nutrition-support">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutrition-support">Nutrition support</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that people with acute pancreatitis are not made 'nil‑by‑mouth' and do not have food withheld unless there is a clear reason for this (for example, vomiting).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer enteral nutrition to anyone with severe or moderately severe acute pancreatitis. Start within 72 hours of presentation and aim to meet their nutritional requirements as soon as possible.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer anyone with severe or moderately severe acute pancreatitis parenteral nutrition only if enteral nutrition has failed or is contraindicated.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing complications" id="ng104-managing-complications">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-complications">Managing complications</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Infected necrosis" id="ng104-infected-necrosis">
        <h5 class="title" id="infected-necrosis">Infected necrosis</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with acute pancreatitis an endoscopic approach for managing infected or suspected infected pancreatic necrosis when anatomically possible.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer a percutaneous approach when an endoscopic approach is not anatomically possible.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When deciding on how to manage infected pancreatic necrosis, balance the need to debride promptly against the advantages of delaying intervention. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Pseudocysts" id="ng104-pseudocysts">

          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Pancreatic ascites and pleural effusion" id="ng104-pancreatic-ascites-and-pleural-effusion">
        <h5 class="title" id="pancreatic-ascites-and-pleural-effusion">Pancreatic ascites and pleural effusion</h5>

          </div>
        </article>
      </div>

    <div class="section" title="Referral for specialist treatment" id="ng104-referral-for-specialist-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-for-specialist-treatment">Referral for specialist treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person develops necrotic, infective, haemorrhagic or systemic complications of acute pancreatitis: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>seek advice from a specialist pancreatic centre within the referral network <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss whether to move the person to the specialist centre for treatment of the complications. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_2_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When managing acute pancreatitis in children: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>seek advice from a paediatric gastroenterology or hepatology unit and a specialist pancreatic centre <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss whether to move the child to the specialist centre.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>

  <div class="section" title="1.3 Chronic pancreatitis" id="ng104-chronic-pancreatitis">
    <h3 class="title" id="chronic-pancreatitis">1.3 Chronic pancreatitis</h3>
    <p>People with chronic pancreatitis usually present with chronic or recurrent abdominal pain. This guideline assumes that people with chronic abdominal pain will already have been investigated using CT scan, ultrasound scan or upper gastrointestinal endoscopy to determine a cause for their symptoms. The guideline committee looked at evidence on diagnosing chronic pancreatitis, and the evidence review can be found in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng104/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6535536157" target="_top">full guideline</a>. We have made a <a class="link" href="recommendations-for-research#diagnosis-of-chronic-pancreatitis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations-for-research" data-original-url="#diagnosis-of-chronic-pancreatitis">recommendation for research on the most accurate diagnostic test to identify whether chronic pancreatitis is present in the absence of a clear diagnosis following these tests</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Investigating upper abdominal pain" id="ng104-investigating-upper-abdominal-pain">
      <h4 class="title" id="investigating-upper-abdominal-pain">Investigating upper abdominal pain</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Think about chronic pancreatitis as a possible diagnosis for people presenting with chronic or recurrent episodes of upper abdominal pain and refer accordingly.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Identifying the cause" id="ng104-identifying-the-cause-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="identifying-the-cause-2">Identifying the cause</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not assume that a person's chronic pancreatitis is alcohol-related just because they drink alcohol. Other causes include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>genetic factors</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>autoimmune disease, in particular IgG4 disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>metabolic causes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>structural or anatomical factors.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutrition support" id="ng104-nutrition-support-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutrition-support-2">Nutrition support</h4>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that all people with chronic pancreatitis are at high risk of malabsorption, malnutrition and a deterioration in their quality of life.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use protocols agreed with the specialist pancreatic centre to identify when advice from a specialist dietitian is needed, including advice on food, supplements and long-term pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy, and when to start these interventions.<br /><br />In September 2024, supplies of pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy were disrupted, so availability varies. Use the <a class="link" href="https://www.sps.nhs.uk/articles/prescribing-and-ordering-available-pancreatic-enzyme-replacement-therapies/" target="_top">Specialist Pharmacy Service's prescribing and ordering available pancreatic enzyme replacement therapies</a> resource and tool to help identify equivalent licensed products.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider assessment by a dietitian for anyone diagnosed with chronic pancreatitis.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on nutrition support for people with chronic alcohol-related pancreatitis, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg100/chapter/Recommendations#enzyme-supplementation-for-chronic-alcohol-related-pancreatitis" target="_top">section on enzyme supplementation for chronic alcohol-related pancreatitis in the NICE guideline on alcohol-use disorders</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng104-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on nutrition support, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32/">NICE guideline on nutrition support for adults</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing complications" id="ng104-managing-complications-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-complications-2">Managing complications</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Pancreatic duct obstruction" id="ng104-pancreatic-duct-obstruction">
        <h5 class="title" id="pancreatic-duct-obstruction">Pancreatic duct obstruction</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.8</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgery (open or minimally invasive) as first-line treatment in adults with painful chronic pancreatitis that is causing obstruction of the main pancreatic duct.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider extracorporeal shockwave lithotripsy for adults with pancreatic duct obstruction caused by a dominant stone if surgery is unsuitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Pseudocysts" id="ng104-pseudocysts-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="pseudocysts-2">Pseudocysts</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer endoscopic ultrasound (EUS)-guided drainage, or endoscopic transpapillary drainage for pancreatic head pseudocysts, to people with symptomatic pseudocysts (for example, those with pain, vomiting or weight loss).</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider EUS-guided drainage, or endoscopic transpapillary drainage for pancreatic head pseudocysts, for people with non-symptomatic pseudocysts that meet 1 or more of the following criteria:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>they are associated with pancreatic duct disruption</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>they are creating pressure on large vessels or the diaphragm </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>they are at risk of rupture </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>there is suspicion of infection.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.12</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgical (laparoscopic or open) drainage of pseudocysts that need intervention if endoscopic therapy is unsuitable or has failed. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Neuropathic pain" id="ng104-neuropathic-pain">
        <h5 class="title" id="neuropathic-pain">Neuropathic pain</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.13</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For adults with neuropathic pain related to chronic pancreatitis, follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg173" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg173">NICE guideline on neuropathic pain in adults</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Pancreatic ascites and pleural effusion" id="ng104-pancreatic-ascites-and-pleural-effusion-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="pancreatic-ascites-and-pleural-effusion-2">Pancreatic ascites and pleural effusion</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.14</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider referring a person with pancreatic ascites and pleural effusion for management in a specialist pancreatic centre.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Type 3c diabetes" id="ng104-type-3c-diabetes-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="type-3c-diabetes-2">Type 3c diabetes</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Assess people with type 3c diabetes every 6 months for potential benefit of insulin therapy. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.16</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For guidance on managing type 3c diabetes for people who are not using insulin therapy, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng28" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng28">NICE guidelines on type 2 diabetes in adults</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18">diagnosing and managing diabetes in children and young people</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.17</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For guidance on managing type 3c diabetes for people who need insulin, see the recommendations on insulin therapy and insulin delivery (including rotating injection sites within the same body region) in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17">NICE guidelines on type 1 diabetes in adults</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18">diagnosing and managing diabetes in children and young people</a>. <strong>[2018, amended 2020]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.18</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For guidance on education and information for people with pancreatitis and type 3c diabetes requiring insulin, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17/chapter/Recommendations#education-and-information" target="_top">recommendations on education and information in the NICE guideline on diagnosing and managing type 1 diabetes in adults</a>, and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18/chapter/1-Recommendations#type-1-diabetes" target="_top">diagnosing and managing diabetes in children and young people</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.19</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For guidance on self-monitoring blood glucose for people with pancreatitis and type 3c diabetes requiring insulin, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17/chapter/Recommendations#blood-glucose-management" target="_top">recommendations on blood glucose management in the NICE guideline on diagnosing and managing type 1 diabetes in adults</a>, and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng18/chapter/Recommendations#blood-glucose-and-hba1c-targets-and-monitoring" target="_top">blood glucose monitoring in the NICE guideline on diagnosing and managing diabetes in children and young people</a>. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>

    <div class="section" title="Follow-up investigation" id="ng104-follow-up-investigation">
      <h4 class="title" id="follow-up-investigation">Follow-up investigation</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Follow-up of pancreatic exocrine function" id="ng104-follow-up-of-pancreatic-exocrine-function">
        <h5 class="title" id="follow-up-of-pancreatic-exocrine-function">Follow-up of pancreatic exocrine function</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.20</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with chronic pancreatitis monitoring by clinical and biochemical assessment, to be agreed with the specialist centre, for pancreatic exocrine insufficiency and malnutrition at least every 12 months (every 6 months in under 16s). Adjust the treatment of vitamin and mineral deficiencies accordingly.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.21</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer adults with chronic pancreatitis a bone density assessment every 2 years.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Follow-up to identify pancreatic cancer" id="ng104-follow-up-to-identify-pancreatic-cancer">
        <h5 class="title" id="follow-up-to-identify-pancreatic-cancer">Follow-up to identify pancreatic cancer</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.22</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Be aware that people with chronic pancreatitis have an increased risk of developing pancreatic cancer. The lifetime risk is highest, around 40%, in those with hereditary pancreatitis.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.23</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider annual monitoring for pancreatic cancer in people with hereditary pancreatitis.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Follow-up to identify diabetes" id="ng104-follow-up-to-identify-diabetes">
        <h5 class="title" id="follow-up-to-identify-diabetes">Follow-up to identify diabetes</h5>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.24</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Be aware that people with chronic pancreatitis have a greatly increased risk of developing diabetes, with a lifetime risk as high as 80%. The risk increases with duration of pancreatitis and presence of calcific pancreatitis. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng104-1_3_25" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.25</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with chronic pancreatitis monitoring of HbA1c for diabetes at least every 6 months. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>

  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng104-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Moderately severe acute pancreatitis" id="ng104-moderately-severe-acute-pancreatitis">
      <h4 class="title" id="moderately-severe-acute-pancreatitis">Moderately severe acute pancreatitis</h4>
      <p>Moderately severe acute pancreatitis is characterised by organ failure that resolves within 48 hours (transient organ failure), or local or systemic complications in the absence of persistent organ failure (as defined by the revised <a class="link" href="http://gut.bmj.com/content/62/1/102" target="_top">Atlanta classification published in GUT</a>). </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Severe acute pancreatitis" id="ng104-severe-acute-pancreatitis">
      <h4 class="title" id="severe-acute-pancreatitis">Severe acute pancreatitis</h4>
      <p>Severe acute pancreatitis is characterised by single or multiple organ failure that persists for more than 48 hours (persistent organ failure; as defined by the revised Atlanta classification). </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Type 3c diabetes" id="ng104-type-3c-diabetes-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="type-3c-diabetes-3">Type 3c diabetes</h4>
      <p>Diabetes mellitus secondary to pancreatic disease. When this is associated with pancreatitis, the primary endocrine defect is insufficient insulin secretion (the abnormality in type 1 diabetes) rather than insulin resistance (which is characteristic of type 2 diabetes).</p>
    </div>
