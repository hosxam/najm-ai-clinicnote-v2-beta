<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg95-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Providing information for people with chest pain" id="cg95-providing-information-for-people-with-chest-pain">
    <h3 class="title" id="providing-information-for-people-with-chest-pain">1.1 Providing information for people with chest pain</h3>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Discuss any concerns people (and where appropriate their family or carer/advocate) may have, including anxiety when the cause of the chest pain is unknown. Correct any misinformation. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer people a clear explanation of the possible causes of their symptoms and the uncertainties. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Clearly explain the options to people at every stage of investigation. Make joint decisions with them and take account of their preferences:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Encourage people to ask questions. </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Provide repeated opportunities for discussion.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Explain test results and the need for any further investigations. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide information about any proposed investigations using everyday, jargon-free language. Include:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>their purpose, benefits and any limitations of their diagnostic accuracy</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>duration</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>level of discomfort and invasiveness</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>risk of adverse events. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer information about the risks of diagnostic testing, including any radiation exposure. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address any physical or learning difficulties, sight or hearing problems and difficulties with speaking or reading English, which may affect people's understanding of the information offered. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer information after diagnosis as recommended in the relevant disease management guidelines:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126">NICE guideline on stable angina</a>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg113" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg113">NICE guideline on generalised anxiety disorder and panic disorder in adults</a>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg184" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg184">NICE guideline on gastro-oesophageal reflux disease and dyspepsia in adults</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain if the chest pain is non-cardiac and refer people for further investigation if appropriate. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide individual advice to people about seeking medical help if they have further chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 People presenting with acute chest pain" id="cg95-people-presenting-with-acute-chest-pain">
    <h3 class="title" id="people-presenting-with-acute-chest-pain">1.2 People presenting with acute chest pain</h3>
    <p>This section of the guideline covers the assessment and diagnosis of people with recent acute chest pain or discomfort, suspected to be caused by an acute coronary syndrome (ACS). The term ACS covers a range of conditions including unstable angina, ST‑segment-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI) and non‑ST‑segment-elevation myocardial infarction (NSTEMI).</p>
    <p>The guideline addresses assessment and diagnosis irrespective of setting, because people present in different ways. For early management of these conditions, follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.1 Initial assessment and referral to hospital" id="cg95-initial-assessment-and-referral-to-hospital">
      <h4 class="title" id="initial-assessment-and-referral-to-hospital">1.2.1 Initial assessment and referral to hospital</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Check immediately whether people currently have chest pain. If they are pain free, check when their last episode of pain was, particularly if they have had pain in the last 12 hours. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Determine whether the chest pain may be cardiac and therefore whether this guideline is relevant, by considering:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the history of the chest pain</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the presence of cardiovascular risk factors</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>history of ischaemic heart disease and any previous treatment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>previous investigations for chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Initially assess people for any of the following symptoms, which may indicate an ACS:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pain in the chest and/or other areas (for example, the arms, back or jaw) lasting longer than 15 minutes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>chest pain associated with nausea and vomiting, marked sweating, breathlessness, or particularly a combination of these</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>chest pain associated with haemodynamic instability</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>new onset chest pain, or abrupt deterioration in previously stable angina, with recurrent chest pain occurring frequently and with little or no exertion, and with episodes often lasting longer than 15 minutes. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use people's response to glyceryl trinitrate (GTN) to make a diagnosis. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not assess symptoms of an ACS differently in men and women. Not all people with an ACS present with central chest pain as the predominant feature. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not assess symptoms of an ACS differently in ethnic groups. There are no major differences in symptoms of an ACS among different ethnic groups. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people to hospital as an emergency if an ACS is suspected (see recommendation 1.2.1.3) and:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they currently have chest pain <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they are currently pain free, but had chest pain in the last 12 hours, and a resting 12‑lead ECG is abnormal or not available. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If an ACS is suspected (see recommendation 1.2.1.3) and there are no reasons for emergency referral, refer people for urgent same-day assessment if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they had chest pain in the last 12 hours, but are now pain free with a normal resting 12‑lead ECG <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the last episode of pain was 12 to 72 hours ago. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people for assessment in hospital if an ACS is suspected (see recommendation 1.2.1.3) and:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the pain has resolved <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there are signs of complications such as pulmonary oedema.<br /><br />Use clinical judgement to decide whether referral should be as an emergency or urgent same-day assessment. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a recent ACS is suspected in people whose last episode of chest pain was more than 72 hours ago and who have no complications such as pulmonary oedema:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>carry out a detailed clinical assessment (see recommendations 1.2.4.2 and 1.2.4.3)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>confirm the diagnosis by resting 12‑lead ECG and blood troponin level</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>take into account the length of time since the suspected ACS when interpreting the troponin level.<br /><br />Use clinical judgement to decide whether referral is necessary and how urgent this should be. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people to hospital as an emergency if they have a recent (confirmed or suspected) ACS and develop further chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When an ACS is suspected, start management immediately in the order appropriate to the circumstances (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#immediate-management-of-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#immediate-management-of-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome">section on immediate management of a suspected acute coronary syndrome</a>) and take a resting 12‑lead ECG (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#resting-12-lead-ecg" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#resting-12-lead-ecg">section on resting 12-lead ECG</a>). Take the ECG as soon as possible, but do not delay transfer to hospital. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_1_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If an ACS is not suspected, consider other causes of the chest pain, some of which may be life-threatening (see recommendations 1.2.6.5, 1.2.6.7 and 1.2.6.8). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.2 Resting 12-lead ECG" id="cg95-resting-12-lead-ecg">
      <h4 class="title" id="resting-12-lead-ecg">1.2.2 Resting 12-lead ECG</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take a resting 12‑lead ECG as soon as possible. When people are referred, send the results to hospital before they arrive if possible. Recording and sending the ECG should not delay transfer to hospital. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow local protocols for people with a resting 12‑lead ECG showing regional ST‑segment elevation or presumed new left bundle branch block (LBBB) consistent with an acute STEMI until a firm diagnosis is made. Continue to monitor (see recommendation 1.2.3.4). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a> for people with a resting 12‑lead ECG showing regional ST‑segment depression or deep T wave inversion suggestive of a NSTEMI or unstable angina until a firm diagnosis is made. Continue to monitor (see recommendation 1.2.3.4). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Even in the absence of ST‑segment changes, have an increased suspicion of an ACS if there are other changes in the resting 12‑lead ECG, specifically Q waves and T wave changes. Consider following the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a> if these conditions are likely. Continue to monitor (see recommendation 1.2.3.4). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not exclude an ACS when people have a normal resting 12‑lead ECG. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a diagnosis of ACS is in doubt, consider:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>taking serial resting 12‑lead ECGs </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>reviewing previous resting 12‑lead ECGs</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>recording additional ECG leads.<br /><br />Use clinical judgement to decide how often this should be done. Note that the results may not be conclusive. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Obtain a review of resting 12‑lead ECGs by a healthcare professional qualified to interpret them as well as taking into account automated interpretation. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If clinical assessment (as described in recommendation 1.2.1.10) and a resting 12‑lead ECG make a diagnosis of ACS less likely, consider other acute conditions. First consider those that are life-threatening such as pulmonary embolism, aortic dissection or pneumonia. Continue to monitor (see recommendation 1.2.3.4). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.3 Immediate management of a suspected acute coronary syndrome" id="cg95-immediate-management-of-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome">
      <h4 class="title" id="immediate-management-of-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome">1.2.3 Immediate management of a suspected acute coronary syndrome</h4>
      <p>Management of ACS should start as soon as it is suspected, but should not delay transfer to hospital. The recommendations in this section should be carried out in the order appropriate to the circumstances.</p>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer pain relief as soon as possible. This may be achieved with GTN (sublingual or buccal), but offer intravenous opioids such as morphine, particularly if an acute myocardial infarction (MI) is suspected. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer people a single loading dose of 300 mg aspirin as soon as possible unless there is clear evidence that they are allergic to it. <br /><br />If aspirin is given before arrival at hospital, send a written record that it has been given with the person.<br /><br />Only offer other antiplatelet agents in hospital. Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely administer oxygen, but monitor oxygen saturation using pulse oximetry as soon as possible, ideally before hospital admission. Only offer supplemental oxygen to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with oxygen saturation (SpO<sub>2</sub>) of less than 94% who are not at risk of hypercapnic respiratory failure, aiming for SpO<sub>2</sub> of 94% to 98%</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease who are at risk of hypercapnic respiratory failure, to achieve a target SpO<sub>2</sub> of 88% to 92% until blood gas analysis is available. <strong>[2010]<br /><br /></strong>Be aware that some pulse oximeters can underestimate or overestimate oxygen saturation levels, especially if the saturation level is borderline. Overestimation has been reported in people with dark skin. See also the <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/2018/12/risk-of-harm-from-inappropriate-placement-of-pulse-oximeter-probes/" target="_top">NHS England Patient Safety Alert on the risk of harm from inappropriate placement of pulse oximeter probes</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Monitor people with acute chest pain, using clinical judgement to decide how often this should be done, until a firm diagnosis is made. This should include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>exacerbations of pain and/or other symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pulse and blood pressure</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>heart rhythm</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>oxygen saturation by pulse oximetry</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>repeated resting 12‑lead ECGs <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>checking pain relief is effective. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Manage other therapeutic interventions using the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.4 Assessment in hospital for people with a suspected acute coronary syndrome" id="cg95-assessment-in-hospital-for-people-with-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment-in-hospital-for-people-with-a-suspected-acute-coronary-syndrome">1.2.4 Assessment in hospital for people with a suspected acute coronary syndrome</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take a resting 12‑lead ECG and a blood sample for high-sensitivity troponin I or T measurement (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#use-of-biochemical-markers-for-diagnosis-of-an-acute-coronary-syndrome" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#use-of-biochemical-markers-for-diagnosis-of-an-acute-coronary-syndrome">section on use of biochemical markers for diagnosis of an acute coronary syndrome</a>) on arrival in hospital. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Carry out a physical examination to determine:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>haemodynamic status</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>signs of complications, for example, pulmonary oedema, cardiogenic shock <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>signs of non-coronary causes of acute chest pain, such as aortic dissection. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take a detailed clinical history unless a STEMI is confirmed from the resting 12‑lead ECG (that is, regional ST‑segment elevation or presumed new LBBB). Record:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the characteristics of the pain</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other associated symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any history of cardiovascular disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any cardiovascular risk factors <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>details of previous investigations or treatments for similar symptoms of chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.5 Use of biochemical markers for diagnosis of an acute coronary syndrome" id="cg95-use-of-biochemical-markers-for-diagnosis-of-an-acute-coronary-syndrome">
      <h4 class="title" id="use-of-biochemical-markers-for-diagnosis-of-an-acute-coronary-syndrome">1.2.5 Use of biochemical markers for diagnosis of an acute coronary syndrome</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use high-sensitivity troponin tests for people in whom ACS is not suspected. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people at high or moderate risk of MI (as indicated by a validated tool), perform high-sensitivity troponin tests as recommended in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/dg40" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/dg40">NICE diagnostics guidance on myocardial infarction</a>. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people at low risk of MI (as indicated by a validated tool):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>perform a second high-sensitivity troponin test as recommended in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/dg40" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/dg40">NICE diagnostics guidance on myocardial infarction</a> if the first troponin test at presentation is positive.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider performing a single high-sensitivity troponin test only at presentation to rule out NSTEMI if the first troponin test is below the lower limit of detection (negative). <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that patients understand that a detectable troponin on the first high-sensitivity test does not necessarily indicate that they have had an MI. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use biochemical markers such as natriuretic peptides and high-sensitivity C-reactive protein to diagnose an ACS. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use biochemical markers of myocardial ischaemia (such as ischaemia-modified albumin) as opposed to markers of necrosis when assessing people with acute chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When interpreting high-sensitivity troponin measurements, take into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the clinical presentation</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the time from onset of symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the resting 12‑lead ECG findings</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the pre-test probability of NSTEMI</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the length of time since the suspected ACS</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the probability of chronically elevated troponin levels in some people</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that 99th percentile thresholds for troponin I and T may differ between sexes. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.2.6 Making a diagnosis" id="cg95-making-a-diagnosis">
      <h4 class="title" id="making-a-diagnosis">1.2.6 Making a diagnosis</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When diagnosing MI, use the <a class="link" href="https://academic.oup.com/eurheartj/article/40/3/237/5079081" target="_top">universal definition of myocardial infarction</a>. This is the detection of rise and/or fall of cardiac biomarkers values [preferably cardiac troponin (cTn)] with at least one value above the 99th percentile of the upper reference limit and at least one of the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms of ischaemia</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>new or presumed new significant ST‑segment‑T wave (ST‑T) changes or new LBBB</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>development of pathological Q waves in the ECG</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>imaging evidence of new loss of viable myocardium or new regional wall motion abnormality</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>identification of an intracoronary thrombus by angiography. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When a raised troponin level is detected in people with a suspected ACS, reassess to exclude other causes for raised troponin (for example, myocarditis, aortic dissection or pulmonary embolism) before confirming the diagnosis of ACS. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When a raised troponin level is detected in people with a suspected ACS, follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a> until a firm diagnosis is made. Continue to monitor (see recommendation 1.2.3.4). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When a diagnosis of ACS is confirmed, follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">NICE guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassess people with chest pain without raised troponin levels and no acute resting 12‑lead ECG changes to determine whether their chest pain is likely to be cardiac.<br /><br />If myocardial ischaemia is suspected, follow the recommendations on stable chest pain in this guideline (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#people-presenting-with-stable-chest-pain" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#people-presenting-with-stable-chest-pain">section on people presenting with stable chest pain</a>). Use clinical judgement to decide on the timing of any further diagnostic investigations. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer non-invasive imaging or exercise ECG in the initial assessment of acute cardiac chest pain. <strong>[new 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Only consider early chest computed tomography (CT) to rule out other diagnoses such as pulmonary embolism or aortic dissection, not to diagnose ACS. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider a chest X‑ray to help exclude complications of ACS such as pulmonary oedema, or other diagnoses such as pneumothorax or pneumonia. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_2_6_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If an ACS has been excluded at any point in the care pathway, but people have risk factors for cardiovascular disease, follow the appropriate guidance, for example, the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181">NICE guideline on cardiovascular disease</a> and the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136">NICE guideline on hypertension in adults</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 People presenting with stable chest pain" id="cg95-people-presenting-with-stable-chest-pain">
    <h3 class="title" id="people-presenting-with-stable-chest-pain">1.3 People presenting with stable chest pain</h3>
    <p>This section of the guideline addresses the assessment and diagnosis of intermittent stable chest pain in people with suspected stable angina.</p>
    <article id="cg95-1_3_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Exclude a diagnosis of stable angina if clinical assessment indicates non-anginal chest pain (see recommendation 1.3.3.1) and there are no other aspects of the history or risk factors raising clinical suspicion. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg95-1_3_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If clinical assessment indicates typical or atypical angina (see recommendation 1.3.3.1), offer diagnostic testing (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnostic-testing-for-people-in-whom-stable-angina-cannot-be-excluded-by-clinical-assessment-alone" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnostic-testing-for-people-in-whom-stable-angina-cannot-be-excluded-by-clinical-assessment-alone">sections on diagnostic testing for people in whom stable angina cannot be excluded by clinical assessment alone</a>, <a class="link" href="recommendations#additional-diagnostic-investigations" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#additional-diagnostic-investigations">additional diagnostic investigations</a> and <a class="link" href="recommendations#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia">use of non-invasive functional testing for myocardial ischaemia</a>). <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.2 Clinical assessment" id="cg95-clinical-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="clinical-assessment">1.3.2 Clinical assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take a detailed clinical history documenting:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the age and sex of the person</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the characteristics of the pain, including its location, radiation, severity, duration and frequency, and factors that provoke and relieve the pain</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any associated symptoms, such as breathlessness </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any history of angina, MI, coronary revascularisation or other cardiovascular disease <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any cardiovascular risk factors. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Carry out a physical examination to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>identify risk factors for cardiovascular disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>identify signs of other cardiovascular disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>identify non-coronary causes of angina (for example, severe aortic stenosis, cardiomyopathy) <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>exclude other causes of chest pain. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.3 Making a diagnosis based on clinical assessment" id="cg95-making-a-diagnosis-based-on-clinical-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="making-a-diagnosis-based-on-clinical-assessment">1.3.3 Making a diagnosis based on clinical assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the typicality of chest pain as follows:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Presence of three of the features below is defined as typical angina.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Presence of two of the three features below is defined as atypical angina.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Presence of one or none of the features below is defined as non-anginal chest pain.<br /><br />Anginal pain is:</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>constricting discomfort in the front of the chest, or in the neck, shoulders, jaw or arms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>precipitated by physical exertion </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>relieved by rest or GTN within about 5 minutes. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not define typical and atypical features of anginal chest pain and non-anginal chest pain differently in men and women. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not define typical and atypical features of anginal chest pain and non-anginal chest pain differently in ethnic groups. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take the following factors, which make a diagnosis of stable angina more likely, into account when estimating people's likelihood of angina: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>age</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>whether the person is male </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>cardiovascular risk factors including:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>a history of smoking</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>diabetes</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>hypertension</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>dyslipidaemia </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>family history of premature coronary artery disease (CAD)</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other cardiovascular disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>history of established CAD, for example, previous MI, coronary revascularisation. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Unless clinical suspicion is raised based on other aspects of the history and risk factors, exclude a diagnosis of stable angina if the pain is non-anginal (see recommendation 1.3.3.1). Features which make a diagnosis of stable angina unlikely are when the chest pain is:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>continuous or very prolonged <strong>and/or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>unrelated to activity <strong>and/or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>brought on by breathing in <strong>and/or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>associated with symptoms such as dizziness, palpitations, tingling or difficulty swallowing.<br /><br />Consider causes of chest pain other than angina (such as gastrointestinal or musculoskeletal pain). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider investigating other causes of angina, such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, in people with typical angina-like chest pain and a low likelihood of CAD. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Arrange blood tests to identify conditions which exacerbate angina, such as anaemia, for all people being investigated for stable angina. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Only consider chest X‑ray if other diagnoses, such as a lung tumour, are suspected. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a diagnosis of stable angina has been excluded at any point in the care pathway, but people have risk factors for cardiovascular disease, follow the appropriate guidance, for example, the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181">NICE guideline on cardiovascular disease</a> and the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136">NICE guideline on hypertension in adults</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people in whom stable angina cannot be excluded on the basis of the clinical assessment alone, take a resting 12‑lead ECG as soon as possible after presentation. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not rule out a diagnosis of stable angina on the basis of a normal resting 12‑lead ECG. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer diagnostic testing to people with non-anginal chest pain on clinical assessment (see recommendation 1.3.3.1) unless there are resting ECG ST‑T changes or Q waves. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>A number of changes on a resting 12‑lead ECG are consistent with CAD and may indicate ischaemia or previous infarction. These include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pathological Q waves in particular</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>LBBB</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ST‑segment and T wave abnormalities (for example, flattening or inversion).<br /><br />Note that the results may not be conclusive.<br /><br />Consider any resting 12‑lead ECG changes together with people's clinical history and risk factors. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with confirmed CAD (for example, previous MI, revascularisation, previous angiography) in whom stable angina cannot be excluded based on clinical assessment alone, see recommendation 1.3.4.4 about functional testing. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider aspirin only if the person's chest pain is likely to be stable angina, until a diagnosis is made. Do not offer additional aspirin if there is clear evidence that people are already taking aspirin regularly or are allergic to it. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_3_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126">NICE guideline on stable angina</a> while waiting for the results of investigations if symptoms are typical of stable angina. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.4 Diagnostic testing for people in whom stable angina cannot be excluded by clinical assessment alone" id="cg95-diagnostic-testing-for-people-in-whom-stable-angina-cannot-be-excluded-by-clinical-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="diagnostic-testing-for-people-in-whom-stable-angina-cannot-be-excluded-by-clinical-assessment-alone">1.3.4 Diagnostic testing for people in whom stable angina cannot be excluded by clinical assessment alone</h4>
      <p>The Guideline Development Group emphasised that the recommendations in this guideline are to make a diagnosis of chest pain, not to screen for CAD. Most people diagnosed with non-anginal chest pain after clinical assessment need no further diagnostic testing. However in a very small number of people, there are remaining concerns that the pain could be ischaemic.</p>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Include the typicality of anginal pain features (see recommendation 1.3.3.1) in all requests for diagnostic investigations and in the person's notes. <strong>[2010, amended 2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use clinical judgement and take into account people's preferences and comorbidities when considering diagnostic testing. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer 64‑slice (or above) CT coronary angiography if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>clinical assessment (see recommendation 1.3.3.1) indicates typical or atypical angina <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>clinical assessment indicates non-anginal chest pain but 12‑lead resting ECG has been done and indicates ST‑T changes or Q waves. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with confirmed CAD (for example, previous MI, revascularisation, previous angiography), offer non-invasive functional testing when there is uncertainty about whether chest pain is caused by myocardial ischaemia. See the <a class="link" href="recommendations#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia">section on use of non-invasive functional testing for myocardial ischaemia</a> for further guidance on non-invasive functional testing. An exercise ECG may be used instead of functional imaging. <strong>[2010]<br /><br /></strong>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/mtg32" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/mtg32">NICE's medical technologies guidance on HeartFlow FFRCT for estimating fractional flow reserve from coronary CT angiography</a>. Using HeartFlow FFR<sub>CT</sub> may avoid the need for invasive coronary angiography and revascularisation for some patients</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.5 Additional diagnostic investigations" id="cg95-additional-diagnostic-investigations">
      <h4 class="title" id="additional-diagnostic-investigations">1.3.5 Additional diagnostic investigations</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer non-invasive functional imaging (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia">section on use of non-invasive functional testing for myocardial ischaemia</a>) for myocardial ischaemia if 64‑slice (or above) CT coronary angiography has shown CAD of uncertain functional significance or is non-diagnostic. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer invasive coronary angiography as a third-line investigation when the results of non-invasive functional imaging are inconclusive. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.6 Use of non-invasive functional testing for myocardial ischaemia" id="cg95-use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia">
      <h4 class="title" id="use-of-non-invasive-functional-testing-for-myocardial-ischaemia">1.3.6 Use of non-invasive functional testing for myocardial ischaemia</h4>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering non-invasive functional imaging for myocardial ischaemia use:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>myocardial perfusion scintigraphy with single photon emission computed tomography (MPS with SPECT) <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>stress echocardiography <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>first-pass contrast-enhanced magnetic resonance (MR) perfusion <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>MR imaging for stress-induced wall motion abnormalities.<br /><br />Take account of locally available technology and expertise, the person and their preferences, and any contraindications (for example, disabilities, frailty, limited ability to exercise) when deciding on the imaging method. [This recommendation updates and replaces recommendation 1.1 of <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta73" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta73">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on myocardial perfusion scintigraphy for the diagnosis and management of angina and myocardial infarction</a>]. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use adenosine, dipyridamole or dobutamine as stress agents for MPS with SPECT and adenosine or dipyridamole for first-pass contrast-enhanced MR perfusion. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use exercise or dobutamine for stress echocardiography or MR imaging for stress-induced wall motion abnormalities. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use MR coronary angiography for diagnosing stable angina. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use exercise ECG to diagnose or exclude stable angina for people without known CAD. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="1.3.7 Making a diagnosis following investigations" id="cg95-making-a-diagnosis-following-investigations">
      <h4 class="title" id="making-a-diagnosis-following-investigations">1.3.7 Making a diagnosis following investigations</h4>
      <div class="panel panel--primary" title="Box 1 Definition of significant coronary artery disease">
        <h5 class="title" id="ID0E5HAE">Box 1 Definition of significant coronary artery disease</h5>
        <p>Significant coronary artery disease (CAD) found during CT coronary angiography is  ≥ 70% diameter stenosis of at least one major epicardial artery segment or  ≥ 50% diameter stenosis in the left main coronary artery:</p>
        <div class="section" title="Factors intensifying ischaemia" id="cg95-factors-intensifying-ischaemia">
          <h6 class="title" id="factors-intensifying-ischaemia">Factors intensifying ischaemia</h6>
          <p>Such factors allow less severe lesions (for example,  ≥ 50%) to produce angina:</p>
          <div class="itemizedlist">
            <ul class="itemizedlist">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>reduced oxygen delivery: anaemia, coronary spasm</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>increased oxygen demand: tachycardia, left ventricular hypertrophy</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>large mass of ischaemic myocardium: proximally located lesions</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>longer lesion length.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="section" title="Factors reducing ischaemia which may render severe lesions (≥ 70%) asymptomatic:" id="cg95-factors-reducing-ischaemia-which-may-render-severe-lesions-70-asymptomatic">
          <h6 class="title" id="factors-reducing-ischaemia-which-may-render-severe-lesions-70-asymptomatic">Factors reducing ischaemia which may render severe lesions (≥ 70%) asymptomatic:</h6>
          <div class="itemizedlist">
            <ul class="itemizedlist">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>Well-developed collateral supply.</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>Small mass of ischaemic myocardium: distally located lesions, old infarction in the territory of coronary supply. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Confirm a diagnosis of stable angina and follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126">NICE guideline on stable angina</a> when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>significant CAD (see box 1) is found during invasive or 64‑slice (or above) CT coronary angiography <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>reversible myocardial ischaemia is found during non-invasive functional imaging. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Investigate other causes of chest pain when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>significant CAD (see box 1) is not found during invasive coronary angiography or 64‑slice (or above) CT coronary angiography <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>reversible myocardial ischaemia is not found during non-invasive functional imaging. <strong>[2016]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg95-1_3_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider investigating other causes of angina, such as hypertrophic cardiomyopathy or syndrome X, in people with typical angina-like chest pain if investigation excludes flow-limiting disease in the epicardial coronary arteries. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
