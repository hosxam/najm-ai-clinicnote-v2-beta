<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng136-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/public-involvement/your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>The recommendations on measuring blood pressure and diagnosing hypertension in this guideline apply to all adults, including those with type 2 diabetes. The recommendations on treatment and monitoring link to <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203">NICE's guidelines on chronic kidney disease</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17">type 1 diabetes</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng133" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng133">hypertension in pregnancy</a> at points in the care pathway where treatment differs. The recommendations on treatment and monitoring apply to adults with type 2 diabetes and replace recommendations on diagnosing and managing hypertension in NICE's guideline on type 2 diabetes in adults.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Measuring blood pressure" id="ng136-measuring-blood-pressure">
    <h3 class="title" id="measuring-blood-pressure">1.1 Measuring blood pressure</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Training, technique and device maintenance" id="ng136-training-technique-and-device-maintenance">
      <h4 class="title" id="training-technique-and-device-maintenance">Training, technique and device maintenance</h4>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that healthcare professionals taking blood pressure measurements have adequate initial training and periodic review of their performance. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Because automated devices may not measure blood pressure accurately if there is pulse irregularity (for example, due to atrial fibrillation), palpate the radial or brachial pulse before measuring blood pressure. If pulse irregularity is present, measure blood pressure manually using direct auscultation over the brachial artery. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Healthcare providers must ensure that devices for measuring blood pressure are properly validated, maintained and regularly recalibrated according to manufacturers' instructions. See the <a class="link" href="https://bihs.org.uk/blood_pressure_technology/bp_monitor_validations.aspx" target="_top">British and Irish Hypertension Society's website for a list of validated blood pressure monitoring devices</a>. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When measuring blood pressure in the clinic or in the home, standardise the environment and provide a relaxed, temperate setting, with the person quiet and seated, and their arm outstretched and supported. Use an appropriate cuff size for the person's arm. <strong>[2011, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Postural hypotension" id="ng136-postural-hypotension">
      <h4 class="title" id="postural-hypotension">Postural hypotension</h4>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In people with symptoms of postural hypotension, including falls or postural dizziness:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>measure blood pressure with the person lying on their back (or consider a seated position, if it is inconvenient to measure blood pressure with the person lying down)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>measure blood pressure again after the person has been standing for at least 1 minute. <strong>[2004, amended 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person's systolic blood pressure falls by 20 mmHg or more, or their diastolic blood pressure falls by 10 mmHg or more, after the person has been standing for at least 1 minute:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider likely causes, including reviewing their current medication</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>manage appropriately (for example, for advice on preventing falls in older people, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg161" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg161">NICE's guideline on falls in older people: assessing risk and prevention</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>measure subsequent blood pressures with the person standing</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider referral to specialist care if symptoms of postural hypotension persist despite addressing likely causes. <strong>[2004, amended 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the blood pressure drop is less than the thresholds in recommendation 1.1.6 despite suggestive symptoms and the baseline measurement was previously taken from a seated position, repeat the measurements this time starting with the person lying on their back. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring the person for further specialist assessment if blood pressure measurements do not confirm postural hypotension despite suggestive symptoms. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Diagnosing hypertension" id="ng136-diagnosing-hypertension">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnosing-hypertension">1.2 Diagnosing hypertension</h3>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When considering a diagnosis of hypertension, measure blood pressure in both arms:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>If the difference in readings between arms is more than 15 mmHg, repeat the measurements.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>If the difference in readings between arms remains more than 15 mmHg on the second measurement, measure subsequent blood pressures in the arm with the higher reading. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If blood pressure measured in the clinic is 140/90 mmHg or higher:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Take a second measurement during the consultation.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>If the second measurement is substantially different from the first, take a third measurement.<br /><br />Record the lower of the last 2 measurements as the clinic blood pressure. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If clinic blood pressure is between 140/90 mmHg and 180/120 mmHg, offer ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) to confirm the diagnosis of hypertension. See the <a class="link" href="recommendations#identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review">section on identifying who to refer</a> for people with a clinic blood pressure 180/120 mmHg or higher. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If ABPM is unsuitable or the person is unable to tolerate it, offer home blood pressure monitoring (HBPM) to confirm the diagnosis of hypertension. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>While waiting for confirmation of a diagnosis of hypertension, carry out:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>investigations for <a class="link" href="recommendations#target-organ-damage" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#target-organ-damage">target organ damage</a> (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage">recommendation 1.3.3</a>), <strong>followed by</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>formal assessment of cardiovascular risk using a cardiovascular risk assessment tool (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238/chapter/Recommendations#full-formal-risk-assessment" target="_top">section on full formal risk assessment in NICE's guideline on cardiovascular disease</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When using ABPM to confirm a diagnosis of hypertension, ensure that at least 2 measurements per hour are taken during the person's usual waking hours (for example, between 08:00 and 22:00). Use the average value of at least 14 measurements taken during the person's usual waking hours to confirm a diagnosis of hypertension. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When using HBPM to confirm a diagnosis of hypertension, ensure that:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>for each blood pressure recording, 2 consecutive measurements are taken, at least 1 minute apart and with the person seated <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>blood pressure is recorded twice daily, ideally in the morning and evening <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>blood pressure recording continues for at least 4 days, ideally for 7 days.<br /><br />Discard the measurements taken on the first day and use the average value of all the remaining measurements to confirm a diagnosis of hypertension. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Confirm diagnosis of hypertension in people with a:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>clinic blood pressure of 140/90 mmHg or higher <strong>and</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>ABPM daytime average or HBPM average of 135/85 mmHg or higher. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If hypertension is not diagnosed but there is evidence of target organ damage, consider carrying out investigations for alternative causes of the target organ damage (for information on investigations, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203">NICE's guidelines on chronic kidney disease</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106">chronic heart failure</a>). <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If hypertension is not diagnosed, measure the person's clinic blood pressure at least every 5 years subsequently, and consider measuring it more frequently if the person's clinic blood pressure is less than 140/90 mmHg but still raised. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_2_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.11</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer advice on healthy living in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nhs.uk/live-well/" target="_top">NHS information on healthy living</a> to people who have raised blood pressure but have not been diagnosed with hypertension. <strong>[2026]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="section" title="Annual blood pressure measurement for people with type 2 diabetes" id="ng136-annual-blood-pressure-measurement-for-people-with-type-2-diabetes">
      <h4 class="title" id="annual-blood-pressure-measurement-for-people-with-type-2-diabetes">Annual blood pressure measurement for people with type 2 diabetes</h4>
      <article id="ng136-1_2_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Measure blood pressure at least annually in an adult with type 2 diabetes without previously diagnosed hypertension or renal disease. Offer and reinforce preventive lifestyle advice. <strong>[2009]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Specialist investigations for possible secondary causes of hypertension" id="ng136-specialist-investigations-for-possible-secondary-causes-of-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="specialist-investigations-for-possible-secondary-causes-of-hypertension">Specialist investigations for possible secondary causes of hypertension</h4>
      <article id="ng136-1_2_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider the need for specialist investigations in people with signs and symptoms suggesting a secondary cause of hypertension. <strong>[2004, amended 2011]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#diagnosing-hypertension-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#diagnosing-hypertension-2">rationale and impact section on diagnosing hypertension</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/a-diagnosis-pdf-6896748206" target="_top">evidence review A: diagnosis</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.3 Assessing cardiovascular risk and target organ damage" id="ng136-assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage">
    <h3 class="title" id="assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage">1.3 Assessing cardiovascular risk and target organ damage</h3>
    <p>For guidance on the early identification and management of chronic kidney disease, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203">NICE's guideline on chronic kidney disease</a>.</p>
    <article id="ng136-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Use a formal estimation of cardiovascular risk to discuss prognosis and healthcare options with people with hypertension, both for raised blood pressure and other modifiable risk factors. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Estimate cardiovascular risk in line with the recommendations on identifying and assessing cardiovascular disease risk in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng238">NICE's guideline on cardiovascular disease</a>. Use clinic blood pressure measurements to calculate cardiovascular risk. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For all people with hypertension offer to:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>test for the presence of protein in the urine by sending a urine sample for estimation of the albumin:creatinine ratio and test for haematuria using a reagent strip</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>take a blood sample to measure glycated haemoglobin (HbA1C), electrolytes, creatinine, estimated glomerular filtration rate, total cholesterol and HDL cholesterol</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>examine the fundi for the presence of hypertensive retinopathy</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>arrange for a 12‑lead electrocardiograph to be performed. <strong>[2011, amended 2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Treating and monitoring hypertension" id="ng136-treating-and-monitoring-hypertension">
    <h3 class="title" id="treating-and-monitoring-hypertension">1.4 Treating and monitoring hypertension</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Lifestyle interventions" id="ng136-lifestyle-interventions">
      <h4 class="title" id="lifestyle-interventions">Lifestyle interventions</h4>
      <p>For guidance on the prevention of obesity and cardiovascular disease, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246">NICE's guidelines on overweight and obesity management</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ph25" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ph25">cardiovascular disease prevention</a>.</p>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer lifestyle advice to people with suspected or diagnosed hypertension, and continue to offer it periodically. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask about people's diet and exercise patterns because a healthy diet and regular exercise can reduce blood pressure. Offer appropriate guidance and written or audiovisual materials to promote lifestyle changes. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask about people's alcohol consumption and encourage a reduced intake if they drink excessively, because this can reduce blood pressure and has broader health benefits. See the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ph24/chapter/1-Recommendations#recommendations-for-practice" target="_top">recommendations for practice in NICE's guideline on alcohol-use disorders</a>. <strong>[2004, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discourage excessive consumption of coffee and other caffeine-rich products. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage people to keep their dietary sodium intake low, either by reducing or substituting sodium salt, as this can reduce blood pressure. Note that salt substitutes containing potassium chloride should not be used by older people, people with diabetes, pregnant women, people with kidney disease and people taking some antihypertensive drugs, such as ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor blockers. Encourage salt reduction in these groups. <strong>[2004, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer calcium, magnesium or potassium supplements as a method for reducing blood pressure. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer advice and help to smokers to stop smoking. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng209" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng209">NICE's guideline on tobacco</a>. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Inform people about local initiatives by, for example, healthcare teams or patient organisations that provide support and promote healthy lifestyle change, especially those that include group work for motivating lifestyle change. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee deleted the recommendation on relaxation therapies and how this might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#relaxation-therapies-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#relaxation-therapies-2">rationale and impact section on relaxation therapies</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/h-relaxation-therapies-pdf-6896748213" target="_top">evidence review H: relaxation therapies</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Starting antihypertensive drug treatment" id="ng136-starting-antihypertensive-drug-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="starting-antihypertensive-drug-treatment">Starting antihypertensive drug treatment</h4>
      <p>NICE has produced a <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG136/resources" target="_top">patient decision aid on treatment options for hypertension</a> to help people and their healthcare professionals discuss the different types of treatment and make a decision that is right for each person.</p>
      <p>For advice on shared decision making for medicines, see the information on patient decision aids in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5">NICE's guideline on medicines optimisation</a>. </p>
      <p>To support adherence and ensure that people with hypertension make the most effective use of their medicines, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76">NICE's guideline on medicines adherence</a>.</p>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer antihypertensive drug treatment in addition to lifestyle advice to adults of any age with persistent <a class="link" href="recommendations#stage-2-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#stage-2-hypertension">stage 2 hypertension</a>. Use clinical judgement for people of any age with frailty or multimorbidity (see also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56/">NICE's guideline on multimorbidity</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss starting antihypertensive drug treatment, in addition to lifestyle advice, with adults aged under 80 with persistent <a class="link" href="recommendations#stage-1-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#stage-1-hypertension">stage 1 hypertension</a> who have 1 or more of the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>target organ damage</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#established-cardiovascular-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#established-cardiovascular-disease">established cardiovascular disease</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>renal disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>diabetes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an estimated 10‑year risk of cardiovascular disease of 10% or more. <br /><br />Use clinical judgement for people with frailty or multimorbidity (see also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56/">NICE's guideline on multimorbidity</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with the person their individual cardiovascular disease risk and their preferences for treatment, including no treatment, and explain the risks and benefits before starting antihypertensive drug treatment. Continue to offer lifestyle advice and support them to make lifestyle changes (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#lifestyle-interventions" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#lifestyle-interventions">section on lifestyle interventions</a>), whether or not they choose to start antihypertensive drug treatment. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider antihypertensive drug treatment in addition to lifestyle advice for adults aged under 60 with stage 1 hypertension and an estimated 10‑year risk below 10%. Bear in mind that 10‑year cardiovascular risk may underestimate the lifetime probability of developing cardiovascular disease. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider antihypertensive drug treatment in addition to lifestyle advice for people aged over 80 with stage 1 hypertension if their clinic blood pressure is over 150/90 mmHg. Use clinical judgement for people with frailty or multimorbidity (see also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56/">NICE's guideline on multimorbidity</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults aged under 40 with hypertension, consider seeking specialist evaluation of secondary causes of hypertension and a more detailed assessment of the long-term balance of treatment benefit and risks. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#starting-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#starting-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-2">rationale and impact section on starting antihypertensive drug treatment</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/c-initiating-treatment-pdf-6896748208" target="_top">evidence review C: initiating treatment</a>.</p>
      </div>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Check for postural hypotension (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#postural-hypotension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#postural-hypotension">recommendation 1.1.5</a>) in people with hypertension and:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>type 2 diabetes <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms of postural hypotension (see also recommendation 1.1.7) <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>aged 80 and over. <br /><br />In people with a significant postural drop or symptoms of postural hypotension, treat to a blood pressure target based on standing blood pressure. <strong>[2019, amended 2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with hypertension who choose to self-monitor their blood pressure to use HBPM. (<a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/ourwork/clinical-policy/cvd/home-blood-pressure-monitoring/" target="_top">NHS England is supporting the use of HBPM through the blood pressure@home scheme</a>.) <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider ABPM or HBPM, in addition to clinic blood pressure measurements, for people with hypertension identified as having a <a class="link" href="recommendations#white-coat-effect" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#white-coat-effect">white-coat effect</a> or <a class="link" href="recommendations#masked-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#masked-hypertension">masked hypertension</a> (in which clinic and non-clinic blood pressure results are conflicting). Be aware that the corresponding measurements for ABPM and HBPM are 5 mmHg lower than for clinic measurements (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-hypertension">recommendation 1.2.8 for diagnostic thresholds</a>).<strong> [2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who choose to use HBPM, provide:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>training and advice on using home blood pressure monitors</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>information about what to do if they are not achieving their target blood pressure.<br /><br />Be aware that the corresponding measurements for HBPM are 5 mmHg lower than for clinic measurements (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-hypertension">recommendation 1.2.8 for diagnostic thresholds</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with hypertension aged under 80, reduce clinic blood pressure to below 140/90 mmHg and ensure that it is maintained below that level. See also <a class="link" href="recommendations#monitoring-treatment-and-blood-pressure-targets" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#monitoring-treatment-and-blood-pressure-targets">table 1 for guidance on clinic blood pressure targets for people aged under 80 with type 1 diabetes or severe chronic kidney disease</a>. <strong>[2019, amended 2022]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with hypertension aged 80 and over, reduce clinic blood pressure to below 150/90 mmHg and ensure that it is maintained below that level. Use clinical judgement for people with frailty or multimorbidity (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng56/">NICE's guideline on multimorbidity</a>). See also <a class="link" href="recommendations#monitoring-treatment-and-blood-pressure-targets" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#monitoring-treatment-and-blood-pressure-targets">table 2 for guidance on clinic blood pressure targets for people aged 80 and over with type 1 diabetes or severe chronic kidney disease</a>. <strong>[2019, amended 2022]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When using ABPM or HBPM to monitor the response to treatment in adults with hypertension, use the average blood pressure level taken during the person's usual waking hours (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-hypertension">recommendations 1.2.6 and 1.2.7</a>). Reduce blood pressure and ensure that it is maintained:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>below 135/85 mmHg for adults aged under 80</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>below 145/85 mmHg for adults aged 80 and over. <br /><br />Use clinical judgement for people with frailty or multimorbidity (see also NICE's guideline on multimorbidity). <strong>[2019, amended 2022]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>

    <div class="section" title="Choosing antihypertensive drug treatment (for people with or without type 2 diabetes)" id="ng136-choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes">
      <h4 class="title" id="choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes">Choosing antihypertensive drug treatment (for people with or without type 2 diabetes)</h4>
      <p>The recommendations in this section apply to people with hypertension with or without type 2 diabetes. They replace the recommendations on diagnosing and managing hypertension in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng28" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng28">NICE's guideline on type 2 diabetes in adults</a>. For guidance on choosing antihypertensive drug treatment in people with type 1 diabetes, see also the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17/chapter/1-Recommendations#control-of-cardiovascular-risk" target="_top">section on control of cardiovascular risk in NICE's guideline on type 1 diabetes</a>.</p>
      <div class="panel panel--primary">
        <p>Note that ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists should not be used in pregnant or breastfeeding women or women planning pregnancy unless absolutely necessary, in which case the potential risks and benefits should be discussed. Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-not-for-use-in-pregnancy" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists: not for use in pregnancy</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-recommendations-on-how-to-use-for-breastfeeding" target="_top">recommendations on how to use for breastfeeding</a> and the related <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/clarification-ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists" target="_top">clarification on breastfeeding</a>.</p>
      </div>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_26" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.26</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on choice of antihypertensive medicine in people with chronic kidney disease, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203">NICE's guideline on chronic kidney disease</a>. If possible, offer treatment with drugs taken only once a day. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_27" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.27</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Prescribe non-proprietary drugs if these are appropriate and minimise cost. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_28" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.28</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer people with isolated systolic hypertension (systolic blood pressure 160 mmHg or more) the same treatment as people with both raised systolic and diastolic blood pressure. <strong>[2004]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_29" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.29</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer antihypertensive drug treatment to women of childbearing potential with diagnosed hypertension in line with the recommendations in this guideline. For women considering pregnancy or who are pregnant or breastfeeding, manage hypertension in line with the recommendations on management of pregnancy with chronic hypertension, and on antihypertensive treatment while breastfeeding in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng133" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng133">NICE's guideline on hypertension in pregnancy</a>. <strong>[2010, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_30" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.30</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When choosing antihypertensive drug treatment for adults of Black African or African–Caribbean family origin, consider an angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB), in preference to an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-not-for-use-in-pregnancy" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists: not for use in pregnancy</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-recommendations-on-how-to-use-for-breastfeeding" target="_top">how to use for breastfeeding</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/clarification-ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists" target="_top">clarification on breastfeeding</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng136-1_4_31" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.31</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with cardiovascular disease:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Follow the recommendations for disease-specific indications in the NICE guideline on their condition (for example, when prescribing an ACE inhibitor or an ARB for secondary prevention of myocardial infarction). Relevant recommendations include:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>
                        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185/chapter/Recommendations#drug-therapy-for-secondary-prevention" target="_top">drug therapy for secondary prevention in NICE's guideline on acute coronary syndromes</a>
                      </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>
                        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg187/chapter/1-Recommendations#treatment-after-stabilisation-2" target="_top">treatment after stabilisation in NICE's guideline on acute heart failure</a>
                      </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>
                        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106/chapter/Recommendations#treating-heart-failure-with-reduced-ejection-fraction" target="_top">treating heart failure with reduced ejection fraction in NICE's guideline on chronic heart failure</a>
                      </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>
                        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126/chapter/1-Guidance#drugs-for-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease" target="_top">drugs for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease in NICE's guideline on stable angina</a>
                      </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>
                        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17/chapter/Recommendations#blood-pressure-management" target="_top">blood pressure management in NICE's guideline on type 1 diabetes in adults</a>.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>If their blood pressure remains uncontrolled, offer antihypertensive drug treatment in line with the recommendations in this section. <strong>[2022]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the recommendation on choosing antihypertensive drug treatment for people with cardiovascular disease and how this might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-cardiovascular-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-cardiovascular-disease">rationale and impact section on choosing antihypertensive drug treatment for people with cardiovascular disease</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/evidence-review-k-pdf-11008446879" target="_top">evidence review K: pharmacological treatment in cardiovascular disease</a>.  </p>

      <div class="section" title="Step 1 treatment" id="ng136-step-1-treatment">
        <h5 class="title" id="step-1-treatment">Step 1 treatment</h5>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_32" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.32</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer an ACE inhibitor or an ARB to adults starting step 1 antihypertensive treatment who:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>have type 2 diabetes and are of any age or family origin (see also <a class="link" href="recommendations#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes">recommendation 1.4.30</a> for adults of Black African or African–Caribbean family origin) <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>are aged under 55 but not of Black African or African–Caribbean family origin. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-not-for-use-in-pregnancy" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists: not for use in pregnancy</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-recommendations-on-how-to-use-for-breastfeeding" target="_top">how to use for breastfeeding</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/clarification-ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists" target="_top">clarification on breastfeeding</a>.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.33</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If an ACE inhibitor is not tolerated, for example because of cough, offer an ARB to treat hypertension. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-not-for-use-in-pregnancy" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists: not for use in pregnancy</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-recommendations-on-how-to-use-for-breastfeeding" target="_top">how to use for breastfeeding</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/clarification-ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists" target="_top">clarification on breastfeeding</a>.</p>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.34</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Do not combine an ACE inhibitor with an ARB to treat hypertension. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.35</h6>

                <p>are aged 55 or over and do not have type 2 diabetes <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>are of Black African or African–Caribbean family origin and do not have type 2 diabetes (of any age). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>

          </div>

        <article id="ng136-1_4_37" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">

          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If there is evidence of heart failure, offer a thiazide-like diuretic and follow <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106">NICE's guideline on chronic heart failure</a>. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>

          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If starting or changing diuretic treatment for hypertension, offer a thiazide-like diuretic, such as indapamide in preference to a conventional thiazide diuretic such as bendroflumethiazide or hydrochlorothiazide. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>

          <div class="recommendation__body">

                <p>a CCB <strong>or</strong></p>

              <li class="listitem">

              <li class="listitem">

          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Step 3 treatment" id="ng136-step-3-treatment">
        <h5 class="title" id="step-3-treatment">Step 3 treatment</h5>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_44" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.44</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Before considering next step treatment for hypertension: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>review the person's medications to ensure they are being taken at the optimal tolerated doses <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>discuss adherence (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#step-2-treatment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#step-2-treatment">recommendation 1.4.40</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_45" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.45</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If hypertension is not controlled in adults taking step 2 treatment, offer a combination of:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>an ACE inhibitor or ARB (see also <a class="link" href="recommendations#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#choosing-antihypertensive-drug-treatment-for-people-with-or-without-type-2-diabetes">recommendation 1.4.30</a> for people of Black African or African–Caribbean family origin) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a CCB <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a thiazide-like diuretic. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#step-2-and-3-treatment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#step-2-and-3-treatment">rationale and impact section on step 2 and 3 treatment</a>.</p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/f-step-2-and-step-3-treatment-pdf-6896748211" target="_top">evidence review F: step 2 and step 3 treatment</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Step 4 treatment" id="ng136-step-4-treatment">
        <h5 class="title" id="step-4-treatment">Step 4 treatment</h5>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_46" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.46</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If hypertension is not controlled in adults taking the optimal tolerated doses of an ACE inhibitor or an ARB plus a CCB and a thiazide-like diuretic, regard them as having resistant hypertension. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_47" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.47</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Before considering further treatment for a person with resistant hypertension:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>Confirm elevated clinic blood pressure measurements using ambulatory or home blood pressure recordings. </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>Assess for postural hypotension. </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>Discuss adherence (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#step-2-treatment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#step-2-treatment">recommendation 1.4.40</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_48" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.48</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with confirmed resistant hypertension, consider adding a fourth antihypertensive drug as step 4 treatment or seeking specialist advice. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-not-for-use-in-pregnancy" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on ACE inhibitors and angiotensin II receptor antagonists: not for use in pregnancy</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists-recommendations-on-how-to-use-for-breastfeeding" target="_top">how to use for breastfeeding</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/clarification-ace-inhibitors-and-angiotensin-ii-receptor-antagonists" target="_top">clarification on breastfeeding</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_49" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.49</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider further diuretic therapy with low-dose spironolactone for adults with resistant hypertension starting step 4 treatment who have a blood potassium level of 4.5 mmol/l or less. Use particular caution in people with a reduced estimated glomerular filtration rate because they have an increased risk of hyperkalaemia. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br />In March 2019, this was an off-label use of some preparations of spironolactone. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_50" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.50</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When using further diuretic therapy for step 4 treatment of resistant hypertension, monitor blood sodium and potassium and renal function within 1 month of starting treatment and repeat as needed thereafter. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_51" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.51</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider an alpha-blocker or beta-blocker for adults with resistant hypertension starting step 4 treatment who have a blood potassium level of more than 4.5 mmol/l. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng136-1_4_52" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.52</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If blood pressure remains uncontrolled in people with resistant hypertension taking the optimal tolerated doses of 4 drugs, seek specialist advice. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#step-4-treatment-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#step-4-treatment-2">rationale and impact section on step 4 treatment</a>.</p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/g-step-4-treatment-pdf-6896748212" target="_top">evidence review G: step 4 treatment</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Identifying who to refer for same-day specialist review" id="ng136-identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review">
    <h3 class="title" id="identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review">1.5 Identifying who to refer for same-day specialist review</h3>
    <article id="ng136-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If a person has severe hypertension (clinic blood pressure of 180/120 mmHg or higher), but no symptoms or signs indicating same-day referral (see recommendation 1.5.2), carry out investigations for target organ damage (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessing-cardiovascular-risk-and-target-organ-damage">recommendation 1.3.3</a>) as soon as possible:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>If target organ damage is identified, consider starting antihypertensive drug treatment immediately, without waiting for the results of ABPM or HBPM.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>If no target organ damage is identified, confirm diagnosis by:</p>
            <p>
              <div class="itemizedlist">
                <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>repeating clinic blood pressure measurement within 7 days, <strong>or</strong></p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>considering monitoring using ABPM (or HBPM if ABPM is not suitable or not tolerated), following recommendations 1.2.6 and 1.2.7, and ensuring a clinical review within 7 days. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </div>
            </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer people for specialist assessment, carried out on the same day, if they have a clinic blood pressure of 180/120 mmHg and higher with: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>signs of retinal haemorrhage or papilloedema (<a class="link" href="recommendations#accelerated-hypertension" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#accelerated-hypertension">accelerated hypertension</a>) <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>life-threatening symptoms such as new onset confusion, chest pain, signs of heart failure, or acute kidney injury. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng136-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer people for specialist assessment, carried out on the same day, if they have suspected phaeochromocytoma (for example, labile or postural hypotension, headache, palpitations, pallor, abdominal pain or diaphoresis). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#identifying-who-to-refer-for-same-day-specialist-review-2">rationale and impact section on identifying who to refer for same-day specialist review</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng136/evidence/i-sameday-specialist-review-pdf-248282935380" target="_top">evidence review I: same-day specialist review</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng136-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline. For other definitions see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/Glossary" target="_top">NICE glossary</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Accelerated hypertension" id="ng136-accelerated-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="accelerated-hypertension">Accelerated hypertension</h4>
      <p>A severe increase in blood pressure to 180/120 mmHg or higher (and often over 220/120 mmHg) with signs of retinal haemorrhage and/or papilloedema (swelling of the optic nerve). It is usually associated with new or progressive target organ damage and is also known as malignant hypertension.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Established cardiovascular disease" id="ng136-established-cardiovascular-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="established-cardiovascular-disease">Established cardiovascular disease</h4>
      <p>Medical history of ischaemic heart disease, cerebrovascular disease, peripheral vascular disease, aortic aneurysm or heart failure. Cardiovascular disease is a general term for conditions affecting the heart or blood vessels. It is usually associated with a build-up of fatty deposits inside the arteries (atherosclerosis) and an increased risk of blood clots. It can also be associated with damage to arteries in organs such as the brain, heart, kidneys and eyes through deposition of glassy material within the artery walls (arteriosclerosis). Cardiovascular disease is 1 of the main causes of death and disability in the UK, but it can often largely be prevented by leading a healthy lifestyle. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Masked hypertension" id="ng136-masked-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="masked-hypertension">Masked hypertension</h4>
      <p>Clinic blood pressure measurements are normal (less than 140/90 mmHg), but blood pressure measurements are higher when taken outside the clinic using average daytime ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) or average home blood pressure monitoring (HBPM) blood pressure measurements.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Persistent hypertension" id="ng136-persistent-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="persistent-hypertension">Persistent hypertension</h4>
      <p>High blood pressure at repeated clinical encounters.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Stage 1 hypertension" id="ng136-stage-1-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="stage-1-hypertension">Stage 1 hypertension</h4>
      <p>Clinic blood pressure ranging from 140/90 mmHg to 159/99 mmHg and subsequent ABPM daytime average or HBPM average blood pressure ranging from 135/85 mmHg to 149/94 mmHg.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Stage 2 hypertension" id="ng136-stage-2-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="stage-2-hypertension">Stage 2 hypertension</h4>
      <p>Clinic blood pressure of 160/100 mmHg or higher but less than 180/120 mmHg and subsequent ABPM daytime average or HBPM average blood pressure of 150/95 mmHg or higher. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Stage 3 or severe hypertension" id="ng136-stage-3-or-severe-hypertension">
      <h4 class="title" id="stage-3-or-severe-hypertension">Stage 3 or severe hypertension</h4>
      <p>Clinic systolic blood pressure of 180 mmHg or higher or clinic diastolic blood pressure of 120 mmHg or higher.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Target organ damage" id="ng136-target-organ-damage">
      <h4 class="title" id="target-organ-damage">Target organ damage</h4>
      <p>Damage to organs such as the heart, brain, kidneys and eyes. Examples are left ventricular hypertrophy, chronic kidney disease, hypertensive retinopathy or increased urine albumin:creatinine ratio.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="White-coat effect" id="ng136-white-coat-effect">
      <h4 class="title" id="white-coat-effect">White-coat effect</h4>
      <p>A discrepancy of more than 20/10 mmHg between clinic and average daytime ABPM or average HBPM blood pressure measurements at the time of diagnosis.</p>
    </div>
