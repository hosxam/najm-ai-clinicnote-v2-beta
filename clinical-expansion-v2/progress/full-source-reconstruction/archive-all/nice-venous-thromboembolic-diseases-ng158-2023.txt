<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng158-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/public-involvement/your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Diagnosis and initial management" id="ng158-diagnosis-and-initial-management">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnosis-and-initial-management">1.1 Diagnosis and initial management</h3>
    <p>NICE has produced a <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng158/resources/visual-summary-pdf-11193380893" target="_top">visual summary of the recommendations on diagnosis and initial management of suspected deep vein thrombosis (DVT) and pulmonary embolism (PE)</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Risk factors for DVT" id="ng158-risk-factors-for-dvt">
      <h4 class="title" id="risk-factors-for-dvt">Risk factors for DVT</h4>
      <p>See the <a class="link" href="https://cks.nice.org.uk/topics/deep-vein-thrombosis/background-information/risk-factors/" target="_top">section on risk factors for DVT in the clinical knowledge summary on DVT</a>. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Signs or symptoms of DVT" id="ng158-signs-or-symptoms-of-dvt">
      <h4 class="title" id="signs-or-symptoms-of-dvt">Signs or symptoms of DVT</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who present with signs or symptoms of DVT, such as a swollen or painful leg, assess their general medical history and do a physical examination to exclude other causes. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If DVT is suspected, use the 2‑level DVT <a class="link" href="recommendations#wells-score" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#wells-score">Wells score</a> (table 1) to estimate the clinical probability of DVT. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="informaltable">

      <p>Adapted with permission from Wells et al. (2003) <a class="link" href="http://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa023153" target="_top">Evaluation of D-dimer in the diagnosis of suspected deep-vein thrombosis</a>.</p>
      <div class="section" title="DVT likely (Wells score 2 points or more)" id="ng158-dvt-likely-wells-score-2-points-or-more">
        <h5 class="title" id="dvt-likely-wells-score-2-points-or-more">DVT likely (Wells score 2 points or more)</h5>
        <article id="ng158-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with a <strong>likely </strong>DVT Wells score (2 points or more): </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a proximal leg vein ultrasound scan, with the result available within 4 hours if possible (if the scan result cannot be obtained within 4 hours follow recommendation 1.1.4) </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a D‑dimer test if the scan result is negative. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If a proximal leg vein ultrasound scan result cannot be obtained within 4 hours, offer people with a DVT Wells score of 2 points or more:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a D‑dimer test, <strong>then </strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>interim therapeutic anticoagulation (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#interim-therapeutic-anticoagulation-for-suspected-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#interim-therapeutic-anticoagulation-for-suspected-dvt-or-pe">section on interim therapeutic anticoagulation for suspected DVT or PE</a>) <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a proximal leg vein ultrasound scan with the result available within 24 hours. <strong>[2012, amended 2020]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with a positive proximal leg vein ultrasound scan:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>offer or continue anticoagulation treatment (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">section on anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE</a>) <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>if anticoagulation treatment is contraindicated, offer a mechanical intervention (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#mechanical-interventions" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#mechanical-interventions">section on mechanical interventions</a>). <br /><br />For people with symptomatic iliofemoral DVT, see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#thrombolytic-therapy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#thrombolytic-therapy">section on thrombolytic therapy</a>. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h6>

    <div class="section" title="Interim therapeutic anticoagulation for suspected DVT or PE" id="ng158-interim-therapeutic-anticoagulation-for-suspected-dvt-or-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="interim-therapeutic-anticoagulation-for-suspected-dvt-or-pe">Interim therapeutic anticoagulation for suspected DVT or PE</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the recommendations on when to offer interim therapeutic anticoagulation for suspected <a class="link" href="recommendations#proximal-dvt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#proximal-dvt">proximal DVT</a> or PE in the <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosis-and-initial-management" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosis-and-initial-management">section on diagnosis and initial management</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If possible, choose an interim anticoagulant that can be continued if DVT or PE is confirmed (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">section on anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE</a>). <strong>[2020]</strong><br /><br />In March 2020, direct-acting anticoagulants and some low molecular weight heparins (LMWHs) were off label for the treatment of suspected DVT or PE. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When using interim therapeutic anticoagulation for suspected proximal DVT or PE:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>carry out baseline blood tests including full blood count, renal and hepatic function, prothrombin time (PT) and activated partial thromboplastin time (APTT)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>do not wait for the results of baseline blood tests before starting anticoagulation treatment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>review, and if necessary act on, the results of baseline blood tests within 24 hours of starting interim therapeutic anticoagulation. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE" id="ng158-anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">Anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer anticoagulation treatment for at least 3 months to people with confirmed proximal DVT or PE. For recommendations on treatment after 3 months, see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention">section on long-term anticoagulation for secondary prevention</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If not already done, carry out baseline blood tests, as outlined in recommendation 1.3.4, when starting anticoagulation treatment. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering anticoagulation treatment, take into account comorbidities, contraindications and the person's preferences.<br /><br />Follow the recommendations on anticoagulation treatment in the sections on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-in-people-at-extremes-of-body-weight" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-in-people-at-extremes-of-body-weight">DVT or PE in people at extremes of body weight</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-pe-with-haemodynamic-instability" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-pe-with-haemodynamic-instability">PE with haemodynamic instability</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-renal-impairment-or-established-renal-failure" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-renal-impairment-or-established-renal-failure">DVT or PE with renal impairment or established renal failure</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-active-cancer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-active-cancer">DVT or PE with active cancer</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-triple-positive-antiphospholipid-syndrome" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-triple-positive-antiphospholipid-syndrome">DVT or PE with triple positive antiphospholipid syndrome</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer either apixaban or rivaroxaban to people with confirmed proximal DVT or PE (but see recommendations 1.3.11 to 1.3.20 for people with any of the clinical features listed in recommendation 1.3.7). If neither apixaban nor rivaroxaban is suitable offer:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>LMWH for at least 5 days followed by dabigatran or edoxaban <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>LMWH concurrently with a vitamin K antagonist (VKA) for at least 5 days, or until the international normalised ratio (INR) is at least 2.0 in 2 consecutive readings, followed by a VKA on its own. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer unfractionated heparin (UFH) with a VKA to treat confirmed proximal DVT or PE unless the person has renal impairment or established renal failure (see recommendations 1.3.13 and 1.3.14) or an increased risk of bleeding. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_3_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer self‑management or self‑monitoring of INR to people who have had DVT or PE and are having treatment with a VKA. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider anticoagulation treatment with regular monitoring of therapeutic levels for people with confirmed proximal DVT or PE who weigh less than 50 kg or more than 120 kg, to ensure effective anticoagulation. <br /><br />Note the cautions and requirements for dose adjustment and monitoring in the medicine's summary of product characteristics (SPC), and follow locally agreed protocols or advice from a specialist or multidisciplinary team. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>

        </article>

        </article>

      <div class="section" title="Anticoagulation treatment for DVT or PE with renal impairment or established renal failure" id="ng158-anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-renal-impairment-or-established-renal-failure">
        <h5 class="title" id="anticoagulation-treatment-for-dvt-or-pe-with-renal-impairment-or-established-renal-failure">Anticoagulation treatment for DVT or PE with renal impairment or established renal failure</h5>
        <div class="panel panel--primary">
          <p>In March 2020, some LMWHs were off label for the treatment of DVT or PE in people with severe renal impairment (<a class="link" href="recommendations#estimated-creatinine-clearance" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#estimated-creatinine-clearance">estimated creatinine clearance</a> 15 ml/min to 30 ml/min) or established renal failure (estimated creatinine clearance less than 15 ml/min). See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>. </p>
        </div>
        <article id="ng158-1_3_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.13</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with confirmed proximal DVT or PE and renal impairment (<a class="link" href="recommendations#estimated-creatinine-clearance" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#estimated-creatinine-clearance">estimated creatinine clearance</a> between 15 ml/min and 50 ml/min) one of: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>apixaban </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>rivaroxaban</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>LMWH for at least 5 days followed by:</p>
                <p>
                  <div class="itemizedlist">
                    <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>edoxaban <strong>or</strong></p>
                      </li>
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>dabigatran if estimated creatinine clearance is 30 ml/min or above </p>
                      </li>
                    </ul>
                  </div>
                </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>LMWH or UFH, given concurrently with a VKA for at least 5 days or until the INR is at least 2.0 in 2 consecutive readings, followed by a VKA on its own.<br /><br />Note the cautions and requirements for dose adjustment and monitoring in the medicine's SPC, and follow locally agreed protocols or advice from a specialist or multidisciplinary team. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_3_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.14</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with confirmed proximal DVT or PE and established renal failure (<a class="link" href="recommendations#estimated-creatinine-clearance" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#estimated-creatinine-clearance">estimated creatinine clearance</a> less than 15 ml/min) one of: </p>

              <li class="listitem">
                <p>LMWH</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>UFH</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>LMWH or UFH concurrently with a VKA for at least 5 days or until the INR is at least 2.0 in 2 consecutive readings, followed by a VKA on its own.<br /><br />Note the cautions and requirements for dose adjustment and monitoring in the medicine's SPC, and follow locally agreed protocols or advice from a specialist or multidisciplinary team. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

        <div class="panel panel--primary">
          <p>In March 2020, most anticoagulants were off label for the treatment of DVT or PE in people with active cancer. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>. </p>

        <article id="ng158-1_3_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#active-cancer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#active-cancer">active cancer</a> and confirmed proximal DVT or PE anticoagulation treatment for 3 to 6 months. Review at 3 to 6 months according to clinical need. For recommendations on treatment after 3 to 6 months, see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention">section on long-term anticoagulation for secondary prevention</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_3_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.16</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When choosing anticoagulation treatment for people with active cancer and confirmed proximal DVT or PE, take into account the tumour site, interactions with other drugs including those used to treat cancer, and the person's bleeding risk. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_3_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.17</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider a direct-acting oral anticoagulant (DOAC) for people with active cancer and confirmed proximal DVT or PE. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>

        <article id="ng158-1_3_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.18</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>If a DOAC is unsuitable consider LMWH alone or LMWH concurrently with a VKA for at least 5 days, or until the INR is at least 2.0 in 2 consecutive readings, followed by a VKA on its own. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng158-1_3_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.19</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with confirmed DVT or PE and cancer that is in remission, follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">section on anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>

        <div class="recommendation__body">

    <div class="section" title="NICE technology appraisal guidance on anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE" id="ng158-nice-technology-appraisal-guidance-on-anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="nice-technology-appraisal-guidance-on-anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">NICE technology appraisal guidance on anticoagulation treatment for confirmed DVT or PE</h4>
      <p>For direct-acting oral anticoagulants recommended as options in NICE technology appraisal guidance for treating and preventing confirmed DVT or PE, see the guidance on:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta354/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">edoxaban (TA354, August 2015)</a>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">

            </p>

          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta327/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">dabigatran etexilate (TA327, December 2014)</a>

  <div class="section" title="1.4 Long-term anticoagulation for secondary prevention" id="ng158-long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention">
    <h3 class="title" id="long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention">1.4 Long-term anticoagulation for secondary prevention</h3>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Assess and discuss the benefits and risks of continuing, stopping or changing the anticoagulant with people who have had anticoagulation treatment for 3 months (3 to 6 months for people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#active-cancer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#active-cancer">active cancer</a>) after a <a class="link" href="recommendations#proximal-dvt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#proximal-dvt">proximal DVT</a> or PE. Follow the recommendations on shared decision making and supporting adherence in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5">NICE guidelines on medicines optimisation</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76">medicines adherence</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">patient experience in adult NHS services</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider stopping anticoagulation treatment 3 months (3 to 6 months for people with active cancer) after a <a class="link" href="recommendations#provoked-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#provoked-dvt-or-pe">provoked DVT or PE</a> if the provoking factor is no longer present and the clinical course has been uncomplicated. If anticoagulation treatment is stopped, give advice about the risk of recurrence and provide:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>written information on symptoms and signs to look out for</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>direct contact details of a healthcare professional or team with expertise in thrombosis who can discuss any new symptoms or signs, or other concerns </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>information about out-of-hours services they can contact when their healthcare team is not available. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider continuing anticoagulation beyond 3 months (6 months for people with active cancer) after an <a class="link" href="recommendations#unprovoked-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#unprovoked-dvt-or-pe">unprovoked DVT or PE</a>. Base the decision on the balance between the person's risk of VTE recurrence and their risk of bleeding. Discuss the risks and benefits of long-term anticoagulation with the person, and take their preferences into account. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain to people with unprovoked DVT or PE and a low bleeding risk that the benefits of continuing anticoagulation treatment are likely to outweigh the risks. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not rely solely on predictive risk tools to assess the need for long-term anticoagulation treatment. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider using the <a class="link" href="https://www.mdcalc.com/has-bled-score-major-bleeding-risk" target="_top">HAS-BLED score for major bleeding risk</a> to assess the risk of major bleeding in people having anticoagulation treatment for unprovoked proximal DVT or PE. Discuss stopping anticoagulation if the HAS-BLED score is 4 or more and cannot be modified. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Take into account the person's preferences and their clinical situation when selecting an anticoagulant for long-term treatment. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people who do not have renal impairment, active cancer, established triple positive antiphospholipid syndrome or extreme body weight (less than 50 kg or more than 120 kg):</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>offer continued treatment with the current anticoagulant if it is well tolerated <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>if the current treatment is not well tolerated, or the clinical situation or person's preferences have changed, consider switching to apixaban if the current treatment is a direct-acting anticoagulant other than apixaban. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people with renal impairment, active cancer, established triple positive antiphospholipid syndrome or extreme body weight (less than 50 kg or more than 120 kg), consider carrying on with the current treatment if it is well tolerated. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If anticoagulation treatment fails follow the <a class="link" href="recommendations#treatment-failure" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#treatment-failure">recommendation on treatment failure</a>.<strong> [2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For people who decline continued anticoagulation treatment, consider aspirin 75 mg or 150 mg daily. <br /><br />In March 2020, the use of aspirin for secondary prevention of DVT or PE was off label. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review general health, risk of VTE recurrence, bleeding risk and treatment preferences at least once a year for people taking long-term anticoagulation treatment or aspirin. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2020 recommendations on reviewing anticoagulation treatment and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#long-term-anticoagulation-for-secondary-prevention-2">rationale and impact section on long-term anticoagulation for secondary prevention</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng158/evidence/f-duration-of-pharmacological-treatment-pdf-8710588339" target="_top">evidence review F: what factors determine the optimum duration of pharmacological treatment for DVT or PE in people with a VTE?</a>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng158/evidence/d-pharmacological-treatment-pdf-8710588337" target="_top">evidence review D: pharmacological treatment in people with suspected or confirmed deep vein thrombosis and/or pulmonary embolism</a> (for recommendations 1.4.1 and 1.4.7 to 1.4.11).</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.6 Thrombolytic therapy" id="ng158-thrombolytic-therapy">
    <h3 class="title" id="thrombolytic-therapy">1.6 Thrombolytic therapy</h3>
    <div class="section" title="DVT" id="ng158-dvt">
      <h4 class="title" id="dvt">DVT</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider catheter-directed thrombolytic therapy for people with symptomatic iliofemoral DVT who have:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms lasting less than 14 days <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>good functional status <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a life expectancy of 1 year or more <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a low risk of bleeding.<strong> [2012]<br /><br /></strong>See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg523" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg523">NICE's interventional procedures guidance on ultrasound-enhanced, catheter-directed thrombolysis for deep vein thrombosis</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="PE" id="ng158-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="pe">PE</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider pharmacological systemic thrombolytic therapy for people with PE and haemodynamic instability(see also the <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-pe-with-haemodynamic-instability" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-pe-with-haemodynamic-instability">section on anticoagulation treatment for PE with haemodynamic instability</a>). <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer pharmacological systemic thrombolytic therapy to people with PE and haemodynamic stability with or without right ventricular dysfunction (see also the <a class="link" href="recommendations#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-treatment-for-confirmed-dvt-or-pe">section on anticoagulation treatment for DVT or PE</a>). If the person develops haemodynamic instability, refer to recommendation 1.6.2. <strong>[2015]<br /><br /></strong>See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg524" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg524">NICE's interventional procedures guidance on ultrasound-enhanced, catheter-directed thrombolysis for pulmonary embolism</a>.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.7 Mechanical interventions" id="ng158-mechanical-interventions">
    <h3 class="title" id="mechanical-interventions">1.7 Mechanical interventions</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Inferior vena caval filters" id="ng158-inferior-vena-caval-filters">
      <h4 class="title" id="inferior-vena-caval-filters">Inferior vena caval filters</h4>
      <article id="ng158-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer an inferior vena caval (IVC) filter to people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#proximal-dvt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#proximal-dvt">proximal DVT</a> or PE unless:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>it is part of a prospective clinical study <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>anticoagulation is contraindicated or a PE has occurred during anticoagulation treatment (see recommendations 1.7.2 and 1.7.3). <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider an IVC filter for people with proximal DVT or PE when anticoagulation treatment is contraindicated. Remove the IVC filter when anticoagulation treatment is no longer contraindicated and has been established. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider an IVC filter for people with proximal DVT or PE who have a PE while taking anticoagulation treatment only after taking the steps outlined in the <a class="link" href="recommendations#treatment-failure" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#treatment-failure">recommendation on treatment failure</a>. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng158-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Before fitting an IVC filter, ensure that there is a strategy in place for it to be removed at the earliest possible opportunity. Document the strategy and review it if the clinical situation changes. <strong>[2020]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2020 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#inferior-vena-caval-filters-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#inferior-vena-caval-filters-2">rationale and impact section on IVC filters</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng158/evidence/h-inferior-vena-caval-filters-pdf-8710588341" target="_top">evidence review H: inferior vena caval filters for people with venous thromboembolism</a>.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.9 Thrombophilia testing" id="ng158-thrombophilia-testing">
    <h3 class="title" id="thrombophilia-testing">1.9 Thrombophilia testing</h3>
    <article id="ng158-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer testing for hereditary thrombophilia to people who are continuing anticoagulation treatment. <strong>[2012, amended 2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer thrombophilia testing to people who have had <a class="link" href="recommendations#provoked-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#provoked-dvt-or-pe">provoked DVT or PE</a>. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider testing for antiphospholipid antibodies in people who have had <a class="link" href="recommendations#unprovoked-dvt-or-pe" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#unprovoked-dvt-or-pe">unprovoked DVT or PE</a> if it is planned to stop anticoagulation treatment, but be aware that these tests can be affected by anticoagulants and specialist advice may be needed. <strong>[2012, amended 2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_9_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider testing for hereditary thrombophilia in people who have had unprovoked DVT or PE and who have a first‑degree relative who has had DVT or PE if it is planned to stop anticoagulation treatment, but be aware that these tests can be affected by anticoagulants and specialist advice may be needed. <strong>[2012, amended 2020]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng158-1_9_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer thrombophilia testing to first‑degree relatives of people with a history of DVT or PE and thrombophilia. <strong>[2012]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng158-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Active cancer" id="ng158-active-cancer">
      <h4 class="title" id="active-cancer">Active cancer</h4>
      <p>Receiving active antimitotic treatment; or diagnosed within the past 6 months; or recurrent or metastatic; or inoperable. Excludes squamous skin cancer and basal cell carcinoma.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Estimated creatinine clearance" id="ng158-estimated-creatinine-clearance">
      <h4 class="title" id="estimated-creatinine-clearance">Estimated creatinine clearance</h4>
      <p>Creatinine clearance estimated using the Cockcroft and Gault formula; see the <a class="link" href="https://bnf.nice.org.uk/guidance/prescribing-in-renal-impairment.html" target="_top">BNF's information on prescribing in renal impairment</a>.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Provoked DVT or PE" id="ng158-provoked-dvt-or-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="provoked-dvt-or-pe">Provoked DVT or PE</h4>
      <p>DVT or PE in a person with a recent (within 3 months) and transient major clinical risk factor for VTE, such as surgery, trauma, significant immobility (bedbound, unable to walk unaided or likely to spend a substantial proportion of the day in bed or in a chair), pregnancy or puerperium – or in a person who is having hormonal therapy (combined oral contraceptive pill or hormone replacement therapy).</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Proximal DVT" id="ng158-proximal-dvt">
      <h4 class="title" id="proximal-dvt">Proximal DVT</h4>
      <p>DVT at or above the level of the popliteal trifurcation area. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Unprovoked DVT or PE" id="ng158-unprovoked-dvt-or-pe">
      <h4 class="title" id="unprovoked-dvt-or-pe">Unprovoked DVT or PE</h4>
      <p>DVT or PE in a person with no recent major clinical risk factor for VTE (see provoked DVT or PE) who is not having hormonal therapy (combined oral contraceptive pill or hormone replacement therapy).</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Wells score" id="ng158-wells-score">
      <h4 class="title" id="wells-score">Wells score</h4>
      <p>Clinical prediction rule for estimating the probability of DVT or PE. There are a number of versions of Wells scores available. This guideline recommends the 2‑level DVT Wells score and the 2-level PE Wells score.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
