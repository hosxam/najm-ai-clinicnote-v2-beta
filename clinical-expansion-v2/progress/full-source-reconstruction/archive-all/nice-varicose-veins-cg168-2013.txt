<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg168-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <p>The following guidance is based on the best available evidence. The <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG168/evidence" target="_top">full guideline</a> gives details of the methods and the evidence used to develop the guidance.</p>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding. </p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Information for people with varicose veins" id="cg168-information-for-people-with-varicose-veins">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-for-people-with-varicose-veins">1.1 Information for people with varicose veins</h3>
    <article id="cg168-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Give people who present with varicose veins information that includes:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>An explanation of what varicose veins are.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Possible causes of varicose veins.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>The likelihood of progression and possible complications, including deep vein thrombosis, skin changes, leg ulcers, bleeding and thrombophlebitis. Address any misconceptions the person may have about the risks of developing complications.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Treatment options, including symptom relief, an overview of interventional treatments and the role of compression.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Advice on: </p>
            <p>
              <div class="itemizedlist">
                <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>weight loss (for guidance on weight management, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246">NICE guideline on overweight and obesity management</a>)</p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>light to moderate physical activity </p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>avoiding factors that are known to make their symptoms worse if possible </p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>when and where to seek further medical help.</p>
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </div>
            </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg168-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When discussing treatment for varicose veins at the vascular service tell the person:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>What treatment options are available. </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>The expected benefits and risks of each treatment option.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>That new varicose veins may develop after treatment. </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>That they may need more than 1 session of treatment.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>That the chance of recurrence after treatment for recurrent varicose veins is higher than for primary varicose veins.<br /><br />A vascular service is a team of healthcare professionals who have the skills to undertake a full clinical and duplex ultrasound assessment and provide a full range of treatment.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Referral to a vascular service" id="cg168-referral-to-a-vascular-service-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="referral-to-a-vascular-service-2">1.2 Referral to a vascular service</h3>
    <article id="cg168-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer people with bleeding varicose veins to a vascular service immediately.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg168-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer people to a vascular service if they have any of the following. </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Symptomatic primary or symptomatic recurrent varicose veins. Symptomatic veins are veins found in association with troublesome lower limb symptoms (typically pain, aching, discomfort, swelling, heaviness and itching).</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Lower‑limb skin changes, such as pigmentation or eczema, thought to be caused by chronic venous insufficiency.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Superficial vein thrombosis (characterised by the appearance of hard, painful veins) and suspected venous incompetence.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>A venous leg ulcer (a break in the skin below the knee that has not healed within 2 weeks).</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>A healed venous leg ulcer. </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Assessment and treatment in a vascular service" id="cg168-assessment-and-treatment-in-a-vascular-service-2">
    <h3 class="title" id="assessment-and-treatment-in-a-vascular-service-2">1.3 Assessment and treatment in a vascular service</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Assessment" id="cg168-assessment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment-2">Assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg168-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use duplex ultrasound to confirm the diagnosis of varicose veins and the extent of truncal reflux, and to plan treatment for people with suspected primary or recurrent varicose veins.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Interventional treatment" id="cg168-interventional-treatment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="interventional-treatment-2">Interventional treatment</h4>
      <article id="cg168-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with confirmed varicose veins and truncal reflux: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Offer endothermal ablation (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg8" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/IPG8">NICE's interventional procedures guidance on radiofrequency ablation of varicose veins</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg52" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/IPG52">endovenous laser treatment of the long saphenous vein</a>).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>If endothermal ablation is unsuitable, offer ultrasound‑guided foam sclerotherapy (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg440" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/IPG440">NICE's interventional procedures guidance on ultrasound-guided foam sclerotherapy for varicose veins</a>).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>If ultrasound‑guided foam sclerotherapy is unsuitable, offer surgery.<br /><br />If incompetent varicose tributaries are to be treated, consider treating them at the same time.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg168-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If offering compression bandaging or hosiery for use after interventional treatment, do not use for more than 7 days.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Non-interventional treatment" id="cg168-non-interventional-treatment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="non-interventional-treatment-2">Non-interventional treatment</h4>
      <article id="cg168-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer compression hosiery to treat varicose veins unless interventional treatment is unsuitable.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Management during pregnancy" id="cg168-management-during-pregnancy">
    <h3 class="title" id="management-during-pregnancy">1.4 Management during pregnancy</h3>
    <article id="cg168-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Give pregnant women presenting with varicose veins information on the effect of pregnancy on varicose veins.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg168-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not carry out interventional treatment for varicose veins during pregnancy other than in exceptional circumstances.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg168-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider compression hosiery for symptom relief of leg swelling associated with varicose veins during pregnancy.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
