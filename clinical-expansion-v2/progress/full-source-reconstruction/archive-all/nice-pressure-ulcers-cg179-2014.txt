<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg179-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off‑label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="cg179-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Adults, neonates, infants, children and young people" id="cg179-adults-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">
      <h4 class="title" id="adults-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">Adults, neonates, infants, children and young people</h4>
      <p>This guideline covers people of all ages at risk of, or who have, a pressure ulcer. These terms are defined as follows:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>adults: 18 years or older</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>neonates: under 4 weeks</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>infants: between 4 weeks and 1 year </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>children: 1 year to under 13 years</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>young people: 13 to 17 years.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Risk assessment" id="cg179-risk-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="risk-assessment">Risk assessment</h4>
      <p>This guideline uses the terms 'at risk' and 'at high risk' to identify people who may develop a pressure ulcer. For the purposes of this guideline:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Adults considered to be <strong>at risk</strong> of developing a pressure ulcer are those who, after assessment using clinical judgement and/or a validated risk assessment tool, are considered to be at risk of developing a pressure ulcer.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Adults considered to be <strong>at high risk</strong> of developing a pressure ulcer will usually have multiple risk factors (for example, significantly limited mobility, nutritional deficiency, inability to reposition themselves, significant cognitive impairment) identified during risk assessment with or without a validated risk assessment tool. Adults with a history of pressure ulcers or a current pressure ulcer are also considered to be at high risk.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Neonates, infants, children and young people considered to be <strong>at risk</strong> are those who, after assessment using clinical judgement and/or a validated risk assessment tool, are considered to be at risk of developing a pressure ulcer. </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Neonates, infants, children and young people considered to be <strong>at high risk</strong> of developing a pressure ulcer will usually have multiple risk factors (for example, significantly limited mobility, nutritional deficiency, inability to reposition themselves, significant cognitive impairment) identified during risk assessment with or without a validated risk assessment tool. Those with a history of pressure ulcers or a current pressure ulcer are also considered to be at high risk.<br /><br />Please note that the examples given are not exhaustive.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Prevention: adults" id="cg179-prevention-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="prevention-adults">1.1 Prevention: adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Risk assessment" id="cg179-risk-assessment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="risk-assessment-2">Risk assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that all patients are potentially at risk of developing a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Carry out and document an assessment of pressure ulcer risk for adults:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>being admitted to secondary care or care homes in which NHS care is provided or</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>receiving NHS care in other settings (such as primary and community care and emergency departments) if they have a risk factor, for example:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significantly limited mobility (for example, people with a spinal cord injury)</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significant loss of sensation</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>a previous or current pressure ulcer</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>nutritional deficiency</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>the inability to reposition themselves</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significant cognitive impairment.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using a validated scale to support clinical judgement (for example, the Braden scale, the Waterlow score or the Norton risk-assessment scale) when assessing pressure ulcer risk. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassess pressure ulcer risk if there is a change in clinical status (for example, after surgery, on worsening of an underlying condition or with a change in mobility).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Skin assessment" id="cg179-skin-assessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="skin-assessment">Skin assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer adults who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer a skin assessment by a trained healthcare professional (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg179/chapter/recommendations#healthcare-professional-training-and-education" target="_top" data-original-url="http://publications.nice.org.uk/pressure-ulcers-prevention-and-management-of-pressure-ulcers-cg179/recommendations#healthcare-professional-training-and-education">recommendation 1.3.4</a>). The assessment should take into account any pain or discomfort reported by the patient and the skin should be checked for:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>skin integrity in areas of pressure</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>colour changes or discoloration<br /><br />Healthcare professionals should be aware that non-blanchable erythema may present as colour changes or discolouration, particularly in darker skin tones or types.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>variations in heat, firmness and moisture (for example, because of incontinence, oedema, dry or inflamed skin).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use finger palpation or diascopy to determine whether erythema or discolouration (identified by skin assessment) is blanchable.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Start appropriate preventative action (see recommendations 1.1.1–1.1.17) in adults who have non-blanching erythema and consider repeating the skin assessment at least every 2 hours until resolved.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Repositioning" id="cg179-repositioning">
      <h4 class="title" id="repositioning">Repositioning</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage adults who have been assessed as being at risk of developing a pressure ulcer to change their position frequently and at least every 6 hours. If they are unable to reposition themselves, offer help to do so, using appropriate equipment if needed. Document the frequency of repositioning required.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage adults who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer to change their position frequently and at least every 4 hours. If they are unable to reposition themselves, offer help to do so, using appropriate equipment if needed. Document the frequency of repositioning required.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Skin massage" id="cg179-skin-massage">
      <h4 class="title" id="skin-massage">Skin massage</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer skin massage or rubbing to adults to prevent a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutritional supplements and hydration" id="cg179-nutritional-supplements-and-hydration">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutritional-supplements-and-hydration">Nutritional supplements and hydration</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer nutritional supplements specifically to prevent a pressure ulcer in adults whose nutritional intake is adequate.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer subcutaneous or intravenous fluids specifically to prevent a pressure ulcer in adults whose hydration status is adequate.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Pressure redistributing devices" id="cg179-pressure-redistributing-devices">
      <h4 class="title" id="pressure-redistributing-devices">Pressure redistributing devices</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a high-specification foam mattress for adults who are:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>admitted to secondary care</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer in primary and community care settings. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider a high-specification foam theatre mattress or an equivalent pressure redistributing surface for all adults who are undergoing surgery.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with adults at high risk of developing a heel pressure ulcer and, where appropriate, their family or carers, a strategy to offload heel pressure, as part of their individualised care plan.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider the seating needs of people at risk of developing a pressure ulcer who are sitting for prolonged periods.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider a high-specification foam or equivalent pressure redistributing cushion for adults who use a wheelchair or who sit for prolonged periods.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Barrier creams" id="cg179-barrier-creams">
      <h4 class="title" id="barrier-creams">Barrier creams</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_1_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using a barrier preparation to prevent skin damage in adults who are at high risk of developing a moisture lesion or incontinence-associated dermatitis, as identified by skin assessment (such as those with incontinence, oedema, dry or inflamed skin).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Prevention: neonates, infants, children and young people" id="cg179-prevention-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">
    <h3 class="title" id="prevention-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">1.2 Prevention: neonates, infants, children and young people</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Risk assessment" id="cg179-risk-assessment-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="risk-assessment-3">Risk assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Carry out and document an assessment of pressure ulcer risk for neonates, infants, children and young people:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>being admitted to secondary or tertiary care or</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>receiving NHS care in other settings (such as primary and community care and emergency departments) if they have a risk factor, for example:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significantly limited mobility</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significant loss of sensation</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>a previous or current pressure ulcer</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>nutritional deficiency</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>the inability to reposition themselves</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>significant cognitive impairment.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a scale validated for this population (for example, the Braden Q scale for children), to support clinical judgement.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Skin assessment" id="cg179-skin-assessment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="skin-assessment-2">Skin assessment</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer neonates, infants, children and young people who are assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer a skin assessment by a trained healthcare professional. Take into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>skin changes in the occipital area</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>skin temperature</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the presence of blanching erythema or discoloured areas of skin.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware of specific sites (for example, the occipital area) where neonates, infants, children and young people are at risk of developing a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Repositioning" id="cg179-repositioning-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="repositioning-2">Repositioning</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that neonates and infants who are at risk of developing a pressure ulcer are repositioned at least every 4 hours.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage children and young people who are at risk of developing a pressure ulcer to change their position at least every 4 hours. If they are unable to reposition themselves, offer help to do so, using appropriate equipment if needed. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider more frequent repositioning than every 4 hours for neonates and infants who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer. Document the frequency of repositioning required.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage children and young people who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer to change their position more frequently than every 4 hours. If they are unable to reposition themselves, offer help to do so, using equipment if needed. Document the frequency of repositioning required.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that repositioning equipment is available to aid the repositioning of children and young people, if needed. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that healthcare professionals are trained in the use of repositioning equipment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that patients, parents and carers understand the reasons for repositioning. If children and young people decline repositioning, document and discuss their reasons for declining.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider involving a play expert to encourage children who have difficulty with, or who have declined repositioning.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Relieve pressure on the scalp and head when repositioning neonates, infants, children and young people at risk of developing a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Skin massage" id="cg179-skin-massage-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="skin-massage-2">Skin massage</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer skin massage or rubbing to neonates, infants, children and young people to prevent a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutritional supplements and hydration" id="cg179-nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-2">Nutritional supplements and hydration</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer nutritional supplements specifically to prevent a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people with adequate nutritional status for their developmental stage and clinical condition.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer subcutaneous or intravenous fluids specifically to prevent a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people with adequate hydration status for their development stage and clinical condition.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Pressure redistributing devices" id="cg179-pressure-redistributing-devices-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="pressure-redistributing-devices-2">Pressure redistributing devices</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a high-specification foam cot mattress or overlay for all neonates and infants who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer as part of their individualised care plan.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a high-specification foam mattress or overlay for all children and young people who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer as part of their individualised care plan.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with children and young people at high risk of developing a heel pressure ulcer and their parents and carers, where appropriate, a strategy to offload heel pressure as part of their individualised care plan.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer infants, children and young people who are long-term wheelchair users, regular wheelchair assessments and provide pressure relief or redistribution.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer neonates, infants, children and young people at risk of developing an occipital pressure ulcer an appropriate pressure redistributing surface (for example, a suitable pillow or pressure redistributing pad).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Barrier creams" id="cg179-barrier-creams-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="barrier-creams-2">Barrier creams</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_2_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use barrier preparations to help prevent skin damage, such as moisture lesions, for neonates, infants, children and young people who are incontinent.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Prevention: all ages" id="cg179-prevention-all-ages">
    <h3 class="title" id="prevention-all-ages">1.3 Prevention: all ages</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Care planning" id="cg179-care-planning">
      <h4 class="title" id="care-planning">Care planning</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Develop and document an individualised care plan for neonates, infants, children, young people and adults who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer, taking into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the outcome of risk and skin assessment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the need for additional pressure relief at specific at-risk sites</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their mobility and ability to reposition themselves</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other comorbidities</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>patient preference.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Patient and carer information" id="cg179-patient-and-carer-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="patient-and-carer-information">Patient and carer information</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer timely, tailored information to people who have been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer, and their family or carers. The information should be delivered by a trained or experienced healthcare professional and include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the causes of a pressure ulcer </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the early signs of a pressure ulcer</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ways to prevent a pressure ulcer</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the implications of having a pressure ulcer (for example, for general health, treatment options and the risk of developing pressure ulcers in the future).<br /><br />Demonstrate techniques and equipment used to prevent a pressure ulcer.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take into account individual needs when supplying information to people with:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>degenerative conditions</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>impaired mobility</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>neurological impairment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>cognitive impairment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>impaired tissue perfusion (for example, caused by peripheral arterial disease).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Healthcare professional training and education" id="cg179-healthcare-professional-training-and-education">
      <h4 class="title" id="healthcare-professional-training-and-education">Healthcare professional training and education</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide training to healthcare professionals on preventing a pressure ulcer, including:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who is most likely to be at risk of developing a pressure ulcer</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to identify pressure damage</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what steps to take to prevent new or further pressure damage</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who to contact for further information and for further action.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide further training to healthcare professionals who have contact with anyone who has been assessed as being at high risk of developing a pressure ulcer. Training should include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to carry out a risk and skin assessment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to reposition</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>information on pressure redistributing devices</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discussion of pressure ulcer prevention with patients and their carers</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>details of sources of advice and support.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Management: adults" id="cg179-management-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="management-adults">1.4 Management: adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Ulcer measurement" id="cg179-ulcer-measurement">
      <h4 class="title" id="ulcer-measurement">Ulcer measurement</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Document the surface area of all pressure ulcers in adults. If possible, use a validated measurement technique (for example, transparency tracing or a photograph).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Document an estimate of the depth of all pressure ulcers and the presence of undermining, but do not routinely measure the volume of a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Categorisation" id="cg179-categorisation">
      <h4 class="title" id="categorisation">Categorisation</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Categorise each pressure ulcer in adults using a validated classification tool (such as the International NPUAP‑EPUAP Pressure Ulcer Classification System). Use this to guide ongoing preventative strategies and management. Repeat and document each time the ulcer is assessed.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutritional supplements and hydration" id="cg179-nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-3">Nutritional supplements and hydration</h4>
      <p>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32">the NICE guideline on nutrition support</a> for details of nutrition support other than supplements, and advice on energy and protein intake levels.</p>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer adults with a pressure ulcer a nutritional assessment by a dietitian or other healthcare professional with the necessary skills and competencies.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer nutritional supplements to adults with a pressure ulcer who have a nutritional deficiency.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide information and advice to adults with a pressure ulcer and, where appropriate, their family or carers, on how to follow a balanced diet to maintain an adequate nutritional status, taking into account energy, protein and micronutrient requirements.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer nutritional supplements to treat a pressure ulcer in adults whose nutritional intake is adequate. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer subcutaneous or intravenous fluids to treat a pressure ulcer in adults whose hydration status is adequate.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Pressure redistributing devices" id="cg179-pressure-redistributing-devices-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="pressure-redistributing-devices-3">Pressure redistributing devices</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use high-specification foam mattresses for adults with a pressure ulcer. If this is not sufficient to redistribute pressure, consider the use of a dynamic support surface.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use standard-specification foam mattresses for adults with a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider the seating needs of adults who have a pressure ulcer who are sitting for prolonged periods.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider a high-specification foam or equivalent pressure redistributing cushion for adults who use a wheelchair or sit for prolonged periods and who have a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Negative pressure wound therapy" id="cg179-negative-pressure-wound-therapy">
      <h4 class="title" id="negative-pressure-wound-therapy">Negative pressure wound therapy</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer adults negative pressure wound therapy to treat a pressure ulcer, unless it is necessary to reduce the number of dressing changes (for example, in a wound with a large amount of exudate).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hyperbaric oxygen therapy and electrotherapy" id="cg179-hyperbaric-oxygen-therapy-and-electrotherapy">
      <h4 class="title" id="hyperbaric-oxygen-therapy-and-electrotherapy">Hyperbaric oxygen therapy and electrotherapy</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer the following to adults to treat a pressure ulcer:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>electrotherapy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hyperbaric oxygen therapy.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Debridement" id="cg179-debridement">
      <h4 class="title" id="debridement">Debridement</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the need to debride a pressure ulcer in adults, taking into consideration:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the amount of necrotic tissue</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the category, size and extent of the pressure ulcer</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>patient tolerance</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any comorbidities.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer debridement to adults if identified as needed in the assessment:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>use autolytic debridement, using an appropriate dressing to support it</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider using sharp debridement if autolytic debridement is likely to take longer and prolong healing time.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer adults with a pressure ulcer:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>larval (maggot) therapy </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>enzymatic debridement.<br /><br />Consider larval therapy if debridement is needed but sharp debridement is contraindicated or if there is associated vascular insufficiency.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Systemic antibiotics and antiseptics" id="cg179-systemic-antibiotics-and-antiseptics">
      <h4 class="title" id="systemic-antibiotics-and-antiseptics">Systemic antibiotics and antiseptics</h4>
      <p>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15/chapter/1-Recommendations#recommendations-for-prescribers" target="_top">the NICE guideline on antimicrobial stewardship</a>.</p>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>After a skin assessment, offer systemic antibiotics to adults with a pressure ulcer if there are any of the following: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>clinical evidence of systemic sepsis (see also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng253" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng253">NICE's guidelines on suspected sepsis in people aged 16 or over</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng255" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng255">suspected sepsis in pregnant or recently pregnant people</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>spreading cellulitis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>underlying osteomyelitis</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with a local hospital microbiology department which antibiotic to offer adults with infection to ensure that the chosen systemic antibiotic is effective against local strains of infection.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer systemic antibiotics specifically to heal a pressure ulcer in adults.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer systemic antibiotics to adults based only on positive wound cultures without clinical evidence of infection.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Topical antimicrobials and antiseptics" id="cg179-topical-antimicrobials-and-antiseptics">
      <h4 class="title" id="topical-antimicrobials-and-antiseptics">Topical antimicrobials and antiseptics</h4>
      <p>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15/chapter/1-Recommendations#recommendations-for-prescribers" target="_top">the NICE guideline on antimicrobial stewardship</a>.</p>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely use topical antiseptics or antimicrobials to treat a pressure ulcer in adults.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dressings" id="cg179-dressings">
      <h4 class="title" id="dressings">Dressings</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.23</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with adults with a pressure ulcer and, if appropriate, their family or carers, what type of dressing should be used, taking into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pain and tolerance</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>position of the ulcer</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>amount of exudate</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>frequency of dressing change.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.24</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using a dressing for adults that promotes a warm, moist wound healing environment to treat category 2, 3 and 4 pressure ulcers.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_25" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.25</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer gauze dressings to treat a pressure ulcer in adults.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Heel pressure ulcers" id="cg179-heel-pressure-ulcers">
      <h4 class="title" id="heel-pressure-ulcers">Heel pressure ulcers</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_4_26" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.26</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with adults with a heel pressure ulcer and, if appropriate, their family or carers, a strategy to offload heel pressure as part of their individualised care plan. (See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/chapter/1-Recommendations#diabetic-foot-ulcer" target="_top">the NICE guideline on diabetic foot problems</a> for advice on heel pressure offloading.)</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Management: neonates, infants, children and young people" id="cg179-management-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">
    <h3 class="title" id="management-neonates-infants-children-and-young-people">1.5 Management: neonates, infants, children and young people</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Ulcer measurement" id="cg179-ulcer-measurement-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="ulcer-measurement-2">Ulcer measurement</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Document the surface area of all pressure ulcers in neonates, infants, children and young people, preferably using a validated measurement technique (for example, transparency tracing or a photograph).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Document an estimate of the depth of a pressure ulcer and the presence of undermining, but do not routinely measure the volume of a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Categorisation" id="cg179-categorisation-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="categorisation-2">Categorisation</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Categorise each pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people at onset using a validated classification tool (such as the International NPUAP‑EPUAP) Pressure Ulcer Classification System) to guide ongoing preventative and management options. Repeat and document each time the ulcer is assessed.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nutritional supplements and hydration" id="cg179-nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-4">
      <h4 class="title" id="nutritional-supplements-and-hydration-4">Nutritional supplements and hydration</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer an age-related nutritional assessment to neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer. This should be performed by a paediatric dietitian or other healthcare professional with the necessary skills and competencies.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with a paediatric dietitian (or other healthcare professional with the necessary skills and competencies) whether to offer nutritional supplements specifically to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people whose nutritional intake is adequate.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer advice on a diet that provides adequate nutrition for growth and healing in neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with a paediatric dietitian whether to offer nutritional supplements to correct nutritional deficiency in neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess fluid balance in neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure there is adequate hydration for age, growth and healing in neonates, infants, children and young people. If there is any doubt, seek further medical advice.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Pressure redistributing devices" id="cg179-pressure-redistributing-devices-4">
      <h4 class="title" id="pressure-redistributing-devices-4">Pressure redistributing devices</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using specialist support surfaces (including dynamic support surfaces where appropriate) for neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer, taking into account their current pressure ulcer risk and mobility.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a high-specification cot or bed mattress or overlay for all neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If pressure on the affected area cannot be adequately relieved by other means (such as repositioning), consider a dynamic support surface, appropriate to the size and weight of the child or young person with a pressure ulcer, if this can be tolerated.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Tailor the support surface to the location and cause of the pressure ulcer for neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Negative pressure wound therapy" id="cg179-negative-pressure-wound-therapy-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="negative-pressure-wound-therapy-2">Negative pressure wound therapy</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely use negative pressure wound therapy to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hyperbaric oxygen therapy and electrotherapy" id="cg179-hyperbaric-oxygen-therapy-and-electrotherapy-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="hyperbaric-oxygen-therapy-and-electrotherapy-2">Hyperbaric oxygen therapy and electrotherapy</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use the following to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>electrotherapy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hyperbaric oxygen therapy.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Debridement" id="cg179-debridement-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="debridement-2">Debridement</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider autolytic debridement with appropriate dressings for dead tissue in neonates, infants, children and young people. Consider sharp and surgical debridement by trained staff if autolytic debridement is unsuccessful.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Systemic antibiotics and antiseptics" id="cg179-systemic-antibiotics-and-antiseptics-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="systemic-antibiotics-and-antiseptics-2">Systemic antibiotics and antiseptics</h4>
      <p>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15/chapter/1-Recommendations#recommendations-for-prescribers" target="_top">the NICE guideline on antimicrobial stewardship</a>.</p>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider systemic antibiotics for neonates, infants, children and young people with a pressure ulcer with clinical evidence of local or systemic infection.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with a local hospital microbiology department which antibiotic to offer neonates, infants, children and young people with infection to ensure that the chosen systemic antibiotic is effective against local strains of bacteria.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Topical antimicrobials and antiseptics" id="cg179-topical-antimicrobials-and-antiseptics-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="topical-antimicrobials-and-antiseptics-2">Topical antimicrobials and antiseptics</h4>
      <p>See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15/chapter/1-Recommendations#recommendations-for-prescribers" target="_top">the NICE guideline on antimicrobial stewardship</a>.</p>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely use topical antiseptics or antimicrobials to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dressings" id="cg179-dressings-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="dressings-2">Dressings</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using a dressing that promotes a warm, moist healing environment to treat category 2, 3 and 4 pressure ulcers in neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using topical antimicrobial dressings to treat a pressure ulcer where clinically indicated in neonates, infants, children and young people, for example, where there is spreading cellulitis.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use iodine dressings to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.23</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer gauze dressings to treat a pressure ulcer in neonates, infants, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Heel pressure ulcers" id="cg179-heel-pressure-ulcers-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="heel-pressure-ulcers-2">Heel pressure ulcers</h4>
      <article id="cg179-1_5_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.24</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with the parents or carers of neonates and infants and with children and young people (and their parents or carers if appropriate), a strategy to offload heel pressure as part of their individualised care plan to manage their heel pressure ulcer, taking into account differences in size, mobility, pain and tolerance. (See also <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/chapter/1-Recommendations#diabetic-foot-ulcer" target="_top">the NICE guideline on diabetic foot problems</a> for advice on heel pressure offloading.)</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
