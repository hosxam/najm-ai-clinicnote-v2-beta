<li class="level-1">Ringworm spreads through contact with an infected person, animal, or contaminated surface.</li>

<li class="level-1">Antimicrobial-resistant ringworm and onychomycosis are  emerging concerns.</li>

	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_overview-overview" name="Overview" title="Overview"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-ltu68hwhqv">Overview</h2>
<p>Ringworm, also called tinea, is a common skin infection caused by a group of fungi called dermatophytes. The infection often looks like a circular, scaly rash. Ringworm can appear on different parts of the body including the scalp, feet (athlete's foot), and groin (jock itch). Dermatophytes can also infect nails, leading to onychomycosis (fungal nail infection).</p>
<p> Ringworm spreads easily through direct contact with an infected person, animal, or contaminated surface. Since it can be confused with other types of rashes and skin conditions, diagnostic testing is recommended to ensure appropriate treatment.</p>
<div class="dfe-block cdc-callout cdc-callout--message"><div class="cdc-callout__icon" aria-hidden="true" role="img"></div>
<h3 class="cdc-callout__heading">Emerging severe ringworm</h3>
<div class="cdc-callout__body"><div class="cdc-callout__content"><p>Emerging strains of dermatophytes causing severe ringworm are a growing public health concern. Diagnosis often requires specialized testing. Infections are difficult to treat, and some strains are antimicrobial-resistant.</p><p><a href="/ringworm/hcp/clinical-overview/emerging-ringworm-facts-for-clinicians.html" class="">Learn more about emerging strains</a>, find clinical resources, and access the Emerging Disease Registry to report suspected cases.</p></div>
</div></div>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_types">
	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_types-types" name="Types" title="Types"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-i1jhgw3hqq">Types</h2>
<p>The most common species of fungi that cause ringworm are <em>Trichophyton</em>, <em>Microsporum</em>, and&#8239;<em>Epidermophyton</em>. Ringworm is characterized by the part of the body affected. </p>
<h3 id="heading-kjqh4i7tbu">Skin infections</h3>
<p>Ringworm is often described by the site of the body affected. Examples include:</p>
<ul class="nested-list">
<li class="level-1">Ringworm of the scalp (tinea capitis)</li>
<li class="level-1">Ringworm of the groin (tinea cruris or jock itch)</li>
<li class="level-1">Ringworm on the feet (tinea pedis or athlete's foot)</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="heading-b_rl6-kcfb">Nail infections</h3>
<p>Onychomycosis is the term for fungal nail infections. It can be caused by dermatophytes  or by infection with other types of fungi such as yeasts. Onychomycosis can affect the fingernails or toenails, but toenail infection  is more common.</p>
<figure class="dfe-image dfe-image__small"><div class="image-container fluid aspect-ratio-16-9"><img src="/ringworm/media/images/2025/12/clinical.jpg" alt="Close up of a foot showing a fungal nail infection"></div>
<div class="dfe-image-caption-container"><figcaption class="dfe-image-caption" id="caption-372-69f38cd802ded">Onychomycosis (fungal nail infection) -<em>Photo courtesy of Shari R. Lipner, MD, PhD</em></figcaption></div></figure>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_how_spread">
	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_how_spread-how-it-spreads" name="How%20it%20spreads" title="How it spreads"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-hyrmz-8hyr">How it spreads</h2>
<p>Symptoms usually start 4-14 days after exposure. Ringworm sometimes spreads from one part of the body to another.</p>
<p><strong>Ringworm of the skin</strong> is spread through: </p>
<ul class="nested-list">
<li class="level-1">Direct skin contact with people and animals who are infected.</li>
<li class="level-1">Sharing personal items (e.g., towels, clothing, bedding).</li>
<li class="level-1">Contact with moist surfaces, like shower stall or locker room floors.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Ringworm of the nail</strong><strong>s </strong>usually spreads through a small cut in the skin around the nails. Infections on the foot or between toes are common with nail infections.</p>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_disease_rates">
	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_disease_rates-disease-rates" name="Disease%20rates" title="Disease rates"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-pv-f81u5yu">Disease rates</h2>
<p>It is estimated that up to 25% of people in the world may experience ringworm at some point in their lifetime. </p>
<p>Onychomycosis is estimated to affect around 14% of the general population and is especially common in older adults. People with diabetes are at increased risk for fungal nail infections.</p>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_clin_fea">

<p>Ringworm can present differently on different parts of the body.</p>

<p>The classic ringworm lesion is an erythematous, raised, scaly ring with central clearing. Lesions typically appear red on light skin and gray or brown on darker skin. Multiple lesions might be present. The severity of ringworm can range from mild, scaly lesions to erythematous, exudative lesions if a bacterial superinfection has developed.</p>
<p><strong>Note: </strong>Emerging severe ringworm tends to cause more pronounced infections covering larger areas of the body.</p>
<div class="dfe-block cdc-callout cdc-callout--urgent"><div class="cdc-callout__icon" aria-hidden="true" role="img"></div>
<h3 class="cdc-callout__heading">Avoid corticosteroid creams</h3>
<div class="cdc-callout__body"><div class="cdc-callout__content">Corticosteroid creams can make ringworm worse. It is important to identify and test the infection to determine the appropriate treatment.</div>
</div></div>
<h3 id="heading-dkb4ll7fx0">Nail infections</h3>
<p>Most cases of onychomycosis are not serious. However, some people may experience pain or be bothered by the appearance of their nails. Onychomycosis may cause nails to become discolored, thick, fragile, or cracked. The nail may also separate  from the nail bed. </p>
<p>People who have fungal toenail infections often have a fungal skin infection on the foot, especially between the toes.</p>
<p>Occasionally, a bacterial infection can occur on top of a fungal nail infection and cause serious illness. This is more common in people with diabetes or other conditions that weaken the immune system.</p>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_test_dia">
	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_test_dia-testing-and-diagnosis" name="Testing%20and%20diagnosis" title="Testing and diagnosis"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-aivuy4vxzf">Testing and diagnosis</h2>
<div class="dfe-block cdc-callout cdc-callout--message"><div class="cdc-callout__icon" aria-hidden="true" role="img"></div>
<h3 class="cdc-callout__heading">Clinical consult</h3>
<div class="cdc-callout__body"><div class="cdc-callout__content">Healthcare providers can call 404-639-5168 or email <a href="mailto:fungalconsult@cdc.gov">fungalconsult@cdc.gov</a> for consultation on diagnosis or treatment for suspected or confirmed fungal diseases.</div>
</div></div>
<p>Healthcare providers are recommended to test for suspected ringworm and other fungal skin or nail infections to determine best treatment. On examination, ringworm may be difficult to distinguish from other skin conditions, like psoriasis and eczema. There are also many potential causes for fingernail and toenail abnormalities.</p>
<p>Misdiagnoses and incorrect or delayed treatment allow symptoms to progress. In some cases, incorrect treatment can worsen the infection. For example, corticosteroid creams can be important for treatment of many skin infections. However, if prescribed based on an inaccurate presumption of another cause of symptoms and applied for ringworm, corticosteroids can exacerbate ringworm. </p>
<figure class="dfe-image dfe-image__small"><div class="image-container calculated-infographic aspect-ratio-16-9"><img src="/ringworm/media/images/2025/11/22843_lores.jpg" alt="shoulder of a dark-skinned man with a large lesion"></div>
<div class="dfe-image-caption-container"><figcaption class="dfe-image-caption" id="caption-361-69f38cd80c623">Ringworm lesions can be different sizes.  Lesions typically appear red on light skin and gray or brown on darker skin</figcaption></div></figure>

</div>
<div class="dfe-section" data-section="cdc_clinical_overview_treat_pat">
	<a class="onThisPageAnchor" id="cdc_clinical_overview_treat_pat-antimicrobial-stewardship" name="Antimicrobial%20stewardship" title="Antimicrobial stewardship"><!-- --></a><h2 class="editor-block--title" id="heading-83yqle36d6">Antimicrobial stewardship</h2>
<p>Antimicrobial-resistant ringworm is emerging and is a current public health concern.</p>
<p>Clinicians can help reduce spread, impact, and new emergence through antimicrobial stewardship. Diagnostic testing, appropriate prescribing, and patient education help preserve the limited antifungal medication options. </p>
<p>Clinicians should also become familiar with<a href="/ringworm/hcp/clinical-overview/emerging-ringworm-facts-for-clinicians.html" class=""> emerging ringworm</a>, including some that are antimicrobial-resistant.</p>
