<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng145-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as discussed in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Information for people with thyroid disease, their families and carers" id="ng145-information-for-people-with-thyroid-disease-their-families-and-carers">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-for-people-with-thyroid-disease-their-families-and-carers">1.1 Information for people with thyroid disease, their families and carers</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Presenting information" id="ng145-presenting-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="presenting-information">Presenting information</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that information is presented to facilitate shared decision making, as recommended in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">NICE guideline on shared decision making</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="General information" id="ng145-general-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="general-information">General information</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people with thyroid disease who need treatment, and their family or carers if appropriate, that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Thyroid disease usually responds well to treatment. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>The goal of treatment is to alleviate symptoms and align thyroid function tests within or close to the reference range.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>People may feel well even when their thyroid function tests are outside the reference range.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Even when there are no symptoms, treatment may be advised to reduce the risk of long-term complications.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Even when thyroid function tests are within the reference range, changes to treatment may improve symptoms for some people.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Symptoms may lag behind treatment changes for several weeks to months.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Day-to-day changes in unexplained symptoms are unlikely to be due to underlying thyroid disease because the body has a large reservoir of thyroxine.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide people with thyroid disease, and their family or carers if appropriate, with written and verbal information on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their underlying condition, including the role and function of the thyroid gland and what the thyroid function tests mean</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>risks of over- and under-treatment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their medicines</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>need for and frequency of monitoring </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>when to seek advice from a healthcare professional</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how thyroid disease and medicines may affect pregnancy and fertility.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <p>See the recommendations on testing for coeliac disease in people with a diagnosis of autoimmune thyroid disease in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20">NICE guideline on coeliac disease</a>.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)" id="ng145-hypothyroidism-underactive-thyroid">
      <h4 class="title" id="hypothyroidism-underactive-thyroid">Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid)</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide people with hypothyroidism, and their family or carers if appropriate, with written and verbal information on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>possible drug interactions of thyroid hormone replacements, including interactions with over-the-counter medicines</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how and when to take levothyroxine.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Thyrotoxicosis (overactive thyroid)" id="ng145-thyrotoxicosis-overactive-thyroid">
      <h4 class="title" id="thyrotoxicosis-overactive-thyroid">Thyrotoxicosis (overactive thyroid)</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide people with thyrotoxicosis, and their family or carers if appropriate, with written and verbal information on: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the different causes of thyrotoxicosis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the consequences of untreated thyrotoxicosis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the suitability of individual treatment options (for example, antithyroid drugs may be more suitable for mild uncomplicated Graves' disease, surgery may be best for an enlarged thyroid causing compression, radioactive iodine is not usually suitable before puberty)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the possible benefits/advantages of the treatment options (for example, antithyroid drugs and radioactive iodine are non-invasive treatments, surgery offers rapid relief of symptoms and there is no need to delay pregnancy or fathering a child)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the possible risks/disadvantages of the treatment options (for example, antithyroid drugs may have side effects, radioactive iodine means limited contact with other people for a few weeks and a need to delay pregnancy or fathering a child, surgery is an invasive treatment that leaves scarring on the neck)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the risk of and impact of different treatment options on new and existing thyroid eye disease (for example, radioactive iodine may precipitate or worsen thyroid eye disease)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the need for thyroid hormone replacement if treatment leads to life-long hypothyroidism.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Thyroid enlargement (also known as goitre)" id="ng145-thyroid-enlargement-also-known-as-goitre">
      <h4 class="title" id="thyroid-enlargement-also-known-as-goitre">Thyroid enlargement (also known as goitre)</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide people with thyroid enlargement, and their family or carers if appropriate, with written and verbal information on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the causes of thyroid enlargement, including the fact that goitre and nodules are common and are usually not cancerous</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>red flag symptoms to look out for (for example, shortness of breath, rapid growth of nodules, hoarse voice, swallowing difficulties)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>treatment options.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on information and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#information-for-people-with-thyroid-disease-their-families-and-carers-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#information-for-people-with-thyroid-disease-their-families-and-carers-2">rationale and impact section on information for people with thyroid disease, their families and carers</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Investigating suspected thyroid dysfunction" id="ng145-investigating-suspected-thyroid-dysfunction">
    <h3 class="title" id="investigating-suspected-thyroid-dysfunction">1.2 Investigating suspected thyroid dysfunction</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Indications for tests for thyroid dysfunction" id="ng145-indications-for-tests-for-thyroid-dysfunction">
      <h4 class="title" id="indications-for-tests-for-thyroid-dysfunction">Indications for tests for thyroid dysfunction</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider tests for thyroid dysfunction for <a class="link" href="recommendations#adults-7" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#adults-7">adults</a>, <a class="link" href="recommendations#children-and-young-people-4" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#children-and-young-people-4">children and young people</a> if there is a clinical suspicion of thyroid disease, but bear in mind that 1 symptom alone may not be indicative of thyroid disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer tests for thyroid dysfunction to adults, children and young people with: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>type 1 diabetes or other autoimmune diseases, <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>new-onset atrial fibrillation.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider tests for thyroid dysfunction for adults, children and young people with depression or unexplained anxiety.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider tests for thyroid dysfunction for children and young people with abnormal growth, or unexplained change in behaviour or school performance.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that in <a class="link" href="recommendations#menopausal-women" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#menopausal-women">menopausal women</a> symptoms of thyroid dysfunction may be mistaken for menopause.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not test for thyroid dysfunction during an acute illness unless you suspect the acute illness is due to thyroid dysfunction, because the acute illness may affect the test results.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer testing for thyroid dysfunction solely because an adult, child or young person has type 2 diabetes.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on indications for tests for thyroid dysfunction and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#indications-for-tests-for-thyroid-dysfunction-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#indications-for-tests-for-thyroid-dysfunction-2">rationale and impact section on indications for tests for thyroid dysfunction</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Tests when thyroid dysfunction is suspected" id="ng145-tests-when-thyroid-dysfunction-is-suspected">
      <h4 class="title" id="tests-when-thyroid-dysfunction-is-suspected">Tests when thyroid dysfunction is suspected</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH) alone for adults when secondary thyroid dysfunction (pituitary disease) is not suspected. Then:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the TSH is above the reference range, measure free thyroxine (FT4) in the same sample</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the TSH is below the reference range, measure FT4 and free tri-iodothyronine (FT3) in the same sample.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring both TSH and FT4 for:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adults when secondary thyroid dysfunction (pituitary disease) is suspected</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>children and young people. <br /><br />If the TSH is below the reference range, measure FT3 in the same sample.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider repeating the tests for thyroid dysfunction in recommendations 1.2.8 or 1.2.9 if symptoms worsen or new symptoms develop (but no sooner than 6 weeks from the most recent test).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_2_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask adults, children and young people with suspected thyroid dysfunction about their biotin intake because a high consumption of biotin from dietary supplements may lead to falsely high or low test results. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on tests when thyroid dysfunction is suspected and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#tests-when-thyroid-dysfunction-is-suspected-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#tests-when-thyroid-dysfunction-is-suspected-2">rationale and impact section on tests when thyroid dysfunction is suspected</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Managing primary hypothyroidism" id="ng145-managing-primary-hypothyroidism">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-primary-hypothyroidism">1.3 Managing primary hypothyroidism</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tests for people with confirmed primary hypothyroidism" id="ng145-tests-for-people-with-confirmed-primary-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="tests-for-people-with-confirmed-primary-hypothyroidism">Tests for people with confirmed primary hypothyroidism</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider measuring thyroid peroxidase antibodies (TPOAbs) for adults with TSH levels above the reference range, but do not repeat TPOAbs testing.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children and young people" id="ng145-children-and-young-people">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-and-young-people">Children and young people</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Measure TPOAbs for children and young people with TSH levels above the reference range, with possible repeat TPOAbs testing at the time of transition to adult services. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on tests for people with confirmed primary hypothyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#tests-for-people-with-confirmed-primary-hypothyroidism-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#tests-for-people-with-confirmed-primary-hypothyroidism-2">rationale and impact section on tests for people with confirmed primary hypothyroidism</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing primary hypothyroidism" id="ng145-managing-primary-hypothyroidism-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-primary-hypothyroidism-2">Managing primary hypothyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer levothyroxine as first-line treatment for adults, children and young people with primary hypothyroidism.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer liothyronine for primary hypothyroidism, either alone or in combination with levothyroxine, because there is not enough evidence that it offers benefits over levothyroxine monotherapy, and its long-term adverse effects are uncertain. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer natural thyroid extract for primary hypothyroidism because there is not enough evidence that it offers benefits over levothyroxine, and its long-term adverse effects are uncertain.<br /><br />Natural thyroid extract does not have a UK marketing authorisation so its safety is uncertain.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider starting levothyroxine at a dosage of 1.6 micrograms per kilogram of body weight per day (rounded to the nearest 25 micrograms) for adults under 65 with primary hypothyroidism and no history of cardiovascular disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider starting levothyroxine at a dosage of 25 to 50 micrograms per day with titration for adults aged 65 and over and adults with a history of cardiovascular disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on managing primary hypothyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#managing-primary-hypothyroidism-3" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#managing-primary-hypothyroidism-3">rationale and impact section on managing primary hypothyroidism</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Follow-up and monitoring of primary hypothyroidism" id="ng145-follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism">
    <h3 class="title" id="follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism">1.4 Follow-up and monitoring of primary hypothyroidism</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tests for follow-up and monitoring of primary hypothyroidism" id="ng145-tests-for-follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="tests-for-follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism">Tests for follow-up and monitoring of primary hypothyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Aim to maintain TSH levels within the reference range when treating primary hypothyroidism with levothyroxine. If symptoms persist, consider adjusting the dose of levothyroxine further to achieve optimal wellbeing, but avoid using doses that cause TSH suppression or thyrotoxicosis.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that the TSH level can take up to 6 months to return to the reference range for people who had a very high TSH level before starting treatment with levothyroxine or a prolonged period of untreated hypothyroidism. Take this into account when adjusting the dose of levothyroxine.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-2">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For adults who are taking levothyroxine for primary hypothyroidism, consider measuring TSH every 3 months until the level has stabilised (2 similar measurements within the reference range 3 months apart), and then once a year.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider measuring FT4 as well as TSH for adults who continue to have symptoms of hypothyroidism after starting levothyroxine.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children and young people aged 2 years and over" id="ng145-children-and-young-people-aged-2-years-and-over">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-and-young-people-aged-2-years-and-over">Children and young people aged 2 years and over</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For children aged 2 years and over and young people taking levothyroxine for primary hypothyroidism, consider measuring FT4 and TSH:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 6 to 12 weeks until the TSH level has stabilised (2 similar measurements within the reference range 3 months apart), then</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 4 to 6 months until after puberty, then</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>once a year.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children under 2 years" id="ng145-children-under-2-years">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-under-2-years">Children under 2 years</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For children aged between 28 days and 2 years who are taking levothyroxine for primary hypothyroidism, consider measuring FT4 and TSH:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 4 to 8 weeks until the TSH level has stabilised (2 similar measurements within the reference range 2 months apart), then</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 2 to 3 months during the first year of life, and</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 3 to 4 months during the second year of life.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on tests for monitoring and follow-up of primary hypothyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#follow-up-and-monitoring-of-primary-hypothyroidism-2">rationale and impact section on follow-up and monitoring of primary hypothyroidism</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Managing and monitoring subclinical hypothyroidism" id="ng145-managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hypothyroidism">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hypothyroidism">1.5 Managing and monitoring subclinical hypothyroidism</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tests for people with confirmed subclinical hypothyroidism" id="ng145-tests-for-people-with-confirmed-subclinical-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="tests-for-people-with-confirmed-subclinical-hypothyroidism">Tests for people with confirmed subclinical hypothyroidism</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-3">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-3">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider measuring TPOAbs for adults with TSH levels above the reference range, but do not repeat TPOAbs testing.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treating subclinical hypothyroidism" id="ng145-treating-subclinical-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="treating-subclinical-hypothyroidism">Treating subclinical hypothyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When discussing whether or not to start treatment for subclinical hypothyroidism, take into account features that might suggest underlying thyroid disease, such as symptoms of hypothyroidism, previous radioactive iodine treatment or thyroid surgery, or raised levels of thyroid autoantibodies.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-4">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-4">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider levothyroxine for adults with subclinical hypothyroidism who have a TSH of 10 mlU/litre or higher on 2 separate occasions 3 months apart. Follow the recommendations in section 1.4 on follow-up and monitoring of hypothyroidism.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider a 6-month trial of levothyroxine for adults under 65 with subclinical hypothyroidism who have:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a TSH above the reference range but lower than 10 mlU/litre on 2 separate occasions 3 months apart, <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>symptoms of hypothyroidism.<br /><br />If symptoms do not improve after starting levothyroxine, re-measure TSH and if the level remains raised, adjust the dose. If symptoms persist when serum TSH is within the reference range, consider stopping levothyroxine and follow the recommendations on monitoring untreated subclinical hypothyroidism and monitoring after stopping treatment.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children and young people aged 2 years and over" id="ng145-children-and-young-people-aged-2-years-and-over-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-and-young-people-aged-2-years-and-over-2">Children and young people aged 2 years and over</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider levothyroxine for children aged 2 years and over and young people with subclinical hypothyroidism who have: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a TSH level of 20 mlU/litre or higher, <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a TSH level between 10 and 20 mlU/litre on 2 separate occasions 3 months apart, <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>a TSH level between 5 and 10 mlU/litre on 2 separate occasions 3 months apart, and</p>
                <p>
                  <div class="itemizedlist">
                    <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>thyroid dysgenesis (an underdeveloped thyroid gland), or</p>
                      </li>
                      <li class="listitem">
                        <p>signs or symptoms of thyroid dysfunction.<br /><br />During levothyroxine treatment, follow the recommendations in section 1.4 on follow-up and monitoring.</p>
                      </li>
                    </ul>
                  </div>
                </p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children under 2 years" id="ng145-children-under-2-years-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-under-2-years-2">Children under 2 years</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider levothyroxine for children aged between 28 days and 2 years with subclinical hypothyroidism who have a TSH level of 10 mlU/litre or higher. During levothyroxine treatment, follow the recommendations in section 1.4 on follow-up and monitoring.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Monitoring untreated subclinical hypothyroidism and monitoring after stopping treatment" id="ng145-monitoring-untreated-subclinical-hypothyroidism-and-monitoring-after-stopping-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="monitoring-untreated-subclinical-hypothyroidism-and-monitoring-after-stopping-treatment">Monitoring untreated subclinical hypothyroidism and monitoring after stopping treatment</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-5">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-5">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For adults with untreated subclinical hypothyroidism or adults who have stopped levothyroxine treatment for subclinical hypothyroidism, consider measuring TSH and FT4:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>once a year if they have features suggesting underlying thyroid disease, such as previous thyroid surgery or raised levels of thyroid autoantibodies, <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>once every 2 to 3 years if they have no features suggesting underlying thyroid disease.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children and young people" id="ng145-children-and-young-people-2">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-and-young-people-2">Children and young people</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider measuring TSH and FT4 for children aged 2 years and over and young people with untreated subclinical hypothyroidism and a TSH lower than 10 mlU/litre:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 3 to 6 months if they have features suggesting underlying thyroid disease, such as thyroid dysgenesis (an underdeveloped thyroid gland) or raised levels of thyroid autoantibodies, <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>every 6 to 12 months if they have no features suggesting underlying thyroid disease.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider measuring TSH and FT4 every 1 to 3 months for children aged between 28 days and 2 years with untreated subclinical hypothyroidism. </p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider stopping TSH and FT4 measurement in children and young people if the TSH level has stabilised (2 similar measurements within the reference range 3 to 6 months apart) and there are no features suggesting underlying thyroid disease.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on managing and monitoring subclinical hypothyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hypothyroidism-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hypothyroidism-2">rationale and impact section on managing and monitoring subclinical hypothyroidism</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.6 Managing thyrotoxicosis" id="ng145-managing-thyrotoxicosis">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-thyrotoxicosis">1.6 Managing thyrotoxicosis</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tests for people with confirmed thyrotoxicosis" id="ng145-tests-for-people-with-confirmed-thyrotoxicosis">
      <h4 class="title" id="tests-for-people-with-confirmed-thyrotoxicosis">Tests for people with confirmed thyrotoxicosis</h4>
      <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-6">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-6">Adults</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Differentiate between thyrotoxicosis with hyperthyroidism (for example, Graves' disease or toxic nodular disease) and thyrotoxicosis without hyperthyroidism (for example, transient thyroiditis) in adults by:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>measuring TSH receptor antibodies (TRAbs) to confirm Graves' disease</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>considering technetium scanning of the thyroid gland if TRAbs are negative.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.2</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Only consider ultrasound for adults with thyrotoxicosis if they have a palpable thyroid nodule.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Children and young people" id="ng145-children-and-young-people-3">
        <h5 class="title" id="children-and-young-people-3">Children and young people</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.3</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Differentiate between thyrotoxicosis with hyperthyroidism (Graves' disease) and thyrotoxicosis without hyperthyroidism (for example, transient thyroiditis) in children and young people by:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>measuring TPOAbs and TRAbs </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>considering technetium scanning of the thyroid gland if TRAbs are negative.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Only offer ultrasound to children and young people with thyrotoxicosis if they have a palpable thyroid nodule or the cause of thyrotoxicosis remains unclear following thyroid autoantibody testing and technetium scanning.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on tests for people with confirmed thyrotoxicosis and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#tests-for-people-with-confirmed-thyrotoxicosis-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#tests-for-people-with-confirmed-thyrotoxicosis-2">rationale and impact section on tests for people with confirmed thyrotoxicosis</a>.</p>

    <div class="section" title="Initial treatment in primary/non-specialist care" id="ng145-initial-treatment-in-primarynon-specialist-care">
      <h4 class="title" id="initial-treatment-in-primarynon-specialist-care">Initial treatment in primary/non-specialist care</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that transient thyrotoxicosis without hyperthyroidism usually only needs supportive treatment (for example, beta-blockers). </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider antithyroid drugs along with supportive treatment for adults with hyperthyroidism who are waiting for specialist assessment and further treatment.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on initial management in primary/non-specialist care for people with thyrotoxicosis and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#initial-management-in-primarynon-specialist-care-for-people-with-thyrotoxicosis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#initial-management-in-primarynon-specialist-care-for-people-with-thyrotoxicosis">rationale and impact section on initial management in primary/non-specialist care for people with thyrotoxicosis</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Initial treatment in secondary/specialist care" id="ng145-initial-treatment-in-secondaryspecialist-care">
      <h4 class="title" id="initial-treatment-in-secondaryspecialist-care">Initial treatment in secondary/specialist care</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with adults, children and young people with thyrotoxicosis with hyperthyroidism (and their families and carers as appropriate):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the possible benefits and risks of all treatment options (antithyroid drugs, radioactive iodine, surgery)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the likelihood of a good response to each option. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that people can actively participate in decisions about their treatment by following the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">NICE guidelines on patient experience in adult NHS services</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">shared decision making</a>. This includes presenting information about possible outcomes in a way the person (and their families and carers as appropriate) can understand.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer antithyroid drugs to control hyperthyroidism in adults, children and young people who are waiting for treatment with radioactive iodine or surgery.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>). November 2019 – carbimazole use is off label for children under 2 years. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="section" title="Adults with Graves' disease" id="ng145-adults-with-graves-disease">
        <h5 class="title" id="adults-with-graves-disease">Adults with Graves' disease</h5>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer radioactive iodine as first-line definitive treatment for adults with Graves' disease, unless antithyroid drugs are likely to achieve remission (see recommendation 1.6.11), or it is unsuitable (for example, there are concerns about compression, malignancy is suspected, they are pregnant or trying to become pregnant or father a child within the next 4 to 6 months, or they have active thyroid eye disease).<br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2017/1322/contents/made" target="_top">2017 regulations on medical exposure to ionising radiation</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer a choice of antithyroid drugs (a 12- to 18-month course) or radioactive iodine (following the <a class="link" href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2017/1322/contents/made" target="_top">2017 regulations on medical exposure to ionising radiation</a>) as first-line definitive treatment for adults with Graves' disease if antithyroid drugs are likely to achieve remission (for example, mild and uncomplicated Graves' disease).<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>).</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.12</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer antithyroid drugs (a 12- to 18-month course) as first-line definitive treatment for adults with Graves' disease if radioactive iodine and surgery are unsuitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.13</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer total thyroidectomy as first-line definitive treatment for adults with Graves' disease if:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>there are concerns about compression, or </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>thyroid malignancy is suspected, or</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>radioactive iodine and antithyroid drugs are unsuitable.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng145-1_6_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.14</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider radioactive iodine or surgery for adults with Graves' disease who have had antithyroid drugs but have persistent or relapsed hyperthyroidism.<br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2017/1322/contents/made" target="_top">2017 regulations on medical exposure to ionising radiation</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on treatment for adults with Graves' disease and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#treatment-for-adults-with-graves-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#treatment-for-adults-with-graves-disease">rationale and impact section on treatment for adults with Graves' disease</a>.</p>

      <div class="section" title="Adults with toxic nodular goitre" id="ng145-adults-with-toxic-nodular-goitre">

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer radioactive iodine as first-line definitive treatment for adults with hyperthyroidism secondary to multiple nodules unless it is unsuitable (for example, there are concerns about compression, thyroid malignancy is suspected, they are pregnant or trying to become pregnant or father a child within the next 4 to 6 months, or they have active thyroid eye disease).<br /><br />Follow the <a class="link" href="http://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2017/1322/contents/made" target="_top">2017 regulations on medical exposure to ionising radiation</a>.</p>

        <article id="ng145-1_6_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.16</h6>

            <p>Offer total thyroidectomy or life-long antithyroid drugs as first-line definitive treatment for adults with hyperthyroidism secondary to multiple nodules if radioactive iodine is unsuitable.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>).</p>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.17</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer radioactive iodine (if suitable) or surgery (hemithyroidectomy) as first-line definitive treatment for adults with hyperthyroidism secondary to a single nodule, or life-long antithyroid drugs if these options are unsuitable.</p>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.6.18</h6>

See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>

                <p>offer antithyroid drugs using a titration regimen of carbimazole, <strong>and</strong></p>

    <div class="section" title="Antithyroid drugs for adults, children and young people with hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-antithyroid-drugs-for-adults-children-and-young-people-with-hyperthyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="antithyroid-drugs-for-adults-children-and-young-people-with-hyperthyroidism">Antithyroid drugs for adults, children and young people with hyperthyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Before starting antithyroid drugs for adults, children and young people with hyperthyroidism, check full blood count and liver function tests.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering antithyroid drugs as first-line definitive treatment to adults with Graves' disease, offer carbimazole for 12 to 18 months, using either a block and replace or a titration regimen, and then review the need for further treatment.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.23</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering antithyroid drugs to children and young people with Graves' disease, offer carbimazole, using a titration regimen, and review the need for treatment every 2 years.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>). November 2019 – carbimazole use is off label for children under 2 years. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.24</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When offering life-long antithyroid drugs to adults with hyperthyroidism secondary to a single or multiple toxic nodules, consider treatment with a titration regimen of carbimazole. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_6_25" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.25</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">

          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who experience adverse reactions to carbimazole</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who are pregnant or trying to become pregnant within the following 6 months</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.7 Follow-up and monitoring of hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-follow-up-and-monitoring-of-hyperthyroidism">
    <h3 class="title" id="follow-up-and-monitoring-of-hyperthyroidism">1.7 Follow-up and monitoring of hyperthyroidism</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Monitoring after radioactive iodine treatment" id="ng145-monitoring-after-radioactive-iodine-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="monitoring-after-radioactive-iodine-treatment">Monitoring after radioactive iodine treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring TSH, FT4 and FT3 levels in adults, children and young people every 6 weeks for the first 6 months after radioactive iodine treatment until TSH is within the reference range.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults, children and young people who have hypothyroidism after radioactive iodine treatment and are not on antithyroid drugs, offer levothyroxine replacement therapy and follow recommendations 1.3.6 and 1.3.7 on dosage of levothyroxine for adults and 1.4.1 to 1.4.6 on monitoring of hypothyroidism. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults, children and young people with TSH in the reference range 6 months after radioactive iodine treatment, consider measuring TSH (with <a class="link" href="recommendations#cascading" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#cascading">cascading</a>) at 9 months and 12 months after treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults, children and young people with TSH in the reference range 12 months after radioactive iodine treatment, consider measuring TSH (with cascading) every 6 months unless they develop hypothyroidism (then follow recommendation 1.7.2).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If hyperthyroidism persists after radioactive iodine treatment in adults, children and young people, consider antithyroid drugs until the 6-month appointment.<br /><br />Use of carbimazole is subject to MHRA advice on contraception (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-increased-risk-of-congenital-malformations-strengthened-advice-on-contraception" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>) and risk of acute pancreatitis (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/carbimazole-risk-of-acute-pancreatitis" target="_top">Drug Safety Update, February 2019</a>). November 2019 – carbimazole use is off label for children under 2 years. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If hyperthyroidism persists 6 months after radioactive iodine treatment in adults, children and young people, consider further treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>

    <div class="section" title="Monitoring after surgery" id="ng145-monitoring-after-surgery">
      <h4 class="title" id="monitoring-after-surgery">Monitoring after surgery</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer levothyroxine to adults, children and young people after a total thyroidectomy and follow recommendations 1.3.6 and 1.3.7 on dosage of levothyroxine for adults and 1.4.1 to 1.4.6 on monitoring of hypothyroidism. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring TSH and FT4 at 2 and 6 months after surgery, and then TSH (with cascading) once a year for adults, children and young people who have had a hemithyroidectomy.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Monitoring of antithyroid drugs" id="ng145-monitoring-of-antithyroid-drugs">
      <h4 class="title" id="monitoring-of-antithyroid-drugs">Monitoring of antithyroid drugs</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults, children and young people who are taking antithyroid drugs for hyperthyroidism, consider measuring:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH, FT4 and FT3 every 6 weeks until their TSH is within the reference range, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) every 3 months until antithyroid drugs are stopped.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not monitor full blood count and liver function for adults, children and young people taking antithyroid drugs for hyperthyroidism unless there is a clinical suspicion of agranulocytosis or liver dysfunction.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults who have stopped antithyroid drugs, consider measuring:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) within 8 weeks of stopping the drug, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) every 3 months for a year, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) once a year. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_7_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For children and young people who have stopped antithyroid drugs, consider measuring: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH, FT4 and FT3 within 8 weeks of stopping the drug, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH, FT4 and FT3 every 3 months for the first year, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) every 6 months for the second year, then</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>TSH (with cascading) once a year. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on follow-up and monitoring of hyperthyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#follow-up-and-monitoring-of-hyperthyroidism-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#follow-up-and-monitoring-of-hyperthyroidism-2">rationale and impact section on follow-up and monitoring of hyperthyroidism</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Managing and monitoring subclinical hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">1.8 Managing and monitoring subclinical hyperthyroidism</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Treating subclinical hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-treating-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="treating-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">Treating subclinical hyperthyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider seeking specialist advice on managing subclinical hyperthyroidism in adults if they have:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>2 TSH readings lower than 0.1 Miu/litre at least 3 months apart <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>evidence of thyroid disease (for example, a goitre or positive thyroid antibodies) or symptoms of thyrotoxicosis. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider seeking specialist advice on managing subclinical hyperthyroidism in all children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Untreated subclinical hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-untreated-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="untreated-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">Untreated subclinical hyperthyroidism</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_8_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring TSH every 6 months for adults with untreated subclinical hyperthyroidism. If the TSH level is outside the reference range, consider measuring FT4 and FT3 in the same sample.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_8_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring TSH, FT4 and FT3 every 3 months for children and young people with untreated subclinical hyperthyroidism. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_8_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider stopping TSH measurement for adults, children and young people with untreated subclinical hyperthyroidism if the TSH level stabilises (2 similar measurements within the reference range 3 to 6 months apart).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on managing and monitoring subclinical hyperthyroidism and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hyperthyroidism-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#managing-and-monitoring-subclinical-hyperthyroidism-2">rationale and impact section on managing and monitoring subclinical hyperthyroidism</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Diagnosing, managing and monitoring thyroid enlargement with normal thyroid function" id="ng145-diagnosing-managing-and-monitoring-thyroid-enlargement-with-normal-thyroid-function">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnosing-managing-and-monitoring-thyroid-enlargement-with-normal-thyroid-function">1.9 Diagnosing, managing and monitoring thyroid enlargement with normal thyroid function</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Investigating thyroid enlargement" id="ng145-investigating-thyroid-enlargement">
      <h4 class="title" id="investigating-thyroid-enlargement">Investigating thyroid enlargement</h4>
      <p>The following recommendations apply to adults, children and young people with normal thyroid function.</p>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer ultrasound to image palpable thyroid enlargement or focal nodularity in adults, children and young people with normal thyroid function if malignancy is suspected.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider ultrasound of incidental findings on imaging if clinical factors suggest malignancy as a possibility.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When making decisions about whether to offer fine needle aspiration cytology, use an established system for grading ultrasound appearance that takes into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>echogenicity</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>microcalcifications</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>border</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>shape in transverse plane</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>internal vascularity</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>lymphadenopathy.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reports of ultrasound findings should:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>specify which grading system has been used for the assessment.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>include information on the features in recommendation 1.9.3 <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>provide an overall assessment of malignancy, <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>confirm that both lobes have been assessed, <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p> document assessment of cervical lymph nodes.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use ultrasound guidance when performing fine needle aspiration cytology. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>See the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12">NICE guideline on suspected cancer</a> for recommendations on referral for suspected head and neck cancers (including thyroid cancer). </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on investigating thyroid enlargement and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#investigating-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement-with-normal-thyroid-function" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#investigating-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement-with-normal-thyroid-function">rationale and impact section on investigating non-malignant thyroid enlargement with normal thyroid function</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing non-malignant thyroid enlargement" id="ng145-managing-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement">Managing non-malignant thyroid enlargement</h4>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer treatment to adults with non-malignant thyroid enlargement, normal thyroid function and mild or no symptoms unless:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they have breathing difficulty <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there is clinical concern, for example, because of marked airway narrowing.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Repeat thyroid ultrasound and TSH measurement for adults with non-malignant thyroid enlargement who are not receiving treatment, if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>malignancy is subsequently suspected, <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>compression is suspected.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider repeating thyroid ultrasound and TSH measurement for adults with non-malignant thyroid enlargement who are not receiving treatment, if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the person's symptoms worsen <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they develop symptoms, such as hoarseness, or shortness of breath.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For children and young people with non-malignant thyroid enlargement and normal thyroid function, discuss management with a specialist multidisciplinary team.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with normal thyroid function and a cyst or predominantly cystic nodule with no vascular components, offer aspiration if it is causing compressive symptoms, with possible ethanol ablation if there is re-accumulation of cyst fluid later.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng145-1_9_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with normal thyroid function and a non-cystic nodule or multinodular or diffuse goitre, consider the following if they have compressive symptoms relating to thyroid enlargement:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>surgery, particularly if there is marked airway narrowing <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>radioactive iodine ablation, if there is demonstrable radionuclide uptake, <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>percutaneous thermal ablation (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg562" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg562">NICE interventional procedures guidance on ultrasound-guided percutaneous radiofrequency ablation for benign thyroid nodules</a>).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>To find out why the committee made the recommendations on managing thyroid enlargement and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#managing-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#managing-non-malignant-thyroid-enlargement-2">rationale and impact section on managing non-malignant thyroid enlargement</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng145-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Adults" id="ng145-adults-7">
      <h4 class="title" id="adults-7">Adults</h4>
      <p>People aged 16 years and over.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Cascading" id="ng145-cascading">
      <h4 class="title" id="cascading">Cascading</h4>
      <p>Measuring FT4 in the same sample if TSH is above the reference range and measuring FT4 and FT3 in the same sample if TSH is below the reference range.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Children and young people" id="ng145-children-and-young-people-4">
      <h4 class="title" id="children-and-young-people-4">Children and young people</h4>
      <p>People under 16 years.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-hyperthyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="hyperthyroidism">Hyperthyroidism</h4>
      <p>Excess production and/or secretion of thyroid hormones (overactive thyroid gland).</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hypothyroidism" id="ng145-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="hypothyroidism">Hypothyroidism</h4>
      <p>Inadequate production and secretion of thyroid hormones (underactive thyroid gland).</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Menopausal women" id="ng145-menopausal-women">
      <h4 class="title" id="menopausal-women">Menopausal women</h4>
      <p>This includes women in perimenopause and post menopause.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Subclinical hyperthyroidism" id="ng145-subclinical-hyperthyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="subclinical-hyperthyroidism">Subclinical hyperthyroidism</h4>
      <p>TSH levels below the reference range, with FT3 and FT4 within the reference range.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Subclinical hypothyroidism" id="ng145-subclinical-hypothyroidism">
      <h4 class="title" id="subclinical-hypothyroidism">Subclinical hypothyroidism</h4>
      <p>TSH levels above the reference range, with FT4 within the reference range.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Thyrotoxicosis" id="ng145-thyrotoxicosis">
      <h4 class="title" id="thyrotoxicosis">Thyrotoxicosis</h4>
      <p>Thyrotoxicosis is a disorder of excess circulating thyroid hormones caused by increased production and secretion (hyperthyroidism) or by the release of stored thyroid hormones (thyroiditis).</p>
    </div>
  </div>
