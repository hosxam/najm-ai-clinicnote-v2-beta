<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng182-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Managing insect bites and stings" id="ng182-managing-insect-bites-and-stings">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-insect-bites-and-stings">1.1 Managing insect bites and stings</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Assessment and advice" id="ng182-assessment-and-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment-and-advice">Assessment and advice</h4>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a rapid-onset skin reaction from an insect bite or sting is likely to be an inflammatory or allergic reaction, rather than an infection</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>most <a class="link" href="recommendations#insect-bite-or-sting" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#insect-bite-or-sting">insect bites or stings</a> will not need antibiotics.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the type and severity of the insect bite or stings to identify:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a local inflammatory or allergic skin reaction</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#erythema-migrans" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#erythema-migrans">erythema migrans</a> (bullseye rash), a sign of Lyme disease (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95">NICE guideline on Lyme disease</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms or signs of an infection</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a systemic reaction (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice">recommendations on referral and seeking specialist advice</a>).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with an insect bite or sting that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a community pharmacist can advise about self‑care treatments</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>skin redness and itching are common and may last for up to 10 days</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>it is unlikely that the skin will become infected</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>avoiding scratching may reduce inflammation and the risk of infection</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they should seek medical help if symptoms worsen rapidly or significantly at any time, or they become systemically unwell.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with a known or suspected tick bite, follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95">NICE guideline on Lyme disease</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations, see the <a class="link" href="rationales#assessment-and-advice-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationales" data-original-url="#assessment-and-advice-2">rationale section on assessment and advice</a>.</p>
        <p>For more details, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng182/evidence/evidence-review-pdf-8840056141" target="_top">evidence review</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treating a local inflammatory or allergic skin reaction" id="ng182-treating-a-local-inflammatory-or-allergic-skin-reaction">
      <h4 class="title" id="treating-a-local-inflammatory-or-allergic-skin-reaction">Treating a local inflammatory or allergic skin reaction</h4>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer an antibiotic for an insect bite or sting in people who do not have symptoms or signs of an infection.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that people may wish to consider oral antihistamines (in people aged over 1 year) to help relieve itching, even though there is uncertainty about their effectiveness in managing insect bites or stings. Some antihistamines cause sedation, which may help at night.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treating an infected insect bite or sting" id="ng182-treating-an-infected-insect-bite-or-sting">
      <h4 class="title" id="treating-an-infected-insect-bite-or-sting">Treating an infected insect bite or sting</h4>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with an insect bite or sting who have symptoms or signs of an infection, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng141/chapter/Recommendations#choice-of-antibiotic" target="_top">recommendations on choice of antibiotic in the NICE guideline on cellulitis and erysipelas</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations, see the <a class="link" href="rationales#treatment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationales" data-original-url="#treatment">rationale section on treatment</a>.</p>
        <p>For more details, see the <a class="link" href="summary-of-the-evidence#summary-of-the-evidence" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="summary-of-the-evidence" data-original-url="#summary-of-the-evidence">summary of the evidence</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Reassessment" id="ng182-reassessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="reassessment">Reassessment</h4>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassess people with an insect bite or sting if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms or signs of an infection develop (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng141" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng141">NICE guideline on cellulitis and erysipelas</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their condition worsens rapidly or significantly, or they become systemically unwell</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they have severe pain out of proportion to the wound, which may indicate the presence of toxin‑producing bacteria.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When reassessing people with an insect bite or sting, take account of other possible diagnoses such as Lyme disease (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng95">NICE guideline on Lyme disease</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Referral and seeking specialist advice" id="ng182-referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice">Referral and seeking specialist advice</h4>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people with an insect bite or sting to hospital if they have symptoms or signs suggesting a more serious illness or condition, such as a systemic allergic reaction (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg134" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg134">NICE guideline on anaphylaxis</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng182-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referral or seeking specialist advice for people with an insect bite or sting if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they are systemically unwell</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they are severely immunocompromised, and have symptoms or signs of an infection</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they have had a previous systemic allergic reaction to the same type of bite or sting</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>it is in the mouth or throat, or around the eyes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>it has been caused by an unusual or exotic insect</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>they have fever or persisting lesions associated with a bite or sting that occurred while travelling outside the UK.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations, see the <a class="link" href="rationales#referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationales" data-original-url="#referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice-2">rationale section on referral and seeking specialist advice</a>.</p>
        <p>For more details, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng182/evidence/evidence-review-pdf-8840056141" target="_top">evidence review</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in the guideline" id="ng182-terms-used-in-the-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-the-guideline">Terms used in the guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Cellulitis and erysipelas" id="ng182-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">
      <h4 class="title" id="cellulitis-and-erysipelas">Cellulitis and erysipelas</h4>
      <p>Infections of the tissues under the skin (subcutaneous), which usually result from a contaminated break in the skin. Both conditions are characterised by acute localised inflammation and oedema. The lesions are more superficial in erysipelas and have a well-defined, raised margin.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Erythema migrans" id="ng182-erythema-migrans">
      <h4 class="title" id="erythema-migrans">Erythema migrans</h4>
      <p>Erythema migrans is an expanding rash often seen in the early stage of Lyme disease, and can also (but less commonly) be caused by southern tick-associated rash illness. It usually becomes visible from 1 to 4 weeks (but can appear from 3 days to 3 months) after a tick bite and lasts for several weeks.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Insect bite or sting" id="ng182-insect-bite-or-sting">
      <h4 class="title" id="insect-bite-or-sting">Insect bite or sting</h4>
      <p>For the purpose of this guideline, 'insect bites' also includes bites from spiders and ticks. Insects may bite with their mouthparts when feeding or defending themselves. Stings come from bees, wasps and hornets and are used only for defence.</p>
