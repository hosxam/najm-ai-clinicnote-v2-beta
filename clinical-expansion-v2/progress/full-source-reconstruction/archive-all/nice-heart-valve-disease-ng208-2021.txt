<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng208-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Referral for echocardiography and specialist assessment" id="ng208-referral-for-echocardiography-and-specialist-assessment">
    <h3 class="title" id="referral-for-echocardiography-and-specialist-assessment">1.1 Referral for echocardiography and specialist assessment</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Referral for echocardiography" id="ng208-referral-for-echocardiography">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-for-echocardiography">Referral for echocardiography</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider an echocardiogram for adults with a murmur and no other signs or symptoms if valve disease is suspected based on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the nature of the murmur</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>family history</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>age (especially if over 75), <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>medical history (for example, a history of atrial fibrillation).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer an echocardiogram to adults with a murmur if valve disease is suspected (based on the nature of the murmur, family history, age or medical history) and they have:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>signs (such as peripheral oedema) or symptoms (such as angina or breathlessness) or an abnormal ECG, <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an ejection systolic murmur with a reduced second heart sound but no other signs or symptoms.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#referral-for-echocardiography-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#referral-for-echocardiography-2">rationale and impact section on referral for echocardiography</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/a-symptoms-and-signs-indicating-need-for-echocardiography-or-direct-referral-to-a-specialist-pdf-10887602654" target="_top">evidence review A: symptoms and signs indicating need for echocardiography or direct referral to a specialist</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Referral for urgent specialist assessment or urgent echocardiography" id="ng208-referral-for-urgent-specialist-assessment-or-urgent-echocardiography">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-for-urgent-specialist-assessment-or-urgent-echocardiography">Referral for urgent specialist assessment or urgent echocardiography</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If valve disease is suspected (based on the nature of the murmur, family history, age or medical history):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Offer urgent (within 2 weeks) <a class="link" href="recommendations#specialist-assessment-and-advice" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#specialist-assessment-and-advice">specialist assessment</a> that includes echocardiogram or if not available an urgent echocardiogram alone to adults with a systolic murmur and exertional syncope.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Consider urgent (within 2 weeks) specialist assessment that includes echocardiogram for adults with a murmur and severe symptoms (angina or breathlessness on minimal exertion or at rest) thought to be related to valvular heart disease.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on referral and assessment for adults with murmur and non-exertional syncope, follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg109" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg109">NICE guideline on transient loss of consciousness ('blackouts') in over 16s</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on referral and assessment for adults with breathlessness but no murmur, follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng106">NICE guideline on chronic heart failure in adults</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#referral-for-urgent-specialist-assessment-or-urgent-echocardiography-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#referral-for-urgent-specialist-assessment-or-urgent-echocardiography-2">rationale and impact section on referral for urgent specialist assessment or urgent echocardiography</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/a-symptoms-and-signs-indicating-need-for-echocardiography-or-direct-referral-to-a-specialist-pdf-10887602654" target="_top">evidence review A: symptoms and signs indicating need for echocardiography or direct referral to a specialist</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Referral to a specialist after echocardiography" id="ng208-referral-to-a-specialist-after-echocardiography">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-to-a-specialist-after-echocardiography">Referral to a specialist after echocardiography</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that mild valve disease is common and rarely progresses to become clinically significant.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer referral to a specialist to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adults with moderate or <a class="link" href="recommendations#severe-valve-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#severe-valve-disease">severe valve disease</a> of any type</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adults with bicuspid aortic valve disease of any severity (including mild valve disease).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#referral-to-a-specialist-after-echocardiography-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#referral-to-a-specialist-after-echocardiography-2">rationale and impact section on referral to a specialist after echocardiography</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/b-indications-for-referral-to-a-specialist-following-echocardiography-pdf-10887602655" target="_top">evidence review B: indications for referral to a specialist following echocardiography</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Information, referral and specialist assessment for pregnant women and women considering pregnancy" id="ng208-information-referral-and-specialist-assessment-for-pregnant-women-and-women-considering-pregnancy">
      <h4 class="title" id="information-referral-and-specialist-assessment-for-pregnant-women-and-women-considering-pregnancy">Information, referral and specialist assessment for pregnant women and women considering pregnancy</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that most women with valve disease can have a pregnancy without complications.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer advice on the implications of treatment choices on any future pregnancy to women who need heart valve intervention.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer advice on family planning to women with <a class="link" href="recommendations#severe-valve-disease" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#severe-valve-disease">severe valve disease</a>, particularly aortic and mitral stenosis.</p>
        </div>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">

    <div class="section" title="Aortic stenosis" id="ng208-aortic-stenosis">
      <h4 class="title" id="aortic-stenosis">Aortic stenosis</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring adults with asymptomatic severe aortic stenosis for intervention, if suitable, if they have any of the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Vmax (peak aortic jet velocity) more than 5 m/s on echocardiography</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>aortic valve area less than 0.6 cm<sup>2</sup> on echocardiography</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) less than 55%</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>B‑type natriuretic peptide (BNP) or N-terminal proBNP (NT-proBNP) level more than twice the upper limit of normal</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>symptoms unmasked on exercise testing.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring adults with symptomatic low‑gradient aortic stenosis with LVEF less than 50% for intervention if during dobutamine stress echocardiography the aortic stenosis is shown to be severe by:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a mean gradient across the aortic valve that increases to more than 40 mmHg <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an aortic valve area that remains less than 1 cm<sup>2</sup>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider measuring aortic valve calcium score on cardiac CT if the severity of symptomatic aortic stenosis is uncertain.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer enhanced follow up (for example, more frequent reviews) and further assessment (for example, stress echocardiography) to monitor the need for intervention if mid‑wall fibrosis is detected on cardiac MRI in adults with severe aortic stenosis.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#indications-for-interventions-for-adults-with-aortic-stenosis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#indications-for-interventions-for-adults-with-aortic-stenosis">rationale and impact section on indications for interventions for adults with aortic stenosis</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in:</p>

          <ul class="itemizedlist">

    <div class="section" title="Aortic regurgitation" id="ng208-aortic-regurgitation">
      <h4 class="title" id="aortic-regurgitation">Aortic regurgitation</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring adults with asymptomatic severe aortic regurgitation for intervention, if suitable, if they have either of the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>LVEF less than 55% <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>end systolic diameter (ESD) of more than 50 mm or end systolic diameter index (ESDI) more than 24 mm/m<sup>2</sup> on echocardiography.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">

        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/d-indications-for-intervention-pdf-10887602657" target="_top">evidence review D: indications for intervention</a>.</p>

    <div class="section" title="Mitral regurgitation" id="ng208-mitral-regurgitation">
      <h4 class="title" id="mitral-regurgitation">Mitral regurgitation</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring adults with asymptomatic severe primary mitral regurgitation for intervention, if suitable, if they have any of the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>LVEF less than 60%</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ESD more than 45 mm or ESDI more than 22 mm/m<sup>2</sup> on echocardiography <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an increase of systolic pulmonary artery pressure to more than 60 mmHg on exercise testing.<br /><br />When making decisions about referral for surgery, take into account the suitability of the valve for repair and the presence of atrial fibrillation or systolic pulmonary artery pressure of more than 50 mmHg on echocardiography at rest.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">

        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/d-indications-for-intervention-pdf-10887602657" target="_top">evidence review D: indications for intervention</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/e-stress-testing-and-stress-echocardiography-in-determining-need-for-intervention-pdf-10887602658" target="_top">evidence review E: stress testing and stress echocardiography in determining need for intervention</a>.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.5 Interventions" id="ng208-interventions">
    <h3 class="title" id="interventions">1.5 Interventions</h3>
    <p>See the <a class="link" href="recommendations#indications-for-interventions" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#indications-for-interventions">recommendations on indications for interventions</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Decisions about interventions" id="ng208-decisions-about-interventions">
      <h4 class="title" id="decisions-about-interventions">Decisions about interventions</h4>
      <article id="ng208-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss the possible benefits and risks of interventions with adults who have an indication for valve intervention. Include in the discussion:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the benefits to quality of life (both in the short and long term)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>prosthetic valve durability</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the risks associated with the procedures</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the type of access for surgery (median sternotomy, minimally invasive surgery or, for people at high <a class="link" href="recommendations#risk-of-surgery" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#risk-of-surgery">surgical risk</a>, transcatheter)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the possible need for other cardiac procedures in the future.<br /><br />Follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">NICE guidelines on shared decision making</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">patient experience in adult NHS services</a> and base decisions on the type of intervention on patient characteristics and preferences.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng208-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When surgery is agreed, base the decision on the type of surgery (median sternotomy or minimally invasive surgery) on patient characteristics and preferences. If minimally invasive surgery is the agreed option and is not available locally, refer the person to another centre.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#decisions-about-interventions-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#decisions-about-interventions-2">rationale and impact section on decisions about interventions</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/h-transcatheter-intervention-surgery-or-conservative-management-in-heart-valve-disease-pdf-10887602661" target="_top">evidence review H: transcatheter intervention, surgery or conservative management in heart valve disease</a>.</p>
      </div>

        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer surgical mitral valve replacement (by median sternotomy or minimally invasive surgery) to adults with severe primary mitral regurgitation and an indication for surgery, if the valve is not suitable for repair and surgery is suitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider <a class="link" href="recommendations#suitability-for-transcatheter-edge-to-edge-repair" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#suitability-for-transcatheter-edge-to-edge-repair">transcatheter edge-to-edge repair</a>, if suitable, for adults with severe primary mitral regurgitation and symptoms, if surgery is unsuitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <p>See <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/commissioning/wp-content/uploads/sites/12/2019/07/Clinical-Commissioning-Policy_Percutaneous-mitral-valve-leaflet-repair-for-primary-degenerative-mitral-regurgi.pdf" target="_top">NHS England's clinical commissioning policy on percutaneous mitral valve leaflet repair for primary degenerative mitral regurgitation in adults</a> and the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg649" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg649">NICE interventional procedures guidance on percutaneous mitral valve leaflet repair for mitral regurgitation</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg245" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/Guidance/IPG245">thoracoscopically assisted mitral valve surgery</a>.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Secondary mitral regurgitation" id="ng208-secondary-mitral-regurgitation">
        <h5 class="title" id="secondary-mitral-regurgitation">Secondary mitral regurgitation</h5>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgical mitral valve repair (by median sternotomy or minimally invasive surgery) for adults with severe secondary mitral regurgitation who are having cardiac surgery for another indication, if surgery is suitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.12</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgical mitral valve replacement (by median sternotomy or minimally invasive surgery) for adults with severe secondary mitral regurgitation who are having cardiac surgery for another indication, if the valve is not suitable for repair and surgery is suitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.13</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer medical management to adults with heart failure and severe secondary mitral regurgitation, if surgery is unsuitable.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.14</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider transcatheter mitral edge-to-edge repair for adults with heart failure and severe secondary mitral regurgitation, if surgery is unsuitable and they remain symptomatic on medical management.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#interventions-for-mitral-regurgitation" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#interventions-for-mitral-regurgitation">rationale and impact section on interventions for mitral regurgitation</a>.</p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/h-transcatheter-intervention-surgery-or-conservative-management-in-heart-valve-disease-pdf-10887602661" target="_top">evidence review H: transcatheter intervention, surgery or conservative management in heart valve disease</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Tricuspid regurgitation" id="ng208-tricuspid-regurgitation">
        <h5 class="title" id="tricuspid-regurgitation">Tricuspid regurgitation</h5>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgical tricuspid valve repair at the time of mitral valve surgery when tricuspid regurgitation is moderate or severe.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng208-1_5_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.16</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider surgical tricuspid valve repair at the time of aortic valve surgery when tricuspid regurgitation is severe.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#interventions-for-tricuspid-regurgitation-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#interventions-for-tricuspid-regurgitation-2">rationale and impact section on interventions for tricuspid regurgitation</a>.</p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/h-transcatheter-intervention-surgery-or-conservative-management-in-heart-valve-disease-pdf-10887602661" target="_top">evidence review H: transcatheter intervention, surgery or conservative management in heart valve disease</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Repeat intervention" id="ng208-repeat-intervention">
    <h3 class="title" id="repeat-intervention">1.6 Repeat intervention</h3>
    <article id="ng208-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider transcatheter or redo surgical intervention for adults with severe aortic <a class="link" href="recommendations#degenerated" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#degenerated">degeneration</a> of a biological prosthetic valve and symptoms. Take into account the following factors to inform a shared decision about the choice of intervention:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the short- and long-term benefits</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>type of valve dysfunction and prosthesis</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the risks associated with the procedure</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the possible need for other cardiac procedures in the future.<br /><br />See the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg706" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg706">NICE interventional procedures guidance on using transapical transcatheter mitral valve-in-valve implantation for a failed surgically implanted mitral valve bioprosthesis</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg707" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg707">transapical transcatheter mitral valve-in-ring implantation after failed annuloplasty for mitral valve repair</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg653" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ipg653">valve-in-valve TAVI for aortic bioprosthetic dysfunction</a>.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#repeat-intervention-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#repeat-intervention-2">rationale and impact section on repeat intervention</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/i-repeat-intervention-for-failure-of-biological-or-repaired-valves-pdf-391953695508" target="_top">evidence review I: repeat intervention for failure of biological or repaired valves</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Anticoagulation and antiplatelet therapy" id="ng208-anticoagulation-and-antiplatelet-therapy">
    <h3 class="title" id="anticoagulation-and-antiplatelet-therapy">1.7 Anticoagulation and antiplatelet therapy</h3>
    <article id="ng208-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer anticoagulation after surgical biological valve replacement unless there are other indications for anticoagulation.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider aspirin, or clopidogrel if aspirin is not tolerated, after TAVI.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If people have other indications for anticoagulation or antiplatelet therapy, follow the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng196" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng196">NICE guidelines on atrial fibrillation</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">acute coronary syndromes</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#anticoagulation-and-antiplatelet-therapy-3" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#anticoagulation-and-antiplatelet-therapy-3">rationale and impact section on anticoagulation and antiplatelet therapy</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/j-anticoagulant-andor-antiplatelet-therapy-for-biological-prosthetic-valves-and-after-valve-repair-pdf-391953695509" target="_top">evidence review J: anticoagulant and/or antiplatelet therapy for biological prosthetic valves and after valve repair</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Monitoring after an intervention" id="ng208-monitoring-after-an-intervention">
    <h3 class="title" id="monitoring-after-an-intervention">1.8 Monitoring after an intervention</h3>
    <article id="ng208-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Base decisions on the frequency and type of monitoring for adults who have had an intervention (valve repair or replacement) for valve disease on:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>durability of the prosthetic valve or durability of the repair</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the presence of another condition, including other heart disease</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>residual valve abnormality or consequences of the procedure, for example, paravalvular leak</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>concerns about abnormal function of the prosthetic valve</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the patient's wishes.<br /><br />Advise people and their family members or carers (as appropriate) to seek advice if the heart condition deteriorates.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#monitoring-after-an-intervention-3" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#monitoring-after-an-intervention-3">rationale and impact section on monitoring after an intervention</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/k-monitoring-in-people-with-repaired-or-replaced-heart-valves-pdf-391953695510" target="_top">evidence review K: monitoring in people with repaired or replaced heart valves</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Information and advice" id="ng208-information-and-advice">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-advice">1.9 Information and advice</h3>
    <article id="ng208-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Follow the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">NICE guideline on shared decision making</a> and the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">NICE guideline on patient experience in adult NHS services</a> on:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>involvement of family members and carers</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>communication</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>information</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>tailoring healthcare services.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider providing a point of contact for accessing <a class="link" href="recommendations#specialist-assessment-and-advice" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#specialist-assessment-and-advice">specialist advice</a> between appointments.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware of the psychological impact on the person receiving a diagnosis of valve disease, whether or not they have symptoms. Consider the person's needs for additional information and support.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_9_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide information and advice to adults with valve disease about:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the expected progression and prognosis of their condition, including the likely length of an asymptomatic stage</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>any need for intervention, including the type of intervention</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>pregnancy, if appropriate</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the possible effects of other conditions on long-term outcomes</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>rehabilitation and long‑term outcomes</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>palliative care, if appropriate, including how to access this.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng208-1_9_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Provide information and support to young adults about transition from paediatric to adult services, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng43" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng43">NICE guideline on transition from children's to adults' services for young people using health or social care services.</a></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#information-and-advice-3" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#information-and-advice-3">rationale and impact section on information and advice</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng208/evidence/l-information-and-advice-pdf-391953695511" target="_top">evidence review L: information and advice</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng208-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Degenerated" id="ng208-degenerated">
      <h4 class="title" id="degenerated">Degenerated</h4>
      <p>Degenerated covers progressive degeneration and does not include failure of the valve due to endocarditis or thrombosis.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Risk of surgery" id="ng208-risk-of-surgery">
      <h4 class="title" id="risk-of-surgery">Risk of surgery</h4>
      <p>This is calculated using EuroSCORE II. People have low surgical risk if they score less than 4%, intermediate risk if they score between 4% and 8% and high risk if they score more than 8%.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Severe valve disease" id="ng208-severe-valve-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="severe-valve-disease">Severe valve disease</h4>
      <p>Severity of valve disease is defined in line with the <a class="link" href="https://echo.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1530/ERP-20-0035" target="_top">British Society of Echocardiography guidelines on the assessment of aortic stenosis</a>, <a class="link" href="https://echo.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1530/ERP-20-0033" target="_top">the tricuspid and pulmonary valves</a>, and <a class="link" href="https://echo.biomedcentral.com/articles/10.1530/ERP-20-0034" target="_top">mitral valve disease</a>. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Specialist assessment and advice" id="ng208-specialist-assessment-and-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="specialist-assessment-and-advice">Specialist assessment and advice</h4>
      <p>This could include assessment and advice from a cardiologist with expertise in heart valve disease, a multidisciplinary team or a heart valve clinic.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Suitability for transcatheter aortic valve implantation" id="ng208-suitability-for-transcatheter-aortic-valve-implantation">
      <h4 class="title" id="suitability-for-transcatheter-aortic-valve-implantation">Suitability for transcatheter aortic valve implantation</h4>
      <p>Suitability for transcatheter aortic valve implantation (TAVI) depends on:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>an appropriate access for inserting the TAVI catheter</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the morphology of the valve, aortic root and ascending aorta</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the degree and distribution of calcium in the aortic valve.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <p>It is an option for:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>All people expected to have an unacceptably high risk of mortality or morbidity as a result of surgery (for example, because of a risk of infection in people who are immunosuppressed). See also the definition of <a class="link" href="recommendations#risk-of-surgery" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#risk-of-surgery">high risk of surgery</a> according to EuroSCORE II.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>All people expected to have unacceptably strenuous and prolonged recovery from surgery and an extended need for rehabilitation because of frailty, reduced mobility, or musculoskeletal conditions.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>All people with low life expectancy, either because of their age or because they have life-limiting comorbidities.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Suitability for transcatheter edge-to-edge repair" id="ng208-suitability-for-transcatheter-edge-to-edge-repair">
      <h4 class="title" id="suitability-for-transcatheter-edge-to-edge-repair">Suitability for transcatheter edge-to-edge repair</h4>
      <p>Suitability for transcatheter edge-to-edge repair depends on:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the morphology of the person's valve</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the feasibility of using transoesophageal echocardiography to guide the procedure</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the person's fitness for general anaesthesia.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
