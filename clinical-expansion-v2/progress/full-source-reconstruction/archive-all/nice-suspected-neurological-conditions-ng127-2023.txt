<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations for adults aged over 16" id="ng127-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16">Recommendations for adults aged over 16</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Blackouts in adults" id="ng127-blackouts-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="blackouts-in-adults">1.1 Blackouts in adults</h3>
    <article id="ng127-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>
          <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-urgently" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-urgently">Refer urgently</a> adults with new-onset blackouts (transient loss of consciousness), accompanied by features that are strongly suggestive of epileptic seizures, for neurological assessment in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg109/chapter/1-Guidance#suspected-epilepsy" target="_top">recommendation for people with suspected epilepsy in the NICE guideline on transient loss of consciousness ('blackouts') in over 16s</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng127-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely refer adults with blackouts if there are clear features of vasovagal syncope, even if associated with brief jerking of the limbs. See the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg109/chapter/1-Guidance#making-a-judgement-based-on-initial-assessment" target="_top">recommendation on diagnosing uncomplicated faint in the section on no further immediate management required in the NICE guideline on transient loss of consciousness ('blackouts') in over 16s</a>. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#blackouts-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#blackouts-in-adults-2">rationale section on blackouts in adults</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Dizziness and vertigo in adults" id="ng127-dizziness-and-vertigo-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="dizziness-and-vertigo-in-adults">1.2 Dizziness and vertigo in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Sudden-onset dizziness with a focal neurological deficit" id="ng127-sudden-onset-dizziness-with-a-focal-neurological-deficit">
      <h4 class="title" id="sudden-onset-dizziness-with-a-focal-neurological-deficit">Sudden-onset dizziness with a focal neurological deficit</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with sudden-onset <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#dizziness" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#dizziness">dizziness</a> and a focal neurological deficit such as vertical or rotatory nystagmus, new-onset unsteadiness or new‑onset deafness:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the person has diabetes, check for and treat hypoglycaemia</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if the person does not have diabetes, or treating hypoglycaemia does not resolve the symptoms, and benign paroxysmal positional vertigo or postural hypotension do not account for the presentation, <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-immediately" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-immediately">refer immediately</a> to exclude posterior circulation stroke, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG128">NICE guideline on stroke and transient ischaemic attack in over 16s</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Sudden-onset acute vestibular syndrome" id="ng127-sudden-onset-acute-vestibular-syndrome">
      <h4 class="title" id="sudden-onset-acute-vestibular-syndrome">Sudden-onset acute vestibular syndrome</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with sudden-onset acute vestibular syndrome (vertigo, nausea or vomiting and gait unsteadiness), a HINTS (head-impulse–nystagmus–test-of-skew) test should be performed if a healthcare professional with training and experience in the use of this test is available.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with sudden-onset acute vestibular syndrome who have had a HINTS test:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>be aware that a negative HINTS test makes a diagnosis of stroke very unlikely</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>refer immediately for neuroimaging if the HINTS test shows indications of stroke (a normal head impulse test, direction-changing nystagmus or skew deviation).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer immediately adults with sudden-onset acute vestibular syndrome in whom benign paroxysmal positional vertigo or postural hypotension do not account for the presentation, in line with local stroke pathways, if a healthcare professional with training and experience in the use of the HINTS test is not available.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Sudden-onset dizziness with no imbalance or focal neurological deficit" id="ng127-sudden-onset-dizziness-with-no-imbalance-or-focal-neurological-deficit">
      <h4 class="title" id="sudden-onset-dizziness-with-no-imbalance-or-focal-neurological-deficit">Sudden-onset dizziness with no imbalance or focal neurological deficit</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that dizziness in adults with no imbalance or other focal neurological deficit is unlikely to indicate a serious neurological condition.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Vertigo on head movement" id="ng127-vertigo-on-head-movement">
      <h4 class="title" id="vertigo-on-head-movement">Vertigo on head movement</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with transient rotational vertigo on head movement:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Offer the Hallpike manoeuvre to check for benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV) if a healthcare professional trained in its use is available. If there is no healthcare professional trained in the Hallpike manoeuvre available, refer in accordance with local pathways. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>If BPPV is diagnosed, offer a canalith repositioning manoeuvre (such as the Epley manoeuvre) if a healthcare professional trained in its use is available and if the person does not have unstable cervical spine disease. If there is no healthcare professional trained in a canalith repositioning manoeuvre available, or the person has unstable cervical spine disease, refer in accordance with local pathways. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Be aware that BPPV is common after a head injury or labyrinthitis.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Vestibular migraine" id="ng127-vestibular-migraine">
      <h4 class="title" id="vestibular-migraine">Vestibular migraine</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be alert to the possibility of vestibular migraine (migraine-associated vertigo) in adults who have episodes of dizziness that last between 5 minutes and 72 hours and a history of recurrent headache.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

      <article id="ng127-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that, for adults who have been diagnosed with a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#functional-neurological-disorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#functional-neurological-disorder">functional neurological disorder</a> by a specialist, recurrent dizziness might be part of the disorder and the person might not need re‑referral if there are no new neurological signs. New symptoms or signs in adults who have been diagnosed with a functional neurological disorder by a specialist should be assessed as described in the relevant sections of this guideline.</p>
        </div>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>

          <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults who have sudden-onset difficulty with handwriting that has no obvious musculoskeletal cause for a neurological assessment according to local stroke pathways.</p>

    <div class="section" title="Compression neuropathy" id="ng127-compression-neuropathy">
      <h4 class="title" id="compression-neuropathy">Compression neuropathy</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_7_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with clear features of compression neuropathy of the radial nerve, common peroneal nerve or ulnar nerve and no features of a nerve root lesion (<a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#radiculopathy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#radiculopathy">radiculopathy</a>):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>refer to orthotic services for a splint</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>review the symptoms after 6 weeks, and refer for neurological assessment if there is no evidence of improvement.<br /><br />For adults with features of radiculopathy, see the section on <a class="link" href="recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#cervical-or-lumbar-radiculopathy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#cervical-or-lumbar-radiculopathy">cervical or lumbar radiculopathy</a>. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_7_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise adults with compression neuropathy to avoid any activity that might lead to further pressure on the affected nerve.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Bell's palsy" id="ng127-bells-palsy">
      <h4 class="title" id="bells-palsy">Bell's palsy</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_7_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with an uncomplicated episode of Bell's palsy (unilateral lower motor neurone pattern facial weakness affecting all parts of the face and including weakness of eye closure) and no evidence of another medical condition such as middle ear disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_7_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise adults with Bell's palsy about eye care, and explain that Bell's palsy improves at different rates and maximum recovery can take several months.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_7_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#consider-referring" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#consider-referring">Consider referring</a> adults with Bell's palsy who have developed symptoms of aberrant reinnervation (including gustatory sweating or jaw-winking) 5 months or more after the onset of Bell's palsy for neurological assessment and possible treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#limb-or-facial-weakness-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#limb-or-facial-weakness-in-adults-2">rationale section on limb or facial weakness in adults</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Memory failure and cognitive deterioration" id="ng127-memory-failure-and-cognitive-deterioration">
    <h3 class="title" id="memory-failure-and-cognitive-deterioration">1.8 Memory failure and cognitive deterioration</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Memory problems in adults aged under 50" id="ng127-memory-problems-in-adults-aged-under-50">
      <h4 class="title" id="memory-problems-in-adults-aged-under-50">Memory problems in adults aged under 50</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults aged under 50 with memory problems and no other neurological signs:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>do not routinely refer if brief testing shows memory function to be normal and symptoms are consistent with concentration difficulties</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>be aware that memory problems or concentration difficulties can be caused by:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>recreational, and some prescription, drugs </p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>alcohol</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>affective disorders</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>stress. <br /><br />For more information, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng97/chapter/Recommendations#diagnosis" target="_top">initial assessment in non-specialist settings in the NICE guideline on dementia</a>. </p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Memory problems as part of an anxiety disorder or a functional neurological disorder" id="ng127-memory-problems-as-part-of-an-anxiety-disorder-or-a-functional-neurological-disorder">
      <h4 class="title" id="memory-problems-as-part-of-an-anxiety-disorder-or-a-functional-neurological-disorder">Memory problems as part of an anxiety disorder or a functional neurological disorder</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that, for adults who have an anxiety disorder or have been diagnosed with a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#functional-neurological-disorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#functional-neurological-disorder">functional neurological disorder</a> by a specialist, memory problems and concentration difficulties might be part of the disorder and the person might not need re‑referral if there are no new neurological signs. New symptoms or signs in adults who have been diagnosed with a functional neurological disorder by a specialist should be assessed as described in the relevant sections of this guideline.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Concentration difficulties associated with myalgic encephalomyelitis (or encephalopathy)/chronic fatigue syndrome or fibromyalgia" id="ng127-concentration-difficulties-associated-with-myalgic-encephalomyelitis-or-encephalopathychronic">
      <h4 class="title" id="concentration-difficulties-associated-with-myalgic-encephalomyelitis-or-encephalopathychronic">Concentration difficulties associated with myalgic encephalomyelitis (or encephalopathy)/chronic fatigue syndrome or fibromyalgia</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults for neurological assessment if they have concentration difficulties associated with myalgic encephalomyelitis (or encephalopathy)/chronic fatigue syndrome or fibromyalgia. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Progressive memory problems" id="ng127-progressive-memory-problems">
      <h4 class="title" id="progressive-memory-problems">Progressive memory problems</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on referring adults with progressive memory problems, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng97/chapter/Recommendations#diagnosis" target="_top">initial assessment in non-specialist settings in the NICE guideline on dementia</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dense amnesia" id="ng127-dense-amnesia">
      <h4 class="title" id="dense-amnesia">Dense amnesia</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with a single episode of dense amnesia (inability to recall the recent past or form new memories) if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the episode lasts less than 8 hours <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there is complete recovery <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there are no features suggestive of an epileptic seizure (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg109/chapter/1-Guidance#suspected-epilepsy" target="_top">seizure markers for suspected epilepsy in the NICE guideline on transient loss of consciousness ['blackouts'] in over 16s</a>).<br /><br />Advise the person that they have probably had an episode of transient global amnesia and that the recurrence rate is low.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_8_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults with recurrent episodes of dense amnesia to have an assessment for epileptic amnesia.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#memory-failure-and-cognitive-deterioration-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#memory-failure-and-cognitive-deterioration-2">rationale section on memory failure and cognitive deterioration</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Posture distortion in adults" id="ng127-posture-distortion-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="posture-distortion-in-adults">1.9 Posture distortion in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Dystonia" id="ng127-dystonia">
      <h4 class="title" id="dystonia">Dystonia</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suspect cervical dystonia in adults who have persistent abnormalities of head or neck posture, with or without head tremor, especially if the symptom improves when the person touches their chin with their hand.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer cervical imaging to evaluate suspected cervical dystonia in adults.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that dystonia in adults can affect other parts of the body (for example, it can cause writer's cramp or in‑turned posture of the foot).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_9_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults with suspected dystonia to have an assessment for diagnosis and possible botulinum toxin treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dystonia as a side effect of medications" id="ng127-dystonia-as-a-side-effect-of-medications">
      <h4 class="title" id="dystonia-as-a-side-effect-of-medications">Dystonia as a side effect of medications</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_9_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that antipsychotic and antiemetic medicines can trigger or exacerbate dystonia in adults.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#posture-distortion-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#posture-distortion-in-adults-2">rationale section on posture distortion in adults</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.10 Sensory symptoms including tingling or numbness in adults" id="ng127-sensory-symptoms-including-tingling-or-numbness-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="sensory-symptoms-including-tingling-or-numbness-in-adults">1.10 Sensory symptoms including tingling or numbness in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Numbness and weakness" id="ng127-numbness-and-weakness">
      <h4 class="title" id="numbness-and-weakness">Numbness and weakness</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess sudden-onset transient unilateral numbness in adults in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128">NICE guideline on stroke and transient ischaemic attack in over 16s</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-immediately" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-immediately">Refer immediately</a> adults with rapidly progressive (within hours to days) symmetrical numbness and weakness or imbalance to have a neurological assessment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Sensory disturbances" id="ng127-sensory-disturbances">
      <h4 class="title" id="sensory-disturbances">Sensory disturbances</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-urgently" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-urgently">Refer urgently</a> adults with recurrent, brief (less than 2 minutes), fixed-pattern disturbances of sensation to have an assessment for epilepsy. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults with persistent, distally predominant altered sensation in the limbs, and brisk deep tendon reflexes, to have an assessment for possible brain or spine disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suspect migraine with aura in adults who have sensory symptoms that occur with or without headache and:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are fully reversible <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>develop over at least 5 minutes <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>last between 5 and 60 minutes.<br /><br />For recommendations on diagnosing and managing migraine with aura, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg150" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg150">NICE guideline on headaches in over 12s</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with persistent, distally predominant ('stocking' or 'glove and stocking') altered sensation in the limbs and depressed deep tendon reflexes:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>be alert to the possibility of peripheral neuropathy and consider checking:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>vitamin B12 deficiency</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>thyroid function</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>for evidence of coeliac disease in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng20">NICE guideline on coeliac disease</a></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>renal function</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>blood glucose</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>ESR (erythrocyte sedimentation rate)</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>alcohol consumption, using a tool such as AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test), in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115">NICE guideline on alcohol-use disorders: diagnosis, assessment and management of harmful drinking and alcohol dependence</a></p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if no causes of peripheral neuropathy are found, refer for neurological assessment.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Numbness and tingling as part of a functional neurological disorder" id="ng127-numbness-and-tingling-as-part-of-a-functional-neurological-disorder">
      <h4 class="title" id="numbness-and-tingling-as-part-of-a-functional-neurological-disorder">Numbness and tingling as part of a functional neurological disorder</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that, for adults who have been diagnosed with a <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#functional-neurological-disorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#functional-neurological-disorder">functional neurological disorder</a> by a specialist, recurrent numbness and tingling might be part of the disorder and the person might not need re‑referral if there are no new neurological signs. New symptoms or signs in adults who have been diagnosed with a functional neurological disorder by a specialist should be assessed as described in the relevant sections of this guideline.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise adults with tingling and a diagnosis of functional neurological disorder that the tingling might fluctuate and evolve over time and could increase at times of stress.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Carpal tunnel syndrome" id="ng127-carpal-tunnel-syndrome">
      <h4 class="title" id="carpal-tunnel-syndrome">Carpal tunnel syndrome</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer in line with local pathways if symptoms of carpal tunnel syndrome are severe or persistent after initial management.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Numbness, tingling or pain in the outer thigh" id="ng127-numbness-tingling-or-pain-in-the-outer-thigh">
      <h4 class="title" id="numbness-tingling-or-pain-in-the-outer-thigh">Numbness, tingling or pain in the outer thigh</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassure adults with unilateral or bilateral numbness, tingling or pain in the distribution of the lateral cutaneous nerve of the thigh (meralgia paraesthetica) that the condition is benign and might improve spontaneously. <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#consider-referring" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#consider-referring">Consider referring</a> for pain management only if the symptoms are severe.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Cervical or lumbar radiculopathy" id="ng127-cervical-or-lumbar-radiculopathy">
      <h4 class="title" id="cervical-or-lumbar-radiculopathy">Cervical or lumbar radiculopathy</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with symptoms of cervical <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#radiculopathy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#radiculopathy">radiculopathy</a> that have remained stable for 6 weeks or more unless:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pain is not controlled with analgesics <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the symptoms are disabling <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>1 of the following factors is present:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>age under 20</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>gait disturbance</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>clumsy or weak hands or legs</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>brisk deep tendon reflexes (triceps and lower limbs)</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>extensor plantar responses</p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>new-onset disturbance of bladder or bowel function.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with symptoms of lumbar radiculopathy that have remained stable for 6 weeks or more unless pain is not controlled with analgesics or symptoms are disabling, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng59" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng59">NICE guideline on low back pain and sciatica in over 16s</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Tingling or sensory disturbances on waking from sleep" id="ng127-tingling-or-sensory-disturbances-on-waking-from-sleep">
      <h4 class="title" id="tingling-or-sensory-disturbances-on-waking-from-sleep">Tingling or sensory disturbances on waking from sleep</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_10_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.10.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with recurrent episodes of tingling or sensory disturbance in the limbs that are present on waking from sleep and last less than 10 minutes.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">

  <div class="section" title="1.11 Sleep disorders in adults" id="ng127-sleep-disorders-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="sleep-disorders-in-adults">1.11 Sleep disorders in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Insomnia" id="ng127-insomnia">
      <h4 class="title" id="insomnia">Insomnia</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_11_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.2</h5>

        </div>

          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-urgently" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-urgently">Refer urgently</a> adults with symptoms suggestive of new-onset epileptic seizures in sleep for neurological assessment in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg137" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg137">NICE guideline on epilepsies</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Excessive sleepiness and narcolepsy" id="ng127-excessive-sleepiness-and-narcolepsy">
      <h4 class="title" id="excessive-sleepiness-and-narcolepsy">Excessive sleepiness and narcolepsy</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_11_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with excessive sleepiness:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>use the Epworth score together with history of obstructive symptoms in sleep to assess the likelihood of sleep apnoea</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">refer</a> in accordance with local policy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if appropriate, offer advice on weight reduction, alcohol consumption and smoking cessation, in line with <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/conditions-and-diseases/diabetes-and-other-endocrinal--nutritional-and-metabolic-conditions/obesity" target="_top">NICE guidance on obesity</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/conditions-and-diseases/mental-health-and-behavioural-conditions/alcohol-use-disorders" target="_top">alcohol-use disorders</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/lifestyle-and-wellbeing/smoking-and-tobacco" target="_top">smoking and tobacco</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_11_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.11.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer adults with narcolepsy, with or without cataplexy, for neurological assessment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.12 Smell or taste problems" id="ng127-smell-or-taste-problems">
    <h3 class="title" id="smell-or-taste-problems">1.12 Smell or taste problems</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Distorted sense of smell or taste" id="ng127-distorted-sense-of-smell-or-taste">
      <h4 class="title" id="distorted-sense-of-smell-or-taste">Distorted sense of smell or taste</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_12_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that sudden-onset distortion of sense of smell or taste in adults is rarely associated with structural neurological abnormality and usually resolves within a few months.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Smell or taste hallucinations" id="ng127-smell-or-taste-hallucinations">
      <h4 class="title" id="smell-or-taste-hallucinations">Smell or taste hallucinations</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_12_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults with transient, repetitive smell or taste hallucinations to have a neurological assessment for epilepsy.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Loss of sense of smell or taste" id="ng127-loss-of-sense-of-smell-or-taste">
      <h4 class="title" id="loss-of-sense-of-smell-or-taste">Loss of sense of smell or taste</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_12_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider neuroimaging for adults with unexplained loss of sense of smell or taste that lasts more than 3 months.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_12_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with loss of sense of smell or taste and normal neuroimaging.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_12_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.12.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults who lose their sense of smell or taste immediately after a head injury.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#smell-or-taste-problems-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#smell-or-taste-problems-2">rationale section on smell or taste problems</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>

    <h3 class="title" id="speech-swallowing-and-language-problems-in-adults">1.13 Speech, swallowing and language problems in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Sudden-onset speech or language disturbance" id="ng127-sudden-onset-speech-or-language-disturbance">
      <h4 class="title" id="sudden-onset-speech-or-language-disturbance">Sudden-onset speech or language disturbance</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_13_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.13.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-immediately" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-immediately">Refer immediately</a> adults with sudden-onset speech or language disturbance to have an assessment for a vascular event, in line with local stroke pathways and following the recommendations in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG128">NICE guideline on stroke and transient ischaemic attack in over 16s</a>. <strong>[amended July 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Progressive slurred or disrupted speech" id="ng127-progressive-slurred-or-disrupted-speech">
      <h4 class="title" id="progressive-slurred-or-disrupted-speech">Progressive slurred or disrupted speech</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_13_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.13.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with progressive slurred or disrupted speech:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">refer</a> for an assessment for neuromuscular disorders, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng42/chapter/Recommendations#recognition-and-referral" target="_top">recommendations on recognition and referral in the NICE guideline on motor neurone disease</a></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>refer urgently if there is any evidence of swallowing impairment </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer-immediately" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer-immediately">refer immediately</a> if there is breathlessness at rest or when lying flat. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dysphonia" id="ng127-dysphonia">
      <h4 class="title" id="dysphonia">Dysphonia</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_13_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.13.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#consider-referring" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#consider-referring">Consider referring</a> adults with isolated and unexplained persistent dysphonia (a quiet, hoarse or wobbly voice) to have an assessment for laryngeal dystonia (involuntary contractions of the vocal cords) if hoarseness caused by structural abnormality or malignancy has been excluded by ear, nose and throat examination.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_13_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.13.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that persistent dysphonia in adults may be a presenting symptom of a neurological condition such as Parkinson's disease. For recommendations on the diagnosis and management of Parkinson's disease, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng71" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng71">NICE guideline on Parkinson's disease in adults</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Word-finding difficulties as part of an anxiety disorder or a functional neurological disorder" id="ng127-word-finding-difficulties-as-part-of-an-anxiety-disorder-or-a-functional-neurological-disorder">
      <h4 class="title" id="word-finding-difficulties-as-part-of-an-anxiety-disorder-or-a-functional-neurological-disorder">Word-finding difficulties as part of an anxiety disorder or a functional neurological disorder</h4>

        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that anxiety disorder and <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#functional-neurological-disorder" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#functional-neurological-disorder">functional neurological disorders</a> are the most common causes of minor word-finding difficulties in adults, and people with a diagnosis of anxiety disorder or functional neurological disorder made by a specialist might not need a referral.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#speech-swallowing-and-language-problems-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#speech-swallowing-and-language-problems-in-adults-2">rationale section on speech, swallowing and language problems in adults</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.14 Tics and involuntary movements in adults" id="ng127-tics-and-involuntary-movements-in-adults">
    <h3 class="title" id="tics-and-involuntary-movements-in-adults">1.14 Tics and involuntary movements in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tics" id="ng127-tics">
      <h4 class="title" id="tics">Tics</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with tics (involuntary movements that can be temporarily suppressed at the expense of mounting inner tension) unless the tics are troublesome or accompanied by additional progressive neurological symptoms.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#consider-referring" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#consider-referring">Consider referring</a> adults with a tic disorder for psychological therapy if the disorder distresses them.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring adults who have completed psychological therapy for a tic disorder to have a neurological assessment if their symptoms are severe and the disorder continues to distress them, but tell the person that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there are not many medicines available to treat a tic disorder</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the medicines that are available don't always work well and can have serious side effects.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Involuntary movements" id="ng127-involuntary-movements">
      <h4 class="title" id="involuntary-movements">Involuntary movements</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with isolated involuntary movements of the eyelid unless the movements:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>cause involuntary tight eye closure of both eyes (blepharospasm) <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have persisted for more than 3 months.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In adults with involuntary movements of the face, neck, limbs or trunk that cannot be temporarily suppressed by mental concentration:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">refer</a> for neurological assessment <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>refer to neurology or an eye clinic, according to local provision, if the person has involuntary tight eye closure of both eyes (blepharospasm). </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_14_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.14.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely refer adults with small involuntary muscular twitches (fasciculations) unless these are associated with muscle wasting and weakness or muscle rigidity.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#tics-and-involuntary-movements-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#tics-and-involuntary-movements-in-adults-2">rationale section on tics and involuntary movements in adults</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

    <h3 class="title" id="tremor-in-adults">1.15 Tremor in adults</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Tremor suggesting Parkinson's disease" id="ng127-tremor-suggesting-parkinsons-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="tremor-suggesting-parkinsons-disease">Tremor suggesting Parkinson's disease</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_15_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.15.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> adults with suspected parkinsonian tremor, other asymmetric tremor, or tremor associated with stiffness, slowness, balance problems or gait disorders for neurological assessment before treatment, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng71" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG71">NICE guideline on Parkinson's disease in adults</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Essential tremor" id="ng127-essential-tremor">
      <h4 class="title" id="essential-tremor">Essential tremor</h4>
      <article id="ng127-1_15_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.15.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suspect essential tremor in an adult with symmetrical postural tremor and no symptoms of parkinsonism.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_15_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.15.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In adults with suspected essential tremor:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>review regular medication</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>check thyroid function</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>assess alcohol consumption using a tool such as AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test), in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg115">NICE guideline on alcohol-use disorders: diagnosis, assessment and management of harmful drinking and alcohol dependence</a>.<br /><br /><a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#refer" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#refer">Refer</a> for neurological assessment only if the symptoms are disabling and first-line treatment as specified in the <a class="link" href="https://bnf.nice.org.uk/" target="_top">BNF</a> is ineffective or not tolerated.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng127-1_15_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.15.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>
            <a class="link" href="terms-used-in-this-guideline#consider-referring" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="terms-used-in-this-guideline" data-original-url="#consider-referring">Consider referring</a> adults with troublesome tremor of the head to a movement disorder clinic.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#tremor-in-adults-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#tremor-in-adults-2">rationale section on tremor in adults</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>

    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support">1.16 Information and support</h3>

      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.16.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Follow the principles in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138">NICE guidelines on patient experience in adult NHS services</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">shared decision making</a> in relation to communication (including different formats and languages), information, shared decision making and continuity of care.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng127-1_16_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.16.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Advise adults with suspected neurological conditions to:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>check the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/browse/driving/disability-health-condition" target="_top">government's information on driving with medical conditions</a> to find out whether they might have a condition that needs to be notified to the DVLA (Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>consider telling their employer, school or college if their symptoms might affect their ability to work or study.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16#information-and-support-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-recommendations-for-adults-aged-over-16" data-original-url="#information-and-support-2">rationale section on information and support</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng127/evidence/full-guideline-pdf-6775582573" target="_top">full guideline</a>.</p>
