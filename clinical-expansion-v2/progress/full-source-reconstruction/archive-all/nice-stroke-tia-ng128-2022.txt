<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng128-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off‑label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/resources" target="_top">NICE has also produced patient decision aids on decompressive hemicraniectomy</a>.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Rapid recognition of symptoms and diagnosis" id="ng128-rapid-recognition-of-symptoms-and-diagnosis">
    <h3 class="title" id="rapid-recognition-of-symptoms-and-diagnosis">1.1 Rapid recognition of symptoms and diagnosis</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Prompt recognition of symptoms of stroke and transient ischaemic attack" id="ng128-prompt-recognition-of-symptoms-of-stroke-and-transient-ischaemic-attack">
      <h4 class="title" id="prompt-recognition-of-symptoms-of-stroke-and-transient-ischaemic-attack">Prompt recognition of symptoms of stroke and transient ischaemic attack</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a validated tool, such as FAST (Face Arm Speech Test), outside hospital to screen people with sudden onset of neurological symptoms for a diagnosis of stroke or transient ischaemic attack (TIA). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Exclude hypoglycaemia in people with sudden onset of neurological symptoms as the cause of these symptoms. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who are admitted to the emergency department with a suspected stroke or TIA, establish the diagnosis rapidly using a validated tool, such as ROSIER (Recognition of Stroke in the Emergency Room). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Initial management of suspected and confirmed TIA" id="ng128-initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-tia">
      <h4 class="title" id="initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-tia">Initial management of suspected and confirmed TIA</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer aspirin (300 mg daily), unless contraindicated, to people who have had a suspected TIA, to be started immediately. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this 2019 recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-transient-ischaemic-attack-aspirin" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-transient-ischaemic-attack-aspirin">rationale and impact section on initial management of suspected and confirmed transient ischaemic attack (aspirin)</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/a-aspirin-pdf-6777399566" target="_top">evidence review A: aspirin</a>.</p>
      </div>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer immediately people who have had a suspected TIA for specialist assessment and investigation, to be seen within 24 hours of onset of symptoms. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use scoring systems, such as ABCD2, to assess risk of subsequent stroke or to inform urgency of referral for people who have had a suspected or confirmed TIA. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer secondary prevention, in addition to aspirin, as soon as possible after the diagnosis of TIA is confirmed. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-transient-ischaemic-attack" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#initial-management-of-suspected-and-confirmed-transient-ischaemic-attack">rationale and impact section on initial management of suspected and confirmed transient ischaemic attack</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/b-transient-ischaemic-attack-tia-prediction-rules-pdf-6777399567" target="_top">evidence review B: TIA prediction rules</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Imaging for people who have had a suspected TIA or acute non-disabling stroke" id="ng128-imaging-for-people-who-have-had-a-suspected-tia-or-acute-non-disabling-stroke">
    <h3 class="title" id="imaging-for-people-who-have-had-a-suspected-tia-or-acute-non-disabling-stroke">1.2 Imaging for people who have had a suspected TIA or acute non-disabling stroke</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Suspected TIA" id="ng128-suspected-tia">
      <h4 class="title" id="suspected-tia">Suspected TIA</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer CT brain scanning to people with a suspected TIA unless there is clinical suspicion of an alternative diagnosis that CT could detect. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>After specialist assessment in the TIA clinic, consider MRI (including diffusion-weighted and blood-sensitive sequences) to determine the territory of ischaemia, or to detect haemorrhage or alternative pathologies. If MRI is done, perform it on the same day as the assessment. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#imaging-for-people-who-have-had-a-suspected-tia-or-acute-non-disabling-stroke-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#imaging-for-people-who-have-had-a-suspected-tia-or-acute-non-disabling-stroke-2">rationale and impact section on imaging for people who have had a suspected TIA or acute non-disabling stroke</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/c-tia-imaging-pdf-6777399568" target="_top">evidence review C: TIA imaging</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Carotid imaging" id="ng128-carotid-imaging">
      <h4 class="title" id="carotid-imaging">Carotid imaging</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Everyone with TIA who after specialist assessment is considered as a candidate for carotid endarterectomy should have urgent carotid imaging. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Urgent carotid endarterectomy" id="ng128-urgent-carotid-endarterectomy">
      <h4 class="title" id="urgent-carotid-endarterectomy">Urgent carotid endarterectomy</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that people with stable neurological symptoms from acute non-disabling stroke or TIA who have symptomatic carotid stenosis of 50% to 99% according to the NASCET (North American Symptomatic Carotid Endarterectomy Trial) criteria, or 70% to 99% according to the ECST (European Carotid Surgery Trial) criteria:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are assessed and referred urgently for carotid endarterectomy to a service following current national standards (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/publication/national-stroke-service-model-integrated-stroke-delivery-networks/" target="_top">NHS England and NHS Improvement National Stroke Service Model</a>) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>receive best medical treatment (control of blood pressure, antiplatelet agents, cholesterol lowering through diet and drugs, lifestyle advice). <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that people with stable neurological symptoms from acute non-disabling stroke or TIA who have symptomatic carotid stenosis of less than 50% according to the NASCET criteria, or less than 70% according to the ECST criteria:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>do not have surgery</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>receive best medical treatment (control of blood pressure, antiplatelet agents, cholesterol lowering through diet and drugs, lifestyle advice). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that carotid imaging reports clearly state which criteria (ECST or NASCET) were used when measuring the extent of carotid stenosis. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Specialist care for people with acute stroke" id="ng128-specialist-care-for-people-with-acute-stroke">
    <h3 class="title" id="specialist-care-for-people-with-acute-stroke">1.3 Specialist care for people with acute stroke</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Specialist stroke units" id="ng128-specialist-stroke-units">
      <h4 class="title" id="specialist-stroke-units">Specialist stroke units</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Admit everyone with suspected stroke directly to a specialist acute stroke unit after initial assessment, from either the community, the emergency department, or outpatient clinics. (An acute stroke unit is a discrete area in the hospital that is staffed by a specialist stroke multidisciplinary team. It has access to equipment for monitoring and rehabilitating patients. Regular multidisciplinary team meetings occur for goal setting.). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Brain imaging for the early assessment of people with suspected acute stroke" id="ng128-brain-imaging-for-the-early-assessment-of-people-with-suspected-acute-stroke">
      <h4 class="title" id="brain-imaging-for-the-early-assessment-of-people-with-suspected-acute-stroke">Brain imaging for the early assessment of people with suspected acute stroke</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Perform brain imaging immediately with a non-enhanced CT for people with suspected acute stroke if any of the following apply (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendation-1_3_2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendation-1_3_2">additional information</a>): </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>indications for thrombolysis or thrombectomy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>on anticoagulant treatment</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a known bleeding tendency</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a depressed level of consciousness (Glasgow Coma Score below 13)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>unexplained progressive or fluctuating symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>papilloedema, neck stiffness or fever</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>severe headache at onset of stroke symptoms.<br /><br />If thrombectomy might be indicated, perform imaging with CT contrast angiography following initial non-enhanced CT. Add CT perfusion imaging (or MR equivalent) if thrombectomy might be indicated beyond 6 hours of symptom onset. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Perform scanning as soon as possible and within 24 hours of symptom onset in everyone with suspected acute stroke without indications for immediate brain imaging. <strong>[2008]</strong><br /><br />The <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/publication/national-stroke-service-model-integrated-stroke-delivery-networks/" target="_top">NHS England and NHS Improvement National Stroke Service Model</a> contains a patient-centred national optimal stroke imaging pathway.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Pharmacological treatments and thrombectomy for people with acute stroke" id="ng128-pharmacological-treatments-and-thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-stroke">
    <h3 class="title" id="pharmacological-treatments-and-thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-stroke">1.4 Pharmacological treatments and thrombectomy for people with acute stroke</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Thrombolysis for people with acute ischaemic stroke" id="ng128-thrombolysis-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">
      <h4 class="title" id="thrombolysis-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">Thrombolysis for people with acute ischaemic stroke</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For thrombolytic medicines recommended as options in NICE technology appraisal guidance for treating acute ischaemic stroke within 4.5 hours of symptom onset and when intracranial haemorrhage has been excluded, see the guidance on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta990/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">tenecteplase (TA990, 2024)</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta264/chapter/1-Guidance" target="_top">alteplase (TA264, 2012)</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Administer alteplase or tenecteplase only within a well-organised stroke service with:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>staff trained in delivering thrombolysis and in monitoring for any complications associated with thrombolysis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>nursing staff trained in acute stroke and thrombolysis to provide level 1 and level 2 care (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.datadictionary.nhs.uk/attributes/critical_care_level.html" target="_top">NHS Data Model and Dictionary on critical care level</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>immediate access to imaging and re‑imaging, and staff trained to interpret the images. <strong>[2008, amended 2025]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Staff in emergency departments, if appropriately trained and supported, can administer alteplase or tenecteplase for the treatment of ischaemic stroke provided that patients can be managed within an acute stroke service with appropriate neuroradiological and stroke physician support. <strong>[2008, amended 2025]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that protocols are in place for delivering and managing intravenous thrombolysis, including post-thrombolysis complications. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Thrombectomy for people with acute ischaemic stroke" id="ng128-thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">
      <h4 class="title" id="thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">Thrombectomy for people with acute ischaemic stroke</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer thrombectomy as soon as possible and within 6 hours of symptom onset, together with intravenous thrombolysis (if not contraindicated and within the licensed time window), to people who have:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>acute ischaemic stroke <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>confirmed occlusion of the proximal anterior circulation demonstrated by computed tomographic angiography (CTA) or magnetic resonance angiography (MRA) <br /><br />taking into account the factors in recommendation 1.4.8 (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7">additional information</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer thrombectomy as soon as possible to people who were last known to be well between 6 hours and 24 hours previously (including wake‑up strokes): </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who have acute ischaemic stroke and confirmed occlusion of the proximal anterior circulation demonstrated by CTA or MRA <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if there is the potential to salvage brain tissue, as shown by imaging such as CT perfusion or diffusion-weighted MRI sequences showing limited infarct core volume<br /><br />taking into account the factors in recommendation 1.4.8 (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7">additional information</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider thrombectomy together with intravenous thrombolysis (where not contraindicated and within the licensed time window) as soon as possible for people last known to be well up to 24 hours previously (including wake‑up strokes):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>who have acute ischaemic stroke and confirmed occlusion of the proximal posterior circulation (that is, basilar or posterior cerebral artery) demonstrated by CTA or MRA <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>if there is the potential to salvage brain tissue, as shown by imaging such as CT perfusion or diffusion-weighted MRI sequences showing limited infarct core volume<br /><br />taking into account the factors in recommendation 1.4.8 (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendations-1_4_5-to-1_4_7">additional information</a>). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take into account the person's overall clinical status and the extent of established infarction on initial brain imaging to inform decisions about thrombectomy. Select people who have (in addition to the factors in recommendations 1.4.5 to 1.4.7):</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a pre-stroke functional status of less than 3 on the modified Rankin scale <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a score of more than 5 on the National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale (NIHSS). <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#thrombectomy-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke-2">rationale and impact section on thrombectomy for people with acute ischaemic stroke</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/d-thrombectomy-pdf-6777399569" target="_top">evidence review D: thrombectomy</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Aspirin and anticoagulant treatment" id="ng128-aspirin-and-anticoagulant-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="aspirin-and-anticoagulant-treatment">Aspirin and anticoagulant treatment</h4>
      <div class="section" title="People with acute ischaemic stroke" id="ng128-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">
        <h5 class="title" id="people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">People with acute ischaemic stroke</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer the following as soon as possible, but certainly within 24 hours, to everyone presenting with acute stroke who has had a diagnosis of intracerebral haemorrhage excluded by brain imaging: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>aspirin 300 mg orally if they do not have dysphagia <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>aspirin 300 mg rectally or by enteral tube if they do have dysphagia. <br /><br />Continue aspirin daily 300 mg until 2 weeks after the onset of stroke symptoms, at which time start definitive long-term antithrombotic treatment. Start people on long-term treatment earlier if they are being discharged before 2 weeks. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer a proton pump inhibitor, in addition to aspirin, to anyone with acute ischaemic stroke for whom previous dyspepsia associated with aspirin is reported. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer an alternative antiplatelet agent to anyone with acute ischaemic stroke who is allergic to or genuinely intolerant of aspirin. (Aspirin intolerance is defined as either of the following: proven hypersensitivity to aspirin-containing medicines, or history of severe dyspepsia induced by low-dose aspirin.) <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Do not use anticoagulation treatment routinely for the treatment of acute stroke (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendation-1_4_12" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendation-1_4_12">additional information</a>). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="People with acute venous stroke" id="ng128-people-with-acute-venous-stroke">
        <h5 class="title" id="people-with-acute-venous-stroke">People with acute venous stroke</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.13</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer people diagnosed with cerebral venous sinus thrombosis (including those with secondary cerebral haemorrhage) full-dose anticoagulation treatment (initially full-dose heparin and then warfarin [international normalised ratio 2 to 3]) unless there are comorbidities that preclude its use. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="People with stroke associated with arterial dissection" id="ng128-people-with-stroke-associated-with-arterial-dissection">
        <h5 class="title" id="people-with-stroke-associated-with-arterial-dissection">People with stroke associated with arterial dissection</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.14</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer either anticoagulants or antiplatelet agents to people who have stroke secondary to acute arterial dissection. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="People with acute ischaemic stroke associated with antiphospholipid syndrome" id="ng128-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke-associated-with-antiphospholipid-syndrome">
        <h5 class="title" id="people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke-associated-with-antiphospholipid-syndrome">People with acute ischaemic stroke associated with antiphospholipid syndrome</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Manage acute ischaemic stroke associated with antiphospholipid syndrome in the same way as acute ischaemic stroke without antiphospholipid syndrome (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendation-1_4_15" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendation-1_4_15">additional information</a>). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Reversal of anticoagulation treatment in people with haemorrhagic stroke" id="ng128-reversal-of-anticoagulation-treatment-in-people-with-haemorrhagic-stroke">
        <h5 class="title" id="reversal-of-anticoagulation-treatment-in-people-with-haemorrhagic-stroke">Reversal of anticoagulation treatment in people with haemorrhagic stroke</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.16</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Return clotting levels to normal as soon as possible in people with a primary intracerebral haemorrhage who were receiving warfarin before their stroke (and have elevated international normalised ratio). Do this by reversing the effects of the warfarin using a combination of prothrombin complex concentrate and intravenous vitamin K. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <p>For advice on reversing direct-acting oral anticoagulants (DOACs), see the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/direct-acting-oral-anticoagulants-doacs-reminder-of-bleeding-risk-including-availability-of-reversal-agents#management-of-bleeding-and-availability-of-reversal-agents" target="_top">MHRA safety advice on DOACs for a list of reversal agents</a>.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Anticoagulation treatment for other comorbidities" id="ng128-anticoagulation-treatment-for-other-comorbidities">
        <h5 class="title" id="anticoagulation-treatment-for-other-comorbidities">Anticoagulation treatment for other comorbidities</h5>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.17</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Ensure that people with disabling ischaemic stroke who are in atrial fibrillation are treated with aspirin 300 mg for the first 2 weeks before anticoagulation treatment is considered. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.18</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with prosthetic valves who have disabling cerebral infarction and who are at significant risk of haemorrhagic transformation, stop anticoagulation treatment for 1 week and substitute aspirin 300 mg. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.19</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Ensure that people with ischaemic stroke and symptomatic proximal deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism receive anticoagulation treatment in preference to treatment with aspirin unless there are other contraindications to anticoagulation. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng128-1_4_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.4.20</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Treat people who have haemorrhagic stroke and symptomatic deep vein thrombosis or pulmonary embolism to prevent the development of further pulmonary emboli using either anticoagulation or a caval filter. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Statin treatment" id="ng128-statin-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="statin-treatment">Statin treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Immediate initiation of statin treatment is not recommended in people with acute stroke (see <a class="link" href="additional-information#recommendation-1_4_21" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="additional-information" data-original-url="#recommendation-1_4_21">additional information</a>). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_4_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Continue statin treatment in people with acute stroke who are already receiving statins. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Maintenance or restoration of homeostasis" id="ng128-maintenance-or-restoration-of-homeostasis">
    <h3 class="title" id="maintenance-or-restoration-of-homeostasis">1.5 Maintenance or restoration of homeostasis</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Supplemental oxygen therapy" id="ng128-supplemental-oxygen-therapy">
      <h4 class="title" id="supplemental-oxygen-therapy">Supplemental oxygen therapy</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give supplemental oxygen to people who have had a stroke only if their oxygen saturation drops below 95%. The routine use of supplemental oxygen is not recommended in people with acute stroke who are not hypoxic. <strong>[2008]</strong><br /><br />Be aware that some pulse oximeters can underestimate or overestimate oxygen saturation levels, especially if the saturation level is borderline. Overestimation has been reported in people with dark skin. See also the <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/2018/12/risk-of-harm-from-inappropriate-placement-of-pulse-oximeter-probes/" target="_top">NHS England Patient Safety Alert on the risk of harm from inappropriate placement of pulse oximeter probes</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Blood sugar control" id="ng128-blood-sugar-control">
      <h4 class="title" id="blood-sugar-control">Blood sugar control</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Maintain a blood glucose concentration between 4 and 11 mmol/litre in people with acute stroke. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide optimal insulin therapy, which can be achieved by the use of intravenous insulin and glucose, to all adults with type 1 diabetes with threatened or actual stroke. Critical care and emergency departments should have a protocol for such management. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Blood pressure control for people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage" id="ng128-blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage">
      <h4 class="title" id="blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage">Blood pressure control for people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider rapid blood pressure lowering for people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage who do not have any of the exclusions listed in recommendation 1.5.7 and who: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>present within 6 hours of symptom onset <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have a systolic blood pressure of between 150 and 220 mmHg. <strong>[2022]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Taking into account the risk of harm, consider rapid blood pressure lowering on a case-by-case basis for people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage who do not have any of the exclusions listed in recommendation 1.5.7 and who: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>present beyond 6 hours of symptom onset <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have a systolic blood pressure greater than 220 mmHg. <strong>[2022]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When rapidly lowering blood pressure in people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage, aim to reach a systolic blood pressure of 140 mmHg or lower while ensuring that the magnitude drop does not exceed 60 mmHg within 1 hour of starting treatment. <strong>[2022]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer rapid blood pressure lowering to people who: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have an underlying structural cause (for example, tumour, arteriovenous malformation or aneurysm) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have a score on the Glasgow Coma Scale of below 6</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are going to have early neurosurgery to evacuate the haematoma</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have a massive haematoma with a poor expected prognosis. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When considering blood pressure lowering in young people aged 16 or 17 with acute intracerebral haemorrhage who do not have any of the exclusions listed in recommendation 1.5.7, seek advice from a paediatric specialist. <strong>[2022]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2022 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage-2">rationale and impact section on blood pressure control for people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/e-intensive-interventions-to-lower-blood-pressure-in-people-with-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage-pdf-11018424781" target="_top">evidence review E: intensive interventions to lower blood pressure in people with acute intracerebral haemorrhage</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Blood pressure control for people with acute ischaemic stroke" id="ng128-blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">
      <h4 class="title" id="blood-pressure-control-for-people-with-acute-ischaemic-stroke">Blood pressure control for people with acute ischaemic stroke</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Anti-hypertensive treatment in people with acute ischaemic stroke is recommended only if there is a hypertensive emergency with one or more of the following serious concomitant medical issues:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hypertensive encephalopathy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hypertensive nephropathy</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>hypertensive cardiac failure/myocardial infarction</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>aortic dissection</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>pre-eclampsia/eclampsia. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Blood pressure reduction to 185/110 mmHg or lower should be considered in people who are candidates for intravenous thrombolysis. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Nutrition and hydration" id="ng128-nutrition-and-hydration">
    <h3 class="title" id="nutrition-and-hydration">1.6 Nutrition and hydration</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Assessment of swallowing function" id="ng128-assessment-of-swallowing-function">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessment-of-swallowing-function">Assessment of swallowing function</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>On admission, ensure that people with acute stroke have their swallowing screened by an appropriately trained healthcare professional before being given any oral food, fluid or medication. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the admission screen indicates problems with swallowing, ensure that the person has a specialist assessment of swallowing, preferably within 24 hours of admission and not more than 72 hours afterwards. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People with suspected aspiration on specialist assessment, or who require tube feeding or dietary modification for 3 days, should be:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>re-assessed and considered for instrumental examination</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>referred for dietary advice. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People with acute stroke who are unable to take adequate nutrition, fluids and medication orally should:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>receive tube feeding with a nasogastric tube within 24 hours of admission unless they have had thrombolysis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>be considered for a nasal bridle tube or gastrostomy if they are unable to tolerate a nasogastric tube</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>be referred to an appropriately trained healthcare professional for detailed nutritional assessment, individualised advice and monitoring</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have their oral medication reviewed to amend either the formulation or the route of administration. <strong>[2008, amended 2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Oral nutritional supplementation" id="ng128-oral-nutritional-supplementation">
      <h4 class="title" id="oral-nutritional-supplementation">Oral nutritional supplementation</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Screen all hospital inpatients on admission for malnutrition and the risk of malnutrition. Repeat screening weekly for inpatients. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Screening should assess body mass index (BMI) and percentage unintentional weight loss. It should also consider the time over which a nutrient intake has been unintentionally reduced and/or the likelihood of future impaired nutrient intake. The Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool (MUST), for example, may be used to do this. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When screening for malnutrition and the risk of malnutrition, be aware that dysphagia, poor oral health and reduced ability to self-feed will affect nutrition in people with stroke. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Screening for malnutrition and the risk of malnutrition should be carried out by healthcare professionals with appropriate skills and training. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Routine nutritional supplementation is not recommended for people with acute stroke who are adequately nourished on admission. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Start nutrition support for people with stroke who are at risk of malnutrition. This may include oral nutritional supplements, specialist dietary advice and/or tube feeding. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hydration" id="ng128-hydration">
      <h4 class="title" id="hydration">Hydration</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_6_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess, on admission, the hydration of everyone with acute stroke. Review hydration regularly and manage it so that normal hydration is maintained. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Optimal positioning and early mobilisation for people with acute stroke" id="ng128-optimal-positioning-and-early-mobilisation-for-people-with-acute-stroke">
    <h3 class="title" id="optimal-positioning-and-early-mobilisation-for-people-with-acute-stroke">1.7 Optimal positioning and early mobilisation for people with acute stroke</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Optimal positioning" id="ng128-optimal-positioning">
      <h4 class="title" id="optimal-positioning">Optimal positioning</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the individual clinical needs and personal preferences of people with acute stroke to determine their optimal head position. Take into account factors such as their comfort, physical and cognitive abilities and postural control. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#optimal-positioning-for-people-with-acute-stroke" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#optimal-positioning-for-people-with-acute-stroke">rationale and impact section on optimal positioning for people with acute stroke</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/g-head-positioning-pdf-6777399572" target="_top">evidence review G: head positioning</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Early mobilisation" id="ng128-early-mobilisation">
      <h4 class="title" id="early-mobilisation">Early mobilisation</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Help people with acute stroke to sit out of bed, stand or walk as soon as their clinical condition permits as part of an active management programme in a specialist stroke unit. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If people need help to sit out of bed, stand or walk, do not offer <a class="link" href="recommendations#high-intensity-mobilisation" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#high-intensity-mobilisation">high-intensity mobilisation</a> in the first 24 hours after symptom onset. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#early-mobilisation-for-people-with-acute-stroke" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#early-mobilisation-for-people-with-acute-stroke">rationale and impact section on early mobilisation for people with acute stroke</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/f-very-early-mobilisation-pdf-6777399571" target="_top">evidence review F: very early mobilisation</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Avoiding aspiration pneumonia" id="ng128-avoiding-aspiration-pneumonia">
    <h3 class="title" id="avoiding-aspiration-pneumonia">1.8 Avoiding aspiration pneumonia</h3>
    <article id="ng128-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>To avoid aspiration pneumonia, give food, fluids and medication to people with dysphagia in a form that can be swallowed without aspiration, after specialist assessment of swallowing (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessment-of-swallowing-function" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessment-of-swallowing-function">recommendation 1.6.2</a>). <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Surgery for people with acute stroke" id="ng128-surgery-for-people-with-acute-stroke">
    <h3 class="title" id="surgery-for-people-with-acute-stroke">1.9 Surgery for people with acute stroke</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Acute intracerebral haemorrhage" id="ng128-acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage">
      <h4 class="title" id="acute-intracerebral-haemorrhage">Acute intracerebral haemorrhage</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Stroke services should agree protocols for monitoring, referring and transferring people to regional neurosurgical centres for the management of symptomatic hydrocephalus. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People with intracerebral haemorrhage should be monitored by specialists in neurosurgical or stroke care for deterioration in function and referred immediately for brain imaging when necessary. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Previously fit people should be considered for surgical intervention following primary intracerebral haemorrhage if they have hydrocephalus. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People with any of the following rarely require surgical intervention and should receive medical treatment initially:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>small deep haemorrhages</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>lobar haemorrhage without either hydrocephalus or rapid neurological deterioration</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a large haemorrhage and significant comorbidities before the stroke</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a score on the Glasgow Coma Scale of below 8 unless this is because of hydrocephalus</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>posterior fossa haemorrhage. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Decompressive hemicraniectomy" id="ng128-decompressive-hemicraniectomy">
      <h4 class="title" id="decompressive-hemicraniectomy">Decompressive hemicraniectomy</h4>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider decompressive hemicraniectomy (which should be performed within 48 hours of symptom onset) for people with acute stroke who meet all of the following criteria:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>clinical deficits that suggest infarction in the territory of the middle cerebral artery, with a score above 15 on the NIHSS </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>decreased level of consciousness, with a score of 1 or more on item 1a of the NIHSS</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>signs on CT of an infarct of at least 50% of the middle cerebral artery territory: </p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>with or without additional infarction in the territory of the anterior or posterior cerebral artery on the same side <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>with infarct volume greater than 145 cm<sup>3</sup>, as shown on diffusion-weighted MRI scan. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss the risks and benefits of decompressive hemicraniectomy with people or their family members or carers (as appropriate), taking into account their functional status before the stroke, and their wishes and preferences. <strong>[2019]</strong><br /><br /><a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/resources" target="_top">NICE has produced patient decision aids</a> to support discussions about decompressive hemicraniectomy.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng128-1_9_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.9.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People who are referred for decompressive hemicraniectomy should be monitored by appropriately trained professionals skilled in neurological assessment. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#decompressive-hemicraniectomy-for-people-with-acute-stroke" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#decompressive-hemicraniectomy-for-people-with-acute-stroke">rationale and impact section on decompressive hemicraniectomy for people with acute stroke</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/h-surgery-decompressive-hemicraniectomy-pdf-6777399573" target="_top">evidence review H: surgery (decompressive hemicraniectomy)</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng128-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline. For other definitions, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/Glossary" target="_top">NICE glossary</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="High-intensity mobilisation" id="ng128-high-intensity-mobilisation">
      <h4 class="title" id="high-intensity-mobilisation">High-intensity mobilisation</h4>
      <p>High-intensity mobilisation refers to the very early mobilisation intervention from the <a class="link" href="https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736(15)60690-0/fulltext" target="_top">AVERT trial</a>. (Further details of the intervention performed in the trial can be found in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng128/evidence/evidence-reviews-11018712493?tab=evidence" target="_top">NICE's evidence review F: very early mobilisation</a>.) It includes mobilisation that:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>begins within the first 24 hours of stroke onset</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>includes at least 3 additional out-of-bed sessions compared with usual care </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>focuses on sitting, standing and walking (that is, out of bed) activity.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
