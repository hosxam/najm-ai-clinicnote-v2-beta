<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng23-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>NICE guidelines set out the care and services suitable for people with a specific condition or need, and people in particular circumstances or settings. We aim to improve quality by ensuring that people receive the best care and advice. Using inclusive language in healthcare is important for safety, and to promote equity, respect and effective communication with everyone.</p>
    <p>Some recommendations in this guideline do not use inclusive language because:</p>
    <div class="itemizedlist">
      <ul class="itemizedlist">
        <li class="listitem">
          <p>the evidence has not been reviewed, and expert opinion is that groups covered by these recommendations cannot be extended <strong>or</strong></p>
        </li>
        <li class="listitem">
          <p>the evidence has been reviewed, but the information available for some groups was too limited to make specific recommendations.</p>
        </li>
      </ul>
    </div>
    <p>Healthcare professionals should use their clinical judgement when implementing the recommendations, taking into account each person's circumstances, needs and preferences, and ensuring all people are treated with dignity and respect throughout their care.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>This guideline covers women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth, who currently have menopause-associated symptoms or who will experience menopause in the future. The guideline does not cover people who are currently having gender-affirming hormone therapy. For trans men and non-binary people who have taken such therapy in the past and are no longer taking it, only <a class="link" href="recommendations#gender-affirming-hormone-therapy-past-use" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#gender-affirming-hormone-therapy-past-use">recommendations 1.5.35 and 1.5.36 in the section on gender affirming therapy</a> and the <a class="link" href="recommendations-for-research#impact-of-hrt-on-health-outcomes-for-trans-men-and-non-binary-people-registered-female-at-birth" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations-for-research" data-original-url="#impact-of-hrt-on-health-outcomes-for-trans-men-and-non-binary-people-registered-female-at-birth">recommendation for research on the impact of hormone replacement therapy (HRT) on health outcomes for these groups of people</a> apply. All other recommendations apply to women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth who have never taken gender-affirming hormone therapy.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Individualised care" id="ng23-individualised-care">
    <h3 class="title" id="individualised-care">1.1 Individualised care</h3>
    <article id="ng23-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Tailor your approach to the person at all times when identifying, discussing, investigating and managing menopause, and adapt the approach if symptoms change over time. Follow the recommendations in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138/">NICE's guideline on patient experience in adult NHS services</a>. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For general principles on how to discuss symptom management plans with people, including how to communicate risks, benefits and consequences, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng197">NICE's guideline on shared decision making</a>. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Information and support" id="ng23-information-and-support">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support">1.2 Information and support</h3>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Share information about menopause with people who have associated symptoms or are approaching menopause, and their family members or carers (as appropriate). This information should include all of the following:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>what menopause is, including that it is a life transition which:</p>
            <p>
              <div class="itemizedlist">
                <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>usually takes place in mid-life <strong>and</strong></p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>can also happen earlier because of surgery or medical treatment, an inherited condition, or an unknown cause </p>
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </div>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>commonly associated symptoms (see recommendation 1.2.2) </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>interventions, or changes the person can make to support their health and wellbeing. <strong>[2015, amended 2024]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain that symptoms associated with menopause may vary from minor to severe and be experienced over short or long time periods. As well as changes in menstrual cycle, symptoms may include:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>vasomotor symptoms (hot flushes and sweats)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="recommendations#genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2">genitourinary symptoms</a> (for example, vaginal dryness)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>effects on mood (for example, <a class="link" href="recommendations#depressive-symptoms-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#depressive-symptoms-2">depressive symptoms</a>)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>musculoskeletal symptoms (for example, joint and muscle pain)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>sexual difficulties (for example, low sexual desire). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Share information about contraception with people who have menopause-associated symptoms. See, for example, the <a class="link" href="https://www.fsrh.org/Public/Standards-and-Guidance/Aged-Over-40.aspx" target="_top">Faculty of Sexual and Reproductive Healthcare guidance on contraception for women aged over 40 years</a>. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Give advice on bone health to people experiencing menopause and discuss bone health with them at review appointments (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg146" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg146">NICE's guideline on assessing the risk of fragility fracture in people with osteoporosis</a>). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explain to people experiencing menopause the importance of maintaining muscle mass and strength through physical activity. <strong>[2015, amended 2024]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer support and provide information about menopause and fertility to people who are likely to experience menopause as a result of medical or surgical treatment. Do this before and after they have their treatment. <strong>[2015, amended 2024]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="section" title="Psychological support for early menopause" id="ng23-psychological-support-for-early-menopause">
      <h4 class="title" id="psychological-support-for-early-menopause">Psychological support for early menopause</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer psychological support to people who are experiencing early menopause (that is, menopause between the ages of 40 and 44) and are distressed by their diagnosis or its consequences. If needed, refer them to psychology services. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2024 recommendation and how it might affect practice, see <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#psychological-support-for-early-menopause-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#psychological-support-for-early-menopause-2">rationale and impact section on psychological support for early menopause</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23/evidence/i-early-menopause-pdf-487915383062" target="_top">evidence review I: early menopause</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.3 Identifying perimenopause and menopause" id="ng23-identifying-perimenopause-and-menopause">
    <h3 class="title" id="identifying-perimenopause-and-menopause">1.3 Identifying perimenopause and menopause</h3>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Identify the following, without laboratory tests, in otherwise healthy women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth who are aged 45 or over and have menopause-associated symptoms:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>perimenopause, if they have vasomotor symptoms that have recently started and any changes in their menstrual cycle </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>menopause, if they have not had a period for at least 12 months and are not using hormonal contraception</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>menopause, in those who have had a hysterectomy, based on the type and combination of symptoms they have (for example, vasomotor symptoms). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Take into account that it can be difficult to identify menopause in people who are taking hormonal treatments, for example, for the treatment of heavy menstrual bleeding. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that people from some ethnic minority backgrounds and people with some lifelong conditions may experience menopause at a younger age. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not use the following laboratory and imaging tests to identify perimenopause or menopause in people aged 45 or over:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>anti-Müllerian hormone</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>inhibin A</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>inhibin B</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>oestradiol</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>antral follicle count</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>ovarian volume. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not use a follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) blood test to identify menopause in people using combined oestrogen and progestogen contraception or high-dose progestogen. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider using the person's serum FSH level to confirm menopause only:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>in people aged 40 to 45 with menopause-associated symptoms, including a change in their menstrual cycle</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>in people under 40 in whom menopause is suspected (see also <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">diagnosing and managing premature ovarian insufficiency</a>). <strong>[2015]</strong><br /><br />See also the recommendations on offering psychological support to:</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="recommendations#psychological-support-for-early-menopause" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#psychological-support-for-early-menopause">people experiencing early menopause (aged 40 to 44)</a>
              <strong>and</strong>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>
              <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">people with premature ovarian insufficiency</a>. </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2024 recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#identifying-perimenopause-and-menopause-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#identifying-perimenopause-and-menopause-2">rationale and impact section on identifying perimenopause and menopause</a>. </p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23/evidence/i-early-menopause-pdf-487915383062" target="_top">evidence review I: early menopause</a>.</p>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.4 Discussing management options with people aged 40 or over" id="ng23-discussing-management-options-with-people-aged-40-or-over">
    <h3 class="title" id="discussing-management-options-with-people-aged-40-or-over">1.4 Discussing management options with people aged 40 or over</h3>
    <p>This section only covers people aged 40 or over. For younger (under 40) women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth, see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">recommendations on diagnosing and managing premature ovarian insufficiency, including what to discuss with the person</a>.</p>
    <article id="ng23-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Discuss with the person the benefits and risks associated with each potential management option for menopause-associated symptoms. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="section" title="Hormone replacement therapy" id="ng23-hormone-replacement-therapy">
      <h4 class="title" id="hormone-replacement-therapy">Hormone replacement therapy</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When discussing hormone replacement therapy (HRT) as a possible treatment for menopause-associated symptoms (in line with the recommendations on managing symptoms associated with menopause in people aged 40 and over), talk about the benefits and risks associated with:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#combined-hrt-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#combined-hrt-2">combined</a> versus oestrogen-only HRT (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#starting-hrt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#starting-hrt">recommendation and its rationale on indications for combined and oestrogen-only HRT, in the section on starting HRT</a> about which of the 2 types of HRT the person would be offered, and why)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>transdermal versus oral HRT</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>types of oestrogen and progestogen</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#sequential-combined-hrt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#sequential-combined-hrt">sequential</a> versus <a class="link" href="recommendations#continuous-combined-hrt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#continuous-combined-hrt">continuous combined HRT</a></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>dose and duration. <strong>[2024]</strong><br /><br />Tailor the information about benefits and risks to the person's age, individual circumstances and potential risk factors. Use the information in <a class="link" href="recommendations#managing-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-in-people-aged-40-or-over" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#managing-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-in-people-aged-40-or-over">managing symptoms associated with menopause in people aged 40 or over</a>, and in the <a class="link" href="recommendations#effects-of-hormone-replacement-therapy-on-specific-health-outcomes-in-people-aged-40-or-over" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#effects-of-hormone-replacement-therapy-on-specific-health-outcomes-in-people-aged-40-or-over">effect of HRT on specific health outcomes in people aged 40 or over</a> to support this discussion.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person chooses to take HRT:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss the possible duration of treatment at the outset</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>at every review, rediscuss the benefits and risks of continuing treatment (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone">section on reviewing treatment for anyone</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>explain that symptoms may return when HRT is stopped and discuss the option of restarting treatment if necessary. <strong>[2024] </strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

      <h4 class="title" id="complementary-therapies-and-unregulated-preparations">Complementary therapies and unregulated preparations</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people with menopause-associated symptoms that the efficacy and safety of unregulated hormone preparations are unknown. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people who wish to try complementary therapies for menopause-associated symptoms, that the safety, quality and purity of constituents in unregulated preparations may be unknown. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people that there is some evidence that isoflavones or black cohosh may relieve vasomotor symptoms associated with menopause. However, explain that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>multiple preparations are available, and their safety is uncertain</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>different preparations may vary</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>interactions with other medicines have been reported. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>

        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people with a personal history of, or at high risk of, breast cancer that, although there is some evidence that St John's wort may help relieve vasomotor symptoms associated with menopause, there is uncertainty about:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>appropriate dosage</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>persistence of effect</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>variation in the nature and potency of preparations </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>potential serious interactions with other medicines (including tamoxifen, anticoagulants and anticonvulsants). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>

  <div class="section" title="1.5 Managing symptoms associated with menopause in people aged 40 or over" id="ng23-managing-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-in-people-aged-40-or-over">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-in-people-aged-40-or-over">1.5 Managing symptoms associated with menopause in people aged 40 or over</h3>
    <p>This section only covers people aged 40 or over. For younger (aged under 40) women, trans men, and non-binary people registered female at birth, see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">recommendations on diagnosing and managing premature ovarian insufficiency</a>.</p>
    <p>For information about how comorbidities, contraindications and medical history might affect management choices, see <a class="link" href="recommendations#taking-medical-history-into-account-before-offering-treatment-for-menopause-associated-symptoms" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#taking-medical-history-into-account-before-offering-treatment-for-menopause-associated-symptoms">taking medical history into account before offering treatment</a>.</p>
    <div class="panel panel--primary">
      <p>The benefits and risks of hormone replacement therapy (HRT) described in this guideline only cover the use of HRT within the licensed dosages. </p>
      <p>
        <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> has information about prescribing medicines. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Vasomotor symptoms" id="ng23-vasomotor-symptoms">
      <h4 class="title" id="vasomotor-symptoms">Vasomotor symptoms</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer HRT to people with vasomotor symptoms associated with menopause. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider menopause-specific cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT) as an option for vasomotor symptoms associated with menopause:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>in addition to HRT <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>for people for whom HRT is contraindicated <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>for those who prefer not to take HRT. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Fezolinetant is recommended as an option for treating moderate to severe vasomotor symptoms associated with menopause when HRT is unsuitable. For full details, see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta1143/chapter/1-Recommendations" target="_top">NICE's technology appraisal guidance on fezolinetant (TA1143, 2026)</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs) or clonidine as first-line treatment for vasomotor symptoms alone. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2024 recommendation on CBT and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#cognitive-behavioural-therapy-for-vasomotor-symptoms" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#cognitive-behavioural-therapy-for-vasomotor-symptoms">rationale and impact section on CBT for vasomotor symptoms</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23/evidence/a-cognitive-behavioural-therapy-pdf-13553205086" target="_top">evidence review A: cognitive behavioural therapy</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause" id="ng23-genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause">
      <h4 class="title" id="genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause">Genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause</h4>
      <div class="section" title="People with no history of breast cancer" id="ng23-people-with-no-history-of-breast-cancer">
        <h5 class="title" id="people-with-no-history-of-breast-cancer">People with no history of breast cancer</h5>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer vaginal oestrogen to people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2">genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause</a> (including those using <a class="link" href="recommendations#systemic-hrt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#systemic-hrt">systemic HRT)</a> and review regularly as per the <a class="link" href="recommendations#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone">recommendations on reviews in this guideline</a>. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When discussing the option of vaginal oestrogen, explain that:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>serious adverse effects are very rare</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>their treatment should be reviewed in line with <a class="link" href="recommendations#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#reviewing-treatment-for-anyone">recommendations 1.9.2 and 1.9.3 in the section on reviewing treatment</a></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>symptoms often return when vaginal oestrogen is stopped but treatment can be restarted if necessary</p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>vaginal oestrogen is absorbed locally – a minimal amount is absorbed into the bloodstream (when compared with systemic HRT), but this is unlikely to have a significant effect throughout the body. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When someone chooses vaginal oestrogen, make a shared decision with the person about whether to use an oestrogen cream, gel, tablet, pessary or ring. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Advise people with genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause that vaginal oestrogen can be used on its own or in combination with non-hormonal moisturisers or lubricants. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with genitourinary symptoms in whom vaginal oestrogen preparations are contraindicated, or for people who would prefer not to use vaginal oestrogen, consider non-hormonal vaginal moisturisers or lubricants. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider vaginal prasterone for genitourinary symptoms if vaginal oestrogen, or non-hormonal moisturisers or lubricants have been ineffective or are not tolerated. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.11</h6>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.15</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider vaginal oestrogen for people with a personal history of breast cancer and genitourinary symptoms that have continued despite trying non-hormonal treatments (see also recommendation 1.5.16 for people receiving adjuvant aromatase inhibitor treatment for breast cancer). Vaginal oestrogen may be used in combination with a non-hormonal moisturiser or a lubricant.<br /><br />In November 2024, this was an off-label use of vaginal oestrogen. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>

          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.16</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people currently having aromatase inhibitors as adjuvant treatment for breast cancer, work with a breast cancer specialist to identify treatment options for genitourinary symptoms that have continued despite trying non-hormonal treatments.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.17</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>When assessing the safety of vaginal oestrogens for someone in relation to breast cancer recurrence, take into account all of the following: </p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>the person's general risk factors for breast cancer recurrence (see <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng101/chapter/Recommendations#extended-endocrine-hormone-therapy" target="_top">recommendations 1.7.7 and 1.7.8 in NICE's guideline on early and locally advanced breast cancer for the definitions of medium or high risk and of low risk of recurrence</a>) </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>it is unknown whether vaginal oestrogen affects the risks of breast cancer recurrence </p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>vaginal oestrogen is absorbed locally, and some of it is absorbed into the bloodstream but compared with oestrogen from systemic HRT, the amount is minimal. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.18</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with a personal history of oestrogen receptor-negative breast cancer, recognise that any oestrogen systemically absorbed from taking vaginal oestrogen is unlikely to increase the risk of breast cancer recurrence, and so it is likely to be safe. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <article id="ng23-1_5_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.19</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>For people with a personal history of oestrogen receptor-positive breast cancer, recognise that:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>it is unknown whether any oestrogen systemically absorbed from taking vaginal oestrogen could increase the risk of breast cancer recurrence <strong>and</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>adjuvants that block oestrogen receptors in cancer cells (for example, tamoxifen) would reduce any such potential impact. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>

    <h3 class="title" id="effects-of-hormone-replacement-therapy-on-specific-health-outcomes-in-people-aged-40-or-over">1.6 Effects of hormone replacement therapy on specific health outcomes in people aged 40 or over</h3>

  <div class="section" title="1.7 Diagnosing and managing premature ovarian insufficiency in people under 40" id="ng23-diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnosing-and-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency-in-people-under-40">1.7 Diagnosing and managing premature ovarian insufficiency in people under 40</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Diagnosing premature ovarian insufficiency" id="ng23-diagnosing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency">
      <h4 class="title" id="diagnosing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency">Diagnosing premature ovarian insufficiency</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Take into account the person's clinical history (for example, previous medical or surgical treatment) and family history when diagnosing premature ovarian insufficiency. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Diagnose premature ovarian insufficiency in women, trans men and non-binary people registered female at birth who are under 40 based on:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>menopause-associated symptoms, including no or infrequent periods (taking into account whether the person has had a hysterectomy) <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>elevated follicle stimulating hormone (FSH) levels on 2 blood samples taken 4 to 6 weeks apart. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not diagnose premature ovarian insufficiency on the basis of a single blood test. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely use anti-Müllerian hormone testing to diagnose premature ovarian insufficiency. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If there is doubt about the diagnosis of premature ovarian insufficiency, refer the person to a <a class="link" href="recommendations#healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause">specialist with expertise in menopause</a> or reproductive medicine. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing premature ovarian insufficiency" id="ng23-managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-premature-ovarian-insufficiency">Managing premature ovarian insufficiency</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer sex steroid replacement with a choice of hormone replacement therapy (HRT) or a combined hormonal contraceptive to people with premature ovarian insufficiency, unless contraindicated (for example, in people with hormone-sensitive cancer). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people with premature ovarian insufficiency: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the importance of starting hormonal treatment either with HRT or a combined hormonal contraceptive and continuing treatment until at least the age of natural menopause (unless contraindicated) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that the baseline population risk of diseases such as breast cancer and cardiovascular disease increases with age and is very low in people under the age of 40</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that HRT may have a beneficial effect on blood pressure when compared with a combined oral contraceptive</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that both HRT and combined oral contraceptives offer bone protection </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that HRT is not a contraceptive. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give people with premature ovarian insufficiency and contraindications to hormonal treatments advice, including on bone and cardiovascular health, and on symptom management. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_7_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring people with premature ovarian insufficiency to healthcare professionals with the relevant experience to help them manage all aspects of physical and psychosocial health related to their condition. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

    <h3 class="title" id="starting-and-stopping-hormone-replacement-therapy-for-anyone">1.8 Starting and stopping hormone replacement therapy for anyone</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Starting HRT" id="ng23-starting-hrt">
      <h4 class="title" id="starting-hrt">Starting HRT</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who wish to take hormone replacement therapy (HRT) for symptoms associated with menopause:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>offer <a class="link" href="recommendations#combined-hrt-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#combined-hrt-2">combined HRT</a> to people with a uterus</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>offer oestrogen-only HRT to people who have had a total hysterectomy. <br /><br />Also see recommendation 1.8.2.<strong> [2024]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people with a condition that may be affected by HRT, consider seeking advice on the choice of HRT from a healthcare professional with specialist knowledge of that condition. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person chooses to take HRT, use the lowest effective dosage. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people with a uterus that vaginal bleeding is a common side effect during the following timeframes and that they will be asked about this during their 3-month review:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>during the first 6 months of taking <a class="link" href="recommendations#systemic-hrt" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#systemic-hrt">systemic HRT</a> or</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>within any 3 months of changing the dose or preparation of systemic HRT.<br /><br />Advise them to seek medical help promptly if they experience unscheduled vaginal bleeding beyond these timeframes. See the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12/chapter/Recommendations-organised-by-site-of-cancer#endometrial-cancer" target="_top">section on endometrial cancer in NICE's guideline on suspected cancer</a>. <strong>[2015, amended 2026]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>As noted in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng12/chapter/Recommendations-organised-by-site-of-cancer#unscheduled-bleeding-and-hrt" target="_top">section on unscheduled bleeding and HRT in the suspected cancer guideline</a>, there is limited evidence for people who experience unscheduled vaginal bleeding while on sequential or continuous HRT. The British Menopause Society has published guidance on unscheduled bleeding on HRT (<a class="link" href="https://thebms.org.uk/publications/bms-guidelines/management-of-unscheduled-bleeding-on-hormone-replacement-therapy-hrt/" target="_top">British Menopause Society: Management of unscheduled bleeding on HRT</a>). <strong>[2026]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Stopping HRT" id="ng23-stopping-hrt">
      <h4 class="title" id="stopping-hrt">Stopping HRT</h4>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer people who are stopping HRT a choice of gradually reducing or immediately stopping treatment. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain to people that:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>gradually reducing HRT may limit recurrence of symptoms in the short term</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>gradually reducing or immediately stopping HRT makes no difference to their symptoms in the longer term. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng23-1_8_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Stop systemic HRT in people who are diagnosed with breast cancer in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng101/chapter/Recommendations#menopausal-symptoms" target="_top">recommendations on menopause symptoms in NICE's guideline on early and locally advanced breast cancer</a>. <strong>[2024]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2024 recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#starting-and-stopping-hrt-for-anyone" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#starting-and-stopping-hrt-for-anyone">rationale and impact section on starting and stopping HRT for anyone</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in:</p>
        <div class="itemizedlist">
          <ul class="itemizedlist">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23/evidence/d-breast-cancer-pdf-13553205091" target="_top">evidence review D: breast cancer risk</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng23/evidence/e-endometrial-cancer-pdf-13553205092" target="_top">evidence review E: endometrial cancer risk</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.9 Reviewing treatment for anyone" id="ng23-reviewing-treatment-for-anyone">
    <h3 class="title" id="reviewing-treatment-for-anyone">1.9 Reviewing treatment for anyone</h3>
    <article id="ng23-1_9_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Discuss with people the importance of keeping up to date with nationally recommended health screening. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_9_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review each treatment for symptoms associated with menopause:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>at 3 months to assess efficacy and tolerability</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>annually thereafter, unless there are clinical indications for an earlier review (such as treatment ineffectiveness, side effects or adverse events). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng23-1_9_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.9.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer people to a <a class="link" href="recommendations#healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause">healthcare professional with expertise in menopause</a> if treatments do not improve their menopause-associated symptoms or they have ongoing side effects. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng23-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline. For other definitions, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/glossary" target="_top">NICE glossary</a> and the <a class="link" href="http://www.thinklocalactpersonal.org.uk/Browse/Informationandadvice/CareandSupportJargonBuster/" target="_top">Think Local, Act Personal Care and Support Jargon Buster</a>.</p>
    <div class="section" title="Combined HRT" id="ng23-combined-hrt-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="combined-hrt-2">Combined HRT</h4>
      <p>Hormone replacement therapy (HRT) with oestrogen and progestogen.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Continuous combined HRT" id="ng23-continuous-combined-hrt">
      <h4 class="title" id="continuous-combined-hrt">Continuous combined HRT</h4>
      <p>HRT in which oestrogen and progestogen are taken together, daily. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Depressive symptoms" id="ng23-depressive-symptoms-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="depressive-symptoms-2">Depressive symptoms</h4>
      <p>For the purposes of this guideline, depressive symptoms are any symptoms included in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222/chapter/Recommendations#depression" target="_top">ICD-11 and DSM-5 criteria for the diagnosis of depression, as reproduced in NICE's guideline on managing and treating depression in adults</a>, but the extent, duration and number of which does not lead to a diagnosis of:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>depression, as defined by ICD-11 or DSM-5 <strong>or</strong></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>chronic depressive symptoms, as defined in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng222">NICE's guideline on managing and treating depression in adults</a>. </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause" id="ng23-genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="genitourinary-symptoms-associated-with-menopause-2">Genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause</h4>
      <p>Genitourinary symptoms associated with menopause include vulvovaginal dryness, pain with sex, vulvovaginal discomfort or irritation, and discomfort or pain when urinating. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Healthcare professional with expertise in menopause" id="ng23-healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause">
      <h4 class="title" id="healthcare-professional-with-expertise-in-menopause">Healthcare professional with expertise in menopause</h4>
      <p>Healthcare professionals with specialist knowledge, skills and training who can (in collaboration with the relevant specialists) manage, or advise colleagues in managing, complex menopause-related needs and risk factors affecting decision making, including: </p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>complex medical problems that potentially affect use of treatments for menopause associated symptoms</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>menopause associated symptoms for those at elevated risk of breast or ovarian cancer, or with a personal history of hormone dependent cancer. </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <p>Their training should be recognised by a professional body such as the <a class="link" href="https://thebms.org.uk/menopause-specialists/overview/#:~:text=A%20BMS%20menopause%20specialist%20is,Care%20Professional%20Diploma%20(MCPD)%3B" target="_top">British Menopause Society</a>, the <a class="link" href="https://www.fsrh.org/education-and-training/menopause-care-professional-diploma-mcpd/" target="_top">Faculty of Sexual and Reproductive Healthcare</a> or the <a class="link" href="https://www.rcog.org.uk/" target="_top">Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists</a>. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Sequential combined HRT" id="ng23-sequential-combined-hrt">
      <h4 class="title" id="sequential-combined-hrt">Sequential combined HRT</h4>
      <p>Sometimes also referred to as combined cyclical HRT. A form of HRT in which oestrogen is taken every day, and the progestogen is taken for usually half of the month. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Systemic HRT" id="ng23-systemic-hrt">
      <h4 class="title" id="systemic-hrt">Systemic HRT</h4>
      <p>HRT in which the hormones are absorbed into the bloodstream and have an effect throughout the body. As part of systemic HRT:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>oestrogen can be taken orally, or transdermally, as a patch, gel or spray that delivers the hormone through the skin </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>progestogen can be taken orally, transdermally as a patch, or be delivered through an intrauterine system.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
