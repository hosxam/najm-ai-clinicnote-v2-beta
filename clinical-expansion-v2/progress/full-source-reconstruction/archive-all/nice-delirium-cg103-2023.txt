<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg103-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/public-involvement/your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Think delirium" id="cg103-think-delirium">
    <h3 class="title" id="think-delirium">1.1 Think delirium</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that people in hospital or <a class="link" href="recommendations#long-term-care" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#long-term-care">long-term care</a> may be at risk of delirium. This can have serious consequences (such as increased risk of dementia and/or death) and, for people in hospital, may increase their length of stay in hospital and their risk of new admission to long-term care. For recommendations on managing delirium in palliative care, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng31" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng31/">NICE guideline on care of dying adults in the last days of life</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Risk factor assessment" id="cg103-risk-factor-assessment">
    <h3 class="title" id="risk-factor-assessment">1.2 Risk factor assessment</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When people first present to hospital or <a class="link" href="recommendations#long-term-care" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#long-term-care">long-term care</a>, assess them for the following risk factors. If any of these risk factors are present, the person is at risk of delirium.</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Age 65 years or older.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Cognitive impairment (past or present) and/or dementia (for guidance on diagnosing dementia, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng97/chapter/Recommendations#diagnosis" target="_top">section on diagnosis in the NICE guideline on dementia</a>). If cognitive impairment is suspected, confirm it using a standardised and validated cognitive impairment measure. </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Current hip fracture.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Severe illness (a clinical condition that is deteriorating or is at risk of deterioration; for further information on recognising and responding to acute illness in adults in hospital, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg50" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg50">NICE guideline on acutely ill adults in hospital</a>). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Observe people at every opportunity for any changes in the risk factors for delirium. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Indicators of delirium: at presentation" id="cg103-indicators-of-delirium-at-presentation">
    <h3 class="title" id="indicators-of-delirium-at-presentation">1.3 Indicators of delirium: at presentation</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>At presentation, assess people at risk for recent (within hours or days) changes or fluctuations that may indicate delirium. These may be reported by the person at risk, or a carer or relative. These changes may affect:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>cognitive function: for example, worsened concentration, slow responses, confusion</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>perception: for example, visual or auditory hallucinations</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>physical function: for example, reduced mobility, reduced movement, restlessness, agitation, changes in appetite, sleep disturbance</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>social behaviour: for example, difficulty engaging with or following requests, withdrawal, or alterations in communication, mood and/or attitude.<br /><br />If any of these changes are present, the person should have an assessment (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessment-and-diagnosis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessment-and-diagnosis">recommendation 1.6.1</a>). <strong>[2010, amended 2023]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be particularly vigilant for changes that may indicate <a class="link" href="recommendations#hypoactive-delirium" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#hypoactive-delirium">hypoactive delirium</a>, which are often missed, such as withdrawal, slow responses, reduced mobility and movement, worsened concentration and reduced appetite. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Preventing delirium" id="cg103-preventing-delirium">
    <h3 class="title" id="preventing-delirium">1.4 Preventing delirium</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that people at risk of delirium are cared for by a team of healthcare professionals who are familiar to the person at risk. Avoid moving people within and between wards or rooms unless absolutely necessary. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Give a tailored multicomponent intervention package:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>within 24 hours of admission, assess people at risk for clinical factors contributing to delirium</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>based on the results of this assessment, provide a multicomponent intervention tailored to the person's individual needs and care setting as described in recommendations 1.4.4 to 1.4.13. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>The tailored multicomponent intervention package should be delivered by a multidisciplinary team trained and competent in delirium prevention. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address cognitive impairment and/or disorientation by: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>providing appropriate lighting and clear signage; a clock (consider providing a 24-hour clock in critical care) and a calendar should also be easily visible to the person at risk</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>talking to the person to reorientate them by explaining where they are, who they are and what your role is</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>introducing cognitively stimulating activities (for example, reminiscence)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>facilitating regular visits from family and friends. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address dehydration and/or constipation by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>ensuring adequate fluid intake to prevent dehydration by encouraging the person to drink; consider offering subcutaneous or intravenous fluids if necessary</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>taking advice if necessary, when managing fluid balance in people with comorbidities (for example, heart failure or chronic kidney disease). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Assess for hypoxia and optimise oxygen saturation if necessary, as clinically appropriate.<br /><br />Be aware that some pulse oximeters can underestimate or overestimate oxygen saturation levels, especially if the saturation level is borderline. Overestimation has been reported in people with dark skin. See also the <a class="link" href="https://www.england.nhs.uk/publication/patient-safety-alert-risk-of-harm-from-inappropriate-placement-of-pulse-oximeter-probes/" target="_top">NHS England Patient Safety Alert on the risk of harm from inappropriate placement of pulse oximeter probes</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address infection by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>looking for and treating infection</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>avoiding unnecessary catheterisation</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>implementing infection control procedures in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg139" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg139">NICE guideline on healthcare-associated infections</a>. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address immobility or limited mobility through the following actions:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Encourage people to:</p>
            <p>
              <div class="itemizedlist">
                <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>mobilise soon after surgery </p>
                  </li>
                  <li class="listitem">
                    <p>walk (provide appropriate walking aids if needed; these should be accessible at all times).</p>
                  </li>
                </ul>
              </div>
            </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Encourage all people, including those unable to walk, to carry out active range-of-motion exercises. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address pain by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>assessing for pain</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>looking for non-verbal signs of pain, particularly in those with communication difficulties (for example, people with learning difficulties or dementia, or people on a ventilator or who have a tracheostomy)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>starting and reviewing appropriate pain management in any person in whom pain is identified or suspected. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Carry out a medication review for people taking multiple drugs, taking into account both the type and number of medications. For information on medicines optimisation see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5">NICE guideline on medicines optimisation</a>.<strong> [2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address poor nutrition by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>following the advice given on nutrition in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg32">NICE guideline on nutrition support for adults</a></p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>if people have dentures, ensuring they fit properly. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Address sensory impairment by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>resolving any reversible cause of the impairment, such as impacted ear wax</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>ensuring hearing and visual aids are available to and used by people who need them, and that they are in good working order. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_4_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.13</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Promote good sleep patterns and sleep hygiene by:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>avoiding nursing or medical procedures during sleeping hours, if possible</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>scheduling medication rounds to avoid disturbing sleep</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>reducing noise to a minimum during sleep periods. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Indicators of delirium: daily observations" id="cg103-indicators-of-delirium-daily-observations">
    <h3 class="title" id="indicators-of-delirium-daily-observations">1.5 Indicators of delirium: daily observations</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Observe, at least daily, all people in hospital or <a class="link" href="recommendations#long-term-care" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#long-term-care">long-term care</a> for recent (within hours or days) changes or fluctuations indicating delirium (for example, see <a class="link" href="recommendations#indicators-of-delirium-at-presentation" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#indicators-of-delirium-at-presentation">recommendation 1.3.1</a>). These may be reported by the person at risk, or a carer or relative. If any of these changes are present the person should have an assessment using an appropriate tool (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#assessment-and-diagnosis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#assessment-and-diagnosis">recommendation 1.6.1</a>). <strong>[2010, amended 2023]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that any changes that may indicate delirium are documented in the person's record or notes. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#indicators-of-delirium-daily-observations-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#indicators-of-delirium-daily-observations-2">rationale and impact section on indicators of delirium: daily observations</a>. </p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg103/evidence/a-diagnostic-accuracy-of-tests-to-identify-delirium-pdf-11320715486" target="_top">evidence review A: diagnostic accuracy of tests to identify delirium</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Assessment and diagnosis" id="cg103-assessment-and-diagnosis">
    <h3 class="title" id="assessment-and-diagnosis">1.6 Assessment and diagnosis</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If indicators of delirium are identified, a <a class="link" href="recommendations#health-or-social-care-practitioners" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#health-or-social-care-practitioners">health or social care practitioner</a> who is competent to do so should carry out an assessment using the 4AT. In critical care or in the recovery room after surgery use the Confusion Assessment Method for the Intensive Care Unit (CAM-ICU) or Intensive Care Delirium Screening Checklist (ICDSC) instead of the 4AT. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If the assessment described in recommendation 1.6.1 indicates delirium, a <a class="link" href="recommendations#healthcare-professionals" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#healthcare-professionals">healthcare professional</a> with the relevant expertise should make the final diagnosis. This could be the same person who made the assessment. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If there is difficulty distinguishing between the diagnoses of delirium, dementia or delirium superimposed on dementia, manage the delirium first. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that the diagnosis of delirium is documented both in the person's record or notes, and in their primary care health record. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2023 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#assessment-and-diagnosis-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#assessment-and-diagnosis-2">rationale and impact section on assessment and diagnosis</a>. </p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg103/evidence/a-diagnostic-accuracy-of-tests-to-identify-delirium-pdf-11320715486" target="_top">evidence review A: diagnostic accuracy of tests to identify delirium</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Treating delirium" id="cg103-treating-delirium">
    <h3 class="title" id="treating-delirium">1.7 Treating delirium</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Initial management" id="cg103-initial-management">
      <h4 class="title" id="initial-management">Initial management</h4>
      <article id="cg103-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In people diagnosed with delirium, identify and manage the possible underlying cause or combination of causes. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg103-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure effective communication and reorientation (for example explaining where the person is, who they are and what your role is) and provide reassurance for people diagnosed with delirium. Consider involving family, friends and carers to help with this. Provide a suitable care environment (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#preventing-delirium" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#preventing-delirium">recommendation 1.4.1 in the section on preventing delirium</a>). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Distressed people" id="cg103-distressed-people">
      <h4 class="title" id="distressed-people">Distressed people</h4>
      <article id="cg103-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person with delirium is distressed or considered a risk to themselves or others, first use verbal and non-verbal techniques to de-escalate the situation. For more information on de-escalation techniques, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng10" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng10">NICE guideline on violence and aggression</a>. Distress may be less evident in people with <a class="link" href="recommendations#hypoactive-delirium" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#hypoactive-delirium">hypoactive delirium</a>, who can still become distressed by, for example, psychotic symptoms. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg103-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person with delirium is distressed or considered a risk to themselves or others, and verbal and non-verbal de-escalation techniques are ineffective or inappropriate, consider giving short-term haloperidol (usually for 1 week or less). Start at the lowest clinically appropriate dose and titrate cautiously according to symptoms. Take into account the <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/haloperidol-haldol-reminder-of-risks-when-used-in-elderly-patients-for-the-acute-treatment-of-delirium" target="_top">Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency's advice about the risks of using haloperidol for the acute treatment of delirium in older people</a>, including the risks of cardiac and neurological side effects (especially in people living with Parkinson's disease or dementia with Lewy bodies). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="If delirium does not resolve" id="cg103-if-delirium-does-not-resolve">
      <h4 class="title" id="if-delirium-does-not-resolve">If delirium does not resolve</h4>
      <article id="cg103-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people in whom delirium does not resolve:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>re-evaluate for underlying causes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>follow up and assess for possible dementia (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng97" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG97">NICE guideline on dementia</a>). <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Information and support" id="cg103-information-and-support">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support">1.8 Information and support</h3>
    <article id="cg103-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer information to people who are at risk of delirium or who have delirium, and their family and/or carers, which: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>informs them that delirium is common and usually temporary </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>describes people's experience of delirium</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>encourages people at risk and their families and/or carers to tell their healthcare team about any sudden changes or fluctuations in behaviour</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>encourages the person who has had delirium to share their experience of delirium with the healthcare professional during recovery</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>advises the person of any support groups. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg103-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Ensure that information provided meets the cultural, cognitive and language needs of the person. <strong>[2010]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="cg103-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <p>This section defines terms that have been used in a particular way for this guideline. </p>
    <div class="section" title="Health or social care practitioners" id="cg103-health-or-social-care-practitioners">
      <h4 class="title" id="health-or-social-care-practitioners">Health or social care practitioners</h4>
      <p>Health and social care staff across the wider care team. This could include registered nurses and care workers (also called care assistants or support workers), social workers, therapists, case managers, GPs, lead clinicians, community nurses and allied professionals, such as physiotherapists, occupational therapists and dietitians. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Healthcare professionals" id="cg103-healthcare-professionals">
      <h4 class="title" id="healthcare-professionals">Healthcare professionals</h4>
      <p>Health professionals regulated or licensed with a professional body to provide care and support, for example, generalist and specialist doctors registered with the General Medical Council, and nurses registered with the Nursing and Midwifery Council.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hyperactive delirium" id="cg103-hyperactive-delirium">
      <h4 class="title" id="hyperactive-delirium">Hyperactive delirium</h4>
      <p>A subtype of delirium characterised by people who have heightened arousal and can be restless, agitated or aggressive.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hypoactive delirium" id="cg103-hypoactive-delirium">
      <h4 class="title" id="hypoactive-delirium">Hypoactive delirium</h4>
      <p>A subtype of delirium characterised by people who become withdrawn, quiet and sleepy.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Long-term care" id="cg103-long-term-care">
      <h4 class="title" id="long-term-care">Long-term care</h4>
      <p>Residential care in a home that may include skilled nursing care and help with everyday activities. This includes nursing homes and residential homes.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
