<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng118-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Diagnostic imaging" id="ng118-diagnostic-imaging">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnostic-imaging">1.1 Diagnostic imaging</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer urgent (within 24 hours of presentation) low-dose non-contrast CT to adults with suspected renal colic. If a woman is pregnant, offer ultrasound instead of CT.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer urgent (within 24 hours of presentation) ultrasound as first-line imaging for <a class="link" href="recommendations#children-and-young-people" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#children-and-young-people">children and young people</a> with suspected renal colic.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>If there is still uncertainty about the diagnosis of renal colic after ultrasound for children and young people, consider low-dose non-contrast CT.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#diagnostic-imaging-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#diagnostic-imaging-2">rationale and impact section on diagnostic imaging</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/b-imaging-for-diagnosis-pdf-6653382735" target="_top">evidence review B: imaging for diagnosis</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Pain management" id="ng118-pain-management">
    <h3 class="title" id="pain-management">1.2 Pain management</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) by any route as first-line treatment for adults, children and young people with suspected renal colic.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer intravenous paracetamol to adults, children and young people with suspected renal colic if NSAIDs are contraindicated or are not giving sufficient pain relief.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider opioids for adults, children and young people with suspected renal colic if both NSAIDs and intravenous paracetamol are contraindicated or are not giving sufficient pain relief.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer antispasmodics to adults, children and young people with suspected renal colic.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#pain-management-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#pain-management-2">rationale and impact section on pain management</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/e-pain-management-pdf-6653382738" target="_top">evidence review E: pain management</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Medical expulsive therapy" id="ng118-medical-expulsive-therapy">
    <h3 class="title" id="medical-expulsive-therapy">1.3 Medical expulsive therapy</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider alpha blockers for adults, children and young people with distal ureteric stones less than 10 mm.<br /><br />In January 2019, this was an off-label use of alpha blockers. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#medical-expulsive-therapy-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#medical-expulsive-therapy-2">rationale and impact section on medical expulsive therapy</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/d-medical-expulsive-therapy-pdf-6653382737" target="_top">evidence review D: medical expulsive therapy</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Stenting before shockwave lithotripsy" id="ng118-stenting-before-shockwave-lithotripsy">
    <h3 class="title" id="stenting-before-shockwave-lithotripsy">1.4 Stenting before shockwave lithotripsy</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer pre-treatment stenting to adults having shockwave lithotripsy (SWL) for ureteric or renal stones.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider pre-treatment stenting for children and young people having SWL for renal staghorn stones.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#stenting-before-shockwave-lithotripsy-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#stenting-before-shockwave-lithotripsy-2">rationale and impact section on stenting before shockwave lithotripsy</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/h-stents-before-surgery-pdf-6653382741" target="_top">evidence review H: stents before surgery</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Surgical treatments (including shockwave lithotripsy)" id="ng118-surgical-treatments-including-shockwave-lithotripsy">
    <h3 class="title" id="surgical-treatments-including-shockwave-lithotripsy">1.5 Surgical treatments (including shockwave lithotripsy)</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Renal stones" id="ng118-renal-stones">
      <h4 class="title" id="renal-stones">Renal stones</h4>
      <article id="ng118-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider watchful waiting for asymptomatic renal stones in adults, children and young people if:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the stone is less than 5 mm <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the stone is larger than 5 mm and the person (or their family or carers, as appropriate) agrees to watchful waiting after an informed discussion of the possible risks and benefits.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng118-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the recommendations in table 1 for surgical treatment (including SWL) of renal stones in adults, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="informaltable">
        <table frame="all" border="1" id="ID0E4F">
          <caption>
            <strong>Table 1 Surgical treatment (including SWL) of renal stones in adults, children and young people</strong>
          </caption>
          <thead>
            <tr>
              <th scope="col">Stone type and size</th>
              <th scope="col">Treatment for adults (16 years and over)</th>
              <th scope="col">Treatment for children and young people (under 16 years)</th>
            </tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td>
                <p>Renal stone less than 10 mm</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Offer SWL</p>
                <p>Consider URS:</p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>if there are contraindications for SWL <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>if a previous course of SWL has failed <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>because of anatomical reasons, SWL is not indicated</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
                <p>Consider PCNL if SWL and URS have failed to treat the current stone or they are not an option</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Consider URS or SWL</p>
                <p>Consider PCNL if:</p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>URS or SWL have failed <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>for anatomical reasons, PCNL is the more favourable option</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td>
                <p>Renal stone 10 to 20 mm</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Consider URS or SWL</p>
                <p>Consider PCNL if URS or SWL have failed</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Consider URS, SWL or PCNL</p>
              </td>
            </tr>
            <tr>
              <td>
                <p>Renal stone larger than 20 mm, including staghorn stones</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Offer PCNL</p>
                <p>Consider URS if PCNL is not an option</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Consider URS, SWL or PCNL</p>
              </td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>
        </table>
      </div>
      <p>Abbreviations: PCNL, percutaneous nephrolithotomy; SWL, shockwave lithotripsy; URS, ureteroscopy.</p>
      <p>Use clinical judgement when considering mini or standard PCNL for children and young people under 16 years with renal stones greater than 10 mm, including staghorn stones.</p>
      <p>Use clinical judgement when considering tubeless, mini or standard PCNL, and supine and prone positions for adults (16 years and over) with renal stones greater than 20 mm, including staghorn stones.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Ureteric stones" id="ng118-ureteric-stones">
      <h4 class="title" id="ureteric-stones">Ureteric stones</h4>
      <article id="ng118-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the recommendations in table 2 for surgical treatment (including SWL) of ureteric stones in adults, children and young people.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="informaltable">
        <table frame="all" border="1" id="ID0E5AAC">
          <caption>
            <strong>Table 2 Surgical treatment (including SWL) of ureteric stones in adults, children and young people</strong>
          </caption>

            <tr>
              <th scope="col">Stone type and size</th>
              <th scope="col">Treatment for adults (16 years and over)</th>
              <th scope="col">Treatment for children and young people (under 16 years)</th>
            </tr>
          </thead>
          <tbody>
            <tr>
              <td>
                <p>Ureteric stone less than 10 mm</p>
              </td>
              <td>
                <p>Offer SWL</p>
                <p>Consider URS if:</p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>stone clearance is not possible within 4 weeks with SWL <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>there are contraindications for SWL <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>the stone is not targetable with SWL <strong>or</strong></p>

                    <li class="listitem">

              </td>

                <p>Offer URS</p>

                <p>Consider PCNL for impacted proximal stones when URS has failed</p>
              </td>

                <p>Consider URS or SWL</p>
                <p></p>
              </td>
            </tr>
          </tbody>

      </div>
      <p>Abbreviations: PCNL, percutaneous nephrolithotomy; SWL, shockwave lithotripsy; URS, ureteroscopy.</p>

        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#surgical-treatments-including-shockwave-lithotripsy-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#surgical-treatments-including-shockwave-lithotripsy-2">rationale and impact section on surgical treatments (including shockwave lithotripsy)</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/f-surgical-treatments-pdf-6653382739" target="_top">evidence review F: surgical treatments</a>.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Timing of surgical treatment (including SWL) for adults with ureteric stones and renal colic" id="ng118-timing-of-surgical-treatment-including-swl-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-and-renal-colic">
        <h5 class="title" id="timing-of-surgical-treatment-including-swl-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-and-renal-colic">Timing of surgical treatment (including SWL) for adults with ureteric stones and renal colic</h5>
        <article id="ng118-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Offer surgical treatment (including SWL) to adults with ureteric stones and renal colic within 48 hours of diagnosis or readmission, if:</p>
            <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>pain is ongoing and not tolerated <strong>or</strong></p>
              </li>
              <li class="listitem">
                <p>the stone is unlikely to pass.</p>
              </li>
            </ul>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#timing-of-surgical-treatment-for-ureteric-stones" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#timing-of-surgical-treatment-for-ureteric-stones">rationale and impact section on timing of surgical treatment</a>.</p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/g-timing-of-surgery-pdf-6653382740" target="_top">evidence review G: timing of surgery</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Medical expulsive therapy as an adjunct to SWL for adults with ureteric stones less than 10 mm" id="ng118-medical-expulsive-therapy-as-an-adjunct-to-swl-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-less-than-10-mm">
        <h5 class="title" id="medical-expulsive-therapy-as-an-adjunct-to-swl-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-less-than-10-mm">Medical expulsive therapy as an adjunct to SWL for adults with ureteric stones less than 10 mm</h5>
        <article id="ng118-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
          <h6 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h6>
          <div class="recommendation__body">
            <p>Consider alpha blockers as adjunctive therapy for adults having SWL for ureteric stones less than 10 mm.<br /><br />In January 2019, this was an off-label use of alpha blockers. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
          </div>
        </article>
        <div class="panel">
          <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#medical-expulsive-therapy-as-adjunctive-to-shockwave-lithotripsy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#medical-expulsive-therapy-as-adjunctive-to-shockwave-lithotripsy">rationale and impact section on medical expulsive therapy as adjunctive to shockwave lithotripsy</a>. </p>
          <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/d-medical-expulsive-therapy-pdf-6653382737" target="_top">evidence review D: medical expulsive therapy</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Stenting after ureteroscopy for adults with ureteric stones less than 20 mm" id="ng118-stenting-after-ureteroscopy-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-less-than-20-mm">
    <h3 class="title" id="stenting-after-ureteroscopy-for-adults-with-ureteric-stones-less-than-20-mm">1.6 Stenting after ureteroscopy for adults with ureteric stones less than 20 mm</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer post-treatment stenting to adults who have had ureteroscopy for ureteric stones less than 20 mm.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#stenting-after-ureteroscopy" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#stenting-after-ureteroscopy">rationale and impact section on stenting after ureteroscopy</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/i-stents-after-surgery-pdf-239521778388" target="_top">evidence review I: stents after surgery</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Metabolic testing" id="ng118-metabolic-testing">
    <h3 class="title" id="metabolic-testing">1.7 Metabolic testing</h3>
    <article id="ng118-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider stone analysis for adults with ureteric or renal stones.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Measure serum calcium for adults with ureteric or renal stones.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng118-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Consider referring children and young people with ureteric or renal stones to a paediatric nephrologist or paediatric urologist with expertise in this area for assessment and metabolic investigations. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#metabolic-testing-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#metabolic-testing-2">rationale and impact section on metabolic testing</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/a-metabolic-investigations-pdf-6653382734" target="_top">evidence review A: metabolic investigations</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Preventing recurrence" id="ng118-preventing-recurrence">
    <h3 class="title" id="preventing-recurrence">1.8 Preventing recurrence</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Dietary and lifestyle advice" id="ng118-dietary-and-lifestyle-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="dietary-and-lifestyle-advice">Dietary and lifestyle advice</h4>
      <article id="ng118-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss diet and fluid intake with the person (and their family or carers, as appropriate), and advise:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adults to drink 2.5 to 3 litres of water per day, and children and young people (depending on their age) 1 to 2 litres</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adding fresh lemon juice to drinking water</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p> avoiding carbonated drinks</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>adults to have a daily salt intake of no more than 6 g, and children and young people (depending on their age) 2 to 6 g </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>not restricting daily calcium intake, but maintaining a normal calcium intake of 700 to 1,200 mg for adults, and 350 to 1,000 mg per day for children and young people (depending on their age). </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng118-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Follow the recommendations on maintaining a healthy lifestyle in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng246">NICE guideline on overweight and obesity management</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Potassium citrate" id="ng118-potassium-citrate">
      <h4 class="title" id="potassium-citrate">Potassium citrate</h4>
      <p>The following recommendations apply alongside the recommendations on dietary and lifestyle advice.</p>
      <article id="ng118-1_8_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider potassium citrate for adults with a recurrence of stones that are predominantly (more than 50%) calcium oxalate. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng118-1_8_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider potassium citrate for children and young people with a recurrence of stones that are predominantly (more than 50%) calcium oxalate, and with hypercalciuria or hypocitraturia. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Thiazides" id="ng118-thiazides">
      <h4 class="title" id="thiazides">Thiazides</h4>
      <p>The following recommendation applies alongside the recommendations on dietary and lifestyle advice.</p>
      <article id="ng118-1_8_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.8.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider thiazides for adults with a recurrence of stones that are predominantly (more than 50%) calcium oxalate and hypercalciuria, after restricting their sodium intake to no more than 6 g a day.<br /><br />In January 2019, this was an off-label use of thiazides. See <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/making-decisions-using-nice-guidelines#prescribing-medicines" target="_top">NICE's information on prescribing medicines</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#preventing-recurrence-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#preventing-recurrence-2">rationale and impact section on preventing recurrence</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/c-dietary-interventions-pdf-6653382736" target="_top">evidence review C: dietary interventions</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng118/evidence/k-prevention-of-recurrence-pdf-239521778390" target="_top">evidence review K: prevention of recurrence</a>.</p>
      </div>
