<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng141-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Managing cellulitis and erysipelas" id="ng141-managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">
    <h3 class="title" id="managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">1.1 Managing cellulitis and erysipelas</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Treatment" id="ng141-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="treatment">Treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>To ensure that <a class="link" href="recommendations#cellulitis-and-erysipelas" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#cellulitis-and-erysipelas">cellulitis and erysipelas</a> are treated appropriately, exclude other causes of skin redness such as:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>an inflammatory reaction to an immunisation or an insect bite <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a non-infectious cause such as chronic venous insufficiency. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider taking a swab for microbiological testing from people with cellulitis or erysipelas to guide treatment, but only if the skin is broken <strong>and</strong>:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there is a penetrating injury <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>there has been exposure to water-borne organisms <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the infection was acquired outside the UK.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Before treating cellulitis or erysipelas, consider drawing around the extent of the infection with a single-use surgical marker pen to monitor progress. Be aware that redness may be less visible on darker skin tones.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer an antibiotic for people with cellulitis or erysipelas. When choosing an antibiotic (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#choice-of-antibiotic" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#choice-of-antibiotic">recommendations on choice of antibiotic</a>), take account of:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the severity of symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the site of infection (for example, near the eyes or nose)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the risk of uncommon pathogens (for example, from a penetrating injury, after exposure to water-borne organisms, or an infection acquired outside the UK)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>previous microbiological results from a swab</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the person's meticillin-resistant <em>Staphylococcus aureus</em> (MRSA) status if known.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give oral antibiotics first line if the person can take oral medicines, and the severity of their condition does not require intravenous antibiotics.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If intravenous antibiotics are given, review by 48 hours and consider switching to oral antibiotics if possible.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Manage any underlying condition that may predispose to cellulitis or erysipelas, for example:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>diabetes</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>venous insufficiency</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>eczema</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>oedema, which may be an adverse effect of medicines such as calcium channel blockers.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Advice" id="ng141-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="advice">Advice</h4>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When prescribing antibiotics for cellulitis or erysipelas, give advice about:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>possible adverse effects of antibiotics </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the skin taking some time to return to normal after the course of antibiotics has finished</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>seeking medical help if symptoms worsen rapidly or significantly at any time, or do not start to improve within 2 to 3 days.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Reassessment" id="ng141-reassessment">
      <h4 class="title" id="reassessment">Reassessment</h4>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassess people with cellulitis or erysipelas if symptoms worsen rapidly or significantly at any time, do not start to improve within 2 to 3 days, or the person:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>becomes systemically very unwell <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>has severe pain out of proportion to the infection <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>has redness or swelling spreading beyond the initial presentation (taking into account that some initial spreading may occur, and that redness may be less visible on darker skin tones), see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#treatment" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#treatment">recommendation on drawing around the extent of the infection in the section on treatment</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When reassessing people with cellulitis or erysipelas, take account of:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other possible diagnoses, such as an inflammatory reaction to an immunisation or an insect bite, gout, superficial thrombophlebitis, eczema, allergic dermatitis or deep vein thrombosis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any underlying condition that may predispose to cellulitis or erysipelas, such as oedema, diabetes, venous insufficiency or eczema</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any symptoms or signs suggesting a more serious illness or condition, such as lymphangitis, orbital cellulitis, osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, necrotising fasciitis or sepsis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any results from microbiological testing</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any previous antibiotic use, which may have led to resistant bacteria.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider taking a swab for microbiological testing from people with cellulitis or erysipelas if the skin is broken and this has not been done already.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a swab has been sent for microbiological testing:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>review the choice of antibiotic(s) when results are available <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>change the antibiotic(s) according to results if symptoms or signs of the infection are not improving, using a narrow-spectrum antibiotic if possible. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Referral and seeking specialist advice" id="ng141-referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral-and-seeking-specialist-advice">Referral and seeking specialist advice</h4>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people to hospital if they have any symptoms or signs suggesting a more serious illness or condition, such as orbital cellulitis, osteomyelitis, septic arthritis, necrotising fasciitis or sepsis.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng141-1_1_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider referring people with cellulitis or erysipelas to hospital, or seek specialist advice, if they:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are severely unwell <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have infection near the eyes or nose (including periorbital cellulitis) <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>could have uncommon pathogens, for example, after a penetrating injury, exposure to water-borne organisms, or an infection acquired outside the UK <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have spreading infection that is not responding to oral antibiotics <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>lymphangitis <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>cannot take oral antibiotics (exploring locally available options for giving intravenous antibiotics at home or in the community, rather than in hospital, where appropriate).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations, see the <a class="link" href="summary-of-the-evidence#managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="summary-of-the-evidence" data-original-url="#managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas-2">summary of the evidence on managing cellulitis and erysipelas</a>.</p>

    <p>Give oral antibiotics first line if the person can take oral medicines, and the severity of their symptoms does not warrant intravenous antibiotics. If intravenous antibiotics are given, review by 48 hours and consider switching to oral antibiotics, if possible.</p>
    <p>A longer course length (up to 14 days in total) may be needed based on clinical assessment. However, skin does take some time to return to normal, and full resolution of symptoms at 5 to 7 days is not expected.</p>

  <div class="section" title="1.3 Preventing recurrent cellulitis or erysipelas" id="ng141-preventing-recurrent-cellulitis-or-erysipelas">
    <h3 class="title" id="preventing-recurrent-cellulitis-or-erysipelas">1.3 Preventing recurrent cellulitis or erysipelas</h3>
    <article id="ng141-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer antibiotic prophylaxis to prevent recurrent cellulitis or erysipelas. Give advice about seeking medical help if symptoms of cellulitis or erysipelas develop.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng141-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>For adults who have had treatment in hospital, or under specialist advice, for at least 2 separate episodes of cellulitis or erysipelas in the previous 12 months, specialists may consider a trial of antibiotic prophylaxis. Involve the person in a shared decision by discussing and taking account of:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the severity and frequency of previous symptoms</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the risk of developing complications</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>underlying conditions (such as oedema, diabetes or venous insufficiency) and their management</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the risk of resistance with long-term antibiotic use</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>the person's preference for antibiotic use.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng141-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When choosing an antibiotic for prophylaxis (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#choice-of-antibiotic-prophylaxis" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#choice-of-antibiotic-prophylaxis">recommendations on choice of antibiotic prophylaxis</a>), take account of any previous microbiological results and previous antibiotic use.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng141-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>When antibiotic prophylaxis is given, give advice about:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>possible adverse effects of long-term antibiotics</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>returning for review within 6 months</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>seeking medical help if symptoms of cellulitis or erysipelas recur.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng141-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review antibiotic prophylaxis for recurrent cellulitis or erysipelas at least every 6 months. The review should include:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>assessing the success of prophylaxis</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>discussing continuing, stopping or changing prophylaxis (taking into account the person's preferences for antibiotic use and the risk of antimicrobial resistance).<br /><br />Stop or change the prophylactic antibiotic to an alternative if cellulitis or erysipelas recurs (see <a class="link" href="recommendations#managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#managing-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">recommendation 1.1.4 in the section on treatment</a> for treatment of acute infection).</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="panel">
      <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these recommendations, see the <a class="link" href="summary-of-the-evidence#antibiotic-prophylaxis-for-the-prevention-of-recurrent-cellulitis-and-erysipelas" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="summary-of-the-evidence" data-original-url="#antibiotic-prophylaxis-for-the-prevention-of-recurrent-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">summary of the evidence on antibiotic prophylaxis for the prevention of recurrent cellulitis and erysipelas</a>.</p>
      <p>Full details of the evidence are in the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG141/evidence" target="_top">evidence review</a>.</p>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="section" title="Terms used in the guideline" id="ng141-terms-used-in-the-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-the-guideline">Terms used in the guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Ambulatory care" id="ng141-ambulatory-care">
      <h4 class="title" id="ambulatory-care">Ambulatory care</h4>
      <p>Clinical care that may include diagnosis, observation, treatment and rehabilitation not provided within the traditional hospital bed base or within the traditional outpatient services that can be provided across primary/secondary care.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Cellulitis and erysipelas" id="ng141-cellulitis-and-erysipelas">
      <h4 class="title" id="cellulitis-and-erysipelas">Cellulitis and erysipelas</h4>
      <p>Infections of the tissues under the skin (subcutaneous), which usually result from contamination of a break in the skin. Both conditions are characterised by acute localised inflammation and oedema, with lesions more superficial in erysipelas with a well-defined, raised margin (<a class="link" href="https://digicollections.net/medicinedocs/#d/h2918e" target="_top">World Health Organization, WHO model prescribing information: drugs used in skin diseases, bacterial infections, staphylococcal and streptococcal infections</a>).</p>
