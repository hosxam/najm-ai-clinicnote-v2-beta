<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng19-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off‑label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Care within 24 hours of a person with diabetic foot problems being admitted to hospital, or the detection of diabetic foot problems (if the person is already in hospital)" id="ng19-care-within-24-hours-of-a-person-with-diabetic-foot-problems-being-admitted-to-hospital-or-the">
    <h3 class="title" id="care-within-24-hours-of-a-person-with-diabetic-foot-problems-being-admitted-to-hospital-or-the">1.1 Care within 24 hours of a person with diabetic foot problems being admitted to hospital, or the detection of diabetic foot problems (if the person is already in hospital)</h3>
    <article id="ng19-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Each hospital should have a care pathway for people with diabetic foot problems who need inpatient care. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>A named consultant should be accountable for the overall care of the person, and for ensuring that healthcare professionals provide timely care. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Refer the person to the multidisciplinary foot care service within 24 hours of the initial examination of the person's feet. Transfer the responsibility of care to a consultant member of the multidisciplinary foot care service if a diabetic foot problem is the dominant clinical factor for inpatient care. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>The named consultant and the healthcare professionals from the existing team should remain accountable for the care of the person unless their care is transferred to the multidisciplinary foot care service. <strong>[2011]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Care across all settings" id="ng19-care-across-all-settings">
    <h3 class="title" id="care-across-all-settings">1.2 Care across all settings</h3>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Commissioners and service providers should ensure that the following are in place: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>A foot protection service for preventing diabetic foot problems, and for treating and managing diabetic foot problems in the community.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>A multidisciplinary foot care service for managing diabetic foot problems in hospital and in the community that cannot be managed by the foot protection service. This may also be known as an interdisciplinary foot care service.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Robust protocols and clear local pathways for the continued and integrated care of people across all settings including emergency care and general practice. The protocols should set out the relationship between the foot protection service and the multidisciplinary foot care service.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Regular reviews of treatment and patient outcomes, in line with the <a class="link" href="https://digital.nhs.uk/data-and-information/clinical-audits-and-registries/national-diabetes-foot-care-audit" target="_top">National Diabetes Foot Care Audit</a>. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>The foot protection service should be led by a podiatrist with specialist training in diabetic foot problems, and should have access to healthcare professionals with skills in the following areas:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Diabetology.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Biomechanics and orthoses.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Wound care. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>The multidisciplinary foot care service should be led by a named healthcare professional, and consist of specialists with skills in the following areas:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Diabetology.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Podiatry.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Diabetes specialist nursing.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Vascular surgery.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Microbiology.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Orthopaedic surgery.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Biomechanics and orthoses.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Interventional radiology.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Casting.</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Wound care. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>The multidisciplinary foot care service should have access to rehabilitation services, plastic surgery, psychological services and nutritional services.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Healthcare professionals may need to discuss, agree and make special arrangements for disabled people and people who are housebound or living in care settings, to ensure equality of access to foot care assessments and treatments for people with diabetes.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng19-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Take into account any disabilities, including visual impairment, when planning and delivering care for people with diabetes.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Assessing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem" id="ng19-assessing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">
    <h3 class="title" id="assessing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">1.3 Assessing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Frequency of assessments" id="ng19-frequency-of-assessments">
      <h4 class="title" id="frequency-of-assessments">Frequency of assessments</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For children with diabetes who are under 12 years, give them, and their family members or carers (as appropriate), basic foot care advice. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For young people with diabetes who are 12 to 17 years, the paediatric care team or the transitional care team should assess the young person's feet as part of their annual assessment, and provide information about foot care. If a diabetic foot problem is found or suspected, the paediatric care team or the transitional care team should refer the young person to an appropriate specialist. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For adults with diabetes, assess their risk of developing a diabetic foot problem at the following times:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>When diabetes is diagnosed, and at least annually thereafter (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">recommendation on carrying out reassessments at intervals, depending on the person's risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</a>).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>If any foot problems arise.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>On any admission to hospital, and if there is any change in their status while they are in hospital. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Assessing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem" id="ng19-assessing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem-2">Assessing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When examining the feet of a person with diabetes, remove their shoes, socks, bandages and dressings, and examine both feet for evidence of the following risk factors:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>neuropathy (use a 10 g monofilament as part of a foot sensory examination)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>limb ischaemia (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147">NICE guideline on peripheral arterial disease</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ulceration</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>callus</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>infection and/or inflammation</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>deformity</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>gangrene</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Charcot arthropathy. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use ankle brachial pressure index in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147">NICE guideline on peripheral arterial disease</a>. Interpret results carefully in people with diabetes because calcified arteries may falsely elevate results.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the person's current risk of developing a diabetic foot problem or needing an amputation using the following risk stratification:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Low risk:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>no risk factors present except callus alone.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Moderate risk:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>deformity <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>neuropathy <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>peripheral arterial disease.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>High risk:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>previous ulceration <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>previous amputation <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>on renal replacement therapy <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>neuropathy and peripheral arterial disease together <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>neuropathy in combination with callus and/or deformity <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>peripheral arterial disease in combination with callus and/or deformity.</p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Active diabetic foot problem:</p>
              <p>
                <div class="itemizedlist">
                  <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>ulceration <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>infection <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>chronic limb-threatening ischaemia <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>gangrene <strong>or</strong></p>
                    </li>
                    <li class="listitem">
                      <p>suspicion of an acute Charcot arthropathy, or an unexplained hot, swollen foot with a change in colour, with or without pain. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
                    </li>
                  </ul>
                </div>
              </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee did not change the recommendations that were reviewed in 2023, and how this might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#assessing-the-risk-of-a-diabetic-foot-problem" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#assessing-the-risk-of-a-diabetic-foot-problem">rationale and impact section on assessing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/b-risk-assessment-models-and-tools-for-predicting-the-development-of-diabetic-foot-problems-and-foot-review-frequency-pdf-6953995119" target="_top">evidence review B: risk assessment models and tools for predicting the development of diabetic foot problems and foot review frequency</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Managing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem" id="ng19-managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">
      <h4 class="title" id="managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">Managing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people who are at low risk of developing a diabetic foot problem:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>continue to carry out foot assessments at their annual diabetes review</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>emphasise the importance of foot care (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">section on patient information about the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>advise them that they could progress to moderate or high risk. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Refer people who are at moderate or high risk of developing a diabetic foot problem to the foot protection service. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>The foot protection service should assess newly referred people as follows:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Within 2 to 4 weeks for people who are at high risk of developing a diabetic foot problem.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Within 6 to 8 weeks for people who are at moderate risk of developing a diabetic foot problem. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people at moderate or high risk of developing a diabetic foot problem, the foot protection service should:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Assess the feet.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Give advice about, and provide, skin and nail care of the feet.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Assess the biomechanical status of the feet, including the need to provide specialist footwear and orthoses.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Assess the vascular status of the lower limbs.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Liaise with other healthcare professionals, for example, the person's GP, about the person's diabetes management and risk of cardiovascular disease. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Depending on the person's risk of developing a diabetic foot problem, carry out reassessments at the following intervals:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Annually for people who are at low risk, as part of their annual diabetes review.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Frequently (for example, every 3 to 6 months) for people who are at moderate risk.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>More frequently (for example, every 1 to 2 months) for people who are at high risk, if there is no immediate concern.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Very frequently (for example, every 1 to 2 weeks) for people who are at high risk, if there is immediate concern.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Consider more frequent reassessments for people who are at moderate or high risk, and for people who are unable to check their own feet. <strong>[2023]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People in hospital who are at moderate or high risk of developing a diabetic foot problem should be given a pressure redistribution device to offload heel pressure. On discharge they should be referred or notified to the foot protection service.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee did not change the recommendations that were reviewed in 2023, and how this might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#managing-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem-2">rationale and impact section on managing the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</a>. </p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/b-risk-assessment-models-and-tools-for-predicting-the-development-of-diabetic-foot-problems-and-foot-review-frequency-pdf-6953995119" target="_top">evidence review B: risk assessment models and tools for predicting the development of diabetic foot problems and foot review frequency</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Patient information about the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem" id="ng19-patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">
      <h4 class="title" id="patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">Patient information about the risk of developing a diabetic foot problem</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide information and clear explanations to people with diabetes and/or their family members or carers (as appropriate) when diabetes is diagnosed, during assessments, and if problems arise. Information should be oral and written, and include the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Basic foot care advice and the importance of foot care.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Foot emergencies and who to contact.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Footwear advice.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>The person's current individual risk of developing a foot problem.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Information about diabetes and the importance of blood glucose control (also see recommendation 1.3.14). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_3_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For guidance on education programmes and information about diabetes, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng17/chapter/Recommendations#education-and-information" target="_top">education and information section in the NICE guideline on type 1 diabetes in adults</a>, the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng28/chapter/Recommendations#education" target="_top">education section in the NICE guideline on type 2 diabetes in adults</a>, and the sections on <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG18/chapter/1-Recommendations#type-1-diabetes" target="_top">education and information for children and young people with type 1 diabetes</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/NG18/chapter/1-Recommendations#type-2-diabetes" target="_top">education and information for children and young people with type 2 diabetes</a> in the NICE guideline on diabetes (type 1 and type 2) in children and young people.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>

  <div class="section" title="1.4 Diabetic foot problems" id="ng19-diabetic-foot-problems">
    <h3 class="title" id="diabetic-foot-problems">1.4 Diabetic foot problems</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Referral" id="ng19-referral">
      <h4 class="title" id="referral">Referral</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person has a limb-threatening or life-threatening diabetic foot problem, refer them immediately to acute services and inform the multidisciplinary foot care service (according to local protocols and pathways; also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#care-across-all-settings" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#care-across-all-settings">recommendation on services and protocols commissioners and service providers should ensure are in place</a>), so they can be assessed and an individualised treatment plan put in place. Examples of limb-threatening and life-threatening diabetic foot problems include the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Ulceration with fever or any signs of sepsis.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Ulceration with limb ischaemia (see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg147">NICE guideline on peripheral arterial disease</a>).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Clinical concern that there is a deep-seated soft tissue or bone infection (with or without ulceration).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Gangrene (with or without ulceration). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For all other active diabetic foot problems, refer the person within 1 working day to the multidisciplinary foot care service or foot protection service (according to local protocols and pathways; also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#care-across-all-settings" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#care-across-all-settings">recommendation on services and protocols commissioners and service providers should ensure are in place</a>) for triage within 1 further working day.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Patient information about diabetic foot problems" id="ng19-patient-information-about-diabetic-foot-problems">
      <h4 class="title" id="patient-information-about-diabetic-foot-problems">Patient information about diabetic foot problems</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide information and clear explanations as part of the individualised treatment plan for people with a diabetic foot problem. Information should be oral and written, and include the following:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>A clear explanation of the person's foot problem.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Pictures of diabetic foot problems.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Care of the other foot and leg.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Foot emergencies and who to contact.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Footwear advice.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Wound care.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Information about diabetes and the importance of blood glucose control (also see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#patient-information-about-the-risk-of-developing-a-diabetic-foot-problem">recommendation on additional guidance on education programmes and information about diabetes</a>). <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person presents with a diabetic foot problem, take into account that they may have an undiagnosed, increased risk of cardiovascular disease that may need further investigation and treatment. For guidance on the primary prevention of cardiovascular disease, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg181">NICE guideline on cardiovascular disease: risk assessment and reduction, including lipid modification</a>.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Diabetic foot ulcer" id="ng19-diabetic-foot-ulcer">
    <h3 class="title" id="diabetic-foot-ulcer">1.5 Diabetic foot ulcer</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Investigation" id="ng19-investigation">
      <h4 class="title" id="investigation">Investigation</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a person has a diabetic foot ulcer, assess and document the size, depth and position of the ulcer.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Use a standardised system to document the severity of the foot ulcer, such as the SINBAD (Site, Ischaemia, Neuropathy, Bacterial Infection, Area and Depth) or the University of Texas classification system.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use the Wagner classification system to assess the severity of a diabetic foot ulcer.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treatment" id="ng19-treatment">
      <h4 class="title" id="treatment">Treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer 1 or more of the following as standard care for treating diabetic foot ulcers:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Offloading.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Control of foot infection.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Control of ischaemia.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Wound debridement.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Wound dressings. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer non‑removable casting to offload plantar neuropathic, non-ischaemic, uninfected forefoot and midfoot diabetic ulcers. Offer an alternative offloading device until casting can be provided.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>In line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg179" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg179">NICE guideline on pressure ulcers</a>, use pressure-redistributing devices and strategies to minimise the risk of pressure ulcers developing.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When treating diabetic foot ulcers, debridement in hospital should only be done by healthcare professionals from the multidisciplinary foot care service, using the technique that best matches their specialist expertise and clinical experience, the site of the diabetic foot ulcer and the person's preference.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When treating diabetic foot ulcers, debridement in the community should only be done by healthcare professionals with the relevant training and skills, continuing the care described in the person's treatment plan.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider negative pressure wound therapy after surgical debridement for diabetic foot ulcers, on the advice of the multidisciplinary foot care service.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When deciding about wound dressings and offloading when treating diabetic foot ulcers, take into account the clinical assessment of the wound and the person's preference, and use devices and dressings with the lowest acquisition cost appropriate to the clinical circumstances.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider dermal or skin substitutes as an adjunct to standard care when treating diabetic foot ulcers, only when healing has not progressed and on the advice of the multidisciplinary foot care service.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer the following to treat diabetic foot ulcers, unless as part of a clinical trial:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Electrical stimulation therapy, autologous platelet-rich plasma gel, regenerative wound matrices and dalteparin.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Growth factors (granulocyte colony-stimulating factor [G‑CSF], platelet-derived growth factor [PDGF], epidermal growth factor [EGF] and transforming growth factor beta [TGF‑β]).</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Hyperbaric oxygen therapy. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When deciding the frequency of follow‑up as part of the treatment plan, take into account the overall health of the person with diabetes, how healing has progressed, and any deterioration.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_5_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that the frequency of monitoring set out in the person's individualised treatment plan is maintained whether the person with diabetes is being treated in hospital or in the community.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Diabetic foot infection" id="ng19-diabetic-foot-infection">
    <h3 class="title" id="diabetic-foot-infection">1.6 Diabetic foot infection</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Investigation" id="ng19-investigation-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="investigation-2">Investigation</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a <a class="link" href="recommendations#diabetic-foot-infection-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#diabetic-foot-infection-2">diabetic foot infection</a> is suspected and a wound is present, send a soft tissue or bone sample from the base of the debrided wound for microbiological examination. If this cannot be obtained, take a deep swab because it may provide useful information on the choice of antibiotic treatment.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider an X‑ray of the person's affected foot (or feet) to determine the extent of the diabetic foot problem.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Think about osteomyelitis if the person with diabetes has a local infection, a deep foot wound or a chronic foot wound.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that osteomyelitis may be present in a person with diabetes despite normal inflammatory markers, X‑rays or probe-to-bone testing.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If osteomyelitis is suspected in a person with diabetes but is not confirmed by initial X‑ray, consider an MRI to confirm the diagnosis. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treatment" id="ng19-treatment-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="treatment-2">Treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Start antibiotic treatment for people with suspected diabetic foot infection as soon as possible. Take samples for microbiological testing before, or as close as possible to, the start of antibiotic treatment. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When choosing an antibiotic for people with a suspected diabetic foot infection (see recommendations 1.6.8 and 1.6.9), take account of: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the severity of diabetic foot infection (<a class="link" href="recommendations#mild-diabetic-foot-infection" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#mild-diabetic-foot-infection">mild</a>, <a class="link" href="recommendations#moderate-diabetic-foot-infection" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#moderate-diabetic-foot-infection">moderate</a> or <a class="link" href="recommendations#severe-diabetic-foot-infection" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#severe-diabetic-foot-infection">severe</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the risk of developing complications</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>previous microbiological results</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>previous antibiotic use</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>patient preferences. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#treatment-4" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#treatment-4">rationale and impact section on treatment</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/a-diabetic-foot-infection-antimicrobial-prescribing-pdf-6953995118" target="_top">evidence review A: diabetic foot infection: antimicrobial prescribing</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Choice of antibiotic" id="ng19-choice-of-antibiotic">
      <h4 class="title" id="choice-of-antibiotic">Choice of antibiotic</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When prescribing antibiotics for a suspected diabetic foot infection in adults aged 18 years and over, follow table 1 for a mild infection or table 2 for a moderate or severe infection. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Seek specialist advice when prescribing antibiotics for a suspected diabetic foot infection in children and young people under 18 years. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give oral antibiotics first line if the person can take oral medicines, and the severity of their condition does not require intravenous antibiotics. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If intravenous antibiotics are given, review by 48 hours and consider switching to oral antibiotics if possible. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Base antibiotic course length on the severity of the infection and a clinical assessment of response to treatment. Review the need for continued antibiotics regularly. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>

      <h4 class="title" id="advice">Advice</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When prescribing antibiotics for a diabetic foot infection, give advice about:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>possible adverse effects of the antibiotic(s)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>seeking medical help if symptoms worsen rapidly or significantly at any time, or do not start to improve within 1 to 2 days. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>

        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/a-diabetic-foot-infection-antimicrobial-prescribing-pdf-6953995118" target="_top">evidence review A: diabetic foot infection: antimicrobial prescribing</a>.</p>

      <h4 class="title" id="reassessment">Reassessment</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When microbiological results are available:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>review the choice of antibiotic <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>change the antibiotic according to results, using a narrow-spectrum antibiotic, if appropriate. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Reassess people with a suspected diabetic foot infection if symptoms worsen rapidly or significantly at any time, do not start to improve within 1 to 2 days, or the person becomes systemically very unwell or has severe pain out of proportion to the infection. Take account of:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>other possible diagnoses, such as pressure sores, gout or non-infected ulcers</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>any symptoms or signs suggesting a more serious illness or condition, such as limb ischaemia, osteomyelitis, necrotising fasciitis or sepsis</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>previous antibiotic use. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made these 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#reassessment-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#reassessment-2">rationale and impact section on reassessment</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/a-diabetic-foot-infection-antimicrobial-prescribing-pdf-6953995118" target="_top">evidence review A: diabetic foot infection: antimicrobial prescribing</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Prevention" id="ng19-prevention">
      <h4 class="title" id="prevention">Prevention</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_6_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.6.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer antibiotics to prevent diabetic foot infections. Give advice about seeking medical help if symptoms of a diabetic foot infection develop. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made this 2019 recommendation and how it might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#prevention-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#prevention-2">rationale and impact section on prevention</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng19/evidence/a-diabetic-foot-infection-antimicrobial-prescribing-pdf-6953995118" target="_top">evidence review A: diabetic foot infection: antimicrobial prescribing</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Charcot arthropathy" id="ng19-charcot-arthropathy">
    <h3 class="title" id="charcot-arthropathy">1.7 Charcot arthropathy</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Investigation" id="ng19-investigation-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="investigation-3">Investigation</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that if a person with diabetes fractures their foot or ankle, it may progress to Charcot arthropathy. <strong>[2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suspect acute Charcot arthropathy if there is redness, warmth, swelling or deformity (in particular, when the skin is intact), especially in the presence of peripheral neuropathy or renal failure. Think about acute Charcot arthropathy even when deformity is not present or pain is not reported.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>To confirm the diagnosis of acute Charcot arthropathy, refer the person within 1 working day to the multidisciplinary foot care service for triage within 1 further working day. Offer non-weight-bearing treatment until definitive treatment can be started by the multidisciplinary foot care service.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If acute Charcot arthropathy is suspected, arrange a weight-bearing X‑ray of the affected foot and ankle. Consider an MRI if the X‑ray is normal but Charcot arthropathy is still suspected.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Treatment" id="ng19-treatment-3">
      <h4 class="title" id="treatment-3">Treatment</h4>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the multidisciplinary foot care service suspects acute Charcot arthropathy, offer treatment with a non-removable offloading device. If a non-removable device is not advisable because of the clinical, or the person's, circumstances, consider treatment with a removable offloading device.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer bisphosphonates to treat acute Charcot arthropathy, unless as part of a clinical trial.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Monitor the treatment of acute Charcot arthropathy using clinical assessment. This should include measuring foot–skin temperature difference and taking serial X‑rays until the acute Charcot arthropathy resolves. Acute Charcot arthropathy is likely to resolve when there is a sustained temperature difference of less than 2 degrees between both feet and when X‑ray changes show no further progression.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng19-1_7_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.7.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>People who have a foot deformity that may be the result of a previous Charcot arthropathy are at high risk of ulceration and should be cared for by the foot protection service.<strong> [2015]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng19-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Diabetic foot infection" id="ng19-diabetic-foot-infection-2">
      <h4 class="title" id="diabetic-foot-infection-2">Diabetic foot infection</h4>
      <p>Diabetic foot infection is defined by the presence of at least 2 of the following:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>local swelling or induration</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>erythema</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>local tenderness or pain</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>local warmth</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>purulent discharge. </p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Mild diabetic foot infection" id="ng19-mild-diabetic-foot-infection">
        <h5 class="title" id="mild-diabetic-foot-infection">Mild diabetic foot infection</h5>
        <p>Local infection involving only the skin and subcutaneous tissue; if erythema, must be 0.5 cm to less than 2 cm around the ulcer (exclude other causes of inflammatory response, such as trauma, gout, acute Charcot neuro-osteoarthropathy, fracture, thrombosis and venous stasis).</p>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Moderate diabetic foot infection" id="ng19-moderate-diabetic-foot-infection">
        <h5 class="title" id="moderate-diabetic-foot-infection">Moderate diabetic foot infection</h5>
        <p>Local infection with erythema more than 2 cm around the ulcer or involving structures deeper than skin and subcutaneous tissues (such as abscess, osteomyelitis, septic arthritis or fasciitis), and no systemic inflammatory response signs.</p>
      </div>
      <div class="section" title="Severe diabetic foot infection" id="ng19-severe-diabetic-foot-infection">
        <h5 class="title" id="severe-diabetic-foot-infection">Severe diabetic foot infection</h5>
        <p>Local infection with signs of systemic inflammatory response (such as temperature of more than 38°C or less than 36°C, increased heart rate or increased respiratory rate).</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Diabetic foot problem" id="ng19-diabetic-foot-problem">
      <h4 class="title" id="diabetic-foot-problem">Diabetic foot problem</h4>
      <p>'Diabetic foot problem' refers to any problem affecting the feet in people with diabetes that is caused by loss of sensation (peripheral sensory neuropathy) and/or circulation problems (peripheral arterial disease).</p>
      <p>Diabetic foot problems include:</p>
      <div class="itemizedlist">
        <ul class="itemizedlist">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>diabetic foot ulcers</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>soft tissue infection</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>destruction of the deep tissues such as heel pressure sores</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>osteomyelitis (bone infection)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>Charcot arthropathy.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
      <p>This guideline uses 'diabetic foot problem' throughout, because this is the term healthcare professionals will most commonly recognise for foot problems in people with diabetes. We do not mean to imply that people with diabetes should be blamed for their foot problems, and they should still be treated as individuals with their own needs, preferences and values. </p>
    </div>
  </div>
