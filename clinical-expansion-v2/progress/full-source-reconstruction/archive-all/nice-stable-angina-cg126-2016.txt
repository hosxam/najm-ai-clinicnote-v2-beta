<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg126-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
    <p>Recommendations 1.5.2 and 1.5.12 partially update recommendation 1.2 of the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta73" target="_top" data-original-url="http://guidance.nice.org.uk/TA73">NICE technology appraisal guidance on myocardial perfusion scintigraphy for the diagnosis and management of angina and myocardial infarction</a>.</p>
  </div>
  <p>The following guidance is for people who have a diagnosis of stable angina and is based on the best available evidence. The <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg126/evidence" target="_top">full guideline</a> gives details of the methods and the evidence used to develop the guidance.</p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Diagnosis" id="cg126-diagnosis">
    <h3 class="title" id="diagnosis">1.1 Diagnosis</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Diagnose stable angina according to the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg95" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG95">NICE guideline on recent-onset chest pain of suspected cardiac origin</a>. Diagnose and manage unstable angina and NSTEMI according to the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg95" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG95">NICE guidelines on recent-onset chest pain of suspected cardiac origin</a>, <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg94" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG94">unstable angina and NSTEMI</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng185">acute coronary syndromes</a>.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Information and support for people with stable angina" id="cg126-information-and-support-for-people-with-stable-angina">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-and-support-for-people-with-stable-angina">1.2 Information and support for people with stable angina</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Clearly explain stable angina to the person, including factors that can provoke angina (for example, exertion, emotional stress, exposure to cold, eating a heavy meal) and its long-term course and management. When relevant, involve the person's family or carers in the discussion.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Encourage the person with stable angina to ask questions about their angina and its treatment. Provide opportunities for them to voice their concerns and fears.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Discuss the person's, and if appropriate, their family or carer's ideas, concerns and expectations about their condition, prognosis and treatment. Explore and address any misconceptions about stable angina and its implications for daily activities, heart attack risk and life expectancy.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Advise the person with stable angina to seek professional help if there is a sudden worsening in the frequency or severity of their angina. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Discuss with the person the purpose and any risks and benefits of their treatment. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Assess the person's need for lifestyle advice (for example about exercise, stopping smoking, diet and weight control) and psychological support, and offer interventions as necessary.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Explore and address issues according to the person's needs, which may include:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>self-management skills such as pacing their activities and goal setting</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>concerns about the impact of stress, anxiety or depression on angina</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>advice about physical exertion including sexual activity.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 General principles for treating people with stable angina" id="cg126-general-principles-for-treating-people-with-stable-angina">
    <h3 class="title" id="general-principles-for-treating-people-with-stable-angina">1.3 General principles for treating people with stable angina</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not exclude people with stable angina from treatment based on their age alone.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not investigate or treat symptoms of stable angina differently in men and women or in different ethnic groups.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <div class="section" title="Preventing and treating episodes of angina" id="cg126-preventing-and-treating-episodes-of-angina">
      <h4 class="title" id="preventing-and-treating-episodes-of-angina">Preventing and treating episodes of angina</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer a short-acting nitrate for preventing and treating episodes of angina. Advise people with stable angina:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to administer the short-acting nitrate</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>to use it immediately before any planned exercise or exertion</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>that side effects such as flushing, headache and light-headedness may occur</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>to sit down or find something to hold on to if feeling light-headed.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When a short-acting nitrate is being used to treat episodes of angina, advise people:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>to repeat the dose after 5 minutes if the pain has not gone</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>to call an emergency ambulance if the pain has not gone 5 minutes after taking a second dose.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Drugs for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease" id="cg126-drugs-for-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">
      <h4 class="title" id="drugs-for-secondary-prevention-of-cardiovascular-disease">Drugs for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider aspirin 75 mg daily for people with stable angina, taking into account the risk of bleeding and comorbidities.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitors for people with stable angina and diabetes. Offer or continue ACE inhibitors for other conditions, in line with relevant NICE guidance. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer statin treatment in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg67" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG67">NICE guideline on lipid modification</a>. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer treatment for high blood pressure in line with the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg127" target="_top" data-original-url="http://guidance.nice.org.uk/CG127">NICE guideline on hypertension</a>.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Dietary supplements" id="cg126-dietary-supplements">
      <h4 class="title" id="dietary-supplements">Dietary supplements</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_3_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.3.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer vitamin or fish oil supplements to treat stable angina. Inform people that there is no evidence that they help stable angina. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Anti-anginal drug treatment" id="cg126-anti-anginal-drug-treatment">
    <h3 class="title" id="anti-anginal-drug-treatment">1.4 Anti-anginal drug treatment</h3>
    <div class="section" title="General recommendations" id="cg126-general-recommendations">
      <h4 class="title" id="general-recommendations">General recommendations</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer people optimal drug treatment for the initial management of stable angina. Optimal drug treatment consists of one or two anti-anginal drugs as necessary plus drugs for secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise people that the aim of anti-anginal drug treatment is to prevent episodes of angina and the aim of secondary prevention treatment is to prevent cardiovascular events such as heart attack and stroke.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss how side effects of drug treatment might affect the person's daily activities and explain why it is important to take drug treatment regularly.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Patients differ in the type and amount of information they need and want. Therefore the provision of information should be individualised and is likely to include, but not be limited to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what the medicine is </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how the medicine is likely to affect their condition (that is, its benefits) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>likely or significant adverse effects and what to do if they think they are experiencing them</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to use the medicine </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what to do if they miss a dose</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>whether further courses of the medicine will be needed after the first prescription</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to get further supplies of medicines. [This recommendation is from the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG76">NICE guideline on medicines adherence</a>.]</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Review the person's response to treatment, including any side effects, 2 to 4 weeks after starting or changing drug treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Titrate the drug dosage against the person's symptoms up to the maximum tolerable dosage.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Drugs for treating stable angina" id="cg126-drugs-for-treating-stable-angina">
      <h4 class="title" id="drugs-for-treating-stable-angina">Drugs for treating stable angina</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer either a beta blocker or a calcium channel blocker as first-line treatment for stable angina. Decide which drug to use based on comorbidities, contraindications and the person's preference.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person cannot tolerate the beta blocker or calcium channel blocker, consider switching to the other option (calcium channel blocker or beta blocker).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person's symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled on a beta blocker or a calcium channel blocker, consider either switching to the other option or using a combination of the two. <br /><br />When combining a calcium channel blocker with a beta blocker, use a dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker, for example, slow release nifedipine, amlodipine or felodipine.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not routinely offer anti-anginal drugs other than beta blockers or calcium channel blockers as first-line treatment for stable angina.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the person cannot tolerate beta blockers and calcium channel blockers or both are contraindicated, consider monotherapy with one of the following drugs: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a long-acting nitrate <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ivabradine <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>nicorandil <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ranolazine.<br /><br />Decide which drug to use based on comorbidities, contraindications, the person's preference and drug costs.<br /><br />Since this guidance was produced, the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) have published new advice about safety concerns related to ivabradine (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ivabradine-carefully-monitor-for-bradycardia" target="_top">June 2014</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ivabradine-procoralan-in-the-symptomatic-treatment-of-angina-risk-of-cardiac-side-effects" target="_top">December 2014</a>) and nicorandil (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/nicorandil-ikorel-now-second-line-treatment-for-angina-risk-of-ulcer-complications" target="_top">January 2016</a>).</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>For people on beta blocker or calcium channel blocker monotherapy whose symptoms are not controlled and the other option (calcium channel blocker or beta blocker) is contraindicated or not tolerated, consider one of the following as an additional drug:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>a long-acting nitrate <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ivabradine <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>nicorandil <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>ranolazine.<br /><br />Decide which drug to use based on comorbidities, contraindications, the person's preference and drug costs. <br /><br />Since this guidance was produced, the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) have published new advice about safety concerns related to ivabradine (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ivabradine-carefully-monitor-for-bradycardia" target="_top">June 2014</a> and <a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/ivabradine-procoralan-in-the-symptomatic-treatment-of-angina-risk-of-cardiac-side-effects" target="_top">December 2014</a>) and nicorandil (<a class="link" href="https://www.gov.uk/drug-safety-update/nicorandil-ikorel-now-second-line-treatment-for-angina-risk-of-ulcer-complications" target="_top">January 2016</a>).<br /><br />When combining ivabradine with a calcium channel blocker, use a dihydropyridine calcium channel blocker, for example, slow release nifedipine, amlodipine, or felodipine.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not offer a third anti-anginal drug to people whose stable angina is controlled with two anti-anginal drugs.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_4_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.4.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider adding a third anti-anginal drug only when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the person's symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with two anti-anginal drugs <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the person is waiting for revascularisation or revascularisation is not considered appropriate or acceptable.<br /><br />Decide which drug to use based on comorbidities, contraindications, the person's preference and drug costs.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.5 Investigation and revascularisation" id="cg126-investigation-and-revascularisation">
    <h3 class="title" id="investigation-and-revascularisation">1.5 Investigation and revascularisation</h3>
    <div class="section" title="People with stable angina whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment" id="cg126-people-with-stable-angina-whose-symptoms-are-not-satisfactorily-controlled-with-optimal-medical">
      <h4 class="title" id="people-with-stable-angina-whose-symptoms-are-not-satisfactorily-controlled-with-optimal-medical">People with stable angina whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider revascularisation (coronary artery bypass graft [CABG] or percutaneous coronary intervention [PCI]) for people with stable angina whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer coronary angiography to guide treatment strategy for people with stable angina whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment. Additional non-invasive or invasive functional testing may be required to evaluate angiographic findings and guide treatment decisions. [This recommendation partially updates recommendation 1.2 of the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta73" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/TA73">NICE technology appraisal guidance on myocardial perfusion scintigraphy for the diagnosis and management of angina and myocardial infarction</a>.]</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer CABG to people with stable angina and suitable coronary anatomy when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>revascularisation is considered appropriate <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>PCI is not appropriate.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer PCI to people with stable angina and suitable coronary anatomy when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>revascularisation is considered appropriate <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>CABG is not appropriate. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When either procedure would be appropriate, explain to the person the risks and benefits of PCI and CABG for people with anatomically less complex disease whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment. If the person does not express a preference, take account of the evidence that suggests that PCI may be the more cost-effective procedure in selecting the course of treatment. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>When either procedure would be appropriate, take into account the potential survival advantage of CABG over PCI for people with multivessel disease whose symptoms are not satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment and who:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have diabetes <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>are over 65 years <strong>or</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>have anatomically complex three-vessel disease, with or without involvement of the left main stem.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider the relative risks and benefits of CABG and PCI for people with stable angina using a systematic approach to assess the severity and complexity of the person's coronary disease, in addition to other relevant clinical factors and comorbidities. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure that there is a regular multidisciplinary team meeting to discuss the risks and benefits of continuing drug treatment or revascularisation strategy (CABG or PCI) for people with stable angina. The team should include cardiac surgeons and interventional cardiologists. Treatment strategy should be discussed for the following people, including but not limited to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people with left main stem or anatomically complex three-vessel disease </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>people in whom there is doubt about the best method of revascularisation because of the complexity of the coronary anatomy, the extent of stenting required or other relevant clinical factors and comorbidities. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ensure people with stable angina receive balanced information and have the opportunity to discuss the benefits, limitations and risks of continuing drug treatment, CABG and PCI to help them make an informed decision about their treatment. When either revascularisation procedure is appropriate, explain to the person:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>The main purpose of revascularisation is to improve the symptoms of stable angina.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>CABG and PCI are effective in relieving symptoms. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Repeat revascularisation may be necessary after either CABG or PCI and the rate is lower after CABG. </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>Stroke is uncommon after either CABG or PCI, and the incidence is similar between the two procedures.</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>There is a potential survival advantage with CABG for some people with multivessel disease.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Inform the person about the practical aspects of CABG and PCI. Include information about:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>vein and/or artery harvesting</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>likely length of hospital stay</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>recovery time</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>drug treatment after the procedure.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="People with stable angina whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment" id="cg126-people-with-stable-angina-whose-symptoms-are-satisfactorily-controlled-with-optimal-medical">
      <h4 class="title" id="people-with-stable-angina-whose-symptoms-are-satisfactorily-controlled-with-optimal-medical">People with stable angina whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment</h4>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss the following with people whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>their prognosis without further investigation </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the likelihood of having left main stem disease or proximal three-vessel disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the availability of CABG to improve the prognosis in a subgroup of people with left main stem or proximal three-vessel disease</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the process and risks of investigation</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the benefits and risks of CABG, including the potential survival gain. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>After discussion (see 1.5.11) with people whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment, consider a functional or non-invasive anatomical test to identify people who might gain a survival benefit from surgery. Functional or anatomical test results may already be available from diagnostic assessment. [This recommendation partially updates recommendation 1.2 of the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ta73" target="_top" data-original-url="http://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/TA73">NICE technology appraisal guidance on myocardial perfusion scintigraphy for the diagnosis and management of angina and myocardial infarction</a>.]</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>After discussion (see 1.5.11) with people whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment, consider coronary angiography when:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>functional testing indicates extensive ischaemia or non-invasive anatomical testing indicates the likelihood of left main stem or proximal three-vessel disease <strong>and</strong></p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>revascularisation is acceptable and appropriate. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg126-1_5_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.5.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider CABG for people with stable angina and suitable coronary anatomy whose symptoms are satisfactorily controlled with optimal medical treatment, but coronary angiography indicates left main stem disease or proximal three-vessel disease.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.6 Pain interventions" id="cg126-pain-interventions">
    <h3 class="title" id="pain-interventions">1.6 Pain interventions</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_6_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.6.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not offer the following interventions to manage stable angina:</p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>enhanced external counterpulsation (EECP)</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>acupuncture.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.7 Stable angina that has not responded to treatment" id="cg126-stable-angina-that-has-not-responded-to-treatment">
    <h3 class="title" id="stable-angina-that-has-not-responded-to-treatment">1.7 Stable angina that has not responded to treatment</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_7_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.7.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer people whose stable angina has not responded to drug treatment and/or revascularisation comprehensive re-evaluation and advice, which may include: </p>
        <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>exploring the person's understanding of their condition</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>exploring the impact of symptoms on the person's quality of life</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>reviewing the diagnosis and considering non-ischaemic causes of pain</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>reviewing drug treatment and considering future drug treatment and revascularisation options</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>acknowledging the limitations of future treatment</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>explaining how the person can manage the pain themselves </p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>specific attention to the role of psychological factors in pain</p>
          </li>
          <li class="listitem">
            <p>development of skills to modify cognitions and behaviours associated with pain.</p>
          </li>
        </ul>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.8 Cardiac syndrome X" id="cg126-cardiac-syndrome-x">
    <h3 class="title" id="cardiac-syndrome-x">1.8 Cardiac syndrome X</h3>
    <article id="cg126-1_8_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>In people with angiographically normal coronary arteries and continuing anginal symptoms, consider a diagnosis of cardiac syndrome X.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_8_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Continue drug treatment for stable angina only if it improves the symptoms of the person with suspected cardiac syndrome X.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg126-1_8_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.8.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Do not routinely offer drugs for the secondary prevention of cardiovascular disease to people with suspected cardiac syndrome X.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
