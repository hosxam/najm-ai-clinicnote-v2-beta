<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="cg76-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/nice-and-the-public/making-decisions-about-your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>.</p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <p>The following guidance is based on the best available evidence. The <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg76/evidence" target="_top">full guideline</a> gives details of the methods and the evidence used to develop the guidance.</p>
  <p>
    <a class="link invalid-link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/proxy/?sourceUrl=_Toc204654970" target="_top" data-original-url="_Toc204654970">Recommendation 1.4.2 has been replaced by recommendations in the NICE guideline on medicines optimisation</a>.</p>
  <p>These recommendations apply to all healthcare professionals who prescribe, dispense or review medicines or who have a role in making decisions about medicines with patients. Healthcare professionals are reminded of their duty under the Equality Act (2010) to make reasonable adjustments to ensure that all people have the same opportunity for health. </p>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Patient involvement in decisions about medicines" id="cg76-patient-involvement-in-decisions-about-medicines">
    <h3 class="title" id="patient-involvement-in-decisions-about-medicines">1.1 Patient involvement in decisions about medicines</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Communication" id="cg76-communication">
      <h4 class="title" id="communication">Communication</h4>
      <p>Good communication between healthcare professionals and patients is needed for involvement of patients in decisions about medicines and for supporting adherence. Some patients may find it easier to communicate with their healthcare professional than others. </p>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Healthcare professionals should adapt their consultation style to the needs of individual patients so that all patients have the opportunity to be involved in decisions about their medicines at the level they wish.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider any factors such as physical or learning disabilities, sight or hearing problems and difficulties with reading or speaking English, which may affect the patient's involvement in the consultation. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Establish the most effective way of communicating with each patient and, if necessary, consider ways of making information accessible and understandable (for example, using pictures, symbols, large print, different languages, an interpreter or a patient advocate).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage patients to ask about their condition and treatment. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask patients open˗ended questions because these are more likely to uncover patients' concerns. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that the consultation skills needed for increasing patient involvement can be improved.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Increasing patient involvement" id="cg76-increasing-patient-involvement">
      <h4 class="title" id="increasing-patient-involvement">Increasing patient involvement</h4>
      <p>Patient involvement in the decision˗making process requires that healthcare professionals acknowledge patients' views about their condition and its treatment, and that both healthcare professional and patient have a role in making decisions about treatment. Simple interventions to increase patient involvement do not necessarily increase the overall length of consultation and may be justified by benefits, particularly over the course of a long˗term condition.</p>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer all patients the opportunity to be involved in making decisions about prescribed medicines. Establish what level of involvement in decision-making the patient would like. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss with the patient why they might benefit from the treatment. Clearly explain the disease or condition and how the medicine will influence this. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Explain the medical aims of the treatment to patients and openly discuss the pros and cons of proposed medicines. The discussion should be at the level preferred by the patient. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Clarify what the patient hopes the treatment will achieve. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Avoid making assumptions about patient preferences about treatment. Talk to the patient to find out their preferences, and note any non˗verbal cues that may indicate you need to explore the patient's perspective further. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Healthcare professionals have a duty to help patients to make decisions about their treatment based on an understanding of the likely benefits and risks rather than on misconceptions. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_13" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.13</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Accept that patients may have different views from healthcare professionals about the balance of risks, benefits and side effects of medicines. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_14" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.14</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that increasing patient involvement may mean that the patient decides not to take or to stop taking a medicine. If in the healthcare professional's view this could have an adverse effect, then the information provided to the patient on risks and benefits and the patient's decision should be recorded.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_15" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.15</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Accept that the patient has the right to decide not to take a medicine, even if you do not agree with the decision, as long as the patient has the capacity to make an informed decision and has been provided with the information needed to make such a decision. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_16" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.16</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Assess the patient's capacity to make each decision using the <a class="link" href="http://www.opsi.gov.uk/ACTS/acts2005/ukpga_20050009_en_1" target="_top">principles in the Mental Capacity Act 2005</a>. To lack capacity patients must: (a) have an impairment of or disturbance or malfunction of brain and mind, and (b) demonstrate lack of capacity to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>understand the information relevant to the decision</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>retain information for long enough to use it in the decision</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>use or weigh information as part of the process of making the decision</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>communicate the decision (whether by talking, using sign language or any other means). <br /><br />More information is available in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng108" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng108">NICE's guideline on decision-making and mental capacity</a>.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_17" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.17</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If the patient has specific concerns, record a summary of the discussion, because this may be helpful in future consultations. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_18" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.18</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Encourage and support patients, families and carers to keep an up˗to˗date list of all medicines the patient is taking. The list should include the names and dosages of prescription and non˗prescription medicines and herbal and nutritional supplements. If the patient has any allergic or adverse reactions to medicines, these should be noted. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Understanding the patient's knowledge, beliefs and concerns about medicines" id="cg76-understanding-the-patients-knowledge-beliefs-and-concerns-about-medicines">
      <h4 class="title" id="understanding-the-patients-knowledge-beliefs-and-concerns-about-medicines">Understanding the patient's knowledge, beliefs and concerns about medicines</h4>
      <p>There is evidence that patients make decisions about medicines based on their understanding of their condition and the possible treatments, their view of their own need for the medicine and their concerns about the medicine. </p>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_19" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.19</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that patients' concerns about medicines, and whether they believe they need them, affect how and whether they take their prescribed medicines.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_20" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.20</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask patients what they know, believe and understand about medicines before prescribing new treatments and when reviewing medicines. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_21" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.21</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask if the patient has any specific concerns about their medicines, whenever you prescribe, dispense or review medicines. These may include concerns about becoming dependent on medicines and concerns about adverse effects. Address these concerns. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_22" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.22</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that patients may wish to minimise how much medicine they take. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_23" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.23</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that patients may wish to discuss: </p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what will happen if they do not take the medicine suggested by their healthcare professional</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>non˗pharmacological alternatives to medicines</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to reduce and stop medicines they may have been taking for a long time, particularly those known to be associated with withdrawal symptoms</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to fit taking the medicine into their daily routine</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to make a choice between medicines if they believe they are taking too many medicines. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Providing information" id="cg76-providing-information">
      <h4 class="title" id="providing-information">Providing information</h4>
      <p>Patients need information about their condition and possible treatments if they are to be involved in making informed decisions about medicines. The format and content of the information provided should meet the needs of individual patients.</p>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_24" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.24</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer patients information about medicines before the medicines are prescribed. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_25" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.25</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Offer patients information that is relevant to their condition, possible treatments and personal circumstances, and that is easy to understand and free from jargon. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_26" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.26</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Check that patients have any information they wish about medicines when the medicines are dispensed. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_27" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.27</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Discuss information on medicines with the patient rather than just presenting it. The discussion should take into account what the patient understands and believes about the condition and treatment. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_28" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.28</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not assume that the patient information leaflets (PILs) that patients receive with their medicines will meet each patient's needs. Address concerns that patients may have after reading the standard PILs.<br /><br />PILs contain information for patients on how medicines should be used. It is a legal requirement that this information is included on the label or within the packaging of a medicine.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_29" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.29</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Patients differ in the type and amount of information they need and want. Therefore, the provision of information should be individualised and is likely to include, but not be limited to:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what the medicine is </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how the medicine is likely to affect their condition (that is, its benefits) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>likely or significant adverse effects and what to do if they think they are experiencing them</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to use the medicine </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what to do if they miss a dose</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>whether further courses of the medicine will be needed after the first prescription</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to get further supplies of medicines.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_30" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.30</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be careful not to make assumptions about a patient's ability to understand the information provided. Check with the patient that they have understood the information. Information for patients should be clear and logical and, if possible, tailored to the needs of the individual patient. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_31" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.31</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Suggest where patients might find reliable information and support after the consultation: for example, by providing written information or directing them to other resources (for example, <a class="link" href="http://www.nhs.uk/" target="_top">the NHS website</a>).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_1_32" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.1.32</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Provide inpatients with the same information as patients in other settings. Information should include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what the medicine is </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how the medicine is likely to affect their condition (that is, its benefits) </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>likely or significant adverse effects and what to do if they think they are experiencing them</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to use the medicine </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>what to do if they miss a dose</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>whether further courses of the medicine will be needed after the first prescription</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>how to get further supply after discharge.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Supporting adherence" id="cg76-supporting-adherence">
    <h3 class="title" id="supporting-adherence">1.2 Supporting adherence</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Assessing adherence" id="cg76-assessing-adherence">
      <h4 class="title" id="assessing-adherence">Assessing adherence</h4>
      <p>Patients do not always take their medicines exactly as prescribed, and healthcare professionals are often unaware of how patients take their medicines. The purpose of assessing adherence is not to monitor patients but rather to find out whether patients need more information and support.</p>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Recognise that non˗adherence is common and that most patients are non˗adherent sometimes. Routinely assess adherence in a non˗judgemental way whenever you prescribe, dispense and review medicines. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider assessing non-adherence by asking the patient if they have missed any doses of medicine recently. Make it easier for them to report non˗adherence by:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>asking the question in a way that does not apportion blame </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>explaining why you are asking the question</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>mentioning a specific time period such as 'in the past week'</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>asking about medicine-taking behaviours such as reducing the dose, stopping and starting medicines. </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider using records of prescription re˗ordering, pharmacy patient medication records and return of unused medicines to identify potential non˗adherence and patients needing additional support. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Interventions to increase adherence" id="cg76-interventions-to-increase-adherence">
      <h4 class="title" id="interventions-to-increase-adherence">Interventions to increase adherence</h4>
      <p>Patients may need support to help them make the most effective use of their medicines. This support may take the form of further information and discussion, or involve practical changes to the type of medicine or the regimen. Any interventions to support adherence should be considered on a case˗by˗case basis and should address the concerns and needs of individual patients.</p>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If a patient is not taking their medicines, discuss with them whether this is because of beliefs and concerns or problems about the medicines (intentional non˗adherence) or because of practical problems (unintentional non˗adherence).</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Be aware that although adherence can be improved, no specific intervention can be recommended for all patients. Tailor any intervention to increase adherence to the specific difficulties with adherence the patient is experiencing.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Find out what form of support the patient would prefer to increase their adherence to medicines. Together, you and your patient should consider options for support.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Address any beliefs and concerns that patients have that result in reduced adherence.</p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Because evidence supporting interventions to increase adherence is inconclusive, only use interventions to overcome practical problems associated with non˗adherence if a specific need is identified. Target the intervention to the need. Interventions might include:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>suggesting that patients record their medicine˗taking</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>encouraging patients to monitor their condition</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>simplifying the dosing regimen</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>using alternative packaging for the medicine</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>using a multi˗compartment medicines system.</p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Side effects can be a problem for some patients. If this is the case you should:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss how the patient would like to deal with side effects</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>discuss the benefits, side effects and long˗term effects with the patient to allow them to make an informed choice</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider adjusting the dosage</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider switching to another medicine with a different risk of side effects</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>consider what other strategies might be used (for example, timing of medicines). </p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="cg76-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Ask patients if prescriptions charges are a problem for them. If they are, consider possible options to reduce costs. </p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.3 Reviewing medicines" id="cg76-reviewing-medicines">
    <h3 class="title" id="reviewing-medicines">1.3 Reviewing medicines</h3>
    <p>Patients may use medicines long term. The initial decision to prescribe medicines, the patient's experience of using the medicines and the patient's needs for adherence support should be reviewed regularly. The patient's own list of medicines may be a useful aid in a medicines review.</p>
    <article id="cg76-1_3_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review patient knowledge, understanding and concerns about medicines, and a patient's view of their need for medicine at intervals agreed with the patient, because these may change over time. Offer repeat information and review to patients, especially when treating long˗term conditions with multiple medicines.</p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg76-1_3_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Review at regular intervals the decision to prescribe medicines, according to patient choice and need. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg76-1_3_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Enquire about adherence when reviewing medicines. If non˗adherence is identified, clarify possible causes and agree any action with the patient. Any plan should include a date for a follow˗up review. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg76-1_3_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.3.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Be aware that patients sometimes evaluate prescribed medicines using their own criteria such as their understanding of their condition or the symptoms most troubling to them. They may, for example, stop and start the medicine or alter the dose and check how this affects their symptoms. Ask the patient whether they have done this. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.4 Communication between healthcare professionals" id="cg76-communication-between-healthcare-professionals">
    <h3 class="title" id="communication-between-healthcare-professionals">1.4 Communication between healthcare professionals</h3>
    <p>Patients may be under the care of healthcare professionals from different disciplines and specialties at the same time; responsibility for patients' care may be transferred between healthcare professionals, and medicines reviews may be carried out by healthcare professionals other than the prescriber. Therefore good communication between healthcare professionals is required to ensure that fragmentation of care does not occur.</p>
    <article id="cg76-1_4_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Healthcare professionals involved in prescribing, dispensing or reviewing medicines should ensure that there are robust processes for communicating with other healthcare professionals involved in the patient's care. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg76-1_4_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>This recommendation has been replaced by recommendations in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng5/chapter/1-Recommendations#medicines-related-communication-systems-when-patients-move-from-one-care-setting-to-another" target="_top">section 1.2 in the NICE guideline on medicines optimisation</a>. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="cg76-1_4_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.4.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Healthcare professionals involved in reviewing medicines should inform the prescriber of the review and its outcome. This is particularly important if the review involves discussion of difficulties with adherence and further review is necessary. </p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
