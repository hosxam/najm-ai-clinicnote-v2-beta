<div class="chapter" title="Recommendations" id="ng125-recommendations" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml">
  <h2 class="title" id="recommendations">Recommendations</h2>
  <div class="panel panel--primary">
    <p>People have the right to be involved in discussions and make informed decisions about their care, as described in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/nice-communities/public-involvement/your-care" target="_top">NICE's information on making decisions about your care</a>. </p>
    <p>
      <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/about/what-we-do/our-programmes/nice-guidance/nice-guidelines/using-NICE-guidelines-to-make-decisions" target="_top">Making decisions using NICE guidelines</a> explains how we use words to show the strength (or certainty) of our recommendations, and has information about prescribing medicines (including off-label use), professional guidelines, standards and laws (including on consent and mental capacity), and safeguarding.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.1 Information for patients and carers" id="ng125-information-for-patients-and-carers">
    <h3 class="title" id="information-for-patients-and-carers">1.1 Information for patients and carers</h3>
    <article id="ng125-1_1_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.1</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer patients and carers clear, consistent information and advice throughout all stages of their care. This should include the risks of <a class="link" href="recommendations#surgical-site-wound-infection" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#surgical-site-wound-infection">surgical site infections</a>, what is being done to reduce them and how they are managed. For more guidance on providing information to adults and discussing their preferences with them, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/cg138" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/CG138/">NICE guideline on patient experience in adult NHS services</a>. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng125-1_1_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.2</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer patients and carers information and advice on how to care for their wound after discharge. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng125-1_1_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.3</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Offer patients and carers information and advice about how to recognise a surgical site infection and who to contact if they are concerned. Use an integrated care pathway for healthcare-associated infections to help communicate this information to both patients and all those involved in their care after discharge. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
    <article id="ng125-1_1_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
      <h4 class="recommendation__number">1.1.4</h4>
      <div class="recommendation__body">
        <p>Always inform patients after their operation if they have been given antibiotics. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
      </div>
    </article>
  </div>
  <div class="section" title="1.2 Preoperative phase" id="ng125-preoperative-phase">
    <h3 class="title" id="preoperative-phase">1.2 Preoperative phase</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Preoperative showering" id="ng125-preoperative-showering">
      <h4 class="title" id="preoperative-showering">Preoperative showering</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_1" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.1</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Advise patients to shower or have a bath (or help patients to shower, bath or bed bath) using soap, either the day before, or on the day of, surgery. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Nasal decolonisation" id="ng125-nasal-decolonisation">
      <h4 class="title" id="nasal-decolonisation">Nasal decolonisation</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_2" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.2</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Consider nasal mupirocin in combination with a chlorhexidine body wash before procedures in which Staphylococcus aureus is a likely cause of a surgical site infection. This should be locally determined and take into account:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the type of procedure </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>individual patient risk factors</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the increased risk of side effects in preterm infants (see the <a class="link" href="recommendations#antiseptic-skin-preparation" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#antiseptic-skin-preparation">recommendation on risks in babies in the section on antiseptic skin preparation</a>)</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>the potential impact of infection. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
            </li>
          </ul>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_3" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.3</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Maintain surveillance on antimicrobial resistance associated with the use of mupirocin. For information on antimicrobial stewardship programmes, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15" target="_top" data-original-url="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng15">NICE guideline on antimicrobial stewardship: systems and processes for effective antimicrobial medicine use</a>. <strong>[2019]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <div class="panel">
        <p>For a short explanation of why the committee made the 2019 recommendations and how they might affect practice, see the <a class="link" href="rationale-and-impact#nasal-decolonisation-2" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="rationale-and-impact" data-original-url="#nasal-decolonisation-2">rationale and impact section on nasal decolonisation</a>.</p>
        <p>Full details of the evidence and the committee's discussion are in <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng125/evidence/nasal-decontamination-in-the-prevention-of-surgical-site-infection-pdf-6727104398" target="_top">evidence review A: nasal decontamination in the prevention of surgical site infection</a>. </p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hair removal" id="ng125-hair-removal">
      <h4 class="title" id="hair-removal">Hair removal</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_4" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.4</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use hair removal routinely to reduce the risk of surgical site infection. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_5" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.5</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>If hair has to be removed, use electric clippers with a single-use head on the day of surgery. Do not use razors for hair removal, because they increase the risk of surgical site infection. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Patient theatre wear" id="ng125-patient-theatre-wear">
      <h4 class="title" id="patient-theatre-wear">Patient theatre wear</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_6" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.6</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give patients specific theatre wear that is appropriate for the procedure and clinical setting, and that provides easy access to the operative site and areas for placing devices, such as intravenous cannulas. Take into account the patient's comfort and dignity. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Staff theatre wear" id="ng125-staff-theatre-wear">
      <h4 class="title" id="staff-theatre-wear">Staff theatre wear</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_7" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.7</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>All staff should wear specific non-sterile theatre wear in all areas where operations are undertaken. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Staff leaving the operating area" id="ng125-staff-leaving-the-operating-area">
      <h4 class="title" id="staff-leaving-the-operating-area">Staff leaving the operating area</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_8" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.8</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Staff wearing non-sterile theatre wear should keep their movements in and out of the operating area to a minimum. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Mechanical bowel preparation" id="ng125-mechanical-bowel-preparation">
      <h4 class="title" id="mechanical-bowel-preparation">Mechanical bowel preparation</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_9" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.9</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Do not use mechanical bowel preparation routinely to reduce the risk of surgical site infection. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Hand jewellery, artificial nails and nail polish" id="ng125-hand-jewellery-artificial-nails-and-nail-polish">
      <h4 class="title" id="hand-jewellery-artificial-nails-and-nail-polish">Hand jewellery, artificial nails and nail polish</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_10" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.10</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>The operating team should remove hand jewellery before operations. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_11" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.11</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>The operating team should remove artificial nails and nail polish before operations. <strong>[2008]</strong></p>
        </div>
      </article>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Antibiotic prophylaxis" id="ng125-antibiotic-prophylaxis">
      <h4 class="title" id="antibiotic-prophylaxis">Antibiotic prophylaxis</h4>
      <article id="ng125-1_2_12" class="numbered-paragraph recommendation">
        <h5 class="recommendation__number">1.2.12</h5>
        <div class="recommendation__body">
          <p>Give antibiotic prophylaxis to patients before:</p>
          <ul class="itemizedlist indented">
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#surgical-wound-classification" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#surgical-wound-classification">clean surgery</a> involving the placement of a prosthesis or implant</p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#surgical-wound-classification" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#surgical-wound-classification">clean-contaminated surgery</a>
              </p>
            </li>
            <li class="listitem">
              <p>
                <a class="link" href="recommendations#surgical-wound-classification" target="_top" rel="internal" data-chapter="recommendations" data-original-url="#surgical-wound-classification">contaminated surgery</a>. <strong>[2008]<br /><br /></strong>For advice on antibiotic prophylaxis before caesarean birth, see the <a class="link" href="https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng192/chapter/Recommendations#timing-of-antibiotic-administration" target="_top">section on timing of antibiotic administration in NICE's guideline on caesarean birth</a>.   <div class="section" title="Terms used in this guideline" id="ng125-terms-used-in-this-guideline">
    <h3 class="title" id="terms-used-in-this-guideline">Terms used in this guideline</h3>
    <div class="section" title="Decolonisation" id="ng125-decolonisation">
      <h4 class="title" id="decolonisation">Decolonisation</h4>
      <p>The process of eradicating or reducing asymptomatic carriage of methicillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA). This used to be referred to as decontamination. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Healing by primary intention" id="ng125-healing-by-primary-intention">
      <h4 class="title" id="healing-by-primary-intention">Healing by primary intention</h4>
      <p>Occurs when a wound has been sutured after an operation and heals to leave a minimal, cosmetically acceptable scar.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Healing by secondary intention" id="ng125-healing-by-secondary-intention">
      <h4 class="title" id="healing-by-secondary-intention">Healing by secondary intention</h4>
      <p>Occurs when a wound is deliberately left open at the end of an operation because of excessive bacterial contamination, particularly by anaerobes or when there is a risk of devitalised tissue, which leads to infection and delayed healing. It may be sutured within a few days (delayed primary closure), or much later when the wound is clean and granulating (secondary closure), or left to complete healing naturally without suturing.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Interactive dressing" id="ng125-interactive-dressing">
      <h4 class="title" id="interactive-dressing">Interactive dressing</h4>
      <p>Dressings designed to promote the wound healing process through the creation and maintenance of a local, warm, moist environment underneath the chosen dressing, when left in place for a period indicated through a continuous assessment process. </p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Surgical site (wound) infection" id="ng125-surgical-site-wound-infection">
      <h4 class="title" id="surgical-site-wound-infection">Surgical site (wound) infection</h4>
      <p>A surgical wound with local signs and symptoms of infection, for example, heat, redness, pain and swelling, and (in more serious cases) with systemic signs of fever or a raised white blood cell count. Infection in the surgical wound may prevent healing, causing the wound edges to separate, or it may cause an abscess to form in the deeper tissues. </p>
      <p>Definitions of the severity of surgical site infections vary and this should be taken into account when comparing reported rates of surgical site infection.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="section" title="Surgical wound classification" id="ng125-surgical-wound-classification">
      <h4 class="title" id="surgical-wound-classification">Surgical wound classification</h4>
      <p>Clean: an incision in which no inflammation is encountered in a surgical procedure, without a break in sterile technique, and during which the respiratory, alimentary or genitourinary tracts are not entered.</p>
      <p>Clean-contaminated: an incision through which the respiratory, alimentary, or genitourinary tract is entered under controlled conditions but with no contamination encountered.</p>
      <p>Contaminated: an incision undertaken during an operation in which there is a major break in sterile technique or gross spillage from the gastrointestinal tract, or an incision in which acute, non-purulent inflammation is encountered. Open traumatic wounds that are more than 12 to 24 hours old also fall into this category.</p>
      <p>Dirty or infected: an incision undertaken during an operation in which the viscera are perforated or when acute inflammation with pus is encountered (for example, emergency surgery for faecal peritonitis), and for traumatic wounds if treatment is delayed, there is faecal contamination, or devitalised tissue is present </p>
    </div>
  </div>
